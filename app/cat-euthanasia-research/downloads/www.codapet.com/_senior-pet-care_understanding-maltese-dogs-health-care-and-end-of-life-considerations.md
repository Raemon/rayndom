# Understanding Maltese Dogs: Health, Care, and End-of-Life Considerations

[Dr. Gary Hsia](/experts/dr-gary-hsia)

July 21, 2025

Maltese dogs often live 12–15 years with proper care. Learn how to support their health with nutrition, grooming, and veterinary care—and how to ensure dignity and comfort in their final days.

## Summary

Maltese dogs are beloved companions known for their longevity and charming personalities. This article explores the various aspects of Maltese health, care, and end-of-life considerations, providing valuable insights for pet owners to ensure their furry friends live long, happy lives.

##### Key takeaways:

  1. Maltese dogs have an average lifespan of 12-15 years with proper care.
  2. Regular veterinary check-ups and a balanced diet are crucial for maintaining Maltese health.
  3. Genetic testing can help identify and manage potential hereditary conditions.
  4. Proper grooming and dental care are essential for Maltese well-being.
  5. Compassionate end-of-life care is important for ensuring dignity in a Maltese's final days.



## Understanding the Maltese Breed

### Physical characteristics of Maltese dogs

Maltese dogs are renowned for their striking appearance, characterized by a pure white, silky, and straight coat that can reach the ground. Their compact size and elegant demeanor contribute to their charm, with adults typically weighing under 7 pounds. The Maltese's expressive face is framed by dark, alert eyes that appear black or very dark brown, enhancing their endearing expression.  
  
A distinctive feature is their black nose, which can sometimes fade to brown or pink due to lack of sunlight or exposure to harsh weather conditions. The breed's facial aesthetics are further accentuated by the desired "halos" - solid black rims around both eyes.

### Temperament and personality traits

The Maltese breed is renowned for its affectionate and gentle temperament, making it an ideal companion dog. These small canines are known for their loving nature, often forming strong bonds with their family members. Despite their diminutive size, Maltese dogs possess a lively and playful personality, maintaining their puppy-like enthusiasm well into adulthood.

This knowledge can help you provide the best care throughout their life, including their final days.

### Historical background and breed origins

The Maltese breed boasts an illustrious history spanning over three millennia, with its origins shrouded in the mists of antiquity. While the exact birthplace of the breed remains debated, many historians believe the Maltese was introduced to the island of Malta by the Phoenicians, who dominated Mediterranean trade before the rise of Greece.  
  
During the Roman Empire, the Maltese achieved unprecedented popularity among the aristocracy, becoming a symbol of status and loyalty. This "Roman Ladies' Dog" was so cherished that it featured prominently in myths, poems, and fables of the era.

## Average Lifespan of Maltese Dogs

### Typical age range for Maltese longevity

The Maltese breed is known for its impressive longevity, with a typical lifespan ranging from 12 to 15 years. This small, robust dog often surpasses the average life expectancy of many other breeds, making it an excellent choice for those seeking a long-term canine companion.  
  
Recent studies have further confirmed the breed's longevity, with a 2024 UK study reporting a life expectancy of 13.1 years for Maltese dogs, slightly higher than the average for purebreds (12.7 years) and crossbreeds (12 years).

### Factors influencing Maltese life expectancy

Several factors play crucial roles in determining a Maltese dog's lifespan:

  1. Genetics: Responsible breeding practices can help mitigate the risk of hereditary health issues that may impact longevity.
  2. Environment: Providing a safe and stimulating environment for Maltese puppies is essential for their proper development and long-term health.
  3. Nutrition: A balanced diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals is crucial to support growth and development.
  4. Exercise: Despite their small size, Maltese still require daily physical activity to stay fit and healthy



### Comparison to other small dog breeds

When comparing the Maltese lifespan to other small dog breeds, they generally fall within a similar range. Maltese dogs typically live between 12 to 15 years, which is comparable to many other toy and small breeds.  
  
It's worth noting that while Maltese dogs have a good average lifespan, individual dogs may live shorter or longer lives depending on factors such as genetics, diet, exercise, and overall care. Regular veterinary check-ups, proper nutrition, and a loving home environment can all contribute to maximizing a Maltese dog's potential for a long and healthy life.

## Health Considerations for Maltese Dogs

### Common health issues affecting Maltese lifespan

Maltese dogs, despite their generally healthy nature, are prone to several health issues that can impact their lifespan. Some common concerns include:

  1. Luxating patella: A condition where the kneecap slips out of place, potentially requiring surgical intervention in severe cases.
  2. Dental problems: Regular tooth brushing and professional cleanings are necessary to maintain oral health.
  3. Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA): An inherited heart condition that can lead to heart failure if left untreated.
  4. Liver shunts: A congenital issue that can cause stunted growth and neurological symptoms.
  5. White Dog Shaker Syndrome: Characterized by tremors and loss of coordination, primarily affecting white-colored dogs like the Maltese.



### Genetic predispositions in the breed

Understanding genetic predispositions is crucial for Maltese owners and breeders to ensure early detection, proper management, and potentially improved breeding practices. Some genetic conditions to be aware of include:

  1. Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA): An inherited degenerative eye condition that can lead to blindness.
  2. Portosystemic shunts: A congenital issue affecting blood flow to the liver, leading to poor growth and neurological symptoms in severe cases.



### Importance of regular veterinary check-ups

Regular veterinary check-ups are paramount for maintaining the health and longevity of Maltese dogs. These visits allow veterinarians to establish baseline health parameters, detect early signs of disease, and monitor age-related changes.  
  
During wellness exams, veterinarians conduct comprehensive evaluations of various body systems, including eyes, ears, mouth, skin, heart, lungs, abdomen, joints, and nervous system. This thorough approach helps identify potential issues before they escalate into more serious conditions.

## Nutrition and Diet for Longevity

### Optimal diet for Maltese health

Providing an optimal diet for Maltese health involves careful consideration of their nutritional needs. Maltese dogs typically require about 45 calories per pound of body weight daily, with smaller dogs needing 1/4 to 1/2 cup of food per day and larger ones consuming 1/2 to 3/4 cups.  
  
When selecting commercial dog food, opt for brands that list a named, whole animal protein as the first ingredient and avoid those with non-meat fillers, artificial preservatives, or unnamed meat products.

### Importance of portion control

Portion control is crucial for maintaining optimal health in Maltese dogs. These small, delicate canines require surprisingly little food to meet their nutritional needs. Generally, Maltese weighing 2 to 4 pounds should consume about 1/4 to 1/2 cup of food per day, while those 5 pounds and above may require 1/2 to 3/4 cups daily.  
  
Overfeeding can quickly lead to obesity in Maltese dogs, potentially exacerbating health issues such as joint problems and heart disease. To maintain an ideal weight, it's recommended to divide their daily food allowance into two or three balanced meals, complemented by regular exercise.

### Supplements that may benefit Maltese dogs

Certain supplements can significantly benefit Maltese dogs:

  1. Fish oil supplements: Provide essential omega-3 fatty acids, improving cognitive health and protecting against osteoarthritis.
  2. Green-lipped mussel supplements: May offer additional support for joint health.
  3. Astaxanthin: A powerful antioxidant that has demonstrated improvements in mitochondrial function in dogs.



Always consult with a veterinarian before adding any supplements to your Maltese's diet, as dogs fed a complete and balanced commercial diet typically do not require additional vitamins unless recommended by a professional.

## Exercise and Mental Stimulation

### Appropriate exercise routines for Maltese dogs

Maltese dogs require moderate daily exercise to maintain their health and happiness. Generally, 30 minutes to 1 hour of physical activity per day is sufficient for most Maltese, which can be divided into shorter sessions to prevent fatigue.  
  
A combination of indoor and outdoor activities is ideal for this breed. Indoor play, such as fetch with soft toys or interactive games, can satisfy a significant portion of their exercise needs while providing mental stimulation.

### Mental enrichment activities

Mental enrichment activities are crucial for maintaining cognitive health and preventing boredom in Maltese dogs. Some effective activities include:

  1. Puzzle toys: Offer an excellent way to stimulate their minds during mealtime.
  2. Snuffle mats: Mimic foraging behavior and provide a satisfying mental workout.
  3. Hide-and-seek games: Encourage your Maltese to use their keen sense of smell.
  4. Training sessions: Keep their minds sharp and reinforce the bond between dog and owner.



### Balancing physical activity with rest

Balancing physical activity with rest is crucial for maintaining the health and longevity of Maltese dogs. While these small companions require regular exercise, it's essential to recognize their limits and provide adequate rest periods.  
  
Monitoring signs of exhaustion, such as excessive panting or slowing down, is vital during exercise to avoid overexertion. Incorporating rest periods between activities allows for recovery and prevents stress on their small bodies.

## Grooming and Hygiene for Maltese Health

### Coat care and maintenance

The Maltese's luxurious white coat requires daily care to maintain its pristine condition. Daily brushing or combing is essential to prevent mats and tangles, which can lead to skin infections if left unattended.  
  
Regular bathing, typically every 4 to 6 weeks, keeps their coat clean and healthy. When bathing, it's crucial to rinse thoroughly and dry the hair completely to prevent skin irritation.

### Dental hygiene practices

Dental hygiene is crucial for maintaining the overall health and longevity of Maltese dogs. Their small mouths and teeth make them particularly susceptible to dental issues, including periodontal disease, tooth decay, and gum infections.  
  
To prevent these problems, establish a daily tooth brushing routine using a soft-bristled toothbrush and dog-specific toothpaste. Regular professional dental cleanings, typically every 6-12 months, are essential to remove tartar buildup and assess oral health.

### Eye and ear care routines

Maltese dogs require special attention to their eyes and ears due to their unique physical characteristics. For eye care, regularly check for tear staining, which is common in this breed. Gently wipe the area around the eyes with a damp cloth to remove any discharge and prevent staining.  
  
Ear care is crucial for Maltese due to their drop ears, which can trap moisture and lead to infections. Clean their ears weekly using a veterinarian-approved ear cleaner and cotton balls. Gently wipe the visible part of the ear canal, being careful not to insert anything deep into the ear.

## Environmental Factors Affecting Maltese Lifespan

### Creating a safe home environment

Creating a safe home environment for your Maltese is crucial for their well-being and longevity. Start by puppy-proofing your living space, removing potential hazards such as toxic plants, small objects that could be swallowed, and securing loose wires.  
  
Provide a comfortable bed in a quiet area where your Maltese can retreat when feeling overwhelmed. Install baby gates to restrict access to stairs or dangerous areas, especially for puppies or senior dogs with mobility issues.

### Managing stress and anxiety

Recognizing stress signs in Maltese dogs is crucial for maintaining their health and longevity. Common indicators include pacing, shaking, excessive vocalization, changes in eye and ear positioning, altered body posture, increased shedding, panting without physical exertion, and changes in bodily functions.  
  
To alleviate stress, remove the dog from the stressor and provide a quiet space for regrouping. Engaging in routine commands can offer a sense of normalcy and distraction. Regular exercise, such as walks or play sessions, helps release tension for both the dog and owner.

### Temperature considerations for Maltese comfort

Maltese dogs, with their delicate build and fine coat, require careful temperature management for optimal comfort and health. These small companions thrive in moderate climates, with ideal temperatures ranging between 60°F and 70°F (15.5°C to 21°C).  
  
When outdoor temperatures drop below 45°F (7°C), Maltese owners should exercise caution and limit exposure, as these dogs are particularly susceptible to cold. Conversely, high temperatures can also pose risks for Maltese dogs. Their small size and brachycephalic features make them prone to overheating, necessitating close monitoring when temperatures exceed 85°F (29°C).

## Breeding and Genetics

### Impact of responsible breeding on lifespan

Responsible breeding practices play a crucial role in extending the lifespan of Maltese dogs. Ethical breeders prioritize genetic health testing to identify and eliminate hereditary conditions that could shorten a dog's life.  
  
By carefully selecting breeding pairs based on their health profiles, breeders can reduce the risk of passing on genetic disorders such as luxating patella, progressive retinal atrophy, and portosystemic shunts.

### Genetic testing for hereditary conditions

Genetic testing plays a crucial role in maintaining the health and longevity of Maltese dogs. Responsible breeders utilize DNA tests to screen for inherited conditions such as luxating patella, progressive retinal atrophy, and portosystemic shunts.  
  
These tests help identify carriers and affected individuals, allowing breeders to make informed decisions about breeding pairs and reduce the risk of passing on genetic disorders.

### Choosing a reputable Maltese breeder

When choosing a reputable Maltese breeder, it's crucial to meet them in person or via video conference. Observe the breeder's environment, ensuring it's clean and odor-free, and pay attention to how the dogs interact with the breeder and strangers.  
  
A responsible breeder will be patient, answer all your questions, and provide comprehensive information about the breed's health issues and genetic testing. They should allow you to see the puppy's parents and provide full medical histories, including OFA and CAER certificates.

## End of Life Care: Maltese Euthanasia

Recognizing when it's time for euthanasia is a challenging aspect of Maltese end-of-life care. Pet owners should observe their dog's daily activities, noting if moments of discomfort outweigh the capacity to enjoy life. Signs such as excessive panting, reclusiveness, reluctance to move, and food pickiness may indicate pain or distress.  
  
Consulting with a veterinarian is crucial, as they can provide medical insights and guide the decision-making process. The euthanasia procedure itself is painless and peaceful, typically involving a sedative followed by a special medication, taking only 10 to 20 seconds.  
  
For Maltese owners considering in-home euthanasia, this option can provide a familiar and comfortable setting for their pet's final moments. After euthanasia, pet owners have various options for memorializing their Maltese, including cremation, burial, or donating to a meaningful organization in their pet's name.  
  
While the decision to euthanize is deeply personal, it's essential to prioritize the pet's comfort and quality of life, remembering that it's often better to act slightly early rather than too late in alleviating suffering.
