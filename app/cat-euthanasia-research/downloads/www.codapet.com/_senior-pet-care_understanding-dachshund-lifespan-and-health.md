# Understanding Dachshund Lifespan and Health

[Dr. Karen Whala](/experts/dr-karen-whala)

June 23, 2025

Dachshunds live 12–16 years on average, but their long backs make them prone to spinal issues. Learn how to support their health, manage common risks, and improve quality of life as they age.

## Summary

Dachshunds are beloved companions known for their unique body shape and charming personalities. This article explores the lifespan, health considerations, and care requirements for Dachshunds, providing valuable insights for owners to ensure their furry friends live long, healthy lives.

##### Key takeaways:

  1. Dachshunds have an average lifespan of 12-16 years.
  2. Their unique body shape predisposes them to certain health issues, particularly spinal problems.
  3. Regular veterinary check-ups and proper nutrition are crucial for maintaining Dachshund health.
  4. Exercise and weight management are essential to prevent obesity-related complications.
  5. Adapting the home environment and providing mental stimulation can greatly improve quality of life for aging Dachshunds.



## Overview of Dachshund Lifespan

### Average life expectancy of Dachshunds

Dachshunds are known for their impressive longevity, with an average life expectancy of 12-16 years. This surpasses the typical 10-13 year lifespan of most dog breeds, a testament to the resilience of these charming companions. Miniature Dachshunds, in particular, can reach up to 16 years of age, showcasing the potential for even greater longevity in smaller varieties.  
  
The remarkable lifespan of Dachshunds is attributed to their small size, as smaller canines generally tend to outlive their larger counterparts. This longevity advantage allows Dachshund owners to enjoy many years with their beloved pets, creating lasting memories and deep bonds.

### Factors influencing Dachshund longevity

Several key factors play a crucial role in determining the lifespan of Dachshunds. Genetics, while important, is just one piece of the puzzle. Proper nutrition, regular exercise, and overall health management significantly influence how long a Dachshund will live.  
  
One of the most critical aspects of Dachshund care is maintaining a healthy weight. Due to their unique body shape, excess weight can put undue stress on their elongated spine, potentially leading to serious health issues. As discussed in our article on [end-of-life care for dogs](https://www.codapet.com/how-will-i-know-when-its-time/end-of-life-care-how-to-help-a-dying-dog), proper weight management is essential throughout a dog's life, but becomes even more crucial in their senior years.  
  
Regular veterinary check-ups are vital for early detection and management of potential health problems. These check-ups should include comprehensive physical examinations, dental care, and preventive measures such as vaccinations and parasite control.

## Common Health Issues in Dachshunds

### Intervertebral Disc Disease (IVDD)

Intervertebral Disc Disease (IVDD) is a significant concern for Dachshunds, affecting 19 to 24 percent of the breed. This condition occurs when the shock-absorbing discs between vertebrae harden and lose elasticity, potentially leading to spinal cord compression and associated complications.  
  
Symptoms of IVDD can vary but may include:

  * Reluctance to move
  * Shivering
  * Poor appetite
  * Uncoordinated walking



In severe cases, dogs may experience complete paralysis and loss of bladder and bowel control. Early intervention is crucial for managing IVDD effectively. Treatment options range from conservative management, including strict crate rest and pain management, to surgical intervention in more severe cases.

### Hip dysplasia and patella luxation

While more commonly associated with larger breeds, hip dysplasia can also affect Dachshunds. This genetic condition results from abnormal growth of the hip joint, potentially leading to degenerative joint disease. Symptoms may include limping, stiffness, and atrophied thigh muscles.  
  
Patella luxation, another orthopedic issue, manifests as a popping out of the kneecap from its normal groove. This condition can cause intermittent limping or a skipping gait as the dog runs. The severity of patella luxation is graded on a scale of 1 to 4, with higher grades potentially requiring surgical intervention.  
  
Both hip dysplasia and patella luxation underscore the importance of maintaining a healthy weight in Dachshunds. Excess weight can exacerbate these conditions and lead to earlier onset of arthritis, further impacting the dog's quality of life.

### Eye problems and vision loss

Dachshunds are prone to several eye problems that can lead to vision loss if left untreated. Some common ocular issues include:

  * Progressive retinal atrophy (PRA)
  * Distichiasis
  * Persistent pupillary membrane (PPM)
  * Cataracts
  * Corneal diseases



Regular eye examinations and genetic testing can help detect and manage these conditions early, ensuring better eye health for Dachshunds throughout their lives.

### Obesity and weight management challenges

Dachshunds are particularly susceptible to obesity due to their long, low-slung bodies and hearty appetites. Excess weight can exacerbate health issues like intervertebral disc disease and joint problems, making weight management a crucial aspect of Dachshund care.  
  
To maintain a healthy weight, Dachshunds require a balanced diet tailored to their age and size, with portion control being paramount. Adult Dachshunds should be fed twice daily, while seniors may need reduced portions to prevent weight gain.  
  
Regular exercise is essential, with at least 30 minutes of daily activity recommended. This can include walks, interactive games, and swimming for low-impact exercise. Mental stimulation through puzzle toys can also help prevent boredom-induced overeating.

## Preventive Care and Health Management

### Regular veterinary check-ups and screenings

Regular veterinary check-ups are crucial for maintaining the health of Dachshunds. These examinations should include comprehensive physical assessments, observing the dog's gait, body condition, and alertness, as well as inspecting the skin, coat, eyes, ears, mouth, and teeth.  
  
Wellness screening tests, such as complete blood count, biochemistry profile, urinalysis, and thyroid hormone testing, are often recommended to detect underlying health issues before they become apparent. For older Dachshunds, additional screenings like chest or abdominal radiographs may be advised to assess internal organs and skeletal health.

### Proper nutrition and diet

A balanced diet is essential for maintaining the health and longevity of Dachshunds. Their diet should consist of high-quality proteins, comprising 18-25% of their food intake, with animal sources like chicken, turkey, or fish as the primary ingredient. Fats should make up 10-15% of their diet, including essential omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids for coat and skin health.  
  
Due to their propensity for obesity, portion control is crucial. Feeding twice daily helps manage energy levels and weight. Treats should not exceed 10% of daily calorie intake, with healthy options like small fruit or vegetable pieces preferred.

### Exercise and physical activity guidelines

Exercise is vital for maintaining the health and well-being of Dachshunds. Adult Dachshunds generally require about 45-60 minutes of moderate exercise per day, which can include activities like walking and interactive play. It's essential to adapt exercise routines based on the dog's life stage and individual needs.  
  
Interestingly, research indicates that Dachshunds over the age of 3 who are highly or moderately active have a lower likelihood of suffering from Intervertebral Disc Disease (IVDD) compared to less active dogs. However, it's crucial to avoid activities that put excessive strain on their spine, such as jumping or climbing steep stairs.

### Dental care and grooming

Dental care is crucial for Dachshunds, as periodontal disease can affect them by age three. Regular brushing with dog-specific toothpaste is ideal, but for reluctant dogs, dental wipes offer an alternative. Dental treats and chews designed to remove plaque can also be beneficial.  
  
Grooming needs vary among Dachshund coat types—smooth, long-haired, and wire-haired. Regular brushing not only reduces shedding but also allows for skin abnormality checks. This breed's unique body shape necessitates a safe home environment to prevent injuries.

## Improving Quality of Life for Aging Dachshunds

### Adapting the home environment for senior Dachshunds

As Dachshunds age, adapting their home environment becomes crucial for their comfort and safety. Consider the following modifications:

  * Install non-slip surfaces on floors and stairs to prevent falls
  * Use ramps or shallow stairs for furniture and car access
  * Elevate food and water bowls to reduce neck strain
  * Provide orthopedic beds with memory foam for joint support
  * Create a designated play area with soft toys for gentle physical activity and mental stimulation



These adaptations can significantly improve the quality of life for senior Dachshunds, allowing them to navigate their surroundings more comfortably and safely.

### Managing age-related health issues

Managing age-related health issues in Dachshunds requires a comprehensive approach. Regular veterinary check-ups become even more critical in their senior years, with twice-yearly exams recommended to detect and address potential health problems early.  
  
Maintaining an appropriate diet is vital, with senior Dachshunds benefiting from low-fat, calorie-controlled meals to prevent obesity and reduce strain on their joints. Regular, gentle exercise tailored to their abilities helps maintain muscle tone and joint flexibility, with multiple short walks recommended over long, strenuous sessions.

### Cognitive support and mental stimulation

Cognitive support and mental stimulation are crucial for maintaining the quality of life in aging Dachshunds. Engaging their minds through puzzle toys can provide hours of entertainment while challenging their problem-solving skills. Teaching new tricks to senior Dachshunds is another effective way to combat cognitive decline.  
  
Scent games are particularly beneficial for Dachshunds, given their keen sense of smell. Hiding treats or toys around the house or backyard encourages them to use their natural hunting instincts, providing both mental and mild physical exercise.

## Conclusion

Understanding the unique needs of Dachshunds throughout their lifespan is crucial for ensuring they live long, healthy, and happy lives. From proper nutrition and regular exercise to adapting their environment as they age, every aspect of care plays a vital role in their well-being.
