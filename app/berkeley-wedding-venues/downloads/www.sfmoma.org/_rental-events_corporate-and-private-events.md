# Corporate and Private Events

In the heart of SOMA and just steps from Moscone Center, SFMOMA has unique event spaces, each one open and welcoming. A LEED Gold–certified building designed with sustainability in mind by renowned architecture firm SnØhetta, SFMOMA has unique event spaces that work for a variety of event types: large cocktail receptions, corporate dinners, product launches, film premieres, meetings, galas, or weddings.

Rental fees support SFMOMA’s exhibitions, education, and public programs.

#### Evelyn and Walter Haas, Jr. Atrium 

Maximum capacity : 450 standing and 250 seated

With its soaring ceilings, elegant architecture, and sweeping staircase, the iconic SFMOMA Atrium is an unmistakable backdrop that’s flexible enough to accommodate a range of events from cocktail receptions to dancing and DJs!

#### Helen and Charles Schwab Hall

Maximum capacity: 600 standing and 450 seated

At the heart of the museum with views over the Atrium and the Roberts Family Gallery, the spacious Schwab Hall is an open and versatile space perfect for large dinners, banquets, or receptions.

#### Gina and Stuart Peterson White Box

Max capacity: 200 standing and 150 seated

With its high ceilings, natural light, and clean white walls, the White Box is a sleek and elegant stage for your dinner, presentation, or wedding. Available day or evening, it overlooks the Living Wall.

#### Rooftop Garden + Pavilion

Max capacity: 200 standing and 150 seated

Stunning city views and majestic artwork greet your guests as they stroll between the expansive Pavilion and the Jean and James Douglas Family Sculpture Garden. With sliding doors, this space transforms seamlessly from indoor to outdoor, and is ideal for intimate cocktail receptions, seated dinners, wedding and commitment ceremonies, and more.

#### Pat & Bill Wilson Sculpture Terrace

Maximum capacity: 100 standing

A highlight of our expansion is our Living Green Wall. This thirty-foot vertical garden lines the Sculpture Terrace, a partially covered outdoor space spanning the distance between Minna and Natoma Streets. This stunning space is located immediately outside the Calder Gallery and features modern sculptures. 

#### Phyllis C. Wattis Theater

Max capacity: 260 seated

With state-of-the-art audio, video, and projection equipment, plus easy access for all abilities, the Wattis Theater is a comfortable, sophisticated venue for presentations, lectures, screenings, and live performances.

#### Howard Street Mural Alley 

Maximum: 150 standing

The Howard Steet Mural Alley provides a perfect outdoor option for your next Happy Hour or entrance to your event. Tucked into the museum, with a glass wall viewing into the Roberts Family Gallery, it provides an ideal spot for guests to gather.
