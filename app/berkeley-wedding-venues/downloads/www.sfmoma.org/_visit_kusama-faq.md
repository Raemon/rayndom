# Yayoi Kusama Infinity Mirror Room FAQ

### _Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_

##  Second navigation 

### Ticket Information

###  How do I purchase tickets for _Yayoi Kusama: Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_? 

Expand details

The Infinity Mirror Room _Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_ is now included with general admission. 

###  Do I need a timed ticket for entry? 

Expand details

No, entry is on a first-come, first-served basis with a general admission ticket. You may encounter a wait before entering the Infinity Mirror Room.

###  I’m a member, do I need to book a ticket? 

Expand details

Yes, members must still book a general member ticket to view this Infinity Mirror Room.

###  I purchased a museum ticket from a third party. Does my third-party general admission ticket provide access to _Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_? 

Expand details

Yes, all general admission tickets include the opportunity to view this Infinity Mirror Room.

###  Can I return or exchange my ticket? 

Expand details

Tickets are nontransferable, nonrefundable, and cannot be resold.

###  Can I book private time in _Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_? 

Expand details

We cannot provide private time inside this presentation during regular museum hours. Please contact our rental events team to learn about [private rentals](https://www.sfmoma.org/rental-events/).

### Experience in the Rooms

###  How much time can I spend in the installation? 

Expand details

Please limit your time inside _Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_ to allow as many people as possible to experience the presentation. Staff may activate a time limit to support viewing on high traffic days.

###  Can I take my bag or stroller into the room? 

Expand details

Space inside is limited. **Large items are not allowed in the Infinity Mirror Room. Bags and purses exceeding 12 x 6 x 3 inches must be left at the coat check in the Evelyn and Walter Haas, Jr. Atrium or left outside the exhibition.** Strollers may be parked outside the room while you are inside. Mobility devices are allowed in the Infinity Mirror Room.

###  Can I touch the surfaces within the Infinity Mirror Room? 

Expand details

No, please do not touch the surfaces of the artworks while inside or outside the Infinity Mirror Room. Maintain a distance of at least 18 inches from all artwork, and at least six feet from other guests.

###  Are photography and videography allowed? 

Expand details

Yes, photography/videography is allowed for personal, noncommercial use. However, flash photography, tripods, and selfie sticks are not allowed. Also please maintain a distance of at least 18 inches from all artwork, and at least six feet from other guests.

### Accessibility

###  Is this presentation wheelchair accessible? What are the doorway heights and widths? 

Expand details

_Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_ is wheelchair and stroller accessible. If you need help, a staff member will assist you. _Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_ (2023), is accessed through a low-height 1/4 circle doorway. The entryway is wheelchair accessible but may not support physical access for all visitors due to this low height. The rounded doorway is 59 inches (1.5 meters) at its highest point on the upper right side of the doorway and 46 inches (1.1 meters) high at its midpoint. It is 59 inches (1.5 meters) across the base. 

###  Are there sensory conditions I should consider? 

Expand details

Experiencing _Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_ involves being enclosed in a mirrored room. The Infinity Mirror Room features a dark enclosed space with colorful light. This may cause disorientation and may not be suitable for people who are sensitive to light and/or dark spaces. Sound blocking headphones and sensory toys are available at the entrance to _Dreaming of Earth’s Sphericity, I Would Offer My Love_ on Floor 6. Please inquire with a staff member upon your arrival.

###  Where can I learn more about accessibility affordances at SFMOMA? 

Expand details

Please visit our [Accessibility at SFMOMA](https://www.sfmoma.org/visit/accessibility/) webpage for more information.
