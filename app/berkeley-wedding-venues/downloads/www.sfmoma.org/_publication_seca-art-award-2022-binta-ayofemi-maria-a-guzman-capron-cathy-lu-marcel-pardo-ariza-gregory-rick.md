Edited by Andrea Nitsche-Krupp and Jovanna Venegas

Foreword by Christopher Bedford; introduction and essays by Andrea Nitsche-Krupp and Jovanna Venegas; interviews with Binta Ayofemi, Maria A. Guzmán Capron, Cathy Lu, Marcel Pardo Ariza, and Gregory Rick

40 pages, 7 ½ x 10 inches, softcover

Published in 2022

Established in 1967 by the Society for the Encouragement of Contemporary Art (SECA), the biennial SECA Art Award honors exceptional Bay Area artists who have not yet received significant recognition from major institutions. The five 2022 SECA Art Award recipients—Binta Ayofemi, Maria A. Guzmán Capron, Cathy Lu, Marcel Pardo Ariza, and Gregory Rick—share a focus on identifying, celebrating, and often reconfiguring the places, materials, and points of connection that define our shared human experience. Published to accompany the exhibition at SFMOMA, this bold and inspiring catalogue includes engaging essays, enlightening conversations between the artists, and full-color reproductions that reveal the artists’ distinct visions and crucial contributions to the art of our time. 

Published on the occasion of the exhibition _2022 SECA Art Award: Binta Ayofemi, Maria A. Guzmán Capron, Cathy Lu, Marcel Pardo Ariza, Gregory Rick,_ held at the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art (December 17, 2022–May 29, 2023)
