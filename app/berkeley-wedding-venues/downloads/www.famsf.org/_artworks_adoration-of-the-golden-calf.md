  * ###  Provenance 

As provided by the Kress Foundation: https://www.kressfoundation.org/kress-collection/repository/fine-arts-museums-of-san-francisco:  
  
Quirin van Biesum, Rotterdam, by 1719;  
  
His sale, Rotterdam, 18 October 1719, no. 12.   
  
Flinck Family, Amsterdam, by 1754;  
  
Sale, Loesel, 4 November 1754, no. 14 [1].   
  
Earl of Harewood, Harewood House, Yorkshire (probably Henry George Charles Lascelles, 6th earl of Harewood, 1882-1947) [2].   
  
Rudolph Bottenwieser, Paris.   
  
[Sackville Gallery, London, by 1919] [3].   
  
Ernst May, Paris.   
  
[Durlacher Brothers, New York, exhibited in 1940].   
  
[Rudolph J. Heinemann, New York].   
  
[Frederick Mont Inc., New York];   
  
Sold to the Samuel H. Kress Foundation on 30 January 1952;   
  
Gift to Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco in 1961  
  
[1] Sale is recorded in Frits Lugt as "Flinck (Govert) peintre; Flinck (Nicolaas Antoni), fils" posthumous sale for both.   
[2] Not included in Harewood sale Christie's London 1 May 1858.   
[3] J. H. Johnstone, "An Early Picture by Nicolas Poussin," Burlington Magazine, xxxv, 1919, p. 91.  


  * ###  Exhibition History 

1940, New York. Durlacher Brothers Gallery. ”Loan Exhibition of Works by Poussin,” March, no. 1.  
  
1978-1979, Denver-New York-Minneapolis. ”Masterpieces of French Art: The Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco.” Denver Art Museum, 26 December 1978 - 18 February 1979; New York, Wildenstein & Co., Inc., 14 March - 27 April 1979; The Minneapolis Institute of Arts, 24 May - 1 July 1979, no. 34.  
  
1982, San Francisco. The de Young Museum. ”16 Paintings in Search of an Artist,” 28 August - 14 November, no catalogue.
