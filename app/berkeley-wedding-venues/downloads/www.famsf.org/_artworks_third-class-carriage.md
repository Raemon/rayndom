#  Third-Class Carriage 

ca. 1856–1858

[ Honoré Daumier ](https://www.famsf.org/art-finder?artist=177)

Honoré Daumier painted many pictures of passengers on trains,  
a new form of transport in the nineteenth century. The growth of France's rail  
network made travel swifter and more accessible, but the journey was not the  
same for everyone. Railway companies separated passengers into three classes  
through a tiered ticketing system that corresponded to the level of comfort  
onboard. Here Daumier depicted travelers who have paid the lowest fare: a  
peasant mother and child, an old woman, and a worker in a rumpled smock.  
Crowded together in a dark compartment, these careworn figures exemplify  
Daumier’s uncompromising Realism.
