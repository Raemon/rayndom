#  Confetti 

1894

[ Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec ](https://www.famsf.org/art-finder?artist=75)

##  Additional information 

Legion of Honor 

Gallery 022

![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Legion of Honor museum](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d9c1d718-e3c8-4798-9da1-09d8c0d6ce42/627-legion-covid2-social-garysexton-10-29-20.jpg?crop=4418,4418,x1125,y21&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Legion of Honor museum](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d9c1d718-e3c8-4798-9da1-09d8c0d6ce42/627-legion-covid2-social-garysexton-10-29-20.jpg?crop=4418,4418,x1125,y21&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec received the commission for this poster from J. & E. Bella, a paper manufacturer in London that produced specialty items such as paper banners and confetti. Paper confetti was a relatively new invention in the late nineteenth century, replacing the rice, flower petals, leaves, or bits of plaster that had previously been tossed over a bride and groom at the conclusion of their wedding ceremony. The artist visualized the paper confetti using his famous crachis, or splatter technique. The hands that playfully throw confetti onto the female figure echo the artist’s act of splattering color onto the lithographic stone.

San Francisco, Legion of Honor, April 18, 1998–July 5, 1998. "The First Fifty Years: A Celebration of the Achenbach Foundation for Graphic Arts, 1948–1998."

San Francisco, Legion of Honor, June 5, 2010–September 26, 2010. "Impressionist Paris: City of Light."

San Francisco, Legion of Honor, May 24, 2025–January 4, 2026. "Printing Color: Chiaroscuro to Screenprint."
