#  ZAPCRLM 

2021

[ Alicia McCarthy ](https://www.famsf.org/art-finder?artist=6422)

##  Additional information 

Legion of Honor 

Gallery 022

![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Legion of Honor museum](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d9c1d718-e3c8-4798-9da1-09d8c0d6ce42/627-legion-covid2-social-garysexton-10-29-20.jpg?crop=4418,4418,x1125,y21&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Legion of Honor museum](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d9c1d718-e3c8-4798-9da1-09d8c0d6ce42/627-legion-covid2-social-garysexton-10-29-20.jpg?crop=4418,4418,x1125,y21&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Alicia McCarthy translated into print her complex painting process, in which she seems to weave color through intricately intersecting lines. The artist worked with printers at Berkeley’s Paulson Fontaine Press to produce this eighty-four-color aquatint prepared à la poupée, a process in which multiple colors are applied separately onto a single plate that is pulled through the press. Aware of the tremendous skill and labor involved in printing this aquatint on two pieces of paper, which were later joined together, McCarthy titled the artwork _ZAPCRLM_ : the initials of every person who collaborated on the project.
