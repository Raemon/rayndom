  * ###  Provenance 

The artist’s daughter, Jeanne Pissarro-Bonin (1881-1948), Paris [1]  
[Sold to Wildenstein & Co., ca. 1947]  
Sold to a New York collector, 3 January 1961  
Sale, New York, Christie’s, 11 May 1995, lot 106  
Purchased by Ann and Gordon Getty, San Francisco  
Their sale, Christie’s, New York, 21 October 2022, lot 168  
Purchased by the Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco   
  
[1] See Pissarro and Durand-Ruel 2006 III, p. 802, no. 1297.  


  * ###  Exhibition History 

New York, Wildenstein & Co. Inc., Summer 1950. “The Woman in French Painting, XVIth to XXth Century,” no. 42.  
  
New York, Wildenstein & Co. Inc., April–May 1957. “Portrait Exhibition,” no cat. no.   
  
London, Wildenstein & Co. Inc., June–July 1960. “Painting and Drawings by Continental Masters, XVIth–XXth Centuries,” no. 42.  
  
New York, Wildenstein & Co. Inc.; Waltham, Brandeis University, Rose Art Museum, April–June 1962. “Modern French Painting,” no. 49.   
  
New York, The Metropolitan Museum of Art, Summer 1962. “Paintings from Private Collections,” no. 67.  
  
New York, Wildenstein & Co. Inc., March–May 1965. “Camille Pissarro,” no. 77.  
  
New York, The Metropolitan Museum of Art, July–September 1968. “New York Collects: Paintings, Watercolors and Sculpture from Private Collections,” no. 160.  
  
New York, Wildenstein & Co. Inc., November–December 1972. “Faces from the World of Impressionism and Post–Impressionism,” no. 52.   
  
Williamstown, Clark Art Institute, June 1–October 2, 2011; Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco (Legion of Honor), October 22, 2011–January 22, 2012. “Pissarro's People,” no cat. no.
