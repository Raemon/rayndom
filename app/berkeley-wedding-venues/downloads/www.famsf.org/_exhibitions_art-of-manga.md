###  Additional info 

Tickets are timed. A limited number of [untimed tickets](https://ticketing.famsf.org/events/01980027-f015-5ff1-4bc1-94fae6c84ad9) are available for an additional fee. Extended hours: Open until 8:30 pm Jan 16–17 and Jan 23–24.  


Manga — Japanese comics and graphic novels — have become a global phenomenon. Featuring rarely presented original drawings by major artists, this exhibition showcases the world of manga from the 1970s to today. The exhibition explores manga as a powerful medium for visual storytelling, highlighting themes across genres, from friendship to sexuality to the human condition. Looking closely at each artist’s narrative worlds and creative processes, the exhibition also spotlights manga’s cultural impact today and possibilities for the future.

  * ###  ONE PIECE ONLY: How Manga Is Made 

Accompanying the exhibition, this installation gives visitors a glimpse into the creative process behind the celebrated _ONE PIECE_ manga series. The installation, shown for the first time in the United States, follows the [journey of the manga](https://www.famsf.org/stories/one-piece-only-how-manga-is-made) from the hand of Oda Eiichiro to the printing presses. A series of “treasure boxes” and films reveal how a single sheet of paper is transformed into millions of printed volumes. Serialized in over 1,151 episodes and collected in 111 volumes (as of June 17, 2025), _ONE PIECE_ follows the thrilling adventures of Monkey D. Luffy and his crew, the Straw Hat Pirates.

##  Art of Manga + cosplay policy 

We aim to provide a welcoming and safe environment for all staff and visitors, including those who participate in cosplay. This policy ensures safety, respect, and enjoyment for all while preserving and considering the integrity and atmosphere of the museum and art collections.  


  * ###  Cooperation with museum policies 

By participating in cosplay activities at the Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco, you agree to adhere to all policies set forth by the Museums. Failure to comply may result in removal from the premises. [General museum policies](/visit/museum-policies) apply.  


  * ###  Respect for exhibitions and artwork 

    * Cosplay costumes and props must not interact physically with the exhibitions, artwork, or display cases. Props are prohibited in galleries. Props can be checked at the CosCheck on the lower level.
    * Costume elements that do not align with existing museum guidelines will need to be checked at the CosCheck area. The Museums’ are not responsible for lost or damaged items.
    * Play fighting and roughhousing is not allowed in museum spaces.  


  * ###  Costume safety and guidelines 

    * Anyone wearing a costume considered inappropriate or disruptive may be asked to adjust or remove it by museum staff.
    * Costumes must be safe to wear in the museum and meet the following guidelines: 
    * No sharp objects or weapons that could pose a risk to others.
    * No live flame, smoke, or pyrotechnics.
    * No recreational wheels (e.g., roller skates, in-line skates, skateboards)
    * Props should be lightweight and nonthreatening in appearance.
    * No oversize costumes or accessories that block pathways, doorways, or exits.  


  * ###  Photography and social media 

    * Visitors in cosplay are encouraged to take photos in designated areas.
    * Please respect the privacy of others; ask for permission before taking photos of other visitors or staff. Cosplay is not consent.
    * Some exhibitions or art may have photography restrictions. Please follow posted signage.  


  * ###  Respect for staff and volunteers 

    * Please cooperate with visitor experience staff, security staff, and volunteers who may need to direct or assist you. They are here to ensure the safety and smooth operation of the museum.
    * Any actions that hinder a staff member’s ability to do their job will be addressed immediately and in the appropriate manner.
