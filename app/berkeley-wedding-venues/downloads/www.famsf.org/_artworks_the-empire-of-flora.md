#  The Empire of Flora 

ca. 1743

[ Giovanni Battista Tiepolo ](https://www.famsf.org/art-finder?artist=155)

#  Artwork Viewer

Giovanni Battista Tiepolo is best remembered for vast decorative paintings that graced palaces across Europe, but this exquisitely preserved gallery picture conveys a sense of his dazzling achievement on a more intimate scale. Before a walled garden, beneath a sky of brilliant blue, Flora, the ancient Roman goddess of spring and flowers, rolls up in a carriage drawn by winged cherubs and surrounded by dancing nymphs. Two supplicants—one carrying a shepherd’s crook, the other dressed in armor—kneel at left, representing the mortal men transformed into flowers in Ovid’s first-century mythological poem The Metamorphoses.
