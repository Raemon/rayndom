##  Color into Line: Pastels from the Renaissance to the Present 

By Furio Rinaldi

![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Book jacket featuring close-up pink pastel painting with "Color into Line" text](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/a1f4584e-b892-4148-724f-0bd42cc65aad/pastels_cover.jpg?crop=1800,2079,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Book jacket featuring close-up pink pastel painting with "Color into Line" text](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/a1f4584e-b892-4148-724f-0bd42cc65aad/pastels_cover.jpg?crop=1800,2079,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

_Color into Line: Pastels from the Renaissance to the Present_ offers a fresh understanding of the art of pastel. With the appearance of a painting, the immediacy of a drawing, and the timeless matte finish of an ancient fresco, the pastel is one of the most versatile media used throughout history. Told through a selection of works that spans five centuries, and drawn primarily from the collections of the Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco, this handsome volume features masterworks by Rosalba Carriera, Mary Cassatt, Edgar Degas, John Altoon, Wayne Thiebaud, and many others.

## Authors

**Furio Rinaldi** is a curator in the Achenbach Foundation for Graphic Arts at the Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco.
