#  La partie de dames (The Game of Checkers), from the album Paysages et intérieurs (Landscapes and Interiors) 

1899

[ Edouard Vuillard ](https://www.famsf.org/art-finder?artist=3849)

![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Legion of Honor museum](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d9c1d718-e3c8-4798-9da1-09d8c0d6ce42/627-legion-covid2-social-garysexton-10-29-20.jpg?crop=4418,4418,x1125,y21&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Legion of Honor museum](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d9c1d718-e3c8-4798-9da1-09d8c0d6ce42/627-legion-covid2-social-garysexton-10-29-20.jpg?crop=4418,4418,x1125,y21&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Edouard Vuillard’s lithograph, produced in the studio of the printer Auguste Clot, represents the dramatist Tristan Bernard (left) and an unidentified figure hunched over a checkerboard. The setting, a warm-toned interior decorated with floral wallpaper, is likely the apartment of Vuillard’s patron Thadée Natanson, on the rue Saint- Florentin in Paris. The scene is presided over by Misia, Natanson’s wife, a leading light of the French fin-de-siècle art world who was the object of Vuillard’s unrequited love. In a style characteristic of Vuillard, the interior scene is built up with layers of overlapping color, each printed from a separately inked stone.
