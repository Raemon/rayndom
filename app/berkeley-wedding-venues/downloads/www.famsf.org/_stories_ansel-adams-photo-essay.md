#  Same Sight, Different Views: Revisiting Ansel Adams’s Landscapes  


By Magnolia Molcan

May 15, 2023 

![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Grand Teton National Park reflected on the ground](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/2910aea6-cc0d-4cee-bbff-d4b635870436/ansel-adams-photoessay-1-sc440813.jpg?crop=3861,2174,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Grand Teton National Park reflected on the ground](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/2910aea6-cc0d-4cee-bbff-d4b635870436/ansel-adams-photoessay-1-sc440813.jpg?crop=3861,2174,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Abelardo Morell (American, b.Cuba, 1948), _Tent-Camera Image on Ground: View of Mount Moran and the Snake River from Oxbow Bend, Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming_(detail), 2011. Inkjet print, Framed: 60 x 45 in. (152.4 x 114.3 cm). Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, Gift of the artist in memory of Robert Andrew McElaney. © Abelardo Morell/Courtesy Bonni Benrubi Gallery, NYC. Courtesy Museum of Fine Arts, Boston  


Ansel Adams’s iconic images have become synonymous with the American West. In _Ansel Adams in Our Time_ , Adams’s photographs are shown in conversation with those of the 19th-century photographers who inspired him and contemporary photographers examining the western landscape anew. Take a closer look at some of Adams’s most striking landscapes and how contemporary artists have reenvisioned the same sights.

## Inspiration Point, Yosemite National Park  


![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Black and white photograph of Yosemite by Ansel Adams](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/7f5f1b49-cc31-4db1-a9a7-5d4310872c51/ansel-adams-preserving-california-1-sc387465.jpg?crop=3641,2865,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Black and white photograph of Yosemite by Ansel Adams](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/7f5f1b49-cc31-4db1-a9a7-5d4310872c51/ansel-adams-preserving-california-1-sc387465.jpg?crop=3641,2865,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Ansel Adams (American, 1902–1984), _Clearing Winter Storm, Yosemite National Park,_ about 1937. Gelatin silver print, Framed: 15 1/8 x 19 1/8 x 1 5/8 in. (38.418 x 48.578 x 4.128 cm), Image: 7 3/8 x 9 1/4 in. (18.733 x 23.495 cm). Museum of Fine Arts, Boston. The Lane Collection. © The Ansel Adams Publishing Rights Trust, Courtesy Museum of Fine Arts, Boston  


![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Black and white photograph of Yosemite with Minkkinen’s outstretched arms mirroring the curve of the valley](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/10ee2119-625c-4bed-b159-5b63cebeeb82/ansel-adams-photoessay-2-zay7shuio6danzcifysuhfvabkmnokpxws6ojsporpm-3631w.jpg?crop=,,x,y&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Black and white photograph of Yosemite with Minkkinen’s outstretched arms mirroring the curve of the valley](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/10ee2119-625c-4bed-b159-5b63cebeeb82/ansel-adams-photoessay-2-zay7shuio6danzcifysuhfvabkmnokpxws6ojsporpm-3631w.jpg?crop=,,x,y&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Arno Rafael Minkkinen (American, b. Finland, 1945), _Homage to Watkins, Yosemite_ , 2007. Inkjet print, Framed: 42 1/4 x 59 1/4 x 2 5/8 in. (107.315 x 150.495 x 6.668 cm). Sophie M. Friedman Fund. © Arno Rafael Minkkinen. Courtesy Museum of Fine Arts, Boston  


Adams called this image of Yosemite Valley one of his “Mona Lisas” due to its immense popularity. This view, taken from Inspiration Point (or Tunnel View), has become one of Yosemite’s most photographed, with countless tourists taking photos from the same spot.

In his photographs, Arno Rafael Minkkinen challenges the images of pristine, unpopulated wilderness created by predecessors like Adams and 19th-century photographer Carleton Watkins. In Minkkinen’s image from Inspiration Point, he inserts his own body into the landscape. His outstretched arms mirror the curve of the valley.

## Snake River, Grand Teton National Park  


![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Black and white photograph of Grand Teton National Park by Ansel Adams](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/e3fdf46b-65ef-4607-99aa-7d4840fe4fdb/ansel-adams-photoessay-3-rt6dhsrjermhss-lrc3ftjoenura2kthwjtnkcnamk.png?crop=569,439,x17,y14&format=jpg&quality=80&width=569) ![Black and white photograph of Grand Teton National Park by Ansel Adams](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/e3fdf46b-65ef-4607-99aa-7d4840fe4fdb/ansel-adams-photoessay-3-rt6dhsrjermhss-lrc3ftjoenura2kthwjtnkcnamk.png?crop=569,439,x17,y14&format=jpg&quality=80&width=569)

Ansel Adams (American, 1902–1984), _The Tetons and Snake River, Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming_ , 1942. Gelatin silver print, Framed: 29 1/4 x 37 1/4 x 2 1/4 in. (74.295 x 94.615 x 5.715 cm), Image (Sight): 18 9/16 x 24 1/8 in. (47.1 x 61.2 cm). Museum of Fine Arts, Boston. The Lane Collection. © The Ansel Adams Publishing Rights Trust. Courtesy Museum of Fine Arts, Boston  


![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Grand Teton National Park reflected on the ground](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/2910aea6-cc0d-4cee-bbff-d4b635870436/ansel-adams-photoessay-1-sc440813.jpg?crop=,,x,y&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Grand Teton National Park reflected on the ground](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/2910aea6-cc0d-4cee-bbff-d4b635870436/ansel-adams-photoessay-1-sc440813.jpg?crop=,,x,y&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Abelardo Morell (American, b.Cuba, 1948), _Tent-Camera Image on Ground: View of Mount Moran and the Snake River from Oxbow Bend, Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming_ , 2011. Inkjet print, Framed: 60 x 45 in. (152.4 x 114.3 cm). Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, Gift of the artist in memory of Robert Andrew McElaney. © Abelardo Morell/Courtesy Edwynn Houk Gallery. Courtesy Museum of Fine Arts, Boston  


Adams was known for crisp, clear photography that captured vivid detail and texture. Through his adapted camera obscura, Abelardo Morell captures similarly vivid details in an entirely new way. By reflecting the landscape onto the ground, Morell transforms it, remaking the view through the textures of the pine needles, grass, and sand under his feet. 

## Golden Gate, San Francisco Bay  


![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Ansel Adams black and white photograph of the San Francisco bay](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d4d370a4-4080-424e-874a-dd62fbd9df70/ansel-adams-exhibition-fullsize-sc387476.jpg?crop=3974,2830,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Ansel Adams black and white photograph of the San Francisco bay](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d4d370a4-4080-424e-874a-dd62fbd9df70/ansel-adams-exhibition-fullsize-sc387476.jpg?crop=3974,2830,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Ansel Adams (American, 1902–1984), _The Golden Gate Before the Bridge_ , 1932. Gelatin silver print, Framed: 33 1/4 x 45 1/8 x 1 3/4 in. (84.455 x 114.618 x 4.445 cm), Image: 19 1/2 x 27 1/2 in. (49.5 x 69.9 cm). Museum of Fine Arts, Boston. The Lane Collection. © The Ansel Adams Publishing Rights Trust. Courtesy, Museum of Fine Arts, Boston

![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Golden Gate bridge](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/a32dfbf3-c1b9-453e-b53e-cdbd9a00d869/ansel-adams-photoessay-4-e5wgsmiytgrunnsucn-rvz8wqbbd4-5hjk34mbvvtm.jpg?crop=3000,2360,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Golden Gate bridge](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/a32dfbf3-c1b9-453e-b53e-cdbd9a00d869/ansel-adams-photoessay-4-e5wgsmiytgrunnsucn-rvz8wqbbd4-5hjk34mbvvtm.jpg?crop=3000,2360,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Richard Misrach (American, b. 1949),  _Golden Gate Bridge, 2.13.01, 5:32pm_ , 2001, printed 2017. Pigment print, framed: 25 x 29 in. (63.5 x 73.66 cm), sheet: 20 x 24 in. (50.8 x 60.96 cm). Courtesy Fraenkel Gallery, San Francisco. © Richard Misrach. Courtesy Pace Gallery, New York; Fraenkel Gallery, San Francisco; Marc Selwyn Fine Art, Los Angeles  


“One beautiful storm-clearing morning, I looked out the window of our San Francisco home and saw magnificent clouds rolling from the north over the Golden Gate. I grabbed the 8-by-10 equipment and drove to the end of 32nd Avenue, at the edge of Sea Cliff,” Adams recalled of this image, taken one year before construction began on the Golden Gate Bridge.

Sixty-five years later, in 1997, Richard Misrach felt a similar calling, setting up his own 8-by-10-inch camera on the porch of his Berkeley Hills home. He spent the next three years photographing the Golden Gate Bridge from the same spot, capturing hundreds of views of the span against the ever-changing sky.

## Pacific Coast, California  


![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Black and white photograph of ocean surf by Ansel Adams](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d2121968-3e16-4689-83ba-42709f735a3f/ansel-adams-photoessay-5-nyl4ggvg3dgpu9boezy2-8g3pyhetdtg-vvq1snfqta.png?crop=636,512,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=636) ![Black and white photograph of ocean surf by Ansel Adams](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/d2121968-3e16-4689-83ba-42709f735a3f/ansel-adams-photoessay-5-nyl4ggvg3dgpu9boezy2-8g3pyhetdtg-vvq1snfqta.png?crop=636,512,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=636)

Ansel Adams (American, 1902–1984), _Surf Sequence #2,_ 1940, printed 1973. Gelatin silver print, 10 5/8 x 12 11/16 in. (26.2 x 32.2 cm). Museum of Fine Arts, Boston. Gift of Saundra B. Lane in honor of Clifford S. Ackley. © The Ansel Adams Publishing Rights Trust. Courtesy Museum of Fine Arts, Boston  


![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![A truck restoring a Pacific coast beach](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/673849a8-7d2b-4fa7-8d48-7fd94c3c97cb/ansel-adams-photoessay-6-fizjycsn6zlrvvs15lnehyljctxoozssfonvfzffdl0.png?crop=614,458,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=614) ![A truck restoring a Pacific coast beach](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/673849a8-7d2b-4fa7-8d48-7fd94c3c97cb/ansel-adams-photoessay-6-fizjycsn6zlrvvs15lnehyljctxoozssfonvfzffdl0.png?crop=614,458,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=614)

Lucas Foglia (American, b. 1983), _Beach Restoration after El Niño Waves_ , 2016. Inkjet print, Framed: 34 7/8 x 44 7/8 x 2 1/8 in. (88.583 x 113.983 x 5.398 cm). Courtesy of the Artist and Fredericks & Freiser, NY. © Lucas Foglia. Courtesy Museum of Fine Arts, Boston  


In 1940, Ansel Adams shot the series Surf Sequence from the cliffs off Highway One in Northern California. Lucas Foglia’s _Beach Restoration after El Niño Waves_(2016) consciously echoes Adams’s work. Foglia’s photographs document how humans use technology in an effort to combat climate change. Here, excavators and trucks are employed to restore a Pacific coast beach in an ever-losing battle.

## Sierra Nevada, California  


![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Ansel Adams photograph of snowcapped range](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/33608fa4-0eb6-4e80-ab35-851383cc0f79/ansel-adams-photoessay-7.png?crop=1101,807,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Ansel Adams photograph of snowcapped range](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/33608fa4-0eb6-4e80-ab35-851383cc0f79/ansel-adams-photoessay-7.png?crop=1101,807,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Ansel Adams (American, 1902–1984), _Mount Williamson, Sierra Nevada, from Owens Valley, California_ , ca. 1939. Gelatin silver print, Framed: 15 1/8 x 19 1/8 x 1 5/8 in. (38.418 x 48.578 x 4.128 cm); Image: 6 9/16 x 9 7/16 in. (16.669 x 23.971 cm). Museum of Fine Arts, Boston  


![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7) ![Sierra Nevada mountains by Trevor Paglen](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/cd3807bc-0bcb-40c5-a569-ba930c7efe27/ansel-adams-photoessay-8.png?crop=1350,1009,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000) ![Sierra Nevada mountains by Trevor Paglen](https://www.famsf.org/storage/images/cd3807bc-0bcb-40c5-a569-ba930c7efe27/ansel-adams-photoessay-8.png?crop=1350,1009,x0,y0&format=jpg&quality=80&width=1000)

Trevor Paglen (American, b. 1974), _Subsatellite Ferret-D Over the Eastern Sierra (Electronic Intelligence Satellite; USA 3)_ , 2012. Chromogenic print, Framed: 40 7/8 x 54 3/8 x 2 1/8 in. (103.823 x 138.113 x 5.398 cm). Museum of Fine Arts, Boston  


Trevor Paglen’s image of the Sierra Nevada mountains was taken seven decades after Adams photographed the snowcapped range. While other contemporary photographers capture wilderness areas overrun by tourists, Paglen exposes the presence of government surveillance over supposedly empty areas of the American West. Here, using a telephoto lens and a long exposure, he reveals the trail of a secret government satellite, just visible at the upper left of the frame.
