# The High Line: Wild by Design

  * Saturday, February 1, 2025
  * 10:30 AM 12:00 PM


  * Regional Parks Botanic Garden 1550 Wildcat Canyon Road Berkeley, CA, 94708 United States [(map)](http://maps.google.com?q=1550 Wildcat Canyon Road Berkeley, CA, 94708 United States)


  * [Google Calendar](http://www.google.com/calendar/event?action=TEMPLATE&text=The%20High%20Line%3A%20Wild%20by%20Design&dates=20250201T183000Z/20250201T200000Z&location=1550%20Wildcat%20Canyon%20Road%2C%20Berkeley%2C%20CA%2C%2094708%2C%20United%20States) [ICS](/events-and-classes/events/horticultural-lessons-from-the-high-line?format=ical)



The High Line is the most famous example of Dutch garden designer Piet Oudolf's four-season, naturalistic garden designs. The High Line also serves as an important example of urban ecology, featuring many native plants and providing access to nature for many species, most importantly for the more than six million annual human visitors. Join senior director of horticulture, Richard Hayden for an exploration of the ecological habitats, important native plants and the horticultural techniques for evolving and caring for this New York City treasure. This talk will be via ZOOM only. 

[ Register for ZOOM Meeting ](https://us02web.zoom.us/meeting/register/h3kKICS6ROCF6rYbFGS-pg)

Richard Hayden is a professional horticulturist dedicated to an ecological and naturalistic approach to garden care and design. Since 2022 he has served as the Senior Director of Horticulture at the High Line, an elevated railway in New York City reclaimed as a spectacular naturalistic garden.
