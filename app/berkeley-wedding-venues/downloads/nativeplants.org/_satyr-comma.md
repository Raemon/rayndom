**Satyr Comma (_Polygonia satyrus_)** is a medium-sized butterfly, with a wingspan of 1.75 to 2.5 inches. In our local area, it is seen in January-February and then again May to August, with scattered sightings in September and October. There’s a small peak in February and a bigger one in June. Hosts plants are various species of Nettle (Urticaceae), especially Stinging Nettle (_Urtica dioica_).

Adults feed on tree sap, rotting fruit, and nectar.

Satyr Comma is very similar to [Oreas Comma](/oreas-comma) (which can be a rare sighting in the Garden). They can be distinguished by the shape of the “comma” on the underside of the hindwing. For the Oreas Comma it looks like a check-mark, whereas for the Satyr Comma, it looks like a fish hook.

This butterfly is found only in North America.
