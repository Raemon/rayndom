**Gray Buckeye _(Junonia grisea)_** is a medium-sized butterfly, with a wingspan of 1.625 to 2.75 inches. It can be seen from March to November. In our local area, numbers are uniformly high from May through July. Host plants include Snapdragon (_Antirrhinum_) and Toadflax (_Linari_ a) from the Snapdragon family, as well as Plantain (_Plantago_), and also _Ruellia_ from the Acanthus family.  
  
Before 2018, it was considered a sub-species of Common Buckeye. The entire population west of the Rockies was then elevated as a separate species.

The eyespots serve as a defense mechanism, fooling predators into thinking they are seeing part of a much larger creature.

It is found along the west coast from Oregon through Baja California, and also in the southwestern states.
