# Hotels in Cairo, Egypt

Cairo, the largest city in the Arab world, is a vibrant metropolis where ancient history meets modern life. The city is home to iconic landmarks such as the Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx, which stand as timeless testaments to the ingenuity of the pharaohs. Explore the bustling streets of Cairo and discover its rich heritage, from the Egyptian Museum, housing the world’s most extensive collection of pharaonic antiquities, to the historic Khan El Khalili bazaar, where you can find traditional crafts and local delicacies. Marriott Bonvoy’s hotels in Cairo offer the perfect blend of comfort and luxury, providing easy access to these must-see attractions and the city’s dynamic neighborhoods.

  


For a more immersive cultural experience, our Cairo Marriott Bonvoy hotels are conveniently located near the Nile River, giving you the opportunity to take a relaxing river cruise or explore the city’s lively nightlife by the water. Many of our hotels offer modern amenities, including fitness centers, business services, and complimentary Wi-Fi, so you can make the most of your time in the city. Whether you’re traveling for business or leisure, our hotels are strategically placed to help you discover the city’s historical landmarks, contemporary shopping areas, and diverse dining options, all while enjoying the renowned hospitality of Cairo.

Cairo, a city steeped in history and culture, also boasts a surprising number of natural attractions that provide a welcome respite from the urban rush. The Cairo Zoo, one of the oldest in Africa, is a serene haven where you can wander through verdant gardens and observe a diverse array of wildlife, from regal lions to mischievous monkeys. For a more tranquil experience, the Al-Azhar Park, located in the heart of the city, beckons with its beautifully manicured gardens, sweeping vistas, and a chance to escape the city's clamor. It's the perfect place for a leisurely stroll, a peaceful picnic, or simply to bask in the fresh air and lush surroundings.

Just on the outskirts of the city, the Nile River offers a plethora of activities for the outdoor enthusiast. A felucca ride along the Nile is a great way to enjoy the gentle breeze and take in the stunning views of the city's skyline. For a more adventurous day trip, head out to the desert and go sandboarding or take a camel ride across the vast dunes. These natural landscapes offer a unique way to experience the beauty of Cairo.

Cairo’s culture is a mesmerizing blend of ancient traditions and modern influences, offering an unparalleled experience for every visitor. Staying at one of our hotels in Cairo places you at the heart of the city’s rich heritage, where the echoes of the past can be felt in every corner. Explore the historic streets of Islamic Cairo, where ornate mosques and bustling bazaars transport you back in time. The city’s vibrant arts scene can be discovered in the numerous galleries and cultural centers, such as the Townhouse Gallery, which showcases contemporary works by local and international artists. After a day of cultural immersion, enjoy the comfort and luxury of your Marriott Bonvoy accommodations, complete with modern amenities and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

To further immerse yourself in the city’s rich cultural heritage, a visit to the Egyptian Museum is a must. The museum is home to an extensive collection of ancient artifacts, including the world’s largest collection of pharaonic antiquities and the treasures of Tutankhamun. Cairo’s literary heritage is also a highlight, with the city being a hub of intellectual and creative energy. The historic Al-Azhar Park is another must-see, offering a peaceful respite from the city’s hustle and bustle, as well as hosting cultural events and performances that celebrate the city’s diverse arts and traditions.

Cairo's hot desert climate is marked by distinct seasonal variations, ensuring each visit is a unique experience. Summers, from June to August, are sizzling, with temperatures often exceeding 95°F (35°C). While the heat can be intense, the city's wealth of historical sites and modern amenities, such as the cool interiors of ancient temples and air-conditioned shopping centers, offer respite. Opting for a stay at a Marriott Bonvoy hotel in the city center guarantees access to refreshing pools and cool, comfortable rooms to escape the midday sun.

Winters, from December to February, are much milder, with temperatures averaging around 68°F (20°C) during the day and dropping to the 50s°F (10-15°C) at night. This season is ideal for exploring the city’s outdoor attractions, as the cooler weather makes it more comfortable to walk through the bustling streets and archaeological sites. Spring and Fall, from March to May and September to November, respectively, bring pleasant temperatures in the 70s-80s°F (21-27°C), making these seasons perfect for enjoying the city’s vibrant nightlife and scenic Nile River views. Marriott Bonvoy hotels are strategically located to help you make the most of Cairo’s climate, whatever the time of year.

For those arriving by air, Cairo International Airport is the city’s main gateway and is well-served by flights from major global destinations. Once you land, you’ll find a variety of transportation options to get you to your hotel, including taxis, ride-sharing services and airport shuttles provided by many Marriott Bonvoy hotels. The city’s extensive road network and proximity to the airport make it easy to drive into town, with several car rental agencies available both at the airport and downtown.

Once you've landed in Cairo, getting around is a breeze, with a variety of public and private transportation options at your disposal. The Cairo Metro is a reliable and efficient way to get to your destination, with stops near many of the city's key areas and tourist sites. Buses and microbuses are also widely used and offer a more local experience, though they can be crowded during peak hours. Taxis are plentiful and can be hailed from the street or booked through apps, providing a flexible and convenient way to explore the city's historic neighborhoods and modern attractions. Cairo is a city of ancient wonders that will inspire the young explorers in your family. At the Egyptian Museum, kids can get up close to mummies, golden treasures and the famous Tutankhamun exhibit.
