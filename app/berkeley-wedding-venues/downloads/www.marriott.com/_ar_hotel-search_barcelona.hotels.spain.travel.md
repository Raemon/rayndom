# Hotels in Barcelona, Spain

Barcelona, a city that pulses with culture and innovation, beckons you to experience its unique tapestry of ancient and contemporary. Nestled in the heart of this captivating metropolis, Marriott Bonvoy’s collection of hotels in Barcelona places you mere moments from iconic sites such as the Sagrada Familia, Park Güell, and the bustling promenade of La Rambla, each with its own tale to tell of the city's artistic legacy. Our hotels are poised to provide you with a comfortable and lavish retreat, ensuring your visit is nothing short of seamless and delightful, with amenities crafted to elevate your stay and create lasting memories.

  


From the sun-drenched shores of Barceloneta to the lively markets of La Boqueria, Barcelona is a city that has something for everyone. Many of Marriott Bonvoy’s hotels are ideally located to help you make the most of the city’s top shopping areas, such as Passeig de Gràcia, and its vibrant nightlife. Whether you’re in town for business or leisure, the combination of first-rate service, modern amenities, and proximity to key attractions will ensure that your time in Barcelona is nothing short of exceptional.

Barcelona is a city that has it all—from beautiful beaches to lush parks, and everything in between. The Montjuïc area is a must-see, with its stunning gardens, the Montjuïc Castle, and the Magic Fountain. The Montjuïc Communication Tower, designed by the famous architect Santiago Calatrava, is a sight to behold. If you’re looking for a peaceful place to relax, the Ciutadella Park is the perfect spot. It’s a beautiful park right in the heart of the city, with a lake, botanical gardens, and even a zoo. It’s a great place to go for a walk, have a picnic, or just relax and enjoy the sunshine.

Just a short distance from the city, the Collserola Range offers an extensive network of trails for hiking and biking, leading through lush forests and providing breathtaking views of Barcelona and the Mediterranean Sea. The Turó Park, nestled in the Gràcia district, is a local favorite, featuring elevated green spaces and play areas for children. For those who prefer the sand between their toes, Barcelona’s coastline is home to several beautiful beaches, including the popular Barceloneta Beach, where you can soak up the sun, take a refreshing dip, or try your hand at various water sports. Whether you seek the peace of a park or the thrill of a mountain trail, Barcelona’s natural attractions are certain to invigorate your soul.

Barcelona's rich culture is a vibrant tapestry, the result of centuries of artistic and intellectual achievement. The city is famous for its modernist architecture, most notably the works of the legendary Antoni Gaudí, including the iconic Sagrada Familia and the whimsical Park Güell. The Gothic Quarter features a labyrinth of streets and squares, with many buildings dating from medieval times. The city has a rich art scene, with the Fundació Joan Miró and the MACBA (Museu d’Art Contemporani de Barcelona) showcasing the works of some of the most influential modern artists. Whether you're exploring the bustling promenade of La Rambla or relaxing in a peaceful park, you'll be surrounded by the city's history and innovation.

The cuisine of Barcelona is a vital part of the city’s culture. The local food scene is a celebration of Mediterranean flavors, with markets such as La Boqueria providing fresh fruit and vegetables, seafood, and local products. You can enjoy traditional Catalan dishes such as paella, escalivada, and fideuà in both tapas bars and high-end restaurants. The city’s love of food doesn’t stop at mealtimes, with a vibrant nightlife that includes everything from live music in fashionable bars to late-night cocktails in rooftop lounges. The cultural rhythm of the city means that every meal and every evening is an opportunity to experience the local way of life.

Barcelona is a city that basks in a Mediterranean climate, which means you can expect warm, sunny days and mild winters, making it an inviting destination throughout the year. Summers, from June to August, are characterized by hot and dry weather with average daytime temperatures ranging from the mid-70s to the mid-80s Fahrenheit. The warm evenings are perfect for strolling along the city’s vibrant streets and enjoying the bustling nightlife. While the heat can be intense, the sea breeze from the Mediterranean provides a refreshing respite, and many of the city’s Marriott Bonvoy hotels offer outdoor pools and air-conditioned rooms to help you stay cool.

Winters in Barcelona are generally mild, with temperatures averaging in the 50s to 60s Fahrenheit during the day and cooler nights. Rainfall is more common during these months, but the city’s charming architecture and indoor attractions, such as the Gothic Quarter and the Picasso Museum, make for a delightful experience even on gray days. Spring and fall, from March to May and September to November, are particularly pleasant with temperatures ranging from the 60s to the 70s Fahrenheit, ideal for exploring the city’s beautiful parks and outdoor markets. Marriott Bonvoy hotels in the city provide the perfect base for enjoying Barcelona’s year-round appeal, whether you’re soaking up the sun or seeking shelter from the occasional rain.

For those arriving by air, Barcelona-El Prat Airport is the main gateway to the city, with a high volume of flights connecting to major cities worldwide. The airport is well-served by a range of transportation options to the city center, including the Aerobus, a direct and efficient shuttle service that runs every 5 to 10 minutes and stops at key locations. The Renfe Rodalies train also offers a scenic route into the city, with stations at both Terminal 1 and Terminal 2, connecting to major transport hubs such as Sants and Passeig de Gràcia. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also readily available, providing a convenient and flexible way to reach your destination.

Once you're in Barcelona, getting around is a breeze. The city is well-connected by an extensive public transportation network. The metro is fast and reliable, with most of the city covered and trains running until late at night. Buses and trams offer additional routes and are a great way to get to areas not served by the metro. If you prefer to be more active, the city is very bike-friendly, with a large network of bike lanes and the Bicing bike-sharing service. Car rental is less common due to the city’s compact size and excellent public transport, but there are options available if you want to explore further afield. Whether you're moving from one neighborhood to another or heading to the beach, Barcelona’s transportation options make it easy to see the city.

Barcelona is a city that loves families and kids. There are so many things to do in Barcelona with kids, you might have a hard time deciding what to do first. The Barcelona Zoo is a great place to start. The zoo is home to many animals, including primates, big cats, and dolphins. There are also interactive exhibits, such as the Children’s Area and the Penguin Beach, to keep your kids entertained. If you’re looking for a more cultural experience, the CosmoCaixa Science Museum is a great place to visit. This interactive science museum features a rainforest exhibit, a planetarium, and many other hands-on activities that will keep your kids entertained and engaged.

When it’s time to let the kids run around, head to Ciutadella Park. This sprawling green space is home to a large playground, a small lake where you can rent paddle boats, and even a miniature train. The park is also home to the Barcelona Aquarium, which is a great place to learn about marine life and see sharks, rays, and colorful fish up close.
