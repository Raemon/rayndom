## Experiences

Solaz Resort offers a variety of active pursuits for both fitness and wellness enthusiasts. Four Olympic-sized infinity pools border the entire resort’s beach frontage for peerless vistas. Each has been luxuriously designed to include butterfly green granite, emulating the turquoise waters of the Sea of Cortez. An expansive 17,614-square-foot pool deck and 8,430-square-foot beach deck invite days of reprieve. Enjoy five Jacuzzi® spas, several fire pits, and 1,246 linear feet of white sand beaches.

## Rejuvenate Yourself

Named after a coastal lagoon located in the northwestern area of Baja California Sur and taking inspiration from the magnificent Sea of Cortez, Ojo de Liebre Spa has found a fabled selection of converted local ingredients into treatments curated specially to offer a transcendent well-being experience. Guests are welcomed to immerse themselves in a world of stimulating experiences for the senses, while discovering the textures and exquisite aromas of this colorful oasis. The only Thalasso therapy wellness spa in Baja allows you to enjoy the seawater and fresh water pools, 12 treatment rooms, hot and ice-cold tubs, aromatherapy, chromotherapy light showers and our exclusive Cryotherapy chamber. With a full range of spa & salon services, arrive to a haven of relaxation and return rejuvenated.

### Cabo Sport Complex

The Cabo Sport Complex has an approximate area of 5.3 hectares, in which there are buildings and spaces suitable for hosting any type of sporting, cultural or social event. It is a space designed to take advantage of large open spaces as The Los Cabos ATP.

## Gabinete del Barco Exhibit

El Gabinete “Del Barco” is the indigenous gallery featuring regional artifacts and open spaces in which to walk while enjoying the breathtaking natural scenery. Viewed through the eyes of Baja California explorer, Miguel del Barco themed exhibits display a suspended skeleton of a 13-meter-long grey whale, ancient maps of the Americas based on limited navigation discoveries, 360˚ virtual photography; video interviews with archeologists; and replicas of harpoons, knives, and other tools from the region.
