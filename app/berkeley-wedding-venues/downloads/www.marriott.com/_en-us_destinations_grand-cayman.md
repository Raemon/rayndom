# Hotels in Grand Cayman, Cayman Islands

Grand Cayman's crystalline waters and kaleidoscopic coral reefs beckon, offering a tropical haven for those in search of an idyllic escape. With the powder-soft sands of Seven Mile Beach and a lively waterfront, the city is a sanctuary for both sun-worshippers and aquatic adventurers. The hotels of Marriott Bonvoy in Grand Cayman are your gateway to unparalleled comfort and opulence, many with direct beach access and a plethora of on-site activities to elevate your experience. Whether your plans include a romantic rendezvous, a family sojourn, or a corporate gathering, our hotels in the city are primed to cater to your every need, ensuring a seamless blend of relaxation and discovery.

  


The marine life in the city is a must-see, with world-famous diving sites like the Stingray City Sandbar and the wreck of the USS Kittiwake. On land, you’ll find a mix of high-end shopping, local markets, and a wide range of dining options to suit every taste. Marriott Bonvoy’s hotels in the city also offer cutting-edge conference facilities and business services, making them the perfect place to mix business with pleasure. From the tranquility of the beaches to the buzz of the city’s nightlife, a stay at a Marriott Bonvoy hotel in the city ensures you see the best of Grand Cayman.

Grand Cayman is a nature lover’s dream, with its unspoiled beaches and pristine waters providing a wealth of opportunities for both relaxation and adventure. Seven Mile Beach, a postcard-perfect stretch of coastline on the western shore, beckons with its powdery sands, ideal for sunbathing, leisurely strolls, and underwater explorations. For a more intimate encounter with the island’s marine life, a visit to the Cayman Islands Turtle Centre is a must, where you can even swim alongside these gentle creatures. The Queen Elizabeth II Botanic Park is a serene retreat, showcasing native flora, vibrant orchids, and the historic Mastic Trail, a path through ancient woodlands that offers a glimpse into the island’s diverse ecosystem.

But there is more to Grand Cayman than the beaches. The island’s interior is a treasure trove of natural beauty, with mangrove forests and wetlands that are perfect for kayaking and paddleboarding. The Boatswain’s Beach complex in the East End offers a range of eco-adventures, including guided tours through the mangroves and visits to the Cayman Airways Aviary, home to a variety of exotic birds. For a more secluded experience, the private islands and cays of the North Sound are perfect for picnics, snorkeling, and diving, making Grand Cayman an ideal destination for both relaxation and adventure.

The culture in Grand Cayman is a vibrant tapestry woven from the threads of British, Jamaican, and African influences, creating a unique and inviting atmosphere. Staying at one of our hotels in the city places you at the heart of this diverse heritage, where you can explore historical sites such as Pedro St. James Castle, a 19th-century plantation house that played a pivotal role in the island’s early governance. The city’s culture is also celebrated through its annual festivals, like the Batabano Festival, which showcases traditional music, dance, and crafts, offering a deeper connection to the local way of life. After a day of cultural exploration, unwind in the comfort of your Marriott Bonvoy accommodations, featuring modern amenities and stunning views of the Caribbean Sea.

Art aficionados will be thrilled by the creative expression found in Grand Cayman, from the impressive collection at the Cayman Islands National Gallery to the local and international pieces on display in George Town’s many art galleries and studios. The city’s cultural scene is further enriched by local artists who often host workshops and exhibitions, offering a hands-on experience with the island’s artistic traditions. Whether you’re exploring the markets or taking in a performance at the Harquail Theatre, the city’s artistic and cultural offerings are sure to leave a lasting impression.

Grand Cayman boasts a tropical marine climate, with consistently warm and inviting weather year-round. The city basks in an average temperature range of 77°F to 86°F (25°C to 30°C), complemented by abundant sunshine and gentle sea breezes. From January to April, the dry season prevails, creating the perfect setting for sun-seekers and aqua adventurers to revel in the island's lively coral reefs and pristine waters. With lower humidity and minimal rainfall, the skies are clear, and the conditions are ideal for outdoor pursuits. Our Marriott Bonvoy hotels are primed to enhance your experience, offering private beaches and spacious, air-conditioned accommodations to ensure you make the most of Grand Cayman's dry season.

The wet season in Grand Cayman begins in May and ends in December, with the most rainfall occurring in September and October. Despite the increased precipitation, the city's weather remains warm and inviting, with occasional afternoon showers that can bring a refreshing break from the heat. These showers are usually short-lived, allowing for plenty of sunny intervals and ideal conditions for lush tropical gardens and vibrant wildlife. Guests can enjoy the city’s many indoor attractions, such as the Cayman Islands National Museum and the Cayman Turtle Centre, or take advantage of the hotel’s spa services and indoor pools to relax and rejuvenate. Whether you visit during the dry or wet season, Marriott Bonvoy hotels ensure a comfortable and enjoyable stay with a range of activities and amenities tailored to the climate.

When you arrive in Grand Cayman, you’ll have a choice of transportation options to kick off your island adventure. Most visitors fly into Owen Roberts International Airport, where you can easily catch a taxi or a ride with services like Cayman Cab and Cayman Express to your Marriott Bonvoy hotel. For a more leisurely and scenic arrival, you can also take a boat transfer from the city's marinas to hotels along the coast. Some Marriott Bonvoy properties even offer complimentary shuttle services to and from the airport, so you can start relaxing as soon as you land.

Once you're in the city, getting around is a breeze. Grand Cayman boasts well-maintained roads and a variety of car rental options. Many visitors choose to rent a car to explore the island's hidden gems at their own pace. For a more laid-back experience, public buses and taxis are readily available to take you to the city's vibrant neighborhoods and key attractions. If you're looking for a two-wheeled adventure, scooter and bike rentals are also popular and offer a fun and flexible way to explore the island's picturesque routes. Whether you choose the convenience of a car or the charm of a scooter, Grand Cayman makes it easy to get where you need to go.

Grand Cayman is a tropical paradise that’s just as exciting for kids as it is for grown-ups. Start your family adventure at the Cayman Turtle Centre, where children can get up close and personal with sea turtles, learn about conservation efforts, and even feed and touch these gentle creatures. The city’s stunning beaches, like Seven Mile Beach, offer plenty of space for building sandcastles and splashing in the warm, clear waters. For a unique underwater experience, try the Stingray City Sandbar, where kids can snorkel and interact with friendly southern stingrays in their natural habitat.

For a day of adventure and learning, visit the Boatswain’s Beach at the Kaibo. This eco-park is the perfect blend of excitement and education. Here, your little ones can explore interactive animal exhibits, play in the water park, and take part in a variety of activities that will keep them entertained and engaged. Grand Cayman is also home to several family-friendly hotels that offer kid-specific amenities, such as kids' clubs, splash pools, and family suites, ensuring a comfortable and fun stay for the whole family. After a day of exploration, treat your taste buds to the vibrant flavors of the island at one of the city’s many kid-friendly restaurants.
