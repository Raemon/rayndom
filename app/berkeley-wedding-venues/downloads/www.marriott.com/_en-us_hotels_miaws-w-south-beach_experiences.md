# Enjoy the Sun at Our Beachfront Hotel in Miami, Florida

## Experiences 

Anchored on Collins Avenue with sweeping Atlantic views, W South Beach puts Miami’s cultural pulse at your doorstep. Stroll to the Miami Beach Convention Center, Lincoln Road’s boutiques, and landmark Art Deco blocks; after dark, Michelin‑star dining and legendary nightlife are minutes away. Nearby, the Pérez Art Museum, New World Symphony, and Miami City Ballet satisfy any art itinerary, while the Miami Beach Golf Club offers a convenient escape. Between city energy and ocean calm, our resort offers an effortless beachside stay elevated by a vibrant WET® pool deck, private cabanas, and anticipatory service.

Bass Art Museum Partnership 

In partnership with the Bass Museum of Art, guests of W South Beach can enjoy a variety of activities for adults and children to explore one of Miami Beach's most notable galleries, with complimentary admission.

Children and families can experience a variety of specialty art programs and activities designed for ages 7 to 12, such as Portfolio Workshops, Creativity Center Drop-in Studio Hours, Art Scavenger Hunt, Family Day and more.

Please contact our W Insider for additional information or to learn more.

**About Bass Art Museum**

The Bass is Miami Beach's contemporary art museum that focuses on exhibitions of international contemporary art. The Bass seeks to expand the interpretation of contemporary art by incorporating disciplines of contemporary culture, such as design, fashion and architecture, into the exhibition program. Recognized for organizing the first solo museum exhibitions in the United States of international artists such as Erwin Wurm, The Bass also presents major exhibitions by influential artists such as El Anatsui, Isaac Julien, Eve Sussman, and Piotr Uklański. The exhibition program encompasses a wide range of media and artistic points of view that bring new thought to the diverse cultural context of Miami Beach.

The Bass Museum is located across the street from the W South Beach at 2100 Collins Avenue Miami Beach, Florida. The museum is open Wednesday - Sunday from 10:00 am - 5:00 pm.
