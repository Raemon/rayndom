# Hotels in New York City, NY

City of dreams, City of endless possibilities—New York never sleeps, and neither will your excitement as you explore its iconic landmarks and hidden gems. From the dazzling lights of Times Square to the serene beauty of Central Park, the city offers a unique blend of urban energy and green retreats. Marriott Bonvoy’s hotels in New York are strategically located to bring you closer to the heart of it all, whether you’re here for business or a family trip. Each property is designed to offer comfort, luxury, and the convenience you need to make the most of your stay. 

  


Immerse yourself in the city’s cultural scene by visiting iconic institutions such as the Metropolitan Museum of Art and the American Museum of Natural History, or take in a Broadway show. New York’s diverse neighborhoods, from the fashionable boutiques of SoHo to the charming streets of Greenwich Village, offer a wealth of experiences. Marriott Bonvoy’s hotels offer amenities like complimentary Wi-Fi, modern fitness centers, and on-site dining, so you have everything you need to enjoy the city’s vibrant nightlife, lively markets, and world-class dining.

## Explore New York City

While New York City is famous for its towering skyscrapers and bustling streets, it is also home to a number of parks and natural areas that many don't think or know about. Central Park, an 843-acre urban oasis, is the perfect place to relax with its large meadows, quiet bodies of water, and carefully landscaped gardens. You can rent a rowboat to paddle across The Lake, take a horse-drawn carriage ride, or simply have a picnic. The High Line is a more peaceful alternative, an elevated park built on a historic freight rail line. It features beautiful gardens, public art, and views of the Hudson River, and is a great place to take a leisurely walk. 

Urban nature enthusiasts will also appreciate the Brooklyn Botanic Garden, which offers 52 acres of beautiful gardens and peaceful places to relax. From the cherry blossoms in the Japanese Hill-and-Pond Garden to the vibrant rose gardens, this is a great place to visit year-round. If you’re willing to travel a bit further, the Jamaica Bay Wildlife Refuge, part of Gateway National Recreation Area, is a great place to go bird watching or take nature photos. With its many types of wildlife and different types of habitats, you can get a nice break from the hustle and bustle of the city.

The culture of New York City is a dynamic tapestry woven from the threads of its diverse neighborhoods and the myriad of influences of its residents. Staying at one of our hotels in NYC places you at the epicenter of this vibrant cultural mosaic, where you can explore world-renowned museums like the Metropolitan Museum of Art and the Museum of Modern Art, each offering a glimpse into the past and a vision of the future. The city’s historic neighborhoods, such as Greenwich Village and Harlem, are alive with the spirit of the arts, music, and literature, inviting you to experience the places where legends were born and movements took shape. After a day of cultural immersion, retreat to the comfort and style of your Marriott Bonvoy accommodations, where the city’s energy is reflected in every detail. 

New York City’s cultural offerings extend far beyond its iconic museums and historic districts. The city is a global center for the performing arts, with Broadway shows that dazzle audiences from around the world and off-Broadway productions that push creative boundaries. For a more intimate experience, visit one of the city’s many art galleries and studios, or take in a performance at the Lincoln Center, where classical music, opera and dance come together. The city’s street art and murals, particularly in neighborhoods like Bushwick and the Lower East Side, offer a unique perspective on its ever-evolving cultural landscape. Whether you’re a seasoned art enthusiast or a curious traveler, New York City’s cultural offerings are sure to inspire and delight.

New York City is a city of seasons, and its weather can change with the blink of an eye. Summer, from June to August, is warm and humid, with average temperatures ranging from the mid-70s to mid-80s Fahrenheit. But don’t be surprised if you experience a heatwave, with temperatures soaring into the 90s. When the weather gets hot, you can cool off in the comfort of air-conditioned Marriott Bonvoy hotels. Summer is also the wettest time of year, with frequent thunderstorms and scattered showers. But don’t let that put a damper on your plans. The rain helps keep Central Park and other green spaces in the city lush and beautiful. 

Winters in the city, from December to February, are cold, with temperatures often hovering in the 30s and 40s Fahrenheit. Snowfall can vary, but the city typically receives around 25 to 30 inches of snow annually, creating a picturesque winter wonderland, especially in areas like Times Square and Central Park. Spring and fall are milder, with temperatures ranging from the 50s to the 70s, making these seasons ideal for exploring the city’s vibrant neighborhoods and outdoor attractions. The crisp fall air and vibrant foliage, along with the blooming flowers of spring, add a seasonal charm to the city’s bustling streets and scenic parks that you can enjoy from any of our New York City hotels.

Visitors to New York City have an array of transportation options to choose from, ensuring easy access to the city's vibrant neighborhoods and attractions. Many travelers arrive at one of the city's major airports: John F. Kennedy International Airport, LaGuardia Airport, or Newark Liberty International Airport. From there, a variety of services are available to reach your Marriott Bonvoy hotel, including taxis, ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft, and the AirTrain, which connects to the city's extensive subway system. Some hotels also offer complimentary shuttle services, providing an added convenience for guests. 

Once you're in the city, the subway is a fast and efficient way to get around, with 24/7 service and stations located throughout New York City. Buses and bike-sharing programs like Citi Bike offer flexible options for shorter trips, and walking is a great way to explore the streets of Manhattan, particularly in areas like Midtown and the Financial District. For a more leisurely journey, hop-on-hop-off sightseeing buses and water taxis provide scenic routes and a unique perspective of the city. Whether you're headed to Central Park, the Brooklyn Bridge or Times Square, New York City's diverse transportation options make it easy to get around and see all the city has to offer.

New York City is a playground for kids of all ages. Start your family adventure at the American Museum of Natural History, where kids can explore dinosaur fossils, interactive exhibits, and even a planetarium that brings the cosmos to life. For a more playful experience, Central Park offers endless possibilities, from the iconic Central Park Zoo, with its charming penguins and monkeys, to the sprawling lawns and iconic playgrounds that are perfect for running and climbing. The city’s vibrant energy and iconic landmarks make every day an adventure and you'll be close to it all in one of our local family-friendly properties. 

For those families looking to add a little creativity to their trip, the Children’s Museum of the Arts is the perfect place for kids to get hands-on with art activities and view inspiring exhibits. The Brooklyn Children’s Museum is also a sweet spot for those with a sweet tooth, offering chocolate-making workshops. And the New York Transit Museum, located in a decommissioned subway station, is a unique way to experience the city’s transportation history, with vintage subway cars to explore. With so many family-friendly activities, New York City is sure to provide a memorable experience for everyone.
