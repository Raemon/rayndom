# Hotels in London, United Kingdom

London, a city that effortlessly marries history with contemporary culture, is a treasure trove of iconic landmarks, prestigious museums, and vibrant neighborhoods. From the storied Tower of London to the grandeur of Buckingham Palace, the modern marvel of the London Eye to the cutting-edge Tate Modern, each corner of the city tells a story of innovation and tradition. Our collection of Marriott Bonvoy’s hotels in London offers the ideal starting point for your urban adventure, ensuring you are never far from the pulse of the city.

  


Whether you’re here to savor the city’s diverse culinary scene, shop along the trendy streets of Notting Hill and Covent Garden, or experience the thrill of a West End show, London’s hotels by Marriott Bonvoy cater to every traveler’s needs. With amenities such as complimentary Wi-Fi, fitness centers, and easy access to public transportation, your stay is designed to be as comfortable as it is memorable. Choose from a variety of locations, from the heart of the city to quieter neighborhoods, and discover the magic of London with every visit.

## Explore London

London is a city of contrasts, where the traditional and the cutting edge sit side by side. You’ll find historic parks alongside modern architecture, and world-class museums and galleries next to some of the coolest social spaces in the world. Hyde Park is one of the largest parks in central London and one of the Royal Parks, which also include Regent’s Park, St James’s Park and The Green Park. The park has a number of famous landmarks which are popular visitor attractions. It is home to a number of memorial features, as well as the Serpentine Lake, which is a popular location for boating and swimming. 

Beyond the city center, London offers numerous hidden gems for nature lovers. The ancient Epping Forest, just a short journey away, boasts miles of woodland trails, perfect for hiking and mountain biking. Closer to the heart of the city, the London Wetland Centre is a wildlife sanctuary with boardwalks and observation hides, ideal for bird watching and photography. Whether you’re seeking a peaceful walk or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, London’s natural landscapes provide the perfect backdrop for your outdoor explorations.

The city of London is a captivating blend of historical significance and modern culture, offering a myriad of experiences for every guest. Our hotels in London are strategically located, placing you at the heart of this dynamic metropolis, where iconic landmarks like the Tower of London and Buckingham Palace coexist with modern attractions such as the Tate Modern and the London Eye. Each of our hotels is meticulously designed to mirror the city’s sophisticated and multifaceted culture, with amenities that cater to both leisure and business travelers. After a day of exploration, you can relax in your Marriott Bonvoy guest room, complete with the convenience of on-site dining, spa facilities, and fitness centers. 

London’s cultural and artistic offerings are second to none, and our hotels are perfectly positioned to help you make the most of them. The city’s many theaters, from the world-famous West End to the cutting-edge fringe, offer a wide range of performances, from classic plays to the latest in contemporary drama. For art lovers, the British Museum and the National Gallery are essential stops, home to some of the most important collections in the world. And with its many different neighborhoods, each with its own unique character, London is a city that rewards exploration, whether you’re discovering street art, browsing a market, or enjoying a meal in a local café.

London's climate is temperate and generally mild, with each season offering its own unique character. Summers, from June to August, are typically warm with average daytime temperatures ranging from the mid-60s to the mid-70s Fahrenheit (18-24°C). However, the city can experience occasional heatwaves, pushing temperatures into the 80s or even higher. It’s a great time to enjoy the city’s numerous parks and gardens, and many Marriott Bonvoy hotels offer outdoor terraces and air-conditioned rooms to keep you comfortable. Rainfall is moderate and can occur at any time, so it’s wise to carry an umbrella or a light rain jacket. 

Winter in London, from December to February, is cool and often wet, with temperatures generally ranging from 2 to 7 degrees Celsius (mid-30s to mid-40s Fahrenheit). Snow is rare but can add a touch of magic to the city’s landmarks. Indoor attractions like museums, galleries, and restaurants are perfect for colder days, and many Marriott Bonvoy hotels feature warm, inviting lobbies and spas. Spring and autumn, from March to May and September to November, are mild and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 10 to 21 degrees Celsius (50s to 70s Fahrenheit). These seasons are ideal for exploring the city’s neighborhoods and outdoor events, with spring bringing blooming flowers and autumn offering stunning foliage.

When you visit London, you have a world of transportation options at your fingertips to make your stay as seamless and enjoyable as possible. Many visitors arrive at one of the city’s major airports—Heathrow, Gatwick, or City—where the Heathrow Express, Gatwick Express, and the DLR provide efficient and direct links to central London. Taxis and ride-sharing apps like Uber are also readily available, offering a convenient way to reach any of our hotels. If you’re traveling by train, London is connected to the rest of the country and Europe by an extensive network of mainline and Eurostar services. 

Once in London, you’ll find that the city is well connected by the iconic London Underground, or Tube, which offers an extensive network of lines and stations. Buses are another popular and cost-effective way to get around, with many routes providing access to the city’s top attractions and neighborhoods. For a more scenic journey, boat services along the Thames River offer not only transportation but also the chance to take in some of London’s most famous landmarks. Along with taxis and ride-sharing services, there are many car rental options throughout the city, including at the airports and major rail stations. Many of the city’s most popular areas—including the West End and the South Bank—are also highly walkable, making it easy to explore on foot from any of the Marriott Bonvoy hotels in central London.

London is a city that has something for everyone, including kids. Start your family adventure at the London Zoo, where your little ones can meet a diverse array of animals, from majestic lions to playful penguins. The zoo’s interactive zones, like the Land of the Lions and the Penguin Beach, provide hands-on learning and plenty of photo opportunities. For a bit of magic, head to the Harry Potter Studio Tour, where kids can explore the magical sets, costumes, and props from the beloved films. They can even ride a broomstick and sip butterbeer, making it a truly enchanting day out. 

In London, history is more than just a subject—it's an adventure waiting to be explored. The Museum of London is a prime example, with its interactive exhibits and activities that bring the city's past to life. Younger children can don historical costumes and play, while older kids can dive into the stories of the Roman and medieval eras. Outdoor enthusiasts will find a haven of discovery at Hampstead Heath, with its vast green spaces, ponds, and even a petting zoo. After a day of exploration, you can all sit down to a family-friendly meal at one of the many kid-welcoming restaurants, refueling for the next adventure.
