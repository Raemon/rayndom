###  The Old Harbor 

The charming Old Harbour was revitalized into a vibrant neighborhood when energetic individuals renovated old fishermen’s huts. Just minutes from the city center, it now boasts seafood restaurants, Iceland's original burger joint, Hamborgarabúllan, whale and bird watching tours, design boutiques, arts and crafts shops, coffee shops, and a cinema.

###  Downtown Reykjavik 

Discover Reykjavík's vibrant city center Home to the Harpa Concert Hall, Hallgrímskirkja church, and more. This compact, charming capital is easily walkable but packed with attractions. Enjoy the top sights and attractions by day, then party with locals at the best bars by night. In winter, head to the seafront for Northern Lights views. Visit trendy cafés, shop for Icelandic crafts, and savor delicious meals.

###  Harpa Concert and Conference Hall 

Harpa is one of Reykjavík’s most striking landmarks and a center of cultural and social life in the very heart of the city. Despite its young age, Harpa has garnered numerous awards for its architecture and as a concert and conference center. These include the prestigious Mies van der Rohe - European Union Prize for Contemporary Architecture in 2013.
