# Hotels in Moab, UT

Moab, the gateway to the red rock wonderlands of the American Southwest, is a city that effortlessly combines adventure with relaxation. Nestled amidst the towering mesas and rugged canyons of the Colorado Plateau, Moab offers a front-row seat to some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in the region. Whether you're gearing up for a hike in Arches National Park, a thrilling river rafting excursion down the Colorado River, or a leisurely drive through the scenic vistas of Canyonlands, the city's close proximity to these iconic destinations makes it the perfect home base for your explorations. Marriott Bonvoy’s hotels in Moab are the ideal starting point, providing comfortable lodgings and top-tier amenities to ensure your stay is both comfortable and thrilling.

  


Moab is more than just a pretty place. It’s got a charming downtown with local restaurants, boutiques and art galleries, and a friendly, small-town vibe. The community is tight-knit and there’s always something going on, from mountain biking events to stargazing parties. After a day of outdoor adventure, you can relax with a swim, spa treatment or a great meal at one of our hotels. Whether you prefer a modern, upscale hotel or a more rustic, laid-back vibe, Marriott Bonvoy’s Moab hotels have something for every type of traveler, so you can make the most of your time in this breathtaking destination.

Moab is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with a wealth of natural wonders to explore. The iconic Arches National Park, just a stone's throw away, showcases over 2,000 natural sandstone arches, including the famed Delicate Arch, providing a playground for hikers, photographers, and stargazers alike. For a more contemplative experience, the nearby Canyonlands National Park offers vast panoramas and peaceful river canyons to be savored on foot or by river raft. The La Sal Mountains, with their cool forests and sparkling alpine lakes, are a welcome respite from the desert sun, inviting you to mountain bike, fish, and camp in their refreshing embrace.

Moab is a city that is surrounded by public lands and trails that are perfect for day trips. The Potash Trail, which is a scenic biking and walking path along the Colorado River, offers stunning views and a chance to spot local wildlife. For a more challenging hike, the Moab Rim Trail provides a difficult route with panoramic views of the city and the surrounding red rock formations. Whether you are a seasoned climber or a casual nature lover, Moab’s natural beauty is sure to leave a lasting impression.

The culture in Moab is a unique blend of rugged outdoor adventure and artistic expression, reflecting the city's deep connection to the natural world. Staying at one of our hotels in Moab places you at the heart of the community, where you can experience the vibrant arts scene through local galleries, artisan shops, and the Moab Museum, which showcases the area’s rich history and geological wonders. After a day of exploration, unwind in the comfort of your Marriott Bonvoy accommodations, which offer modern amenities and a cozy atmosphere.

Moab’s cultural scene is a rich tapestry of events that honor the city’s varied heritage and stunning landscapes. The Moab Music Festival presents top-tier performances in intimate settings, while the Red Rock Film Festival spotlights the work of local and international filmmakers. To savor the local cuisine, head to the farmers' market or dine at one of the many restaurants that feature regional ingredients. Whether you’re drawn to the great outdoors or the world of art, Moab promises a vibrant and immersive cultural journey.

Moab is a place of contrasts, with a semi-arid climate that delivers hot summers and cool winters, catering to those who relish diverse weather. In the peak of summer, from June to August, temperatures can soar into the 90s and even reach 100 degrees Fahrenheit. To stay comfortable, we recommend a hotel with air conditioning, and to plan your outdoor activities for the cooler mornings or evenings. Don't be deterred by the heat, as summer is also the time for the occasional afternoon thunderstorm, which can create awe-inspiring sights like waterfalls and flash floods in the canyons. Spring and fall, from March to May and September to November, are the most popular times to visit, with temperatures ranging from the 60s to 80s. These seasons offer the best conditions for exploring Moab's outdoor attractions, with fewer visitors and milder weather.

Moab's winters are generally mild, with daytime temperatures hovering around 50 and dropping to around 30 at night. Snowfall is minimal, typically less than 10 inches per year, but when it does snow, the city and its surroundings take on a serene, picturesque quality. The cooler temperatures are perfect for indoor activities and relaxation, and the city’s hotels often feature cozy amenities like fireplaces and spa services. Whether you’re planning a summer adventure or a winter retreat, Moab’s climate ensures that there’s always a perfect time to explore and enjoy the city’s unique beauty.

Your journey to Moab typically begins at the Canyonlands Field Airport, where rental car services and shuttle providers ensure a seamless transition to your accommodations. If you're driving, the well-kept highways and picturesque byways leading to Moab will set the stage for your adventure. For those opting for bus travel, rest assured that services like Greyhound and local shuttles offer regular connections to neighboring cities.

Once you're in Moab, getting around is a breeze. The most popular and practical way to explore the area is by car. Moab is within easy driving distance of many of the region's natural wonders and outdoor activities. If you prefer not to drive, local taxi services and ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft are available for quick and flexible travel around the city. Whether you're heading to the red rock formations or the bustling downtown, Moab's transportation options ensure that you can enjoy your stay with ease and convenience.

Moab is a haven for families seeking outdoor excitement. Start your adventure at the Moab Giants Dinosaur Park, where children can marvel at life-sized dinosaur replicas and engage with the prehistoric world through interactive displays and walking trails. For an adrenaline rush, head to the Moab Adventure Center, offering activities such as rock climbing, ziplining, and inflatable fun, ensuring every family member, from the littlest ones to adventurous teens, finds something to enjoy.

  


As the day winds down, the fun continues at the Moab Museum of Film and Western Heritage, which offers a unique blend of entertainment and education with exhibits that bring the wild west and the history of filmmaking to life. For a bit of relaxation, take a dip in the Moab swimming pool, where the kids can splash and play while you soak up the serene desert views. Whether exploring ancient history or enjoying modern adventures, Moab captivates every member of the family.
