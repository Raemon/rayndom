# Hotels in Kathmandu, Nepal

Kathmandu, a city of ancient tradition and vibrant culture, is a place of spiritual and historical importance. The city's skyline is marked by the presence of ancient temples, pagodas, and monasteries, each with its own story to tell about the rich heritage of the area. As a UNESCO World Heritage site, the city is home to seven historic monuments within a small area, making it easy to visit iconic sites such as Durbar Square, Swayambhunath (the Monkey Temple), and Boudhanath Stupa. The hotels of Marriott Bonvoy in Kathmandu offer a comfortable place to relax, with modern amenities and traditional charm, ensuring that your stay is both relaxing and enriching.

  


Whether you’re a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or a culinary adventurer, Kathmandu has something to offer everyone. The city’s bustling markets, such as Asan and Indra Chowk, are a treasure trove of local crafts, spices, and textiles, providing an immersive shopping experience. For those seeking natural beauty, the nearby Himalayas offer breathtaking views and opportunities for trekking, while the city’s numerous cafes and restaurants serve up a delightful array of Nepalese cuisine, from momos to dal bhat. Marriott Bonvoy’s hotels in Kathmandu are strategically located to provide easy access to these attractions, ensuring your visit is seamless and memorable.

Kathmandu, the city in the heart of the Himalayas, is the perfect starting point for exploring some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the world. Just a short drive from the city, the Shivapuri Nagarjun National Park offers a peaceful retreat with its dense forests, cascading waterfalls, and sweeping mountain views. The park is a paradise for hikers and nature enthusiasts, with well-maintained trails that take you through a variety of landscapes, from subtropical to temperate forests. For those looking for a more spiritual experience, the park is also home to ancient temples and shrines, where you can soak in the natural beauty and cultural significance.

In the Kathmandu Valley, closer to the city, you’ll find a number of peaceful gardens and parks. The Bhrikuti Mandap Garden and Kupondol Park are great spots to relax and enjoy the greenery. Take a leisurely walk or pack a picnic and enjoy the local flora and fauna. The Bagmati River, which flows through the city, is another great place to enjoy the outdoors. Take a walk along the riverbank or visit in the evening to see the city lights reflected in the water. Whether you’re exploring the mountains or enjoying the valley, our Marriott Bonvoy hotels in Kathmandu keep you close to nature.

The culture in Kathmandu is a captivating blend of ancient traditions and modern influences, offering a unique experience to every traveler. A stay at one of Marriott Bonvoy's hotels in the city places you at the heart of this vibrant tapestry, where intricate temples and bustling markets are just steps away. Explore the UNESCO World Heritage Sites of Kathmandu, such as the Durbar Square, where the palaces and temples of the Malla kings stand as a testament to the city’s rich history. The aroma of incense and the sound of prayer bells create a sensory journey through the city’s spiritual heart, making every visit an unforgettable cultural immersion.

In Kathmandu, the city’s rich artistic and cultural heritage is on display in its many woodcarving, metalwork, and thangka painting workshops. Visit the Patan Durbar Square to see the Newari architecture and wood carvings that have been perfected over centuries. The city’s numerous galleries and museums, including the Hanuman Dhoka Palace Museum, offer a glimpse into the artistic traditions of Kathmandu. After a day of sightseeing, return to your Marriott Bonvoy hotel to relax in modern accommodations with a touch of traditional hospitality.

Kathmandu is a city of contrasts, and its subtropical highland climate creates a unique experience in each of its four seasons. Summer (June to August) is warm and humid, with average highs around 80 degrees Fahrenheit. This is also the monsoon season, which means heavy and prolonged rainfall. However, the rain is what makes the city so lush and green. Staying in a centrally located Marriott Bonvoy hotel means you can easily visit indoor attractions and cultural sites, and you won’t have to worry about the weather.

The winter months in Kathmandu, from December to February, are cool and dry, with daytime temperatures often in the low 50s and dropping to the 30s at night. While snow in the city is rare, the surrounding mountains may be dusted with a light layer of white, offering a stunning view. The most pleasant times to visit are spring and autumn, from March to May and September to November, when temperatures range from the 60s to the 70s, and there is minimal rainfall. These seasons are perfect for exploring the city’s temples, markets and scenic viewpoints, and for taking day trips to the nearby natural wonders. No matter the season, Marriott Bonvoy hotels in Kathmandu offer a luxurious and comfortable base for exploring the city’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.

When you arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport, you’ll find a variety of transportation options to help you get to your Marriott Bonvoy hotel in Kathmandu. Taxis and private car services are a comfortable and convenient way to get to the city center. If you’re looking for a more budget-friendly option, local buses and ride-sharing services like Tootle and CabHut are also available. Some Marriott Bonvoy hotels in Kathmandu even offer complimentary shuttle services to make your trip even easier.

Once you're in Kathmandu, you'll find the city and its surroundings are easy to explore. The public transportation system includes local buses and micro-buses, which are a cost-effective way to get around, though they can be crowded and may not adhere to strict schedules. Motorbike and bicycle rentals are widely available, offering a more convenient and flexible way to explore the city. You can also take advantage of Kathmandu’s network of cycle rickshaws and auto-rickshaws, which are perfect for short trips and sightseeing. Whether you choose to walk, drive or ride, Kathmandu’s diverse transportation options ensure you can discover all the city has to offer with ease.

Kathmandu is a city of wonders, both man-made and natural, that will captivate young explorers. Start your family adventure at the Kathmandu Zoo, home to over 100 species of animals, including the majestic royal Bengal tiger and playful monkeys. The zoo offers interactive feeding sessions and educational talks, making it a perfect spot for animal lovers. For a bit of history and tradition, visit the Pashupatinath Temple, where kids can learn about the city’s rich spiritual heritage. The temple’s intricate architecture and the peaceful Bagmati River provide a serene backdrop for a family outing.

For those who love the great outdoors, the city has plenty of natural beauty to explore. The Garden of Dreams is a beautifully landscaped neo-classical garden with fountains, pavilions, and children’s play areas. It’s the perfect spot for a leisurely family picnic and some fun and games. For a more educational day out, the National Museum of Kathmandu is a great place to start. The museum has a range of historical and cultural exhibits, as well as hands-on activities and scavenger hunts for children.
