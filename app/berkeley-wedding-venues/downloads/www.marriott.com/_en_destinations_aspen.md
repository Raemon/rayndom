# Hotels in Aspen, CO

Aspen is a year-round playground for the senses, combining world-class skiing, snowboarding and snowmobiling with a vibrant arts and culture scene, exciting nightlife, great restaurants and luxury accommodations. This historic mining town is surrounded by the majestic peaks of the Rocky Mountains and is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts with its beautiful hiking, mountain biking, horseback riding and whitewater rafting in the summer. Aspen is also a center for the arts, hosting the annual Aspen Music Festival and the Food & Wine Classic, as well as a number of high-end art galleries.

  


The Marriott Bonvoy portfolio of hotels in Aspen provides the perfect base for your mountain adventure. Our hotels are located near the city’s top attractions and amenities, many of which offer complimentary Wi-Fi, fitness centers, and on-site dining. Whether you’re planning a family vacation, a romantic getaway, or a business trip, our hotels in Aspen offer accommodations for every type of traveler. Explore our options and find the perfect place to stay in Aspen for your next visit.

Aspen is a haven for those who cherish the great outdoors, with a vast selection of activities to enjoy throughout the year. Situated amidst the breathtaking Rocky Mountains, the iconic Maroon Bells beckon as a must-see for hikers and nature enthusiasts. The nearby Rio Grande Trail, a 42-mile path, is perfect for cyclists and those who prefer a leisurely stroll, offering a glimpse of the region's diverse landscapes. To deepen your connection with the environment, the Aspen Center for Environmental Studies (ACES) provides educational programs and guided walks through its nature preserves, ensuring a richer understanding of the local ecosystem.

In the winter, Aspen becomes a snow-dusted playground. The four ski areas—Aspen Mountain, Aspen Highlands, Buttermilk, and Snowmass—offer a combined 5,303 acres of skiable terrain, so you can find runs for every skill level. But there’s more to winter than skiing and snowboarding. You can snowshoe or cross-country ski on groomed trails that wind through the forests. One of the most popular routes is the Hunter Creek Trail, which is especially beautiful when it’s covered in a blanket of fresh snow. Or, you can take a quiet, peaceful walk through the John Denver Sanctuary, a garden filled with native plants and stones inscribed with the singer’s lyrics.

Aspen is more than just a pretty face. It’s a place where the arts and culture are as much a part of the landscape as the mountains themselves. Throughout the year, the city plays host to a number of cultural events, including the Aspen Music Festival and School, which brings world-class performances and educational programs to the community. The Aspen Art Museum is another must-see, offering a dynamic exhibition space that showcases contemporary art from both local and international artists. When you stay at a Marriott Bonvoy hotel in Aspen, you’re just steps away from these enriching experiences, with the added convenience of on-site amenities and a cozy place to rest your head after a day of cultural exploration.

Aspen’s cultural scene is as diverse as it is vibrant. From its world-class museums and festivals to its eclectic dining and nightlife, there’s something for everyone in this mountain town. The city is home to an array of restaurants, from cozy mountain bistros to sophisticated fine dining establishments, each reflecting the city’s unique blend of rustic charm and urban sophistication. For a taste of Aspen’s nightlife, head to the Wheeler Opera House, a historic venue that hosts everything from live music to theater performances, or explore the local bars and galleries that come alive in the evening, making it easy to immerse yourself in the city’s cultural heartbeat.

Aspen’s high-altitude location provides a year-round climate of cool, fresh air and low humidity. In the summer months of June through August, daytime temperatures range from 60 to 70 degrees Fahrenheit, with evening lows in the 40s and 50s. Summer is the perfect time to enjoy the great outdoors, with clear, blue skies and sunshine, ideal for hiking, biking and enjoying the many festivals and events in town. Marriott Bonvoy hotels in Aspen offer a variety of amenities to make your stay comfortable, from outdoor patios to take in the warm sun to cozy fireplaces for the cool evenings.

Winter, from December through February, is a snowy wonderland, with average daytime temperatures in the 20s to 30s Fahrenheit and nights in the teens. The city sees a lot of snow, with an average annual snowfall of over 100 inches, making it a top destination for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports. Spring and fall are mild and pleasant, with daytime temperatures in the 40s to 60s and beautiful wildflowers and fall foliage. Marriott Bonvoy hotels provide a warm and welcoming place to stay, with heated pools and spa services to help you unwind after a day of outdoor fun.

Many visitors to Aspen arrive via the Aspen-Pitkin County Airport, just a few miles from downtown. From the airport, you can easily reach your accommodations with a taxi, shuttle, or private car service. For those flying into a major airport, the Eagle County Regional Airport is located just 70 miles from Aspen in Vail and offers a variety of flight options. The drive from Eagle to Aspen is about 2.5 hours and is a beautiful drive. Driving into Aspen is also a popular choice. The city is accessible via well-maintained highways and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Once you're in Aspen, getting around is a breeze. The Roaring Fork Transit Authority (RFTA) offers a convenient bus service that links Aspen with other nearby towns and ski resorts. If you prefer to explore at your own pace, car rentals are available in the city. Taxis, as well as ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft, are also on hand for quick and comfortable travel. Aspen's downtown area is compact and pedestrian-friendly, so you can easily stroll to shops, restaurants, and attractions.

Aspen is a wonderland of kid-friendly activities, perfect for keeping the little ones entertained. Start your journey at the Aspen Science Center, where interactive exhibits and hands-on experiments ignite curiosity and discovery. The Aspen Center for Environmental Studies offers a deeper dive into the natural world, with wildlife exhibits and engaging programs. For a splash of creativity, the Red Brick Center for the Arts provides workshops and activities that let children express themselves through painting, sculpture, and more.

When it’s time to head outside, Aspen’s natural beauty provides a stunning backdrop for family fun. Take a scenic ride up the Aspen Mountain on the Silver Queen Gondola, where the views are as breathtaking as the playground at the base. The city’s extensive network of trails is perfect for family hikes, and you can rent bikes to explore the serene landscapes at your own pace. In the winter, the Snowmass Village offers a perfect playground with its dedicated kids’ ski and snowboard programs, ensuring that every member of the family, no matter their age or skill level, can enjoy the slopes. After a day of adventure, cozy up at one of Aspen’s family-friendly restaurants for a delicious meal that will have everyone smiling.
