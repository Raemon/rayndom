## Luxurious Event Spaces

Our Penthouse is the perfect space for hosting memorable events. Whether you're planning a lavish dinner, a productive boardroom meeting, or a drinks reception. With its expansive wrap-around terrace boasting breathtaking 360-degree views of London, you'll create lasting impressions against the city's iconic skyline. This luxurious space includes a private dining room, kitchen, separate living room, two private bathrooms. Plus, the Penthouse comes equipped with state-of-the-art AV, fast and free Wi-Fi, a surround sound system, and a DJ patch panel to ensure your event is a seamless success. Elevate your gatherings to new heights in our Penthouse, where every detail is designed to impress.

Dynamic London Venues

###  Basement 

With custom lighting by Patrick Woodroffe, a state-of-the-art sound system and a built-in bar, BASEMENT is the ideal setting for film screenings, product launches, exclusive private parties and fashion events.

[ Learn More ](/en-us/hotels/loneb-the-london-edition/dining/)

###  Penthouse 

Three landscaped terraces offering 360-degree views of London sets the Penthouse apart from other event spaces in London. A private dining room and living area make it the ideal setting for seated dinners, board meetings and small receptions.

[ Learn More ](/en-us/hotels/loneb-the-london-edition/rooms/)

###  Private Dining Room 

Located above Berners Tavern, this intimate dining room features rich chocolate wainscoting, a dark walnut table for 14 guests and luxurious rose-upholstered seating. Custom menus are available from Michelin-starred Executive Chef Jason Atherton.

[ Learn More ](/en-us/hotels/loneb-the-london-edition/dining/)

###  Studio 1 

Located on the first floor, this natural light-filled venue is perfect for a close-knit meeting of up to 12 guests. The room features the latest AV equipment including a plasma screen for presentations, complimentary high-speed WiFi, teleconferencing and iPads. 

###  Studio 2 

Filled with natural daylight, this event space can accommodate various setups to host a reception of 80, dinner for 40 or board meeting of 26.

###  Punch Room 

Punch Room can be hired privately, with a unique, award-winning cocktail list, Punch Room is perfect for private celebrations and initmate meetings.

Versatile Spaces 

### Your Event Your Way

Whether you're planning an intimate affair or a large gathering, our Studio event spaces can be tailored to your London event venue needs. The light-filled Studio 2 is our most flexible option, offering a variety of configurations. A board room for 26 or a U-shape plan for 28 will set the scene for high-powered meetings and presentations. The same space can be reconfigured to host between 40-80 guests for a seated dinner, a viewing for 60 or a cocktail reception for 80. Our team is available to assist with table settings, floral arrangements, catering and any other services you may need.

###  Room Set-up Examples

Close 

Conference and Hollow Square 

Appropriate for interactive discussions and note-taking sessions for fewer than 25 people. Many hotels have elegant "boardrooms" for 10 to 20 people, equipped with full audiovisual capabilities, a writing board, cork board and a flip chart. 

###  Room Set-up Examples

Close 

E-Shape, U-Shape and T-Shape 

Appropriate for groups of fewer than 40 people. These are best for interaction with a leader seated at the head of the setup. Audiovisual is usually best set up at the open end of the seating. 

###  Room Set-up Examples

Close 

Theater 

Appropriate for large sessions and short lectures that do not require extensive note-taking. This is a convenient setup to use before breaking into discussion or role-playing groups because chairs can be moved. 

###  Room Set-up Examples

Reception 

Stand-up social function where beverages and light foods are served. Foods may be presented on small buffet tables or passed by servers. May precede a meal function.

Schoolroom or Classroom 

The most desirable setup for medium to large-size lectures. Requires a relatively large room. Tables provide attendees with space for spreading out materials and taking notes.
