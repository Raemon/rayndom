Keller Beach at Miller/Knox Regional Shoreline Water Quality Conditions

  * [Swim Beach Conditions](https://www.ebparks.org/sites/default/files/stw_KB_Current_Cond-no%20geo%20means_0.pdf) [PDF] 
  * [Monitoring Program](https://www.ebparks.org/recreation/swimming/keller-beach#monitor)



A beautifully situated shoreline picnic area, a secluded cove with swimming beach, a fishing pier at a historic site a hilltop with excellent panoramic views of the North Bay Area and a model railroad museum - all these attractions are part of 307-acre Miller/Knox Regional Shoreline in Richmond.

The park was named in honor of the late State Senator George Miller, Jr., and for the late John T. Knox, a Point Richmond resident and former State Assemblyman.

Keller Beach and Ferry Point are official [San Francisco Bay Water Trail](http://sfbaywatertrail.org/) sites.




The park is located on Dornan Drive, south through the automobile tunnel from Garrard Boulevard in Point Richmond.

Turn left onto South Garrard Boulevard, and continue south through the tunnel. After the tunnel, South Garrard Boulevard becomes Dornan Drive. The park entrance is less than 0.5 miles ahead on the right.

## Attractions

A mile-long jogging and bicycling trail loops around the lagoon which is home and feeding ground to many waterfowl.

### Picnicking

The picnic facilities are on a tree-shaded lawn surrounding a saltwater lagoon. Several of the larger picnic sites are reservable for group parties. For more information, view our [Miller/Knox Picnic Areas](/recreation/picnics/miller-knox-picnic-areas) webpage, and call Reservation to reserve..**Please note: Alcohol including beer and wine, is no longer permitted at this park at any time.**

### Swimming

Keller Beach, a gift to the park from the City of Richmond, is at the north end of Miller/Knox. There, visitors can wade and swim in San Francisco Bay. No lifeguards are on duty. Picnic tables and restrooms are nearby. For more information regarding hours and swimming conditions, check the [Keller Beach swimming page.](https://www.ebparks.org/recreation/swimming/keller-beach)

Ferry Point is at the south end of Miller/Knox. Anglers may have good luck catching starry flounder, perch, sand dab, striped bass, and other Bay fish from the fishing pier located at this historic ferry terminal. There is an ADA accessible ramp leading to the high tide line at Ferry Point Beach that kayakers can use to get their boats to the water.

A hike to the top of the nearby grassy hills is rewarded by spectacular views in all directions ([hike description](/sites/default/files/miller_knox_hike.pdf "Click here to download PDF"), 68 kb pdf). Mount Tamalpais, the San Francisco skyline, Brooks Island, and East Bay hills are all visible on clear days. The prevailing winds make for excellent kite flying.

Dogs must be on-leash (six-foot maximum leash length) in the main part of Miller/Knox - the area west of Dornan Drive. Dogs are not allowed in the lagoon.

Learn about the [Ferry Point Loop](http://www.pointrichmond.com/baytrail/ferrypointshipyard3.htm "Click here to learn about the Ferry Point Loop, opens a new browser window") of the San Francisco Bay Trail encircling Miller/Knox.

Keller Beach and Ferry Point are designated [San Francisco Bay Water Trail](http://sfbaywatertrail.org/trailheads/ "San Francisco Bay Water Trail") trailheads.

Don't miss the [Golden State Model Railroad Museum](http://www.gsmrm.org/ "Go to Golden State Model Railroad Museum website, opens a new browser window") across from the park entrance.
