# Good Neighbor Access (GNA) Permit

__Back to top

For emergencies requiring immediate access, contact [[email protected]](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#1b5a78787e68684b7e6976726f685b7e796b7a6970683574697c). Emergency is defined as a breakdown in machinery, equipment or facilities, a natural disaster or other sudden occurrence, a threat to public health, safety or welfare, that necessitates immediate access to District property to abate the emergency.  
---  
  
Consistent with the Park District’s Good Neighbor Guidelines:  
Contractors or neighbors who want access to cross District land to get to private property for a short period of time (one day to one week) may apply for an Access Permit through the Maintenance Division. [_Good Neighbor Guidelines, EBRPD Board Resolution 1993-4-82_]

### Good Neighbor Access (GNA) Permit:

  * Access to their private property through Park District property
  * Up to 7 days
  * No work on Park District property is authorized under this permit
  * No staging, parking vehicles or equipment on Park District property
  * Requires insurance documents 
  * If your permit exceed one week, please visit [Temporary Park Access (TPA) Permit](/things-to-do/permits/temporary-park-access).



[ APPLY ONLINE (Opens in new window) ](https://ebparks.my.site.com/s/login/?ec=302&startURL=/s/)

### Review Period

The review period is two weeks. The review period begins when a complete application is submitted. Incomplete applications will not be reviewed. 

### Processing Time

Turnaround time is typically 2-3 weeks for a Good Neighbor Access Permit dependent upon availability, impact to the park, and volume of requests.

### Minimum Requirements for GNA Permits

  1. Fully completed application form, with a full description of the project including all vehicles and equipment required to access the site. (i.e.; 1 pick-up truck, 2 chippers).
  2. Required insurance documents; requirements may vary based on the description of project/activity.
  3. Maps and/or KMZ files showing the location of the project and the access route you are requesting to use. Do not send latitude and longitude coordinates. [View Example Map](https://www.ebparks.org/sites/default/files/Map-Redacted.JPG)



### How to Apply

  * #### Step 1:

Create an account or log in to your account in the [Online Access Permit Portal](https://ebparks.my.site.com/).
  * #### Step 2:

Fill out the required fields.
  * #### Step 3:

Upload the required documents. See Minimum Requirements above.
  * #### Step 4:

Pay the $50 application fee.



### Fees & Fee Payment

  * All GNA Permits will require a $50 application fee due at the time of submission.
  * GNA Permits may be subject to review, inspection, coordination and/or temporary park use fees.
  * Electronic payment will be required for all GNA Permit fees and deposits.
  * See full listing of fees: [Fee Schedule](/public-info/budget#park-user-fees).



#### Payment

  * Credit Card
    * All fees and deposits may be paid by credit card using the [Online Access Permit Portal](https://ebparks.my.site.com/s/login/?ec=302&startURL=%2Fs%2F).
    * Credit card address must match the applicant address. 
  * Check
    * If credit card and bank transfer are not available, make checks payable to East Bay Regional Park District.
    * Mail to: Attn:  
Accounts Receivable/Access Permits  
2950 Peralta Oaks Court  
Oakland, CA 94605  
  
Include the Permit Number on the check.
