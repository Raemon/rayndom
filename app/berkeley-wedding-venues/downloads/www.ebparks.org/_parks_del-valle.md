## 

Del Valle Regional Park is open with limited amenities

  * The Marina Store is open between 7 a.m. and 5 p.m. daily to purchase bait, tackle, fishing licenses, snacks, and drinks.
  * The Camp Store is open 4 p.m. to 8 p.m. on Fridays; 8 a.m. to 8 p.m. on Saturdays; and 8 a.m. to 12 p.m. on Sundays.
  * Boat rentals (motorboats, patio boats) are on a first-come, first-served basis, weather permitting. 
    * Daily Hours: Sunrise to 1:45 p.m.; boats must be returned by 4 p.m. 
  * Use caution on storm-damaged trails. Signs are posted.
  * Construction equipment/noise may be present during normal working hours.
  * The Visitor Center is open. See hours below.



##### The following amenities remain closed:

  * The Swim Beach Food Concession remains closed until further notice.
  * Entire Campground is opened with the exception of a few sites located in **Lower Phase 2**. These sites are under repair. **Please contact Reservations if you have questions about cancellations.**

Deep in a valley framed by oak-covered hills, with sailboats and sailboards skimming over its waters, Del Valle is like a lakeside resort only 10 miles south of Livermore.

The centerpiece of the park is a lake five miles long with a variety of water-oriented recreation, from swimming to windsurfing and boating, surrounded by 4,395 acres of beautiful land for hiking, horseback riding, and nature study. Del Valle also is the eastern gateway to the [Ohlone Wilderness Trail](/parks/ohlone), 28 miles of scenic back-country trail.

## Park Accessibility

All bathroom buildings and many water fountains are wheelchair accessible. The family campground has two wheelchair accessible campsites. Paved and gently sloped paths include portions of the Westshore and Eastshore trails.

## Attractions

### Visitor Center

The [Del Valle Visitor Center](/parks/visitor-centers/del-valle) is on the lake's west side and contains exhibits and information about the natural history of Del Valle and surrounding wilderness. Campers and park visitors are encouraged to visit and talk with naturalists and student aides about the park and the lake and to take part in the exciting programs happening year round.

### Swimming 

Del Valle offers two swimming beaches with lifeguards on duty during posted periods. For more information regarding hours and swimming conditions, visit the [Del Valle Swimming](https://www.ebparks.org/recreation/swimming/del-valle) page.

### Picnicking

There is an abundance of landscaped picnic grounds, some with groups of picnic tables that can be reserved for large groups. Visit the [Del Valle Picnic Areas](/recreation/picnics/del-valle-picnic-areas) webpage for more information or call Reservations to reserve.

### Camping

Download the [Del Valle Campground Map (PDF)](/sites/default/files/DelValle-Camp-Map.pdf) and campsite rules. All campers with watercraft must go through a boat inspection prior to checking into the campground.

**Family Campground** : The Del Valle Family Campground has 150 sites (two accessible), 21 of them with water and sewage, and electrical hook-ups (120 volt, 50 and 30 amp). The sites are served by centrally located toilets and shower facilities. For reservations up to 12 weeks in advance, call Reservations or [Book a Del Valle Regional Park campsite online](https://reserveamerica.com/explore/del-valle/EB/110003/overview "Click here to book a Del Valle campsite now, opens a new browser window"). 

**Cabin Camping** : The Del Valle Family Campground has 5 cabin sites which can accommodate up to 8 people (cabin sleeps 5). Reservations can be made 3 or more business days in advance by phone only. For more information see [Cabin Camping FAQs](/recreation/camping/cabin-camping-faqs).  
**Group camps** : There are several group campsites for groups of 17 people or more, available by phone reservation only at least 3 business days in advance. Call Reservations to reserve. 

**Equestrian camps** : The Little Chaparral horse camp area has 4 small paddocks. The reservation of one paddock includes space for 1-2 horses, 4 people maximum, and parking for one trailer and rig only. Equestrian groups should reserve the Caballo Loco equestrian group camp as Little Chaparral is not designed for group use. Please note that horses are not allowed in developed areas of the park. Reservations are by phone only. Call Reservations to reserve.

Visit our [Camping](/parks/del-valle/camping) page for more information about camping in the parks.

### Fishing

Lake Del Valle is known for excellent fishing. The lake is stocked regularly with trout and catfish. There are also large- and smallmouth bass, striped bass, and panfish. Visit the [Fishing](/recreation/fishing) page for more information.

### Watersports Rental, Boat Rental and Launching

Fees for Motorized Vessels (including ALL electric and gas motors) are $5.00 each for launches and $10.00 each for inspections. For Non-Motorized Vessels, the fees are $3.00 each for launches and $5.00 each for inspections. For information on mandatory boat inspection for invasive mussels, see our [Invasive Mussel Program](/activities/boatingsailing/invasivemussels) page.

You can rent motorboats and patio boats at the East Beach marina area. For more information contact [RockyMountainRec.com](http://RockyMountainRec.com) or call (925) 449-5201.  
Kayaks and paddleboards can be rented seasonally at the Kayak Center at the east beach. For more information contact OutbackAdventures.com or call: Del Valle (925)-245-9500.  
Any size boat may be launched at the public boat ramp. For more information and a schedule of fees, visit the [Boating and Watercraft](/recreation/boating) page.  
Hydrofoils and E-Foils are not permitted on the lake per [Ordinance 38](https://library.municode.com/ca/east_bay_regional_park_district/codes/ordinance_38_-_rules_and_regulations?nodeId=CHVPAUSQUBO_S501ARDELAPRBOI).  
The boat speed limit is **10 miles per hour** on the entire lake.

### Lake Tours

Interpretive staff accompany visitors on scheduled natural and cultural history boat tours of the lake. Call the Del Valle Visitor Center at [(510) 544-3146](https://www.ebparks.org/parks/del_valle/default.htm# "Call using Talkdesk") for information.
