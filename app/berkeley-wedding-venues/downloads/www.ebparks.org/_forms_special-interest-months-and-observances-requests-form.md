# Special Interest Months and Observances Requests Form

__Back to top

Submit the form below to have your request for a new Special Interest Months or Observances considered by the Park District. Requests for one-time Observances should be made with as much advance notice as possible, with a minimum of 3 months' notice. Requests for annual Observances should be submitted before July 31 of the preceding year for priority consideration.

First Name (required)

Last Name (required)

Requesting Organization or Group

Email (required)

Phone Number

Name of Proposed Special Interest Month or Observance (required)

Date(s) of Proposed Special Interest Month or Observance (required)

Please provide any supplemental information or resources that advertise the dates of the Proposed Special Interest Month or Observance. (e.g. website link to the organization originating the Observance)

Type of Request Date (required)

One-Time request for the upcoming date(s) only

Request for the celebration to be added to the Park District's annual Special Interest Months and Observances Calendar

Please explain how the proposed Special Interest Month or Observance meets any or all of the following criteria:

**Mission Alignment** \- Does this celebration align with the mission, vision, and values of the Park District?

**Equity** \- Does this celebration align with the Park District’s guiding equity statement, themes, and goals?

**Community Relevance** \- What community groups are being celebrated and supported by this request?

**Educational Impact** \- Will this celebration offer unique learning opportunities about important causes and/or underrepresented communities that are not already regularly highlighted in Park District programming?

**Existing Celebrations** \- Are there other celebrations already adopted by the Park District that have similar focuses and, if yes, what is unique about this celebration that warrants its designation?

**Alignment with Other Agencies** – Is this Special Interest Month or Observance celebrated by other public agencies and/or community organizations?
