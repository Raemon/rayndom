Wedding registry planning these days can feel like a maze of endless choices. The perfect place to register for your special day seems out of reach with so many options now accessible to more people.

The good news? We’ve done the heavy lifting by researching the best wedding registry options. Modern couples love Zola’s 20% completion discounts, while Amazon’s versatility lets you register for almost anything you can imagine. The numbers speak for themselves – The Knot has helped more than 25 million couples celebrate their love story. Target stands out too, and with good reason – their generous benefits include free returns up to a year.

This piece covers the 23 coolest registry options for 2025, perfect for couples who love classic homewares or unique, vibrant finds. You’ll discover standout features that make each option special. ## [Zola](https://www.zola.com/wedding-registry)

Image Source: [Zola](https://www.zola.com)

Zola started in 2013 when creators Shan-Lyn Ma and Nobu Nakaguchi spotted a gap in digital [wedding planning](https://withjoy.com/). The platform has grown into what people now call “The Apple Store of Weddings”. It gives couples a fresh take on creating their registry.

### Zola Key Features

The platform connects you to more than 800 top brands and adds new items weekly. Couples can add gifts from any store to their universal registry, which creates a single list for guests. The platform lets couples collect cash funds without fees when paid through bank transfer or Venmo. Big-ticket items like furniture become more affordable through group gifting options.

Zola stands out with smart features that let you control shipping dates. This helps avoid packages showing up during your honeymoon. You can make virtual exchanges before items ship, and get a 20% discount valid for six months after your wedding. The registry advisor team helps guide you through the whole process.

### Zola Pros & Cons

**Pros:**

  * Complete all-in-one platform with registry, website, and planning tools
  * Group gifting makes expensive items available to more people
  * You can exchange items before shipping
  * Free shipping options under your control
  * Price matching you can use (with restrictions)



**Cons:**

  * Cash funds have a 2.5% fee for credit card transactions
  * Android app support ended in January 2023
  * Some registry items cost more than original sites
  * Customer service slows down on weekends



### Zola Pricing

Zola comes free to use. The company makes money through a 20% commission on registry sales and a 2.5% processing fee for credit card cash gifts. Couples decide if they or their guests pay this fee for cash funds.

### Zola Best For

Zola shines for couples who want everything in one place. Zola’s survey shows 83% of couples think having a wedding registry matters, and 86.62% include cash funds in their registry. The platform works great for couples who want traditional gifts and modern options like [honeymoon funds](https://withjoy.com/honeymoon-registry/) or home down payments all in one spot.

## [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/wedding)

Amazon stands out as the top registry choice for modern couples, with 45% choosing it for their [wedding gift](https://withjoy.com/blog/wedding-gift-ideas/) lists. The world’s largest online retailer gives couples a huge selection and makes creating a registry simple.

### Amazon Key Features

Amazon’s wedding registry shines with its “Earth’s biggest selection” of products from brands like Nespresso, iRobot, Le Creuset, and Instant Pot. Their universal registry button lets you add items from any website through a browser extension. You’ll find group gifting options for expensive items, fee-free gift card funds, and tools to highlight your must-have gifts. The “Thank You List” tracks gift purchases and makes writing thank you notes much easier.

### Amazon Pros & Cons

**Pros:**

  * Qualifying registrants get a 20% completion discount after $500+ in purchases
  * Registry gifts have a 180-day return window
  * Prime members get free shipping on eligible items over $25
  * Guests feel comfortable since they know Amazon well
  * Select brands offer bonus gifts when you meet specific registry amounts



**Cons:**

  * The completion discount applies only to items [Amazon.com](http://Amazon.com) sells and ships directly
  * You can’t get more than $300 in completion discounts (on $1,500 of purchases)
  * Popular items tend to sell out fast
  * The selection of high-end brands falls short of specialized registry sites
  * Wedding planning tools are limited



### Amazon Pricing

Setting up an Amazon wedding registry costs nothing. Couples who receive $500+ in registry purchases qualify for the 20% completion discount. This one-time discount works on select products that [Amazon.com](http://Amazon.com) ships and sells directly, up to $1,500 in total purchases (maximum $300 discount). Amazon makes money from registry sales instead of charging couples fees.

### Amazon Best For

Couples who want the biggest selection and convenience will love Amazon’s registry. The service works great if your guests already shop on Amazon or you want to add items from different stores through the universal registry feature. The platform has everything from [KitchenAid mixers](https://withjoy.com/blog/best-kitchen-appliances-for-wedding-registry/?srsltid=AfmBOoptn1H_Lq9WHsliMO9YzvclTL_C0r1xXup3IbkOpxhXYgtdDlgO) to Beckham Hotel Collection pillows, making it perfect for couples who need practical items to start their life together.

## [Target](https://www.target.com/gift-registry/create-wedding-registry)

Target makes wedding registry convenient with nearly 1,900 locations across the country. The retail giant ranks as a top wedding registry choice in 10 states. Their combination of affordable prices and stylish options through exclusive brands makes them a popular choice.

### Target Key Features

The registry system at Target stands out with several unique features. Couples can add items from other retailers’ websites through their universal registry option. The system automatically enables group gifting for items over $100, so friends can team up for bigger purchases. The Target app makes registry management simple with features like in-store scanning and registry checklists.

Exclusive brands like **Hearth & Hand™ with Magnolia** and **Figmint™** give couples modern [wedding registry ideas](https://withjoy.com/blog/wedding-registry-etiquette-dos-and-donts/) they can’t find anywhere else. The registry comes with great perks – free two-day shipping on orders over $35 and a price match guarantee.

### Target Pros & Cons

**Pros:**

  * Returns and exchanges accepted up to one year after your wedding
  * 15% completion discount works twice (online and in-store)
  * Select brands offer bonus gifts with qualifying purchases
  * Almost 1,900 store locations nationwide make shopping convenient
  * Shipping is free with easy pickup options



**Cons:**

  * Premium or luxury brand options remain limited
  * Some items might not match higher-end alternatives in quality
  * Product stock varies between stores
  * Stores don’t have registry consultants or experts
  * Some retailers don’t work well with the universal registry feature



### Target Pricing

The [Target wedding registry](https://withjoy.com/blog/17-best-wedding-registry-sites-that-make-gift-lists-easy-2025/?srsltid=AfmBOoqfA3imy4e6LlJAyxYOf-o4bdJ3QXasn8wKeT5Updq8E0T2J-ep) costs nothing to set up and manage. Couples get a 15% completion discount the week of their event date that stays valid for six months. The best part? This discount works twice – once online and once in-store.

### Target Best For

Budget-conscious couples who love style without high prices will find Target’s registry perfect. The nationwide store presence makes shopping easy both online and in stores. The registry works great for couples who already enjoy Target’s exclusive brands and want a mix of practical and stylish items.

## Conclusion

Picking your wedding registry is one of the most personal choices you’ll make while planning your wedding. The digital world has changed a lot. Traditional department store wish lists have evolved into diverse platforms that match every lifestyle. We looked at 23 modern registry options for 2025.

Most couples these days pick multiple registries instead of sticking to just one. You could mix Zola’s complete platform with Etsy’s handmade treasures. Another option is to combine Amazon’s big selection with Honeyfund’s experience-based gifts. This strategy will give a good mix of everyday essentials and meaningful experiences.

The savings you get after the wedding can vary. Zola and Joy offer generous 20% discounts, while Food52 gives a more modest 10%. These discounts help cut costs when you buy the remaining items. On top of that, technology keeps making registries better. Zola lets you control shipping dates, Target gives you a full year to return items, and Joy uses AI to help write thank-you notes.

Cash funds have gotten more popular lately. Guests can help pay for honeymoons, home down payments, or other special experiences. In spite of that, many couples still want traditional physical gifts to set up their first home.

Your registry should show who you are as a couple. You might care about sustainability and love Etsy’s handmade items. Maybe you’re foodies who want Food52’s special collections. Or perhaps you dream of adventures through Traveler’s Joy. The best registry mixes practical items with your personality.

Note that wedding registries should make gift-giving easy for guests. They also help you get items you’ll actually use and love. Setting up multiple registries takes extra work at first, but this careful planning guides you toward gifts that improve your married life for years to come.
