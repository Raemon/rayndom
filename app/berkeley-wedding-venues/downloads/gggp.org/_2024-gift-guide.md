Our favorite adult and youth books from this year:

[![The ServiceBerry Book Cover](https://gggp.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/the-serviceberry-9781668072240_xlg-1.jpg)](https://store.sfbg.org/product/serviceberry/12471?cs=true&cst=custom)

**_The Serviceberry: Abundance and Reciprocity in the Natural World_ by Robin Wall Kimmerer  
**Author of the beloved _Braiding Sweetgrass_ and _Gathering Moss,_ Indigenous scientist [Robin Wall Kimmerer ](https://www.robinwallkimmerer.com/)writes a sweet book about the power of the gift economy. Using examples from nature, Kimmerer challenges us to think more about the benefits of gifting in our communities and seeing abundance in the world around us. Get this book where you can find it, because it continues to sell out.  
[Shop now](https://store.sfbg.org/product/serviceberry/12471?cs=true&cst=custom)

[![The State of Fire - Why California Burns Book Cover](https://gggp.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/SFIRcover_web800px-1-1.jpg)](https://store.sfbg.org/product/state-of-fire/12472?cs=true&cst=custom)

**_The State of Fire: Why California Burns_ by Obi Kaufmann**  
A fire book for the holidays? This small, but mighty read is beautifully done by local East Bay author whose other books on California forests, coasts, desserts, and water supply are all equally factual and visually appealing.  
[Shop now  
](https://store.sfbg.org/product/state-of-fire/12472?cs=true&cst=custom)

**_Patterns, Patterns Everywhere_ by Kellie Menendez  
**Pulling from nature and the world around us, local San Francisco author Kellie Menendez finds patterns…everywhere. This story is also currently featured in the [Children’s Garden Story Book Stroll](https://gggp.org/learn/family-community/bean-sprouts-family-days/) at San Francisco Botanical Garden. Go take a look and explore!  
[Shop now](https://store.sfbg.org/product/patterns-patterns-everywhere/12122?cs=true&cst=custom)

![Tiny Troubles Book Cover](https://gggp.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/s590927286411979285_p11346_i1_w421.jpeg)

**_Tiny Troubles: Nelli’s Purpose_ by Sophie Diao**  
Follow a tiny succulent and an inquisitive lithop while they search around for their purpose. Under, over, inside, and outside, what is a purpose anyway and where do you find one? Local author and illustrator Sophie Diao brings up good questions for everyone. Great for ages 4 to 8 years old.
