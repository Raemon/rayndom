# ‘Photosynthesis’ Light Show at the Conservatory Changes with the Seasons

December 10, 2025

## The Conservatory’s light show begins approximately 30 minutes after sunset every night, transforming the building into a canvas of colorful light art.

The Conservatory of Flowers’ free light show is back! Bundle up, bring a warm drink, and spread out a blanket on the lawn in front of the iconic Conservatory of Flowers to enjoy an evening of free music and mesmerizing psychedelic visuals in San Francisco’s Golden Gate Park. Every night starting 30 minutes after sunset, the outside of the Conservatory of Flowers will be illuminated with a seasonal light show that lasts about half an hour start to finish and runs on a loop until midnight.

**Our beloved flower-powered light show is FREE every night and open to all ages! No tickets or RSVP required.**

![](https://gggp.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/104_251024_NN_7036.jpg)

### Where to Watch

Venture up close to the walls of the Conservatory to enjoy the details of the projections or sit back and enjoy a view of the entire building awash with color. The best viewing areas are along car-free [JFK Promenade](https://sfrecpark.org/1538/JFK-Promenade) and on the lawns in front of the Conservatory of Flowers, also known as “Conservatory Valley”. Street parking is available along Nancy Pelosi Drive and Bowling Green Drive, bike racks are available nearby, and the best ride share drop off location is in front of the Goldman Tennis Center. For more information on how to get to the Conservatory of Flowers, see our [Getting Here](https://gggp.org/visit/getting-here/) page.

![](https://gggp.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/090_251024_NN_6979.jpg)

Nightly

The outside of the Conservatory of Flowers will be illuminated from dusk to midnight every night. 

### About the Light Show

This beautiful light art installation was developed in partnership with the San Francisco Recreation and Park Department and was designed by Lumigeek with music by Tim Digulla. The installation uses special projectors to transform the all-white glasshouse into a series of exquisite scenes inspired by the rare tropical flowers growing within this national landmark.

The light art installation changes twice a year, adapting it’s visuals and music to the spring-summer season and fall-winter season.

This public art installation takes place outside the Conservatory of Flowers and is free for visitors of all ages! No tickets or RSVP required.

Learn more about the [Conservatory of Flowers](https://gggp.org/conservatory-of-flowers/), [how to get here](https://gggp.org/visit/getting-here/), and [admissions & hours](https://gggp.org/visit/admissions-hours/).
