It’s been another busy year in the East Bay dining world. **Original Joe’s** opened, closed, and then reopened in Broadway Plaza, while **Pinstripes** opened and closed permanently down the street. Trends have included upscale Asian (**Pippal, Khaki Bar and Indian Canteen, Tropa**), beer gardens (**Calicraft Brewing Co., Headlands Brewing, Canyon Club Brewery**), and bakeries (the **French Spot, Starter Bakery,** the **Model Bakery**). As is the case every year, the East Bay sadly saw the closure of some classics, **Walnut Creek Yacht Club, Standard Fare Kitchen and Pantry, Rick and Anne’s Restaurant,** and the **Brass Door** among them.

But the question on the eve of the new year is: What’s next? In alphabetical order, here are a few of the most anticipated food openings (that we know of) we’re eyeing in 2026: Half this week, with the rest coming in next week’s _Dish._

As a reminder: Take these estimated opening dates with a grain of salt. These things are tricky, especially in the permit-intensive restaurant world, and you may notice a holdover or two from last year’s list that never quite launched in 2025.

In fact, in some late-breaking _Dish_ news, one of those holdovers is now set to debut _just_ before the deadline: Pleasanton’s beloved soft-serve ice cream destination, [**Meadowlark Dairy**](https://www.instagram.com/meadowlarkdairy/)**,** planned to hold a soft opening for its new Livermore location on Second Street on Monday, December 22, from 11 a.m.-6 p.m. Check their Instagram page for confirmation and details!

### Dish N Dash, Dublin

**Estimated opening:** Winter or early spring

Dishdash has become a go-to for quality, upscale Middle Eastern fare in Silicon Valley, first for its full-service sit-down restaurant opened in Sunnyvale in 2001 and later for its collection of fast-casual [**Dish N Dash**](https://www.dishdash.com) spin-offs around the South Bay. Husband-wife owners **Emad Ibrahim** and **Nadiah Mshasha** are now venturing into the Tri-Valley, which they see as a similar market to Silicon Valley: Ethnically diverse, with busy families looking for healthy, flavorful, and accessible food options. That’s the hallmark of Dish N Dash, which, via a centralized kitchen, prepares scratch-made hummus, falafel, pita bread, and other Middle Eastern staples on a daily basis that other counter-service restaurants often outsource. Set to open by March in the former Applebee’s Grill and Bar spot in the Hacienda Crossings shopping center, the 5,400-square-foot new Dublin location will include an enclosed outdoor patio while serving a full lineup of Dish N Dash favorites that includes fresh salads with grilled meats; falafel pitas; shawarma wraps, plates, and rice bowls; and the restaurant’s popular _mansaf_ lamb-yogurt dish.

### Marufuku Ramen and Mensho, Walnut Creek

**Estimated opening:** Marufuku—early spring; Mensho—February

Not just one, but _two_ highly touted ramen operators are set to debut in the coming months to engage in brothy battle with Ramen Hiroshi in downtown Walnut Creek.

First up is [**Marufuku Ramen**](https://www.marufukuramen.com)**,** which is taking over the former Extreme Pizza space on Cypress Street. The rapidly expanding San Francisco–based ramen outfit is known for rich, creamy Hakata-style tonkatsu ramen using pork bone broth prepared for 20 hours. The totally revamped space, meanwhile, will include a wood-forward, Japanese-themed interior and a bamboo-lined back patio, as well as a full bar with a cocktail program incorporating ingredients such as Japanese citrus and whiskey infused with roasted tea leaves. Look for an early spring debut in Walnut Creek, while a second location in Dublin’s Ulferts Center is on track to open in January.

Second up is [**Mensho**](https://mensho.com)**,** which is eyeing a February opening in the former Essence space on North Main Street. Founded in 2005 by ramen master **Tomoharu Shono,** the “farm-to-bowl” concept with house-made noodles has built a cult following with its use of seasonal and unusual ingredients in noodle soups that have included at other locations (including on Piedmont Avenue in Oakland) spicy lamb miso cumin, duck matcha, Wagyu beef, and several vegan options. Its sixth Bay Area spot will follow a similar formula—creative ramen soups with whole-wheat grains noodles, along with a few sides, plus wine, beer, and sake—but with a menu entirely unique to Walnut Creek.

### Meyhouse, San Ramon

**Estimated opening:** Winter

Owners **Omer Artun** and **Koray Altinsoy** started [**Meyhouse**](https://www.meyhouserestaurant.com) as a South Bay pop-up modeled after on _meyhanes,_ lively tavern-style Turkish eateries known for pairing drinks with small plates similar to Spanish tapas, often with musical accompaniment. They parlayed that into two successful sit-down brick-and-mortar restaurants in Palo Alto and Sunnyvale, which offer modern interpretations of Turkish dishes such as _lakerda_ (house-cured Atlantic bonito with red onion, olive oil, and fresh dill), _Turkmen mantisi_ (steamed Turkish dumplings over garlic yogurt drizzled with paprika butter), and grilled seafood and meats, to go with craft cocktails and a regular lineup of live jazz. The pair are now bringing the same formula to San Ramon’s City Center Bishop Ranch, where they will open a third location in the former Dumpling Time space. Says Altinsoy: “Our mission has always been to bring the vibrant flavors and rich culinary traditions of Turkey to the Bay Area, and this new location is an exciting step forward.” Expected to open soon this winter, Meyhouse San Ramon will have a similar refined feel and menu as the South Bay locations, with live music TBD.

### North Italia, Walnut Creek

**Estimated opening:** Spring

Cheesecake Factory was a phenomenon when it opened in Walnut Creek in 2009 and still packs them in today inside its palatial 11,000-square-foot setting in Plaza Escuela on the edge of Broadway Plaza. The nationwide chain is likely hoping for an encore with an Italian twist when it debuts [**North Italia**](https://www.northitalia.com)—the Italian concept with nearly 50 U.S. locations that it took over six years ago—right next door in the former Tilly’s this spring. Diners can expect a similarly open, modern-neighborhood setting, full bar, and menu of Italian favorites highlighted by handmade pasta, pizza, and craft cocktails.

### Oceania Fish Bar, Walnut Creek

**Estimated opening:** 2027

Yes, it likely won’t open until 2027. But **Oceania Fish Bar** is a big deal in the area, considering it’s taking over for beloved downtown staple Walnut Creek Yacht Club that closed earlier this year. Fortunately, the new operators seem perfectly poised to carry on the Yacht Club’s torch. The **Ghaben siblings,** Mike, David, and Rolla, have a nearly 40-year track record of restaurant success in the Walnut Creek area, ranging from the Hubcaps and Original Mel’s diners to more recent bespoke concepts Broderick, Batch and Brine, Lita, and World Famous Hotboys. And their plan will pay homage to their predecessor’s culinary legacy while completely transforming the mid-century modern space with a sleek, contemporary makeover. The food focus at Oceania will be on a “seafood-forward fish bar utilizing a variety of cooking techniques,” while current plans call for expanded indoor-outdoor seating with 1,360 square feet of added ground floor dining, plus a 1,179-square-foot second floor addition serving as a corporate HQ. While exact food and drink plans remain scarce, there will be plenty of time to tease out the details: Pending city approval, the ambitious project won’t start until 2026 and likely not open until the following year. In the meantime, you can monitor progress at Lita or Broderick, both located at Bonanza and Locust streets where the Ghabens have literally cornered the market.

### Osaka Marketplace, Pleasant Hill

**Estimated opening:** July

[**Osaka Marketplace**](https://www.osakamarketplace.com) general manager **Kazuhiro Takeda** doesn’t just want to offer seafood and meats, snacks, fruits, and other Japanese food products at his expanding string of Bay Area grocery stores. He wants each visit to feel like a mini-trip to Japan—specifically Osaka, one of the food-obsessed country’s culinary hubs. Which is why each store doesn’t just offer the typical Safeway experience, but also food courts with made-to-order Japanese fare, as well as annual events such as a beauty fair featuring exclusive Japanese beauty products and an Osaka Summer Festival serving up Osaka-style street fare. Set to debut this summer in Pleasant Hill, Osaka’s fourth location will be its biggest and most ambitious yet. The 49,000-square-foot market will, of course, have a large grocery component. But also, unique to Pleasant Hill, it will feature an adjacent, nearly standalone gourmet food hall that incorporates elements of Japanese pop culture with food vendors serving up ramen, tempura, curry, gyoza, and more. The idea, says Takeda, is to create a lively environment and immersive experience that combines grocery shopping and dining in one destination to draw customers up and down Interstate 680 from Sacramento to Dublin.

**Bonus:** Two other big Asian grocery stores with food court components are poised to open this year: Japanese-owned [**Tokyo Central Specialty Market**](https://tokyocentral.com/) in Emeryville’s Bay Street mall (opening January 31), and cult Korean American grocery chain [**H Mart**](https://www.hmart.com) in Dublin (opening date still TBD).
