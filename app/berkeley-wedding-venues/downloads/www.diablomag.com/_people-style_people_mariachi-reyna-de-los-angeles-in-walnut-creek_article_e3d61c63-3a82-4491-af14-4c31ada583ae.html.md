Mariachi is a centuries-old Mexican musical tradition, and one that is often dominated by men. Mariachi Reyna de Los Angeles is touted as the genre’s first professional all-female group in the United States, and they will visit Walnut Creek’s Lesher Center for the Arts on June 13.

The band was founded in 1994 by Jóse Hernàndez, who also began Mariachi Sol de Mexico. He was inspired to start an all-female group through his Mariachi Heritage Society after watching a performance from a traveling female Mexican band. “I thought, ‘Well, I could probably get an all-female group from my foundation,’ ” he says. “Before the ‘90s, it was maybe one girl playing with an all-male group, as a violinist [or] a singer.”

Mariachi Reyna de Los Angeles has grown to include 13 female performers, including Hernàndez’s daughter Crystal, a seventh-generation mariachi who joined in 2024. “Looking at a mariachi is like looking at a Mexican flag. It’s representing Mexico and its culture,” says Crystal. “We bring a certain sensitivity and interpret in a way a man cannot, because we process our emotions in a different manner.”

The band, which celebrated its 30th anniversary last year, have recorded several albums, been nominated for U.S. and Latin Grammy Awards, and performed at the White House during the Obama Administration and for the likes of Oprah Winfrey and Tom Cruise.

“Music doesn’t discriminate,” says Crystal. “We want to share our culture and our music with as many people as we possibly can. … [Mariachi] keeps our culture and music alive for the future generations.” _[lesherartscenter.org](http://lesherartscenter.org)._
