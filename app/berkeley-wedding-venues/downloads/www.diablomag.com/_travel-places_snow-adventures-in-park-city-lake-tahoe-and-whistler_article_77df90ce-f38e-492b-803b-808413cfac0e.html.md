**In February, the 2026 Olympic Winter Games will be held at various sites in northern Italy—showcasing the world’s best athletes on snow and ice. For sports enthusiasts, the games are just one more reason to book a mountain vacation where you too can feel like an Olympian.**

**Park City and Deer Valley in Utah, Palisades Tahoe in California, and Whistler in British Columbia have all hosted the Olympics in years past and boast top-tier snow sports and après options. Each resort has activities that let you channel your inner champion, and flight times are less than 2.5 hours from the Bay Area.**

* * *

### PARK CITY, UTAH

## DEER VALLEY & PARK CITY

Deer Valley and Park City resorts not only hosted the 2002 Winter Olympics, but they’ve been awarded the 2034 Winter Games as well. Located just two miles apart, they’re both in Park City, a 19th-century silver-mining town with a postcard-perfect Main Street and lift access to the ski resorts.

![DM2512_072_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Deer Valley’s snow park area.

Courtesy of Deer Valley Resort

Thanks to the nonprofit Utah Olympic Legacy Foundation, the facilities and infrastructure from the 2002 Winter Games have been maintained and are open to the public. At Utah Olympic Park, you can experience the adrenaline rush of the bobsled, luge, or skeleton, and even take ski jump training into a year-round aerated pool. The Utah Olympic Oval has public skating on the ice that hosted the speed skating events. Visitors to Soldier Hollow, the Nordic skiing venue, can cross-country ski or book a biathlon experience. 

![DM2512_074_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Deer Valley draws freestyle athletes to its world-class terrain.

Courtesy of Deer Valley Resort

#### SLOPE STATS

Deer Valley is undergoing the most extensive resort expansion in ski industry history, more than doubling in size under its Expanded Excellence initiative. The resort gets an average of 300 inches of annual snowfall and offers 31 lifts, 202 ski runs, and 4,300 skiable acres. An epic “beginner level” groomer is the Green Monster, a 4.8-mile run from the top of the Revelator Express lift to East Village. Skill-sharpening opportunities include Deer Valley’s Ski with a Champion program (with instructors such as freestyle skier Kris “Fuzz” Feddersen and mogul medalist Shannon Bahrke) and its women’s Empowerment Ski Clinic. 

Park City is the biggest ski resort in the United States, with 7,300 acres of skiable terrain, 43 lifts, 14 bowls, and over 300 trails; the mountain also has eight half pipes and six terrain parks. This winter, the resort opens a new 10-person gondola from Canyons Village to the Red Pine area.

![DM2512_075_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Relax in snowbound waters.

Courtesy of Deer Valley Resort

#### APRÈS OFFERINGS & EPIC ACCOMMODATIONS 

Canyons Village is a high-energy resort hub with a soon-to-be-replaced cabriolet lift that brings riders from the parking lot to the village and the Red Pine gondola that takes skiers and boarders up the mountain. A popular ski-in/ski-out lodge for overnight guests is Pendry Park City, which has lavish amenities such as free Cadillac loaners and Taylor guitars that can be checked out from the front desk. Pendry Park City also has Canyons Village’s only rooftop pool and lounge; thoughtfully appointed rooms, suites, and two-bedroom family residences; and superior dining options like Japanese steak house Kita. Kita draws diners to its fresh sushi bar, as well as specialty meat dishes like elk chops and bison filets. 

Another base hub that’s being built out for the 2034 Olympics is Deer Valley’s East Village, part of Deer Valley resort and its Expanded Excellence initiative. This new pedestrian village is being planned by top developers and gives visitors access to lodging, dining, and shopping, as well as over 3,700 additional skiable acres. 

Nearby, Kimball Junction is a shopping and dining hot spot with comfort food eateries such as Hearth and Hill. But Main Street in Park City remains the big draw, with acclaimed restaurants like Riverhorse on Main and French bistro Le Depot Brasserie.

### OLYMPIC VALLEY, CALIFORNIA 

## PALISADES TAHOE

It was 65 years ago that CBS televised its first Olympics in the U.S., from a resort then known as Squaw Valley. The VIII Winter Games put Lake Tahoe on the map, paving the way for at least a dozen other ski areas to open in the region. 

Today, you can still experience the thrill of runs like Red Dog, where the downhill giant slalom races took place in 1960. Olympic Valley has several vintage buildings that were part of the Winter Games, including athlete housing and a chapel. Take the tram from the Village at Palisades Tahoe to High Camp, and you’ll find a free Olympic Museum with memorabilia from the games. A tribute film plays on a loop, and visitors can browse old newspaper clippings and see everything from participant flags to early Olympic skis, boots, and skates.

![DM2512_088_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Palisades Tahoe has three enclosed lifts, providing comfortable access to its tallest slopes.

Courtesy of Palisades Tahoe

#### SLOPE STATS

With its recent rebranding and 2011 acquisition of the adjacent Alpine Meadows ski area, Palisades Tahoe is the largest mountain resort in California, with 6,000 skiable acres across eight peaks. Its annual snowfall averages 400 inches or more, and it’s the only ski resort in North America with three enclosed lifts that take you to its highest elevations.

#### APRÈS OFFERINGS & EPIC ACCOMMODATIONS 

An overnight stay at the Village at Palisades Tahoe provides a base camp vibe, live music, shopping, and dining. Most rooms include full kitchens, fireplaces, and balconies, while guests have access to pools, hot tubs, and free underground parking. The more luxurious spot, a short shuttle ride away, is Everline Resort and Spa, Lake Tahoe. The year-round resort has a championship golf course, two heated swimming pools, a 150-foot waterslide, and hiking and biking trails. In winter, its resort chair lift accesses intermediate and advanced terrain at Palisades Tahoe—beginners can take a free shuttle to the village tram—while skaters glide across the ice on Everline’s outdoor rink. An on-site rental shop has ski and snowboard equipment, and guests can also rent snowshoes and cross-country skis, along with all-terrain fat tire bikes.

Visitors can enjoy a championship brunch buffet and Bloody Mary/mimosa bar at Cascades. For dinner, Six Peaks Grille earns rave reviews for its 5- and 10-course meals created by chef Chris Watkins. Dishes like smoked rabbit under glass offer comfort food with flair. For dessert, try the Basque cheesecake with its signature “burnt” crust hiding a decadent, creamy center.

### BRITISH COLUMBIA, CANADA

## WHISTLER BLACKCOMB

A scenic two-hour drive from Vancouver International Airport is world-renowned mountain resort Whistler, cohost of the 2010 Winter Games. Located in the British Columbia town of Whistler, Whistler Blackcomb is the largest ski resort in North America. The Olympic alpine events took place on Whistler, while the Nordic and sliding competitions were held at Whistler Olympic Park and Whistler Sliding Centre.

Imagine shredding the mountain with Whistler Blackcomb’s Ski with an Olympian program, where you tackle the terrain under the tutelage of one of Canada’s top pros. Speed demons can race Olympic legend Dave Murray’s downhill course on Whistler Mountain. The biathlon program tests your skills at cross-country skiing and shooting a .22 caliber rifle at the resort’s Olympic range. For thrill-seekers who want guided rock and ice climbing, backcountry skiing, and mountaineering, Mountain Skills Academy and Adventures is the go-to company.

![DM2512_094_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Runs at Whistler Blackcomb vary in difficulty.

Courtesy of Whistler Blackcomb

![DM2512_086_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

The resort is home to two mountains.

Courtesy of Whistler Blackcomb

#### SLOPE STATS

Whistler Blackcomb offers two side-by-side mountains with more than 200 marked runs, 16 alpine bowls, and three glaciers across 8,171 acres of terrain. A favorite for intermediate skiers and riders is the Peak to Creek run from the summit of Whistler Mountain to the Creekside base, the longest run at the resort. Whistler’s Peak 2 Peak Gondola opened in 2008 and is considered an engineering marvel. It also served as an aerial shuttle between mountains for the Olympics. It’s one of the world’s longest and highest continuous lift systems, connecting the peaks of Whistler Mountain and Blackcomb Mountain.

#### APRÈS OFFERINGS & EPIC ACCOMMODATIONS

The five-star Fairmont Château Whistler at the base of Blackcomb Mountain has a standard of excellence that’s visible the moment you enter the property. Dining options run the gamut from the gourmet café Portobello to the Grill Room—a stone- and cherry-wood-decorated steak house that serves Canada’s celebrated AAA prime beef; fresh, sustainable seafood; and organic produce grown by local farmers. Must-order dishes include tomato gin soup with British Columbia mushrooms, double-smoked bacon, or duck confit, flambéed tableside with Sheringham gin.

![DM2512_073_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Dine with a view at Glacier Creek Lodge.

Courtesy of Whistler Blackcomb

Also worth a visit: Christine’s on Blackcomb is a fine-dining restaurant on the mountain, with captivating views and an impressive wine list.

In Whistler Village, where the Olympic rings monument is a popular photo op, there are over 200 culinary options. Lorette Brasserie serves dishes ranging from steak frites to beef cheek bourguignon. For Italian fare, Il Caminetto’s culinary program is directed by one of Canada’s leading chefs and farm-to-table pioneers, James Walt.
