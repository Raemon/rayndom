Local authors Michelle Chouinard, Aiden Thomas, and Matthew Zapruder have engaging new releases.

Courtesy of Minotaur Books

#### _The Serial Killer Guide to San Francisco,_ by Michelle Chouinard

Hayward’s Michelle Chouinard unravels a captivating mystery through serial killer tour host Capri Sanzio, the granddaughter of a convicted murderer in San Francisco. When an imitator of her grandfather emerges and she and her daughter are suspects in the crimes, Sanzio tries to clear the family name.

#### _Twist the Familiar,_ by Gary Hernbroth

Danville’s Gary Hernbroth has been a longtime player in the East Bay business community. In July, he debuted _Twist the Familiar,_ a self-help book that synthesizes the professional lessons he’s learned into a motivational manifesto.

#### _The Lost Coast,_ by Jonathan and Jesse Kellerman

Local mystery fans welcomed the latest Clay Edison novel by Berkeley resident Jesse Kellerman and his father, noted mystery writer Jonathan Kellerman, this summer. This installment sees Edison working as a private investigator and venturing into rural California to pursue a fraud case.

#### _Burdened: Student Debt and the Making of an American Crisis,_ by Ryann Liebenthal

Oakland’s Ryann Liebenthal uses expertise gleaned from her reporting for _n + 1_ and _Mother Jones_ to take an in-depth look at the student loan crisis. She examines the explosion of debt over the last 50 years and the limits it places on upward mobility for a growing number of Americans.

![DM2412_038_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Macmillan

#### _Celestial Monsters,_ by Aiden Thomas

 _New York Times_ best-selling writer and Oakland native Aiden Thomas returns with the conclusion to their Sunbearer Duology. The young adult fantasy saga follows Teo, Niya, and Aurelio as they navigate the aftermath of the Sunbearer Trials.

#### _Tell Me Something, Tell Me Anything, Even If It’s a Lie,_ by Steve Wasserman

Delve into the literary landscape under the guidance of Steve Wasserman (the publisher at Berkeley’s Heyday) with this essay collection. Across 30 pieces, the East Bay native reflects on Barbra Streisand, Susan Sontag, and others through the lens of an experienced publishing professional.

![DM2412_046_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

B.A. Van Sise

#### _I Love Hearing Your Dreams,_ by Matthew Zapruder

Saint Mary’s College associate professor Matthew Zapruder infuses his poems with lush language and a keen point of view on contemporary society. The verses in his latest poetry collection capture the highs and lows of a dream world—many of which cross over into reality.

* * *

### Coming Attractions

####  _My Marriage Sabbatical,_ by Leah Fisher

Orinda-based Leah Fisher had a successful psychotherapy practice for decades. Happily married for more than 30 years, when she was in her early 60s she decided it was time to fulfill a lifelong dream and embarked on a year of solo travel around the world. She recounts her journey—a kind of _Eat Pray Love_ for the older set—and its nurturing impact on herself and her marriage in this heartfelt memoir that will be released in January.

#### _Flirting Lessons,_ by Jasmine Guillory

Jasmine Guillory’s rom-com empire continues to expand. The Oakland author’s new book, set to debut in April, follows the newly single Avery Jensen, who attempts to brush up on her flirting skills by seeking advice from Taylor Cameron, a notorious heartbreaker—only to discover she is falling for her charms instead.

#### _The Girls Who Grew Big,_ by Leila Mottley

Oakland’s Leila Mottley burst onto the literary scene in 2022 with the East Bay–set _Nightcrawling,_ written shortly after her high school graduation. Mottley’s sophomore novel, out in June, captures the bonds of friendship between teenage moms in the Florida panhandle as they encounter a series of crossroads.
