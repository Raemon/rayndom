Olympian Kristi Yamaguchi, left, partnered with Scott Heller to create Heller Jewelers’ Always Dream necklaces.

Sean Gelbaugh

If you’re looking for a gift that gives back this holiday season, Kristi Yamaguchi has you covered. The East Bay resident and Olympic gold-medal-winning figure skater has partnered with San Ramon’s Heller Jewelers on a limited series of necklaces to benefit her children’s literacy organization.

During the 2023-2024 academic year, about 3,600 children and parents across 28 schools spent more than 1 million minutes reading, helped by resources from Kristi Yamaguchi’s Always Dream. The Danville-based nonprofit, founded in 1996, aims to improve literacy rates among underserved preschoolers, transitional kindergarten students, and kindergartners in the Bay Area and Hawaii; the organization is determined to meet its Impact 2027 goal to reach 10,000 individuals each year.

![DM2412_041_DIG_v2.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Blanca Espinoza Studio

![DM2412_040_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Blanca Espinoza Studio

Studies show that consistent literary habits—for these students, fueled by physical books and e-reader tablets, and rooted in parental involvement—have a transformative effect on youngsters. Reading gives students critical skills that put them on the path to scholastic and personal achievement.

Always Dream plans to expand to 36 schools—and 5,000 parents and kids—over this school year. To aid that mission, Yamaguchi has collaborated with Heller Jewelers on a collection of three necklaces, with money from sales benefiting the nonprofit.

![DM2412_042_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Blanca Espinoza Studio

![DM2412_043_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Blanca Espinoza Studio

“Jewelry always tells a story,” Yamaguchi told those in attendance at the collection’s debut at Heller’s location in City Center Bishop Ranch. She worked with store founder Scott Heller on what she called a “dream collaboration.” The necklaces are available in white and yellow gold variations and are adorned with diamonds. The designs evoke Always Dream’s North Star logo—traditionally regarded as a representation of hope and a guiding light—as well as the upward arrow symbolic of the organization’s momentum. Each piece is versatile and can easily be layered together.

![DM2412_045_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Blanca Espinoza Studio
