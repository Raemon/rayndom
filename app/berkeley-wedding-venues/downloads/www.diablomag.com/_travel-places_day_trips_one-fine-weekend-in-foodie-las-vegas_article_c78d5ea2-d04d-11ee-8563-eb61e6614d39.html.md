Las Vegas is a destination for visitors from all over the world, drawn to its attractions, entertainment, and culinary scene. It’s an easy trip from the East Bay, with nonstop flights from Oakland International Airport and Buchanan Field Airport in Concord, and there’s plenty of food and drink to discover.

* * *

![DM2402_005_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Lost Spirits Distillery’s 16-course tasting menu adds dramatic flair.

Christopher DeVargas

### Dinner With a Show

When it comes to Las Vegas, the more outrageous, the better—an ethos that’s true for restaurants, too. For example, consider **Lost Spirits Distillery** ’s 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea dinner, a theatrical 16-course tasting menu that transports you into the Jules Verne novel of the same name.

The seafood-heavy dishes include everything from escargot to fried octopus tentacles, each with its own unique (and often absurd) presentation. The servers and chef seem straight off a casino stage, exhibiting incredible attention to detail and showmanship.

The dining portion is just the beginning of what could be the strangest night of your life. Inside the eatery (located near Area 15, a scene unto itself), the adventure continues after dinner with a mazelike spirit-tasting experience that offers rum-based shots and cocktails in circus-themed rooms featuring a variety of performers.

* * *

![DM2402_013_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Enjoy top-quality steak house fare at Scotch 80 Prime.

Courtesy of Scotch 80 Prime

### Best Bets

Another showstopper is **Bazaar Meat** from James Beard Award–winning and Michelin- starred chef José Andrés at **Sahara Las Vegas.** Andrés, the founder of the humanitarian nonprofit World Central Kitchen, has built a reputation with his inventive cuts of meat, and while any dish is sure to delight, it’s the Ultimate Tasting menu ($290 per person) that will send you to foodie heaven.

This culinary journey includes rich meat slices hanging from metal antlers and Japanese A5 Wagyu beef presented table side before the server dishes out a sampling of treats, including a playful take on buffalo wings prepared with bison, cotton candy foie gras with corn nuts, and _sobrasada_ toast with drunken goat cheese. The culmination is a dessert map of sorts, drawn on the table with melted chocolate and punctuated by cream puffs, tarts, and cookies.

Also a standout is **NoMad Library Restaurant,** a sophisticated hot spot inside the **NoMad Las Vegas.** After slipping into the romantic, dimly lit setting, start your dinner with a cocktail and caviar. NoMad puts its own spin on favorites such as foie gras and oysters. But don’t miss out on its more creative dishes, including the lobster thermidor, a two-and-a-half-pound crustacean served with carrot butter.

Slightly off the Strip, **Scotch 80 Prime** is a swanky steak house specializing in high-end fare and seafood. At first glance, the menu is typical steak house, with premium cuts of meat and plentiful sides, but take a closer look and you’ll discover so much more, such as the miso-glazed Chilean sea bass, braised Wagyu beef cheek, and wood-fired Nigerian prawns. If bone marrow is on the menu, be sure to indulge.

Head into **Spago** by Wolfgang Puck for brunch with a view of the Bellagio fountains (make sure to time your meal with the watery eruptions). Lead with a seasonal seafood tower, a tasting tree filled with treats such as shrimp, oysters, poke, crab legs, and more, served in two or three tiers. And save room for a sweet ending, including gold-flaked desserts.

### Raising the Bar

Although Vegas has a vast selection of watering holes, seek out a speakeasy for extra fun. The **Cosmopolitan** holds several options, including a tequila bar called **Ghost Donkey;** an energetic live music venue named the **Barbershop Cuts and Cocktails;** and **Ski Lodge,** a mountain escape in the middle of the desert. Additionally, the **Mob Museum** is home to the **Underground Speakeasy and Distillery,** where bathtub gin is cheekily served in a miniature bathtub. Sip tequila at **Jalisco Underground,** located in **Resorts World Las Vegas,** or head off-Strip to the internationally acclaimed **Laundry Room.**

Lisa Vanderpump of Bravo reality show fame has opened two cocktail spots in Sin City, one in **Paris Las Vegas** (**Vanderpump à Paris**) and the other in **Caesars Palace** (**Vanderpump Cocktail Garden**). Both are opulently decorated, including live plants and faux jewels, and offer birthday cocktails adorned with tiaras; Cocktail Garden also serves the goat cheese balls made famous on _Vanderpump Rules._ The shareable plates are decadent and cocktails wink at moments from Vanderpump’s series, as well as her affinity for tea and rosé.

* * *

![DM2402_058_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

A visit to Las Vegas isn’t complete without a trip to Easy Donuts at Proper Eats Food Hall.

Chris Wessling

### A Court Above

Seeking a fast-casual meal? Hotels are now adding diverse dining options in the form of elevated mall-style food courts.

The **Proper Eats Food Hall** at **Aria Resort and Casino** holds a selection of international outlets. Digital ordering screens make sampling from multiple locations easy, so guests can try a slice from Steve Aoki’s **Pizzaoki** while also digging into Korean fare from London’s famous **Seoul Bird.** There are eateries from every corner of the world, including **Temaki Bar,** which boasts top-quality hand rolls, and an outpost of New York City’s breakfast spot **Egghead.** Proper Eats has a central bar and several only-in-Vegas concepts, including **Lola’s Burgers,** where smashed burgers are popular, and ramen restaurant **Laughing Buddha.**

Resorts World also excels at the food court concept. Its 24,000-square-foot food hall from Singapore-based Zouk Group boasts 16 stalls, and many have an Asian flair. The format is similar to Proper Eats. Order from multiple places at once and grab your drinks from a central bar before finding a seat.

Both food halls include speakeasies: Resorts World’s **Here Kitty Kitty Vice Den** is an intimate space with suggestively named cocktails, and the “doughnut” shop at Aria doubles as **Easy’s Cocktail Lounge,** which features live music and imaginative drink presentations.

* * *

![DM2402_004_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

The Smoke Show is one of the creative drink presentations at Easy’s Cocktail Lounge.

Stacey Torma

### Tastes of the Town

One of the better ways to try a variety of spots in one day is a food tour. **Lip Smacking Foodie Tours** provides myriad options, including a high-end trek along the Las Vegas Strip and a more laid-back stroll down Fremont Street. Simply choose your theme—from boozy brunch to worldly bites—and let your guide do the rest. For those looking to splurge, the **Savory Bites and Neon Lights** tour starts with three distinguished restaurants and ends with a helicopter ride over the city.

There is so much more to explore in Las Vegas’s iconic food scene—and even this small sampling is undoubtedly more than any individual can eat in one trip. You will just have to come back and satisfy your hunger again and again.
