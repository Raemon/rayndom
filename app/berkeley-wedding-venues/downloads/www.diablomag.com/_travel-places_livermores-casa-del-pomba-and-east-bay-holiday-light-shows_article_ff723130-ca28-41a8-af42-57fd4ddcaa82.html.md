Albert Santoso

Dave Rezendes has built quite a reputation in the Tri-Valley after decades of decorating his Livermore house with an astonishing show of holiday lights every December. In fact, people recognize him all the time—even if they have never met him before.

“I’ll be pushing a shopping cart filled with strings of Christmas lights, and people will say, ‘You must be Deacon Dave!’” Rezendes told _Diablo_ a few years ago. Rezendes is a Catholic deacon whose mission is to provide holiday cheer to his community each Christmas season. 

“I wanted to do something for people of all faiths or no faith,” Rezendes told _People_ last year. His first display in 1982 featured about 2,000 lights and grew each year, with one dark December due to COVID-19. Last year’s presentation featured 902,120 lights—each dedicated as a prayer for world peace—and drew by far the biggest audience yet, as Rezendes’s Casa del Pomba received worldwide acclaim when it was awarded the grand prize on the ABC reality show _The Great Christmas Light Fight._ Rezendes was already working on his plan for his 2025 display last December when he received the $50,000 prize from the television program.

![DM2512_078_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Casa del Pomba

There have been more than 80 marriage proposals over the years under the holiday display, most occurring at the site’s “proposal bridge” fountain. (Rezendes has officiated some of the weddings.) Casa del Pomba features more than 40 interior Christmas trees, although those are off-limits to public visitors. When _Diablo_ visited the home a few years back, our favorite display was a tiny tree in the hallway bathroom, which was adorned with toothbrushes and miniature tubes of toothpaste.

Rezendes depends on a team of about 20–30 volunteers, who work year round planning and producing each new display. The team learned this year’s theme in January but was sworn to secrecy, while the public discovers the theme and number of lights at an opening night procession on November 29. Afterward, guests are invited to visit for free from 6–9 p.m. each evening through New Year’s night.

_Casa del Pomba is located at 352 Hillcrest Avenue in Livermore._ [_casadelpomba.com_](http://casadelpomba.com) _._

### More Local Light Shows

#### ** _Garden of D’Lights_**

** _11/21–1/11_**

This annual event at the 3.5-acre Ruth Bancroft Garden and Nursery in Walnut Creek boasts art pieces, lasers, and thousands of lights. [_ruthbancroft​garden.org_](https://www.ruthbancroftgarden.org/) _._

#### **_2025 Annual Holiday Tree Lighting_**

** _12/4_**

Head to the Dublin Civic Center to send letters to Santa Claus, sip hot chocolate, listen to student musicians, and marvel at the glittering 30-foot tree. [_dublin.ca.gov_](http://dublin.ca.gov) _._

#### _Holiday Parade of Lights and Tree Lighting_

** _12/4_**

Broadway Plaza’s celebration in Walnut Creek includes a vibrant parade, an open house, and a magical moment when Santa Claus illuminates the tree. [_broadwayplaza.com_](http://broadwayplaza.com) _._

#### _Bill Graham Menorah Project First Lighting_

** _12/14_**

Union Square’s holiday displays in San Francisco have featured a huge menorah honoring Hanukkah for the last 50 years. [_billgrahammenorah.org_](http://billgrahammenorah.org) _._

#### _Candy Cane Lane_

** _Dates to Be Announced_**

Pleasanton’s Walnut Drive and Kottinger Barn Plaza become an immersive wonderland of holiday decor and scenes featuring favorite characters at this time of year. [_instagram.com/candycanelane​pleasanton_](https://www.instagram.com/candycanelanepleasanton/) _._ —Emilie White
