![DM2503_041_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

East Bay Artisan’s English muffins come in five flavors.

Shaun Samuels

Light but sturdy with nooks and crannies that can be topped with everything from butter and jam to peanut butter and eggs Benedict, English muffins are a near universally beloved breakfast staple in the United States.

“I don’t know anyone who doesn’t love English muffins,” says Jourdan de Sanctis of East Bay Artisan bakery.

![DM2503_056_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

English muffins, including those from East Bay Artisan, are reaching new levels of popularity.

Shaun Samuels

“They’re great,” adds Muffin Lab’s Janneh Merritt. “They’re more substantial than toast but not as heavy as bagels—just this beautiful, multilayered product that makes whatever you’re putting inside of it 10 times better.”

At his San Ramon–based operation, Merritt produces oversize fluffy, small-batch muffins in unique flavors—maple and bourbon, focaccia, and blueberries and honey—that he sells for delivery and in breakfast/brunch sandwiches at Bay Area pop-ups and East Bay farmers markets. Meanwhile, de Sanctis makes a two-inch-thick sourdough take on English muffins, which he offers in five flavors at farmers markets and uses in breakfast sandwiches at his new Cafe 1277 in Walnut Creek.

The most heralded versions, however, have to be those from Model Bakery. Napa-based co-owner Sarah Mitchell Hansen recently opened a location in downtown Walnut Creek, which doubles as a production facility for her light-as-air, Oprah Winfrey–approved muffins that are made from focaccia bread dough and then dusted in cornmeal and griddled in clarified butter to finish. Model makes upward of 7,000 of them a day in Walnut Creek, which are sold individually and in sandwiches at the North Main Street café and in packs online, at the bakery, and at supermarkets like Whole Foods Market in Napa and Sonoma and Berkeley Bowl Marketplace.

Thomas’, watch out.
