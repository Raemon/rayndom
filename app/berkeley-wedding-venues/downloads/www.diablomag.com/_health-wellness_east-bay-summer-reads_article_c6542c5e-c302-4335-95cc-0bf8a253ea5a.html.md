#### _The Bones of the Apostle,_ by John Amos

Join globe-trotting detectives Petrie and Pettigrew for an archaeological adventure as they attempt to reacquire the apostle Thomas’s bones in this mystery by UC Berkeley alum Amos.

![DM2507_119_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Rough Edges Press

#### _Headless in London,_ by John Bluck

Former NASA employee and current Livermore resident Bluck sends his hero, deputy sheriff Luke Ryder, on his honeymoon in Britain, only for Ryder to find himself in the midst of a spy operation.

![DM2507_120_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Gallery Books

#### _Home of the American Circus,_ by Allison Larkin

East Bay writer Larkin’s latest novel follows down-on-her-luck Freya, who moves back to her hometown and grapples with the life she left behind, including her relationship with her niece.

#### _Your Actual Life May Vary,_ by Linda Lenhoff

The East Bay's Lenhoff takes readers along for the ride on protagonist Patty Grant's hunt for community and belonging, which traverses treacherous real estate, a mysterious theme park, and more.

#### _Who Is Government? The Untold Story of Public Service,_ edited by Michael Lewis

This anthology from Berkeley’s Lewis, featuring essays from the Bay Area’s W. Kamau Bell and Dave Eggers, captures the lives of federal employees and their crucial (often unsung) work.

#### _The Pacific Circuit: A Globalized Account of the Battle for the Soul of an American City,_ by Alexis Madrigal

Madrigal, a journalist from Oakland who writes for _The Atlantic,_ uses his hometown as an example of the benefits and costs of technology.

#### _The Gorgon’s Fury,_ by Brandon Mull

 _New York Times_ bestselling writer and Clayton native Mull kicks off a new story in his Fablehaven universe. In the young adult novel, a pair of satyrs pursue a gorgon who is turning their neighbors to stone while they simultaneously attempt to get a smartphone.

#### _The Library Game,_ by Gigi Pandian

Attend a murder mystery dinner that ends in an actual murder in this fourth Secret Staircase book by Berkeley’s Pandian.

#### _Hard Town,_ by Adam Plantinga

Lamorinda’s Plantinga brings back Kurt Argento for an intense thriller as the former cop tracks a family who vanished in an eerie Arizona desert town.

#### _Healthier Beauty: The Ultimate Guide to Radiant Skin, Hair, and Wellness,_ by Jessica Rose

Rose, a cosmetologist and nutritionist from Walnut Creek, provides practical wellness tips and a nature-based perspective on beauty.

#### _Francine’s Spectacular Crash and Burn,_ by Renee Swindle

Oakland’s Swindle blends humor and heartbreak as grief-stricken Francine rescues a bullied 10-year-old boy—and finds out his foster mom is her teenage crush.

#### _On Muscle: The Stuff That Moves Us and Why It Matters,_ by Bonnie Tsui

Berkeley author Tsui returns with a personal and global dive into human muscles, both their wide-ranging scientific impact and their relation to beauty standards.

#### _Fired Up: How to Turn Your Spark into a Flame and Come Alive at Any Age,_ by Shannon Watts

Overcome barriers and take advantage of your potential with help from the founder of Moms Demand Action (a former Lafayette resident).

#### _Perseverance > Endurance: Lead with Resilience. Grow Through Adversity. Win Together.,_ by Brandon Young and Blayne Smith

Fremont native Brandon Young cowrote this leadership guide involving techniques used both by Army Rangers and executives in corporate boardrooms.
