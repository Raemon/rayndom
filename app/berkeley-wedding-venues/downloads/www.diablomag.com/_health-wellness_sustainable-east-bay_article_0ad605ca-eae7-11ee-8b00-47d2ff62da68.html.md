**Our region has long been a leader in eco-friendly practices, from minimizing waste to dramatically reducing our carbon footprint. In this special section,_Diablo_ looks at some of our area’s most creative green businesses and initiatives—which help make shopping, dining, and living in the East Bay a healthier proposition for us all.**

* * *

![ecolivingLIVE.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

## TOP 10 GREEN LIVING

**Classic establishments as well as newcomers on the scene are making it easier—and stylish!—to embrace a more eco-conscious way of life.**

#### PAINT WITH PURPOSE

Since Annie Sloan developed her Chalk Paint in 1990, its ease of use—including compatibility with various materials and surfaces—has made it a favorite among those revamping furniture. Her company is also mindful of its environmental impact: In addition to sustainable production practices like recycling liquid waste, it trades in water-based paints, with low levels of VOC. Chalk Paint is stocked at Danville’s Whim House. _[whim​house​.com](http://whim%E2%80%8Bhouse%E2%80%8B.com)._

#### NO-WASTE CLEANING PRODUCTS

In 2021, Amy Golan debuted Planet Renu in Walnut Creek. In its REfill shop—a draw for those looking to reduce their carbon footprint—a range of products can be purchased using refillable containers, from detergents and stain removers to hair and skincare items. Customers also flock to the store’s alternatives to plastic wrap and bags. _[planet​renu.com](http://planet%E2%80%8Brenu.com)._

![DM2404_049_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Kindred Motorworks

#### RESTORED VEHICLES

Mare Island’s Kindred Motorworks specializes in restomods (a portmanteau of “restoration” and “modification”)—notably with electric options. The first delivery of its Kindred Bronco, an update of 1966 to 1975 models of the Ford vehicle with all-new interiors and present-day technology, happened in late 2023. Production of its Kindred Chevy 3100 and Kindred VW Bus—revamps of the pickup truck (1947-1953) and van (1954-1967)—are slated to get underway next year. _[kindredmotorworks.com](http://kindredmotorworks.com)._

![DM2404_044_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Kelsey Schmidt/Elsie Green

#### FRENCH FURNISHINGS

In Concord, shop the Elsie Green showroom and neighboring Annex. (The latter is set up like a French apartment.) The team scours the French countryside for myriad goods, from oil paintings to flatware with bone handles to bentwood chairs. About 85 percent of the inventory is vintage and sourced in France; the rest is newly made by California artisans, including the store’s reclaimed wood furniture line, which is customizable. _[elsiegreen.com](http://elsiegreen.com)._

#### WELL-RESTED

Founded in 1990, Berkeley’s Earthsake describes itself as “the very first retailer of natural fiber organic mattresses and bedding—made and grown in the U.S.A.” Eric and Heather Gahan’s small, family-owned business purveys products that help you get a good night’s sleep, extending to furniture, too. There are also items to outfit a natural bathroom, including shower curtains, countertop accessories, and soaps. _[earthsake.com](http://earthsake.com)._

#### CURATED DECOR

Bradley Long opened Curated Alameda in 2019. Its vintage and high-end furnishings and art are a treasure trove for mid-century modern design enthusiasts—not to mention sustainably minded shoppers. On any given visit, you might luck upon a rope rocking chair by Hans Wegner, a Polder sofa by Hella Jongerius for Vitra, or pieces previously eyed at Design Within Reach (at more accessible prices). [_curated​alameda.com._](http://curated%E2%80%8Balameda.com)

![DM2404_042_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Gantri

#### SEE THE LIGHT

While Gantri partners with creators from all over the globe, its lighting designs are made in a state-of-the-art factory in San Leandro, with proprietary plant-based materials that use non-GMO, sustainably farmed sugarcane. Bonus: The company’s 3D-printing process translates to minimal waste. Gantri recently released the limited-edition Bag Table Light by Tommy Bogo. The collaboration marks the first foray into home decor for the Oakland-raised designer’s Tombogo brand. _[gantri.com](http://gantri.com)._

![DM2404_041_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

A Verb for Keeping Warm

Elysa Weitala

#### TO DYE FOR

Kristine Vejar and Adrienne Rodriguez wrote the book—make that two books—on natural dyeing. Think: colors created with marigolds, _Persicaria tinctoria_ (indigo), and locally foraged mushrooms. The couple’s Oakland studio and shop, A Verb for Keeping Warm, carries naturally dyed threads, fabrics, and yarns—including a house line that employs the on-site garden. DIY kits for wall hangings and more are also available, while dyeing classes are planned year-round. _[averbforkeepingwarm.com](http://www.averbforkeepingwarm.com)._

#### RECYCLED REDWOOD

If you’ve admired the outdoor furniture at Cafe Ohlone in Berkeley, like the log seating composed of redwood trunks, good news: The maker, Bay Area Redwood, has a San Ramon warehouse, which contains a showroom that provides customers a look at the fabrication process. The company turns wood from fallen trees into wares, from furniture to wall paneling. _[bay​arearedwood.com](http://bay%E2%80%8Barearedwood.com)._

#### REFURBISHED BIKES

With its bike repair, education, and reuse programs, Berkeley-based Waterside Workshops’ Street Level Cycles is more than just a neighborhood bike shop. Youth interns learn repair skills that are put to use refurbishing old bikes and assisting in its DIY Community Open Shop. Along with sales and repair services, new and used accessories and parts can be purchased. (Street Level gives and accepts bike donations.) _[water​sideworkshops.org](http://water%E2%80%8Bsideworkshops.org)._

## TOP 10 PLANET-FRIENDLY SHOPPING

**From reusable containers to handmade products and thoughtful business practices, there is something sustainable for everyone in the East Bay.**

#### TENDER TOYS

Find sustainably made toys at Whimsy Kidz in Lafayette. There are multiple brands available, including Tender Leaf Toys, which focuses on an ethical, sustainable supply chain. These wooden toys are made in Indonesia using reclaimed rubberwood, and a tree is planted in the place of each one that’s been used. All toys are finished with a hand-painted application. _[whimsy​kidz.com](http://whimsy%E2%80%8Bkidz.com)._

#### RADIANT RINGS

Oakland’s Esqueleto, a jewelry and decor store known for green production practices and ethically sourced materials, features fine jewelry by owner Lauren Wolf as well as pieces by independent designers. If you’re looking for a one-of-a-kind engagement ring, dazzle your special someone with Wolf’s Blue Horizon Sapphire Ring, which features a conflict-free oval sapphire and yellow gold. _[shopesqueleto.com](http://shopesqueleto.com)._

![DM2404_039_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Maker’s Weave

#### WONDERFUL WRAPS

Danville-based Maker’s Weave aims to connect artisans more directly with customers by skipping steps like brokers and distributors. The women-owned company partners with artists who specialize in traditional crafts, such as weaving and embroidery; the result is a highly curated, fair-trade collection of luxurious, handmade stoles and scarves of cashmere, wool, silk, and linen. _[makersweave.com](http://makersweave.com)._

![DM2404_043_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Good Common Sense Naturals

#### HOLISTIC HERBS

Good Common Sense Naturals, which has locations in Pleasanton and Danville, focuses on bath and body products sans harmful chemicals in BPA-free, recyclable containers. Everything is nontoxic and organic. You’ll find curated herbs and spices—and elevate your bath time with their Herbivore bath salts, made with essential oils and organic flowers. _[goodcommonsense.com](http://goodcommonsense.com)._

#### SMART SCENTS

Traditional perfumes are often made with alcohol, but Scents by the Bay in Berkeley takes a different approach with its perfume oils. These last longer in the bottle (up to a dozen years if properly stored) as well as on your skin (a quarter-ounce bottle from Smart Scents can equal up to three department store bottles). The best part? They offer scent dupes of popular mainstream perfumes, so you don’t have to sacrifice your favorites. _[scentsbythebay.com](http://scentsbythebay.com)._

#### CONSCIOUS CLOTHING

Oakland’s Ali Golden aims to keep affordability and sustainable fashion in the same conversation. Find clothing items produced in partnership with a Hong Kong fair-wage factory, a certified fair-trade factory in India, and a Peruvian co-op that’s women-owned. Lilac and sky-blue sweaters, along with kelly green dresses, make for the perfect spring wardrobe staples. _[aligolden.com](http://aligolden.com)._

![DM2404_048_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of ThredUp

#### THOUGHTFUL THRIFTING

You don’t have to leave your living room to combat fast fashion. Simply visit Oakland-based online platform ThredUp to find clothing, shoes, and accessories that have already been gently loved. This modern consignment company is passionate about recycling secondhand items, which often come at a fraction of the cost of mainstream retail stores. High-end deals await from such favorite brands as Theory, Prada, and Vince. _[thredup.com](http://thredup.com)._

![DM2404_040_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Makers Market

#### BEAUTIFUL BAGS

All items at Walnut Creek’s Makers Market are handcrafted in the United States, and each piece is ethically produced, organic, and/or sustainably made. The wide-ranging wares include jewelry, clothing, ceramics, candles, and bath and body products. Be sure to check out the extensive collection of bags for men and women, such as the Escape Canvas Utility bag made by Massachusetts-based Forestbound. _[makersmarket.us](http://makersmarket.us)._

#### REFILLABLE APOTHECARY

Reduce waste by reusing your beauty product containers. Bring any container you wish into Mighty Market in Martinez and get refills of hand soap, shampoo, and much more. All products sold are locally sourced and made in the United States. You can also donate your old receptacles, which will be sterilized and offered to customers who come in without their own. _[mighty​.market](http://mighty%E2%80%8B.market)._

#### NOT-SO-AVERAGE NECKLACE

When your child’s teeth start coming in, there’s no need to expose them to potentially harmful plastics—or sacrifice your sense of style. New moms can wear fun jewelry that their baby can safely teethe on. Chewable Charm in Livermore provides teething necklaces made in the U.S. out of nontoxic, BPA-free components such as food-grade silicone. _[chewable​charm.com](http://chewable%E2%80%8Bcharm.com)._

## TOP 10 ECO-FRIENDLY DINING

**Whether it’s using compostable take-out containers or propping up regenerative family ranchers, the East Bay is packed with ways to make food and drink better for our environment.**

#### BEE HEALTHY

Kelly Knapp, founder of Miss Bee Haven in Brentwood, has been boosting these fruitful little flyers for over two decades. The full-time beekeeper partners with east county farms such as Frog Hollow to pollinate their fruit and nut trees and plant bee-friendly bumper crops; removes hives for homeowners and businesses; and doles out her antioxidant-rich, allergy-defying local honey at farmers markets. _[missbee​honey.com](http://missbee%E2%80%8Bhoney.com)._

#### EVERYDAY LOCAL

Not everyone has time to scour ingredient labels or shop farmers markets, so we rely on our grocery stores to curate quality local food. There are a lot of great ones, but perhaps no grocer has been as deeply ingrained in the community as Diablo Foods. For more than five decades, the Stokes family has provided Lamorinda with access to quality meat, fresh-caught seafood, and local organic produce. _[diablofoods.com](http://diablofoods.com)._

![DM2404_037_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Sabio on Main

Travis Fisher

#### SUSTAINABLE GOURMET

When Francis Hogan first co-opened Sabio on Main in Pleasanton, he was surprised to learn there was no commercial composting. Hogan promptly offered up his restaurant to test a program that’s now employed citywide. He also sources from regenerative ranches, uses humanely caught seafood, and partners with local farms on his hyperseasonal menu, while eschewing disposable products as much as possible. And at his planned Danville wine bar, Hogan will feature 24 wines on tap, an approach that significantly reduces the waste inherent in traditional bottle service. _[sabiopleasanton.com](http://sabiopleasanton.com)._

![DM2404_038_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Cream Co. Meats

#### MEET YOUR MEAT

Oakland’s Cream Co. Meats operates its 15,000-square-foot whole animal butchery in east Oakland, in which it portions, dry ages, and grinds its own hormone- and antibiotics-free meat. It also serves as a conduit between a network of endangered small-scale sustainable, regenerative West Coast ranches and the lucrative wholesale contracts—from individual restaurants to regional grocery stores—that help keep these vital producers in business. _[creamcomeats.com](http://creamcomeats.com)._

#### LOOKING FORWARD

Curtis and Esin deCarion—the team behind Danville’s Esin and Revel, and Lafayette’s Social Bird—go out of their way to run their restaurants as sustainably as possible. This commitment is woven into their DNA, from the use of recycling and composting bins to buying produce, fish, and meat from sustainable purveyors. _[esinrestaurant​.com](http://esinrestaurant%E2%80%8B.com), [revelkitchen​andbar​.com](http://revelkitchen%E2%80%8Bandbar%E2%80%8B.com), [social-​bird.com](http://social-%E2%80%8Bbird.com)._

#### SANDWICH BREAK

Berkeley’s Prime Roots offers a line of plant-based deli meats derived from koji, a quick-growing fungus with long fibrous roots that mimic the look and texture of meat muscle fibers. Minimally processed, these tempting sandwich “meats” are healthy alternatives to the more carbon-heavy fillers found in traditional BLT, turkey club, and ham and cheese sandwiches. _[primeroots.com](http://primeroots.com)._

![DM2404_045_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Ron Essex Photography

#### NATURAL WINES

Whether you call them “biodynamic” or “natural,” there’s no questioning the rise in popularity of wines made from sustainably farmed grapes and produced with minimal intervention. Two East Bay wineries have been doing it since before it was cool. Jared Brandt and Tracey Rogers studied natural winemaking in France before founding Donkey and Goat, which moved to Berkeley in 2006, and have long worked with organic and biodynamic grapes using native yeast and bacteria. Retzlaff Vineyards is now certified through the California Certified Organic Farmers, but the Taylor family has practiced organic, sustainable farming (no chemicals, pesticides, or herbicides) in Livermore since the 1970s. _[donkeyandgoat.com](http://donkeyandgoat.com), [retzlaff​vineyards.com](http://retzlaff%E2%80%8Bvineyards.com)._

![DM2404_046_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Sailing Goat

Clara Rice Photography

#### FISH OF THE DAY

Seafood is one of the most difficult foods to pin down as far as sustainability. Is it OK to buy it farmed, wild, line-caught, local? Head chef Ross Kaplan at Sailing Goat restaurant keeps things simple. He makes his acclaimed fish and chips with locally plentiful rock cod, while other seasonally available seafood is incorporated into stews, chowders, and specials. This year, Sailing Goat will take things a step further by sourcing straight from area fishermen—including those bringing daily catch into the Point San Pablo Harbor marina next to the restaurant. _[sailinggoatrestaurant.com](http://sailinggoatrestaurant.com)._

#### REDUCE, REUSE, REFILL

In 2020, the three founders of Oakland’s WasteWhat, a nonprofit centered on reducing single-use waste, opened Rockridge’s Re-Up Refill shop, which encourages consumers to bring reusable containers to fill (and refill) up with everyday staples such as rice, pasta, coffee, flour, oil, vinegar, milk, eggs, and even maple syrup—all organic and sourced as locally as possible. _[reuprefills.org](http://reuprefills.org)._

#### BACK TO EARTH

Berkeley-based counterculture foodies were responsible for much of the slow-food ethos—from organic veggies to whole-grain bread—that is now mainstream. Three Stone Hearth keeps the incense burning in Berkeley, where the kitchen and co-op sell everything from pasture-raised eggs to grass-fed hot dogs. They offer classes and workshops to get in touch with your inner culinary hippie. _[threestonehearth.com](http://threestonehearth.com)._
