Joy Ofodu wants to use humor to heal. The East Bay–based voice actor, creator, and CEO of Enjoyment Endeavor has explored various forms of expression, but bringing happiness to her audiences is always her motivation.

“Healing humor can be a joke, it can be a story, it can be an audio bite,” says Ofodu. “Just something that cheers and warms you and gets you through a tough day or a tough situation.”

Ofodu has loved performing since she was a child taking dance classes in Livermore. She studied communications at the University of Southern California and picked up social media skills while working at award shows. She eventually joined Instagram as an integrated marketing manager before becoming a full-time content creator and voice actor. Even if you don’t know Ofodu’s name, you might have heard her voicing roles in video games, seen her in ads for Dove and Google, practiced meditation with her via the Daily Shine podcast, and laughed along with her _Dating Unsettled_ podcast. She says being funny is a superpower.

“Right now, we’re in a really tough cultural climate where matters of identity are being weaponized. People are contending with a lot, and there’s a huge loneliness epidemic across America,” says Ofodu, who has received grateful messages from people all over the world. “It’s just this beautiful phenomenon where people have thanked me or told me a story about what [my] humor did to heal them that day. I know that I have a unique gift of delivering human humor.”

Ofodu will make her directorial debut with _Heart of Sunshine,_ a Kickstarter-funded comedic short film that she cowrote. Ofodu will also star in the story about a young Black girl and her father. “It’s going to be a feel-good, comedic comfort food type of film that speaks to expressing love [and] having difficult conversations with your parents,” she says. “I want to show a refreshingly normal picture of Black fatherhood inspired by my dad. … He’s an immigrant [who was] … busy, but he would make time for me in the form of laughter or jokes.” _Heart of Sunshine_ will show in film festivals at the end of 2026.

Ofodu also works with community members, especially Black and Latino creators, to help them secure grants and other funding opportunities. “I want to encourage any young aspiring entertainer to make use of this beautiful Bay Area that we have around us and our proximity to Silicon Valley,” says Ofodu. “This is an incredible place to grow and connect.”
