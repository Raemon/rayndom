![DM2406_068_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

A bedroom in Caroline Lizarraga’s home.

Christopher Stark

For more than two decades, decorative artist Caroline Lizarraga has boosted the wow factor in residential, retail, and restaurant environments throughout the Bay Area. In this spring’s San Francisco Decorator Showcase, her talents were on view in multiple spaces. At the Menlo Park location of Robin restaurant, she manifested a copper-leaf abstract wall as an ethereal backdrop. In Lizarraga’s own Piedmont home, the ceiling in her daughter’s bedroom is lacquered and a bathroom features tadelakt plaster.

![DM2406_069_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

One of Lizarraga’s wallpaper designs.

Parette

Now, with a newly launched wallpaper collection with Parete, her finishes and aesthetic are accessible to a wider audience. “My main objective for the wallpaper was to create almost impossible designs on the wall,” Lizarraga says. “They are all so complex that painting them on walls is nearly cost-prohibitive.” Among the six motifs are Toot My Horn and Honk Your Horn, both printed on metallic cork and offered in palettes that are a departure from traditional horn colors, such as pink and blue. Fringe Benefits evokes macramé, while I Lost My Marbles nods to Italian marbled paper.

![DM2406_072_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Another wallpaper design of Lizarraga’s.

Parette

Raised in West Marin, Lizarraga has long practiced various art forms—from painting to ceramics to jewelry-making—and studied fashion design. She frequented flea markets and garage sales, with an eye on potential candidates for makeovers. After an interior designer offered her $1,200 for a $20 two-tiered table that she had revamped, Lizarraga realized she might be onto something.

She spent a year under the tutelage of a master furniture restorer in Florence, Italy, and, upon returning to California in the late ’90s, discovered a studio in San Francisco that taught European decorative painting. “Chinoiserie, inlaid horn, marble, gilding,” Lizarraga recalls. “I was completely in love.” She waited tables and ran a wine bar while training for several years. Word of mouth led to a succession of jobs, until painting became her full-time profession.

![DM2406_074_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

A third showcasing of Lizarraga’s wallpaper designs.

Parette

“I’ve taken what I’ve learned and put my own twist on it or modernized it,” she says. As part of her process, Lizarraga produces 12-by-12-inch sample boards of the actual finish. Once approved by the client, she gets to work on-site. Two years ago, she began her largest undertaking: a 30,000-square-foot residence in Jackson Hole, Wyoming.

In addition to Jackson Hole—she estimates that project is halfway done—Italy is an oft-visited destination for Lizarraga. In 2020, she and her Italian expat husband, scientist Riccardo Sibilano, started Curiosare Italia, offering experiences in Puglia that include painting lessons with Lizarraga and highlight local art, architecture, and food. “We show you all that we love about this magical place,” she enthuses. The couple recently purchased a 17th-century property in Puglia that they are restoring. Given her talent for transformation, it will no doubt be exquisite.
