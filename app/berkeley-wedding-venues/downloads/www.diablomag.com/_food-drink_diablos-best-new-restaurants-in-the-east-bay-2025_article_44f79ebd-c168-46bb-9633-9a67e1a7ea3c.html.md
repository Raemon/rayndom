Enye Latin Table is helping to make Clayton a dining destination.

Corey Agopian

Every year is different. Looking back on the top new restaurants that debuted in the last 12 months, I was struck by their diversity. This wildly varied group includes a small sourdough pizza success story in Livermore, as well as a regional Italian juggernaut planting its flag in Walnut Creek. Newcomers cover the gamut from carefree Hawaiian, fun-loving Latin, and Instagrammable brunch to cerebral Indian and intimate Filipino. The one common denominator between them: All deliver destination-worthy dining to the East Bay.

![DM2512_081_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Enye Latin Table brought new dining vibes to Clayton this year.

Corey Agopian

### **Enye Latin Table, Clayton**

Enye gave Clayton a jolt of culinary electricity when it opened with a spicy splash in the heart of the city’s downtown. Clayton native Kent Ipsen, the proprietor behind the family-friendly Skipolini’s pizza chain, determined his hometown was ripe for a more upscale option to keep dinner traffic from fleeing to the likes of Walnut Creek. He got more than he bargained for, as diners from greater Contra Costa County and beyond have been lured by the appealing combination of urbane food and drinks with smaller-town hospitality and prices. Pupusas, grilled octopus, and esquites corn ribs are standouts among the small plates, while a showstopping churrasco flank steak with jalapeño chimichurri is alone worth the trip. Excellent cocktails anchor a festive bar scene—and Enye now offers weekend brunch. [_enyelatin​table.com_](https://www.enyelatintable.com/) _._

* * *

![DM2512_044_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Kaia’s serves up Hawaiian eats in Danville.

Ethan Fletcher

### **Kaia’s, Danville**

With dishes such as spam musubi, Kahlua pork nachos, and _loco moco,_ Hawaiian fare isn’t exactly subtle, but it sure is delicious. And much like the famed islands, it has universal appeal that can be appreciated by adults and kids alike. Veteran Danville restaurateur Randy Negi recognized this during family vacations to Hawaii and set about transforming his more restrained, wine-centric Vine and Spirits space into a festive, tropical oasis in the heart of downtown Danville. Kaia’s delivers comforting pan-Asian favorites that include poke bowls and miso-glazed salmon; fun desserts such as hula pie and Dole whip soft serve; an affordable keiki (kids’) menu; and a tiki bar with bracing tropical concoctions both classic and modern. All of this earns the island kitchen a hang 10 out of 10 on the dining scale. [_kaiasdanville.com_](http://kaiasdanville.com) _._

![DM2512_045_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Khaki’s menu was designed by an acclaimed culinary team.

Neetu Laddha

### **Khaki Bar and Indian Canteen, San Ramon**

It’s not easy to modernize Indian cuisine, as evidenced by Curry Up Now’s Mortar and Pestle bar concept that never quite caught on at City Center Bishop Ranch in San Ramon. Owner Akash Kapoor’s latest attempt is much more successful. Khaki, his new eatery led by a Michelin-recognized Indian kitchen team that launched this summer, executes a thoughtful and inventive menu that delicately wraps complex Indian flavors into an appealing, up-to-date package. Crispy cups of _pani puri_ wheat puffs, which you fill yourself with warm, spice-inflected white pea mash and chilled young mango water, can be gobbled whole in one delectable bite. In the _patta_ chaat, charred sweet potato is topped by exquisitely crispy fried kale and spinach drizzled with a piquant medley of chutneys and sweet-and-sour yogurt. Both masterfully balance elements of taste, texture, and temperature that will leave your taste buds humming (in a good way). A fun spirits selection and inviting bar serving Indian-themed cocktails completes this creative, contemporary transformation. [_wearekhaki.com_](http://wearekhaki.com) _._

![DM2512_039_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Original Joe’s has become an instant hit.

Emma Kruch/Emma K Creative

### **Original Joe’s, Walnut Creek**

Yes, there were delays in the initial opening—and then _re_ opening after a fire shut down operations days after a splashy debut in Walnut Creek. But there’s no denying Original Joe’s was worth the wait. The well-drilled kitchen confidently pumps out satisfying Italian American favorites—arancini, chopped salad, spicy rigatoni, veal piccata—while the bar’s martinis, Manhattans, and other enduring cocktails are on point. But more than food and drink, the restaurant conjures up an effortlessly elegant, convivial atmosphere to create a palpable big-city buzz (in Broadway Plaza!) that feels transported straight from its celebrated flagship in San Francisco’s North Beach. It’s not hard to see Original Joe’s becoming _the_ special occasion dining destination in central Contra Costa County. Which was the hope for one of the most anticipated local openings in recent memory. Mission accomplished. [_original​joes.com/walnut-creek_](https://www.originaljoes.com/walnut-creek) _._

![DM2512_049_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Sweet Maple adds Asian touches to brunch mainstays.

Courtesy of Sweet Maple

### **Sweet Maple, Walnut Creek**

With an upscale but family-friendly vibe and a menu that delivers innovative, Asian-tinged options while nailing the classics, the long-awaited Sweet Maple manages to adeptly hit the brunch sweet spot in downtown Walnut Creek. Perhaps it shouldn’t come as a surprise, given the fact that owner Hoyul Steven Choi practically invented the modern breakfast concept 15 years ago at his original San Francisco Sweet Maple, a formula he’s tweaked and refined at new locations in Palo Alto, Santa Monica, Cupertino, and now here. From the effervescent flower espresso tonic and the spicy-sweet Millionaire’s Bacon to the sunny side patio and the spa-like lofty main dining room, Sweet Maple makes you feel like you’re in well-seasoned hands. Did we mention breakfast cocktails? [_sweetmaplesf.com_](http://sweetmaplesf.com) _._

* * *

![DM2512_043_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Tropa’s Filipino fare reflects its chef’s history.

Cat Fennell

### **Tropa, Lafayette**

Dynamic chef JB Balingit returns to his old Hideout Kitchen haunt in Lafayette with a more composed, reflective affair. At Tropa, Balingit explores his culinary roots via refined preparations of comforting Filipino fare such as succulent adobo sticky ribs, tropical _kilawin_ bathed with coconut milk, and an elegant version of oxtail _kare-kare_ with velvety peanut-beef broth. Lovers of the cuisine will also be pleased with thoughtfully straightforward renditions of staples such as chicken lumpia and sizzling _sisig._ And not to worry: Balingit still keeps things festive with a nostalgic Filipino-themed makeover of an already terrific setting, plus Asian-tinged cocktails and a live DJ on Friday and Saturday nights. [_tropaeats.com_](http://tropaeats.com) _._

* * *

![DM2512_051_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Wingen Bakery and Restaurant now offers sourdough pizzas.

Courtesy of Wingen Bakery and Restaurant

### **Wingen Bakery and Restaurant, Livermore**

On the strength of its artisan breads, baked goods, and breakfast sandos, husband-wife team Bryan and Aimee Wingen have methodically expanded Wingen from a traditional bakery to a full-on restaurant with breakfast, lunch, and now dinner service. Earlier this year, they pulled off a beautiful, quick-fire dining room remodel (codesigned and executed themselves) to add a petite bar with craft beers, boutique wines, and lo-fi cocktails and evening fare centered around terrific sourdough pizzas. Rotating seasonal specials like a summer peach pizza with yellow peaches from K and J Farms keep things fresh; the menu is complemented by a curated selection of Cal-Italian crowd-pleasers, including antipasti, meatballs, ricotta _gnudi_ in shallot cream sauce, and tiramisu for dessert. Perks of the owners’ bakery background extend beyond pizza: They make a showstopping focaccia and dust their Caesar salad with sourdough breadcrumbs. [_wingenbakery.com_](http://wingenbakery.com) _._
