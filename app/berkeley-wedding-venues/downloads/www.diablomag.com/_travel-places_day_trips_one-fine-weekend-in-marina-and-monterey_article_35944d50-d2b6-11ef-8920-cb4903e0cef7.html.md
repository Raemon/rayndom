## Seaside Escape

In the town of **Marina,** the **Sanctuary Beach Resort** is a secluded retreat perched on sandy dunes that is perfect for those seeking respite from daily stress. At the Sanctuary, which recently underwent a years-long revitalization, it’s all about bringing outdoor beauty indoors and merging soothing seascape vibes with upscale luxury. Settle into your cabin-like room or suite, many of which feature glorious views of the waves, and take in your surroundings; rooms come equipped with binoculars to make it easy to spot the local fauna from your patio or balcony.

The Sanctuary provides plenty of ways to experience the beautiful setting, including complimentary bicycle loans, access to two pools and a hot tub, and nature walks and morning beach yoga on weekends. Paths also lead guests to private beach access points to take strolls across the sand.

To further its position as a restorative retreat, the Sanctuary is hosting a special Burnout Recovery Journey at its Renewal Studio and Spa through March. Guests can sample organic bone broth and healthy snacks upon arrival and are encouraged to journal and set goals for the day. The experience includes an hour-long relaxation massage and a session on a HigherDose Infared PEMF Mat, which uses infrared therapy and a pulsed electromagnetic field to support the body’s healing. The resort also provides a phone sleeve that blocks signals to devices and promotes disconnection from the outside world. Consider joining a class at the Sanctuary’s studio with partnering wellness group **Monterey Bay Moves.** Yin yoga, geared toward stress relief and mindfulness, can be a particularly therapeutic option.

## Wild Wonders

Marina and the Monterey Bay area are at the heart of the **Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary,** a 6,094-square-mile section of federally protected ocean running from Cambria to Marin. The region is home to countless fish species and more than 36 species of marine mammals, including the sea otters, sea lions, and seals that have become synonymous with the area and can often be spotted in Monterey’s parks and protected spaces.

A stay at the Sanctuary Beach Resort facilitates access to **Marina State Beach,** whose official entrance is just steps away. A favorite with hang gliders, the beach curves with the coastline, making it a lovely spot for picnicking, along with active adventures. Marina is home to **Fort Ord Dunes State Park,** a former military base used to train more than 1,500,000 soldiers during World War II and the Korean and Vietnam wars. Extensive environmental restoration efforts took place after the site was decommissioned in the 1990s. The park now comprises four miles of the **Monterey Bay Sanctuary Scenic Trail,** a popular spot for hikers and joggers, though water recreation at Fort Ord is discouraged due to powerful rip currents and heavy surf.

More natural sights await in nearby **Moss Landing** at **Elkhorn Slough,** one of California’s biggest salt marshes and an immersive wonderland for kayakers. You’re likely to catch glimpses of birds, fish, and other marine creatures.

January and February are also prime times to encounter the orange-and-black monarch butterflies that have made **Pacific Grove** famous among wildlife enthusiasts. At the **Monarch Grove Sanctuary,** visitors can witness these delicate creatures enjoying the bay’s temperate climates at the end of their overwintering season.

## Inspiring Explorations

The Sanctuary is a great home base for exploring lesser-known attractions in Pacific Grove, **Carmel,** and **Monterey,** all less than 20 minutes from the hotel. Carmel has a rich history as an artist colony, and the area still has a thriving arts and culture scene. If you are visiting during the warmer months, pack a picnic and head to **Outdoor Forest Theater** to watch classic plays or musicals—the surrounding forest is often used as an additional immersive set. The **Monterey Museum of Art** highlights Californian artists and features a permanent collection of more than 10,000 pieces.

Bibliophiles in search of retail therapy will love the **Bookworks** in Pacific Grove, which stocks more than 6,000 tomes and features an adjoining coffee shop that invites guests to sip a cappuccino while savoring a favorite read. Carmel also has an eclectic mix of shops in its charming downtown, including **Cottage of Sweets, Carmel Music Box Company,** and **Thinker Toys.**

## Tastes of the Towns

Thanks to freshly caught seafood and the bounty of the nearby Salinas Valley, known as the “salad bowl of the world,” regional cuisine prioritizes local ingredients and a farm-to-table ethos. In Monterey, the **Sardine Factory** serves its wild abalone medallions with Monterey artichokes, while **Louie Linguini’s** offers two different iterations of Monterey rock cod, both with seasonal vegetables. In Carmel, head to **Anton and Michel** for a seafood fricassee with smoked salmon ravioli, shrimp, and scallops with an asparagus mushroom cream sauce. Marina’s **Urban Wine Row** highlights pours from producers on the Monterey peninsula and down into the county’s Chalone region.

The Sanctuary’s restaurant, **Salt Wood Kitchen and Oysterette,** also spotlights locally sourced ingredients. Start your meal with a bowl of chef Nestor Marin’s Monterey Bay clam chowder and an artichoke and crab spread on sourdough before diving into the winter squash risotto, served in a whole pumpkin from Santa Cruz and decorated with maitake mushrooms. Don’t miss the beef-fat potatoes or the Calabrian lobster pasta.

Plan your morning meal in advance by hanging your breakfast order on your door before midnight; Salt Wood’s kitchen delivers vegetable omelets, French toast, pots of coffee, and even mimosas straight to your room in picnic baskets at the hour of your choosing.

Stop by the bonfire pits at the beach’s edge to watch the sunset crest over the dunes and the light-dappled crashing waves. After this rejuvenating retreat, you’ll be ready for whatever lies ahead in the new year.
