## EDITORS' PICKS

#### BEST RESILIENT BAKERY

**WINGEN BAKERY AND RESTAURANT, LIVERMORE**

Aimee and Bryan Wingen’s Livermore bakery gives new meaning to the term labor of love. The husband-wife team started a home cottage baking operation in 2020 in order to care for their newborn daughter, Waylynn, who was born with a terminal genetic disorder. After Waylynn passed away, the couple opened Wingen Bakery in 2021 in her memory. The Wingens soon outgrew their space and expanded next door while adding breakfast, brunch, and lunch offerings to their selection of baked goods. And building off a series of pop-up suppers, Wingen Bakery and Restaurant debuted dinner service in May with an Italian-inspired menu anchored by its popular sourdough pizzas. _[wingenbakery.com](http://wingenbakery.com)._

* * *

#### BEST CREATIVE COOKBOOK AUTHOR

**YING CHANG COMPESTINE, LAFAYETTE**

Author Ying Chang Compestine has published 27 works in various genres over her prolific career, from picture books to young adult novels to memoirs to cookbooks. Her most recent effort is an endearing mix of all four. This March, the Lafayette resident released _Buddha Jumps Over the Wall,_ a graphic novel providing backstories and recipes for 26 traditional Chinese dishes. In the book, beautifully illustrated by Vivian Truong and filled with fun tips and anecdotes, Compestine often draws on deeply personal memories from her childhood growing up in Wuhan: the triumph of mastering chopsticks while eating rice noodles, for example, or how her grandmother made yin-yang rice balls to warm her during weekly “reeducation” manual labor tasks mandated during the Chinese Cultural Revolution. _Buddha_ debuted as a No. 1 New Release on Amazon and has garnered national buzz from the likes of NPR’s _The World_ podcast. Compestine is already hard at work on several new picture books and her next cookbook. _[yingc.com](http://yingc.com)._

#### BEST TWEEN BAKER

**ARIA KARAYIL, DANVILLE**

Little did 7-year-old Aria Karayil know where her mother’s pandemic baking lessons would lead. The now-12-year-old Danville prodigy took to baking like a charm and was soon whipping up sweet treats on her own, often incorporating ingredients from her family’s Indian heritage. That led to an opportunity to compete in this year’s _Kids Baking Championship_ on the Food Network. While she didn’t win the $25,000 prize (she ended up taking fifth place), Karayil had an absolute blast while documenting her journey on Instagram. She’s now parlayed her experience and exposure into taking weekly online orders for cakes, even catering a couple of weddings this spring, with proceeds going to charity.

* * *

#### BEST BOOMING BRUNCH SCENE

**PLEASANT HILL**

Nothing says brunch like Pleasant Hill. Seriously. The oft-overlooked burb north of Walnut Creek has suddenly become Benedict central in Contra Costa County. Joining the likes of Jack’s, Crescent Bistro, T’zunun, Wence’s, and WiseGirl, recent additions to the city’s weekend brunch scene include a second location of Danville favorite Crumbs, upscale breakfast specialist the Royale, and Jack’s Mexican spinoff Cantina Jack’s. Let the mimosas flow.

* * *

#### BEST DIY FOOD FACILITATORS

**PRESERVED, OAKLAND**

While many discovered the joys of pickling, fermenting, and sourdough bread-making during the pandemic, the folks at Preserved have been advocating, teaching, and preserving traditional DIY food-making methods for a decade. They’ve been located at a tiny storefront on Oakland’s Telegraph Avenue since 2016, but the shop’s mission “to empower and inspire a greater connection to food” just got easier with Preserved’s move to Rockridge. The expansive new setting provides a larger footprint in which to sell culinary tools and kitchen wares, along with a second dedicated space to host more workshops, author appearances, and foodie events. [_preservedgoods.com_](http://preservedgoods.com) _._

* * *

#### BEST ICE CREAM ADDITION

**MEADOWLARK DAIRY, LIVERMORE**

A towering swirl of soft serve from Meadowlark Dairy in Pleasanton has become a summer tradition for so many in the Tri-Valley. Too many, perhaps? The omnipresent drive-through and pedestrian queues can be daunting, especially in the summer, which is why owner Jesse Takens decided to expand for the first time in the dairy’s 100-plus-year history. He’s aiming to open a second location on Livermore’s Second Street by July, which should take pressure off Pleasanton—in theory, anyway. Given Meadowlark’s enduring popularity, we’ll believe it when we see it. _[meadowlarkdairy.com](http://meadowlarkdairy.com)._

#### BEST BAR CHEF

**GABY MAEDA, FRIENDS AND FAMILY, OAKLAND**

Hot and sour mushrooms with sesame and chili oil; sardine banh mi toast with maggi aioli; brothy Rancho Gordo beans with charred cabbage, farro verde, and garlic chive pistou. This isn’t the kind of pub grub you would expect to find at your local watering hole, but it was the result when queer-owned Oakland cocktail bar Friends and Family took a chance last year in hiring Gaby Maeda from San Francisco’s Michelin-starred State Bird Provisions. The move paid immediate dividends when Maeda’s creative, contemporary spins on Asian fare earned the Hawaiian-born culinary talent a James Beard Award nomination for best chef in California this year. _[friendsandfamilybar.com](http://friendsandfamilybar.com)._

* * *

#### BEST BREWERY EXPANSION

**CALICRAFT BREWING CO., WALNUT CREEK**

With the acquisition of Heretic’s brewery, distillery, and taproom operations in Fairfield and an enormous new beer garden set to launch in Davis, it’s safe to say that Calicraft Brewing Co. is in growth mode. Still, owner Blaine Landberg hasn’t forgotten where his bread is buttered. He and partner Thomas Vo just completed phase one of an ambitious expansion project at their flagship Walnut Creek headquarters that by next year will include a sparkling new two-story brewery, winery, distillery, and restaurant. Meanwhile, they hope to launch a rooftop cocktail bar this summer atop downtown dining pioneer Va de Vi Bistro and Wine Bar. Says Landberg, “We want to plant our flag here. We want to become a shining beacon for Walnut Creek.” _[calicraft.com](http://calicraft.com)._

* * *

#### BEST ALOHA SPIRIT

**KAIA’S ISLAND KITCHEN AND TIKI BAR, DANVILLE**

Veteran restaurateur Randy Negi has long been enchanted with Hawaii, so he created a little slice of paradise right in downtown Danville. Packed with fun touches like thatched grass ceilings and floral booths, palm-tree-lined sunset beach murals, and wall-mounted surfboards, Kaia’s Island Kitchen and Tiki Bar delivers easy island charm while dishing poke bowls, _loco moco,_ pulled pork sandwiches, and other above-average versions of approachable dishes inspired by Hawaii. And after a Mai Tai or three from the excellent tiki bar, you may just forget that you’re not actually in the islands. _[kaiasdanville.com](http://kaiasdanville.com)._

### Best New Restaurant

**ENYE LATIN TABLE, CLAYTON**

When Kent Ipsen of the family-friendly chain of Skipolini’s pizzerias decided to open an upscale concept in Clayton, it was with the idea of capturing some of his hometown’s finer-dining crowd that was driving to Walnut Creek or elsewhere for a weekend night out. The response to Enye Latin Table has been so positive that out-of-towners are now making a reverse commute to bucolic Clayton to get a taste of the urbane new restaurant’s beautifully executed pan-Latin dishes and fun, tropical cocktails offered at (relatively) reasonable prices. _[enyelatintable.com](http://enyelatintable.com)._

### Best Local Winery

**SHADOWBROOK WINERY, WALNUT CREEK**

We all love Wine Country. But you can get the full experience—and then some—right here in Walnut Creek. Shadowbrook Winery’s 20 acres of vineyards, two-story tasting room, and state-of-the-art winery nestled at the foot of Mount Diablo evoke Napa-Sonoma elegance, while its wine has earned close to 100 medals during its 20-year history. You can now add fare from a pedigreed chef to the mix. When the winery was required to serve food during the pandemic, proprietor and master winemaker Tim Jochner decided to go all in: Shadowbrook’s executive chef, Nicholas Vacakis, draws from experience at Michelin-rated restaurants Lazy Bear and Chez Panisse to craft a menu that changes monthly along with special multicourse and winemaker dinners. _[shadowbrookwinery.com](http://shadowbrookwinery.com)._
