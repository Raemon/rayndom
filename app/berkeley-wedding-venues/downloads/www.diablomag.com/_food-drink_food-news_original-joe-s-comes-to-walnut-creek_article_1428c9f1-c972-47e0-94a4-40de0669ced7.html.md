Original Joe’s brings classic Italian American fare to downtown Walnut Creek.

Emma K Creative

It’s hard to recall a more anticipated restaurant debut in Walnut Creek than Original Joe’s—city officials did, after all, declare the eatery’s opening day on August 14 “Original Joe’s Day.” But why has a dining institution so closely tied to San Francisco caused such outsize buzz in the East Bay? According to John and Elena Duggan, whose family has run the flagship Original Joe’s for 88 years, it could be because many local residents still have strong connections to the famed city across the bay. “We’ve been doing business for almost 90 years,” says John. “There are so many folks that live in this area who used to live in the city, or live here and still work in the city.”

Adds Elena: “The beauty of Joe’s is it is a multigenerational brand in the city that’s so familiar to so many people.”

Indeed, the Duggans’ knack for balancing modern sensibility with a classic Italian American format that harkens back to a glamorous, old-school era of San Francisco dining seems to have struck a sentimental nerve. But Original Joe’s brings more to the table than nostalgia. New menu items special to Walnut Creek include a Dungeness crab roll, bone-in veal parmigiana, and “dirty” saltimbocca with a truffle porcini sauce, joining such longtime favorites as spicy rigatoni, a signature hamburger sandwich on sourdough, and steaks and chops. Plush leather booths, marble floors, and custom brass chandeliers lend a polished, urbane feel to the spacious interior, while three separate outdoor patios—with shade, heating, and cooling options—are perfect for the warmer climate in the East Bay.

There have been hurdles. The opening was delayed, and a kitchen fire temporarily shut the restaurant after its debut. But the Duggans, who promised a quick reopening, remain committed and confident they can live up to the hype.

“I think the East Bay has been ready for something like [Original Joe’s], on this scale,” says John. “I think [the area] deserves it and it’s long overdue, and we were the right people to step in and give it a go. … Not everyone knows who we are, but we’re going to build that trust one smile and one hamburger at a time.” _[originaljoes.com/walnut-creek](http://originaljoes.com/walnut-creek)._
