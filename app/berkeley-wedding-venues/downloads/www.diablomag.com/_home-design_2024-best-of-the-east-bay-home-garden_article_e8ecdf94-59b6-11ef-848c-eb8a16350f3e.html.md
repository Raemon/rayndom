#### BEST NEWCOMER

**MASAYACO, BERKELEY**

![DM2407_131_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

MasayaCo

MasayaCo

Last summer, when MasayaCo opened a sequel to its Nashville, Tennessee, store, the furnishings retailer already had a strong customer base in the Bay Area drawn to its artisanal, sustainable designs. The 1,785-square-foot showroom in Berkeley holds a range of pieces—handcrafted from teak grown in reforestation projects—including seating, tables, beds, and credenzas. In addition to patterns and outdoor collections that are exclusive to Fourth Street, customers can explore accessory dwelling unit models from sister company Masaya Homes. _[masayacompany.com](http://masayacompany.com)._

* * *

#### BEST TRIED-AND-TRUE SPOT

**SLOAT GARDEN CENTER, DANVILLE**

Founded in San Francisco in 1958, Sloat has been an East Bay staple since debuting in Danville in 1999 with two locations on Diablo Road specializing in planting materials, gardening products, and pottery. The company now has four additional outposts serving Contra Costa. With customer interest and individual microclimates in mind, the array of plants and products varies by location. _[sloatgardens.com](http://sloatgardens.com)._

#### BEST HIDDEN GEM

**MORNINGTIDE, ALBANY**

With their lifestyle shop occupying just 240 square feet—mere steps from Solano Avenue in Albany—Lisa Fontaine and Lisa Wong Jackson’s careful curation emphasizes smaller, mostly local brands that ethically and sustainably produce their goods. The inventory at Morningtide encompasses woodwork, candles, art, glassware, and ceramics (including Fontaine’s line, Soil and Slip). Weekend pop-ups showcase food and craft vendors. Slated for July: Peony Creamery, Mochi Koubou, Mt. Kakigori, and Milk Cloud Bakery. _[morningtideshop.com](http://morningtideshop.com)._
