Fernando Castellanos

Chris Cruz @chriscruzphoto

The San Francisco Decorator Showcase is one of the Bay Area’s most anticipated design events, a monthlong annual showcase in which local interior and landscape designers have the opportunity to transform a Pacific Heights mansion. This year’s event, which takes place in a palatial 9,400-square-foot home and runs through May 26, features work from roughly 20 designers, each given a discrete space to reimagine.

Among this year’s talents is Fernando Castellanos, an East Bay interior designer whose distinctive modern sensibility can be seen in his vision for the mansion’s laundry room. “I go to the showcase every year, and I am thrilled to be included,” he says. “My concept was to do an all-black room, and every sheen, shade, and material I have chosen is black. It won’t be a black hole, exactly, but it will be striking.”

![Untitled-1](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Interior design projects from Fernando Castellanos highlight his modern sensibility.

Michael Shepherd

Castellanos, who grew up in the East Bay and studied at the Fashion Institute of Design and Merchandising in San Francisco and Los Angeles, brings a set of impressive credentials to the project. He has designed or renovated numerous single-family homes, primarily in the Bay Area but also in Miami and Los Angeles. His biggest project to date—and one that echoes the dark palette of the Decorator Showcase’s laundry room, but on a far grander scale—is a 22,000-square-foot commercial space for Berner, the rapper-turned-cannabis mogul of the Cookies brand.

Castellanos notes that every project he does is unique—“I never do anything cookie-cutter,” he says—and for that reason, living and working in the Bay Area is the perfect fit for him. “I love the culture here because it is so freeing and open. There’s also a real appreciation of art and design here, which always makes things flow so much better.”
