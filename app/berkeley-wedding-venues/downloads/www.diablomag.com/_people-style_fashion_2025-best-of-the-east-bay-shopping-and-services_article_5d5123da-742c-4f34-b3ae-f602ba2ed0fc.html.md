#### BEST MARRIAGE OF BEVERAGES AND BOOKS

**BOOK SOCIETY, BERKELEY**

Book Society, a combination of bookstore and wine lounge, was born out of a desire to build a community hub. Founder Laura Guzman opened the location in December 2024, pairing wines and books to invite conversation and discovery. The business hosts book clubs such as WTF Did I Just Read? and the Secret Romantasy Fan, author events, and a series called Sunday in the Stacks where participants can do yoga, meditation, or other restorative activities in the store before it opens. Customers are invited to enjoy a book (even without purchase) while sipping a glass of wine (for purchase from a self-pour dispenser), although laptops are not allowed. Book Society also offers subscriptions that include a monthly book and wine selection. “I am so passionate about providing a community space,” says Guzman. “As much as this is a lovely, fun, exciting space, it is also a really important one, particularly at this moment in time.” _[booksociety.social](http://booksociety.social)._

* * *

#### BEST STEPH CURRY–APPROVED VIRAL HAT

**LACES, SAN FRANCISCO**

Although the Golden State Warriors went out in the second round of the NBA playoffs this year, Stephen Curry was the MVP for one local apparel company. Laces, founded by Moraga native Jeff Chu, got a massive sales bump when the injured Curry wore its Bay Area upside-down ball cap during Game 3 against the Minnesota Timberwolves. The hat quickly went viral, selling out and giving Laces a 10,000 percent sales increase overnight. The company cocreates designs with college athletes from around the country, including Sophia Fields and Camille Lara of Saint Mary’s College. _[getlaces.co](http://getlaces.co)._

#### BEST SPOT FOR VINYL LOVERS

**UP THE CREEK RECORDS, WALNUT CREEK**

### Best Driver Training

**GREENLIGHT SIMULATION, DANVILLE**

Preparing a teenager for that all-important driver’s license can be terrifying for most parents, so why not let an expert take the wheel (sort of)? Greenlight Simulation was founded by a winning race car driver and Danville dad with the mission of creating skilled, safe, and confident drivers using simulator-based driving lessons. The sessions use advanced simulators with integrated eye-tracking technology that monitor what the driver is seeing in real time, allowing Greenlight coaches to give accurate feedback. Future drivers can start the simulator training program as early as 15 years of age (no learner’s permit required); Greenlight also offers an online driver’s education course and recently introduced real-life behind-the-wheel training. _[greenlightsimulation.com](http://greenlightsimulation.com)._
