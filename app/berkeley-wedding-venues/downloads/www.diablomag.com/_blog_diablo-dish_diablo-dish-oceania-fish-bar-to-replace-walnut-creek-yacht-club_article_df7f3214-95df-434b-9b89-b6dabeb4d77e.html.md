### Oceania Fish Bar Promises Contemporary Seafood Replacement for Beloved Yacht Club

The closure of **Walnut Creek Yacht Club** earlier this year dealt a huge blow to the local dining scene. But it was one that was softened by the news that the downtown landmark would be taken over by the **Ghaben siblings,** Mike, David, and Rolla, a longtime local restaurant family with a 40-year track record of success in the area ranging from **Original Mel’s** and **Hubcaps** diners to recent modern concepts **Batch and Brine, Broderick Roadhouse,** and **Lita.** Now, details have finally starting to trickle out on their ambitious plans for the Yacht Club, a seafood restaurant to be called **Oceania Fish Bar.** The Ghabens submitted plans to the city of Walnut Creek to completely transform the spot into a striking, contemporary restaurant with 1,360-square-feet of added ground floor space, expanded outdoor seating, and a 1,179-square-foot second-floor corporate office.

![Oceania-\(2\);-Courtesy-Ghaben-family.gif](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Oceania Fish Bar

Courtesy of the Ghaben family

“The latest design will reimagine this mid-century modern building for the future, introducing a modern flair with expanded outdoor seating and an exciting indoor–outdoor dining experience,” said the family in a statement to _Dish._ “The interior design will be fresh, bright, and open—an energetic new concept that will contribute to the continued evolution of downtown Walnut Creek while complementing the family’s existing restaurant portfolio.”

While the Ghabens are keeping a lid on their exact food and drink plans for now, Oceania provides an obvious link to their predecessor’s oceanic legacy by placing the kitchen’s culinary focal point on a “seafood-forward fish bar utilizing a variety of cooking techniques.” Indeed, the main idea, the family said, was to develop “a space designed to honor long-standing traditions while creating new memories for the community.”

![Oceania-\(3\);-Courtesy-Ghaben-family.gif](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Oceania Fish Bar

Courtesy of the Ghaben family

Pending city approval, construction will begin next year (with an opening not likely until 2027) on a project that is particularly significant to the Ghabens given its history and “cherished location” at the corner of Bonanza and Locust streets.

“This site has been important to the Ghaben family for nearly 40 years, having been home to Hubcaps, Broderick, Lita, and now the site of this upcoming new concept,” they said, adding. “Stay tuned for future updates as this exciting project comes to life.”

### Calicraft Upstairs Opening Delayed Until This Saturday

Not so fast! After we reported that Calicraft Brewing Co.’s new rooftop bar concept, [**Calicraft Upstairs**](https://www.calicraft.com)**,** was set to debut last Friday, the Walnut Creek brewery and distillery announced that the opening was delayed “due to circumstances out of our control.” Fortunately, fans won’t have to wait too long, as a new soft opening date has been set for this Saturday, December 20. As a reminder, the neighborhood cocktail bar perched atop Va de Vi Bistro and Wine Bar will offer 20 taps of Calicraft beer, but also a signature cocktail program highlighting house-made vodkas and gins made on an on-site micro-still, and a food program with “fun small plates” centered on an array of savory sourdough waffles. In the interim, Calicraft held a ribbon-cutting ceremony and official grand opening celebration for its revamped beer garden at its Shadelands HQ featuring 50-plus picnic tables, more than 20 taps, bar seating, fire pits with Adirondack chairs, food trucks, and fresh beer brewed on-site.

### Sneak Peek at Western Flyer

[**Western Flyer**](https://wfbrewing.com)’s Logan Jager sent _Dish_ a sneak peek for his upcoming Lafayette brewery set to debut in January, and it looks exciting. Jager promised a sleek, modern take on the standard brewpub vibe, loosely inspired by Costa Mesa’s Salty Bear Brewing Co. taproom, and photos reveal exactly that with elements of light wood, white walls, glass, concrete, and steel within a lofty, light-filled space. The Lafayette banker-turned-brewmaster is now targeting an opening in the first week of February, when he will initially offer 12 site-brewed beers on tap, including two West Cost IPAs and three Hazy IPAs. For food, the kitchen will offer “seasonal, fresh coastal tacos, hearty salads, as well as sharable appetizers. All of our tortillas will be handmade in house daily, with fresh chips cooked around the clock.”

### The Brass Door Closing on Christmas Eve in San Ramon

Beloved San Ramon steak house the [**Brass Door**](https://brassdoor.com) [announced](https://www.facebook.com/TheBrassDoor) it will be closing just before Christmas after nearly 80 years on San Ramon Valley Boulevard. Owners of the old-school institution, known for its prime rib and festive bar scene that brought back generations of loyal repeat customers, announced that its final day of service would be Christmas Eve after negotiations stalled with their landlord.

“Despite our best efforts, we were unable to secure or renew a lease for our location, and we must bring this chapter to a close at the end of that day.”

While the landlord is actively looking for new tenants, the Brass Door owners maintained they were exploring options to reopen in a new location: “While this chapter comes to an end, the spirit and legacy of the Brass Door will remain strong. We hope that one day we may reopen in a new home and welcome you back to a new chapter of this beloved establishment.”

That would be welcome news for Tri-Valley diners, hundreds of whom relayed condolences to and memories of the historic special-occasion destination on social media in response to the announcement.

### Alice Waters Debuts Bar Panisse in Berkeley

After years of radio silence and with little fanfare, **Alice Waters** debuted [**Bar Panisse**](https://www.instagram.com/barpanisse_/) last week to lines of curious customers. Located next door to Chez Panisse in north Berkeley on Shattuck Avenue in (controversially) the space long filled by bar César, it marks Waters’s first new Bay Area project since Café Fanny opened in 1984. According to [several reports](https://sf.eater.com/restaurant-news/210222/bar-panisse-opens-alice-waters-drinks-menu), the revamped space emphasizes traditional dark wood—similar in feel to Chez Panisse—while serving wine, beer, cocktails, and small plates on Thursday through Monday from 4 to 10 p.m. (for now). **Dylan O’Brien** from Emeryville’s Prizefighter Bar consulted on the drink program, which also trends traditional with updated spins on the martini, gimlet, spritz, and other classic cocktails. The chef is **Amelia Telc,** who brings experience from Tartine and Mission Chinese Food in New York City, among other restaurants. Her menu delivers mostly small plates in the local, seasonal Cali-French Chez Panisse tradition, including gougères, anchovies with celery vinaigrette sage, Acme pain _de mie_ with trout roe, and fresh and broiled oysters, with a couple of bigger dishes that currently include a grilled hanger steak with artichoke salad and gulf shrimp with nettles.

### Rodney Worth Expanding to Rossmoor

The Peasant and the Pear restaurateur [**Rodney Worth**](https://www.rodneyworth.com) will be taking over the Creekside Grill located inside the Rossmoor retirement community in Walnut Creek. According to a [story](https://www.rossmoornews.com/2025/12/09/new-operator-shares-his-vision-for-serving-rossmoors-dining-needs/?eedition) published in the _Rossmoor News,_ the Creekside will close December 23 and reopen in February as the **Pear at Rossmoor.** There, Worth is planning a substantial steak-house-like makeover and a farm-to-table breakfast, lunch, and dinner menu of hearty American classics familiar to patrons at his other restaurants, which include his Peasant flagship in Danville, popular Alamo breakfast-lunch spot Peasant’s Courtyard, the Little Pear in Walnut Creek, and his latest concept, the Bourbon Pear, in Livermore.

### Small Bites from Around the East Bay

**Daytrip Counter, Puerto Rican Street Cuisine,** and **Sirene,** all in Oakland, made the _San Francisco Chronicle_ ’s [list](https://www.sfchronicle.com/food/restaurants/article/bay-area-best-new-restaurants-2025-21192945.php) of 15 best new restaurants of 2025.

The guaca-mol appetizer at **Popoca** in Oakland and the coconut-rice pancakes at Berkeley’s **Tanzie’s** made the _New York Times’_ [best dishes of the year](https://www.sfgate.com/food/article/bay-area-city-left-off-nyt-best-dishes-america-21233333.php).

[**Kibi’s Cafe**](https://www.kibiscafe.com)—maker of excellent banh mi sandwiches, as well as pho noodle soup, vermicelli bowls, and Vietnamese coffee and iced tea—[debuted](https://bayareatelegraph.com/2025/12/14/popular-orinda-vietnamese-cafe-opens-new-branch-in-danville/) a new restaurant in Danville, its third East Bay location after opening in Walnut Creek and Orinda.

**Local Vines,** the new tasting room serving Lamorinda wines, gets the [_Chronicle_ treatment](https://www.sfchronicle.com/food/wine/article/lamorinda-local-vines-wine-21197587.php).
