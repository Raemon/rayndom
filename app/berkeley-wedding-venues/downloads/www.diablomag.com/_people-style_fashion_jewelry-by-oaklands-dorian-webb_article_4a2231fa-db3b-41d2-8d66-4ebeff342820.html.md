Jewelry designer Dorian Webb makes stunning pieces that foster conversation.

Erica Cervantez

Dorian Webb was an architecture student at Yale University when an encounter during her junior year abroad in Italy changed her life. On a visit to Venice, she met skilled artisans who worked tirelessly to produce exquisite glass beads, more for the beauty of the craft than for potential profit.

![DM2503_026_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Erica Cervantez

“Here were people who spent so much time making beautiful things for people they didn’t know,” Webb recalls. “I had never seen craft elevated to that level, and immediately I knew this was what I wanted to do.”

After graduating, she launched her career as a jewelry designer, creating pieces out of beads she had picked up in Italy. Success came quickly, with Neiman Marcus carrying her entire collection. “I didn’t know a thing about business, and I had no mentors,” she says, “but I knew that jewelry could be a powerful way to tell stories. And when you wear a piece of jewelry that people comment on, it gives you an opportunity to talk to people you may not otherwise connect with.”

![DM2503_023_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Yulia Soloveva

Today, the Oakland-based designer creates a wide range of pieces that do just that. She designed her Signature Coral Ring, for example, to be a conversation piece. Made from semiprecious stones and Thai gold brass or sterling silver, the ring’s dramatic coral shape is intended to “fearlessly take up space,” Webb says. “It is impossible to overlook, and I wanted to start a conversation that is uplifting about the power we all have.”

Webb works with a range of materials and in varying price points, from her 18-karat gold and diamond Moment Collection to more affordable sterling silver cuffs and hoops.

Later this year, she will launch a line called the Harriet Collection, inspired by abolitionist Harriet Tubman, as well as an Oakland-themed collection that she plans to debut at a retail location in the Oakland airport.

![DM2503_029_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Erica Cervantez

Webb has also long been committed to working as an advocate and mentor to help businesswomen grow and thrive. “I want to help women navigate the challenges of entrepreneurship,” she says. “I believe everyone has gifts and talents that they can use to make the world a better place, and you have to honor them, whatever they are.” _[dorianwebb.com](http://dorianwebb.com)._
