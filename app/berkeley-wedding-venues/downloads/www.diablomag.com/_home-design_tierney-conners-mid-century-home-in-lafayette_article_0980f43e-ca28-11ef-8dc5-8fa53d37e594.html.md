A young family of four loved their sprawling, ranch-style house on a quiet Lafayette street, but they knew it could better meet their needs. As busy professionals with two active school-age children, the owners wanted to improve the house’s functionality as well as its aesthetics. At the top of their list was a renovation of their kitchen, which in many ways is the nerve center of their home. They wanted to modernize it, expand its views of the backyard, and make it feel more connected to the rest of the house.

![DM2501_006_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Tierney Conner added an inviting oak-lined window seat to the kitchen.

The owners contacted Tierney Conner, an Oakland-based firm that provides architectural, interior design, and landscaping services, to work up a plan. In evaluating the project, the architects saw that there were many qualities of this lovely mid-century home that they wanted to retain, but they needed to improve its cohesiveness. “The kitchen was dated and did not really fit with the rest of the house,” says Erin Conner, one of the firm’s principals, along with partners Anne Tierney and J. Conner. “We needed to stitch it together and develop an aesthetic that felt complementary. Overall, it lacked a point of view. And in an attempt to consider the totality of the site and find the soul of the house, the scope of our work gradually grew.”

![DM2501_009_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

An Eames chair, oak paneling, and wooden slats painted in Benjamin Moore’s Ebony King give the living room a welcoming mid-century feel.

Working with Kuhlmann and Cheyne Builders, Tierney Conner began with the kitchen, where they raised the ceiling up to the house’s roofline, added large windows and a cozy window seat, and opened it to the adjacent dining room. One of the most visually dramatic changes was the choice of black custom Fenix HPL cabinets from Bartlett Cabinets with honey-colored oak accents, a color palette that harmonizes with the rest of the house and strengthens its mid-century feel.

* * *

#### “We felt strongly that [the house] should be [both] beautiful and highly functional ... The key, for us, was to respect its integrity while giving it the capacity to evolve over time.”

##### —Erin Conner

* * *

![DM2501_010_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

As the project unfolded, Tierney Conner consulted with the owners about additional upgrades. The architects suggested they could gain much-needed living space by converting the house’s original carport into a gym and mudroom. They refreshed the dining room with graphic Marmaris Peacock wallpaper from Rebel Walls and a backlit onyx bar. And they added black architectural accents to the foyer, living room, and study, creating sophisticated rooms that aesthetically connect to the new kitchen.

Perhaps best of all, the house is designed for real living, indoors and out. The couple’s children play soccer on a sport court in the front yard and swim in a pool in the backyard; the whole family exercises in the home gym. The kitchen is a high-traffic zone that is both appealing and durable. And the house is well positioned to adapt to the family’s changing needs. As Conner points out, “The house needed a modern take on its mid-century design, but we felt strongly that it should be [both] beautiful and highly functional ... The key, for us, was to respect its integrity while giving it the capacity to evolve over time.”
