Whether you crave time on the sand or a surfing adventure, **Santa Cruz** has everything you need to kick-start the summer season. And luckily, the iconic, quirky beach town is only a 90-minute drive from the East Bay (at least, without traffic).

### Riding High

Dating back to the early 1900s, **Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk** is California’s oldest seaside amusement park, but 2024 brings another major milestone as the Giant Dipper wooden roller coaster clocks the century mark. The Boardwalk’s best-known ride, the red-and-white attraction delivers primo views of Monterey Bay as it catapults thrill seekers through stomach-churning dips and turns.

If you can keep your eyes open as you race through the air, you’ll likely spot another well-known component of the city’s public persona: surfers. Santa Cruz is said to be the birthplace of mainland surfing, and beginners will find gentle waves and, just as important, a patient teacher at nearby **Cowell Beach.** Ed Guzman, owner of **Club Ed** surf school, says he’s surfed almost every day of his adult life, and surfing is clearly in his blood. In 1915, his grandmother Dorothy Becker reportedly became the first woman to perform a headstand on a surfboard.

But you don’t have to wriggle into a wet suit to appreciate surfing. There are many spots where you can watch the pros make the sport look easy. From the Boardwalk, take a relaxing stroll along West Cliff Drive to **Steamer Lane,** one of the area’s most coveted surf breaks. Watch for a spell, then learn how the sport came to dominate the area’s rolling swells at the **Santa Cruz Surfing Museum,** housed in the small lighthouse just steps away.

### Sights of the Sea

Visitors flock to **Natural Bridges State Beach** to see its dramatic rock arch shaped by the pounding surf. Of the three original naturally occurring bridges, or arches, the park is named for, only the middle one remains. The park also delivers a stunning view of the Pacific from the short-term parking lot next to the entrance. Walk along the beach during low tide to witness marine life such as sea stars and sea anemones.

Located off Highway 1, the equally breathtaking **Wilder Ranch State Park** stretches approximately 7,000 acres, with 35 miles of hiking, biking, and equestrian trails. The hike along the 2.5-mile Old Cove Landing Trail is mostly flat and rewards walkers with ocean vistas and the opportunity to spot wildlife, ranging from harbor seals and dolphins to whales and an assortment of seabirds. It’s a good idea to wear layers on this hike, as it can be breezy.

### Beyond the Beach

While it’s the sandy side of Santa Cruz most come to see, the city is home to a variety of neighborhoods worthy of your vacation time. Downtown is very walkable, and shoppers can happily lose an afternoon popping in and out of stores, such as surf-centric clothing outlet **Berdels** and family-owned **Bookshop Santa Cruz;** for household goods and decor **Stripe** and **Botanic and Luxe** are both quality stops. **Abbott Square Market** is a culinary hub with several food and drink options that run the gamut from West African–inspired vegan dishes to pizza to Venezuelan cuisine. Nearby **Flower Bar** beautifully does double duty as a café and full-service florist.

For a quick trip back in time, leave the main shopping drag and stroll along Walnut Avenue, considered the first residential street in downtown Santa Cruz. Here, a leafy tree canopy flanks nearly two dozen colorful Victorian homes, some of which date back to the late 1800s. Keep your eyes peeled for the blue oval plaques loaded with historical tidbits on some of the homes’ facades.

![DM2406_036_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Grab a bite at Chaminade‘s Sandbox food truck.

Courtesy of Chaminade Resort and Spa

Reward yourself for those extra steps at the **Penny Ice Creamery;** try a scoop or two of ice cream in rotating flavors, including Irish coffee, blood orange sorbet, and Tahitian vanilla bean.

A bit more off the beaten path, an eclectic mix of businesses surrounds the retro **Rio Theatre** marquee in Midtown (or, depending on who you ask, the Eastside neighborhood). **Midtown Surf Shop** sells surfing essentials and brews cups of coffee; **Home/Work** stocks home goods and jewelry; and vintage Western clothing store **Tomboy** is a great spot to find cowboy boots. **Charlie Hong Kong** is a colorful, casual eatery located in what was once an ice-cream stand. The menu—including rice and noodle bowls, sandwiches, and salads—features local organic produce that will have you happily eating your veggies. The prix fixe dinner menu at upscale newcomer the **Midway** also highlights locally grown and foraged ingredients.

### Seaside Stays

Santa Cruz’s hotel scene is certainly having a moment. Throughout the past year, there has been a flurry of openings and renovations. While **Dream Inn** and its exclusive beachfront address remain a sought-after spot, the newly opened **Marea Sol Hotel,** located across the street from the Boardwalk, adds another accommodation with beach views. And come early 2025, construction is expected to be completed on its luxe new neighbor, **La Bahia Hotel and Spa.**

Fresh off a major renovation of its public spaces, **Hotel Paradox** is loaded with photo-worthy locations—many of which include the work of area artists. **Chaminade Resort and Spa** is sporting a new pool area complete with firepits and a food truck. The property’s parklike setting and mountain views make it feel remote, though it’s less than five miles from the Boardwalk.

Regardless of where you lay your head in Santa Cruz, your trip will be filled with fresh sea air, vitamin D, and a stunning sunset or two.
