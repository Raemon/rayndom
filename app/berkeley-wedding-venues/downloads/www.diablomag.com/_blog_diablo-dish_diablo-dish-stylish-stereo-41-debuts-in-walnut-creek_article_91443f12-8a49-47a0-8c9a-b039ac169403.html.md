### Stereo 41 Opens This Week

The anticipated restaurant and hi-fi lounge by a collaboration of successful young local restaurateurs is set to launch this week in downtown Walnut Creek. After a soft opening over the weekend, [**Stereo 41**](https://www.stereo41.com%20) will officially open this Thursday, November 13, offering an upscale food-and-drink program merging Middle Eastern and Japanese influences to pair with a rotating lineup of live DJs. The sleek, ambitious [project](https://www.instagram.com/stereo41hifi/) is the result of a collaboration between **Victor Ghaben** and **Berk Gibbs** (partners at fried chicken sandwich draw World Famous Hotboys); Victor's wife, **Natalie Alkire;****** and **Sofia Ghaben-Sabet** and her husband, **Helmy Sabet,** of nearby contemporary Caribbean hot spot Lita. It’s also one that seems tailor-made to pump up the volume in the downtown Walnut Creek restaurant scene, which Victor Ghaben confirmed when asked about his ambitions for Stereo 41.

![Stereo41-\(2\); Anna-Wick-Photography.gif](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

“We saw a void in offerings in this city,” he says. “There are lot of places that have a fine-casual vibe, but not too many places that hit the mark for what you might get in a fine dining restaurant in a bigger city like San Francisco. My sister and her husband lived in Dubai for a long time, and they have that type of dining experience: more curated, with a higher level of execution and a little bit of nightlife. We wanted to bring that home to Walnut Creek.”

As for the overall food and vibe, Ghaben says, “The inspiration was combining a Tokyo hi-fi listening bar with our Middle Eastern heritage and the food we grew up with—then trying to modernize that with Japanese influences and technique.”

![Stereo41-\(3\); Anna-Wick-Photography.gif](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

The dinner menu from executive chef Jonathan De La Torre (formerly sous-chef at San Francisco’s Michelin-starred Moroccan restaurant, Mourad) very much reflects that fusion. A selection of some 20 small plates ranges from hamachi and trout sashimi to couscous and lamb kofta, plus hybrid dishes such as a nori falafel and a Lebanese batata _harra_ salad with wasabi and trout roe. Individual entrées include lamb _merguez_ sausage and barramundi fish with Japanese curry, while family-style shared plates include braised lamb neck, Japanese-spiced chicken with preserved lemon, and slow cooked short rib with dates and fermented yuzu kosho seasoning. The drink program, meanwhile, focuses on highballs (typically mixing liquor with a carbonated beverage) and signature cocktails such as the Gimme Gimme gimlet with Japanese vodka, raspberry, cranberry, and makrut lime.

As for the nightlife, expect to see plenty of vinyl records and live DJs spinning nearly every night. Ghaben and Gibbs both have extensive backgrounds in playing and producing music and aim to tap those industry connections for programming, which may range from old-school jazz and R&B early in the week to higher-profile DJs on weekends. And don’t be surprised to see one of the owners manning the booth.

“Berk and I are both pretty well-versed DJs,” says Ghaben. “We can sit and play music for five hours if we need to.”

### Oakland Cocktail Week Kicks Off

Thursday also marks the start of **Oakland Cocktail Week,** a 10-day celebration of one of the best drinking scenes in the country. Among the restaurants and bars offering special cocktail deals from November 13–23 include Acre, Jo’s Modern Thai, Parche, and the new Lucy Blue, among many others. ### Massés May Be Out by the End of the Year

A Walnut Creek sports and pool hall could be in danger of closing. **Massés Sports Bar and Grill,** home to 10,000 square feet packed with 40-plus TVs, 10 billiards tables, bocce courts, shuffleboard tables, darts, and more, announced that it may face imminent eviction from a location where it’s operated since 1994. According to a statement on the sports bar’s [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/MassesSportsBar/posts/pfbid0CZZMqkkLXWFYoCHCrE7MaJ2GugLNyXPpD88XtGEGfFGM6bgfGsLUhZsH1tSMKCL5l), landlords of the North Main Street property are moving forward with development plans, which “likely means we’ll soon receive a notice to vacate our longtime home.” Regardless, Eric Paden and Melisa Barrett—the brother-sister proprietors who inherited Massés from their parents—vowed in the same announcement that they were “already working hard to find a new location right here in the community we love. … We will rebuild bigger, better, and with the same heart that’s made us one of the best sports bars in the Bay Area.”

### Free Thanksgiving Meal for Those in Need at All Locanda Locations

Restaurants have been stepping up all over the Bay Area to provide meals for recipients of SNAP, the supplemental food program threatened by the ongoing government shutdown—which is now showing signs of ending this week. In the meantime, it’s been a tough situation made even more difficult as we enter into the holiday season. Which is why **Enzo Rosano,** owner of the local chain of **Locanda** Italian restaurants, is stepping up to offer [free Thanksgiving meals](https://www.instagram.com/p/DQuQAg6E5GO/?hl=en) at all seven of his locations, including five in the East Bay. From 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. on November 27, Locanda will be providing a complimentary Thanksgiving dinner to those in need: No reservations needed, first come first served. Says Rosano of the gesture: “Times are tough for a lot of people, and when you can’t afford things during the holidays, it makes it even more depressing. So I thought, come to Locanda and enjoy a beautiful dinner with good fun, energy, and music. We’ll try to feed as many people as possible.”

### Elia Expands to the South Bay

**Fatih Ulas** is at it again. The energetic restaurateur behind the popular **Elia** restaurants in Pleasanton and Walnut Creek just signed a lease to open a fourth outpost in downtown Campbell. He is taking over the space previously occupied by Opa! Authentic Greek, the popular chain that recently shuttered all its Bay Area locations (WC Kitchen and Bar recently held a grand opening in the former home of Opa! Walnut Creek). Ulas, who also operates Sultan’s Kebab as well as brunch-centric the Duchess in Pleasanton and the Turkish concept Bódrum Mezze and Cocktails in Dublin, says he hopes to launch Elia Campbell in February, as well as a fourth branch of Sultan’s Kebab right next door. Busy, busy…

### Small Bites From Around the East Bay

Congrats to Livermore’s **Darcie Kent Estate Winery** and **Rosa Fierro Cellars,** which were [recently recognized](https://www.eastbaytimes.com/2025/11/10/livermore-valley-wineries-top-women-owned-wineries/) as among the top 10 women-run wineries in the country. 

[**Sasa**](https://www.instagram.com/p/DN6JkZZj8DH/?img_index=1) recently debuted a nice patio for outdoor dining in downtown Walnut Creek, and **Ramen Hiroshi** is apparently in the process of building its own. 

**[CaliKid](https://www.yelp.com/biz/calikid-burgers-n-shakes-pleasant-hill-2),** a popular burger and shake spot in Livermore across from the San Francisco Premium Outlets, just launched a second location in Pleasant Hill near Diablo Valley College.

Pleasanton is home to [**Philz Coffee**](https://www.sfgate.com/food/article/bay-area-philz-coffee-drive-thru-store-pleasanton-21146719.php)’s first drive-through location. 

**Starter Bakery** ’s new beignets are apparently very popular: The just-opened Albany location [sold out](https://www.sfchronicle.com/food/restaurants/article/starter-bakery-beignet-albany-21138542.php) in a matter of hours. 

Pandemic pop-up sensation **Cenaduria Elvira** is [opening](https://oaklandside.org/2025/11/04/cenaduria-elvira-valera-tostada-raspada-jack-london-square/) a permanent brick-and-mortar location in Jack London Square. Owner Elvira Varela developed a cult following for her Jalisco-style Mexican fare, including _New York Times_ –lauded tostadas _raspadas,_ made out of her Oakland backyard. 

It looks like **‘Ammatka Cafe,** specializing in “Ohlone-forward” snacks, meals, and beverages from the duo behind Cafe Ohlone, is [launching](https://lawrencehallofscience.org/events/ammatka-cafe-grand-opening/) at UC Berkeley’s Lawrence Hall of Science on November 22. 

[**Fatto a Mano**](https://www.instagram.com/p/DQuUGrvD5hN/) is targeting a mid-December opening for its scratch-made porchetta sandwiches and other Tuscan specialties in Oakland’s Prescott Market.
