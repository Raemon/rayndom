The Branford Marsalis Quartet will appear on October 8.

Yvonne Schmedemann

SFJazz is nationally regarded as a premier presenter in its genre—and in 2026, it’s establishing a home away from home in Walnut Creek. Alongside the Lesher Center Presents Headliners Series and the Diablo Regional Arts Association, the organization will host concerts highlighting esteemed performers from the United States and around the world during the SFJazz @ the Lesher Center inaugural series.

The effort is part of SFJazz’s outreach initiative, which has brought artists to festivals around Northern California. “When we decided which venues we would approach, [the Lesher Center] was one of our top targets,” says director of artistic programming for SFJazz, Burkhard Hopper, who led the curation of the series lineup. “It’s similar to our size, so we can service the artist here and add one show in the East Bay.”

Although the concert series officially starts in 2026, East Bay jazz aficionados can attend a special launch event featuring the Branford Marsalis Quartet on October 8. A Grammy winner and member of the Marsalis musical family, Branford Marsalis is considered among the best saxophonists of all time. Then, on January 10, the Lesher Center will host the Kenny Barron Trio and guest Tyreek McDole; other celebrated musicians in the series include Gerald Clayton, Madeleine Peyroux, Sarah McKenzie, and Lee Ritenour.

The SFJazz residency will also help elevate the presence of jazz music in the Lesher Center’s own outreach projects, including its Arts Access School Time events for students, Pay What You Can performances, and Lesher Center in Your Neighborhood programming that takes place outside of the center. For Hopper, jazz has a universal appeal that extends to diverse crowds.

“Jazz [has always been] and still is very popular. Those artists that we bring to the Lesher Center are approachable and … accessible to any audience,” he says. “[The genre’s] musicians can really show their abilities and explore and communicate with each other via their instruments. These are all great artists [to serve] as tastemakers.” _[lesherartscenter​.org](http://lesherartscenter%E2%80%8B.org), [sfjazz.org](http://sfjazz.org)._
