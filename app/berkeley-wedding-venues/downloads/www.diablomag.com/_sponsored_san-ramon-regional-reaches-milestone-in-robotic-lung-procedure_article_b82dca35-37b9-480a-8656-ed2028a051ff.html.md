San Ramon Regional Medical Center recently completed its 100th Monarch Robotic Bronchoscopy, showing once again why it’s a top choice for leading doctors and their patients. This advanced technology allows specialists to find and evaluate possible signs of lung cancer in ways that older methods couldn’t.

Many people don’t know that lung cancer is the number one cause of cancer deaths worldwide, with 1.8 million deaths each year. One major reason is that lung cancer often has no symptoms in its initial stages. That’s why finding it early with advanced tools like the Monarch system is so important.

* * *

### So just what Is the Monarch Robotic System?

The Monarch Platform is a high-tech system that helps doctors perform safer, more accurate lung biopsies. It uses 3D images and a tiny robotic camera to reach deep into the lungs—even to areas that were hard or impossible to access before. This helps doctors spot cancer earlier and treat it sooner.

#### Key Benefits:

**• Greater precision:** Doctors can reach small, tricky areas of the lung more easily.

**• Better visuals:** Clearer views help doctors make faster, more accurate diagnoses.

**• 3D navigation:** A 3D model of the patient’s lungs guides the procedure.

**• Less invasive:** The procedure involves fewer cuts and smaller tools, which means quicker recovery and lower risk.

**• Early detection:** Finding cancer earlier can lead to better treatment and survival rates.

It’s simple in concept and performs with high-tech: A doctor uses a robotic arm to guide a thin, flexible camera through the lungs. They watch everything in real time on a 3D monitor and can take tissue samples or treat problem areas as needed.

#### When the Monarch can help:

**• Biopsies:** Especially for small lung nodules that are hard to reach.

**• Lung cancer diagnosis:** Helps catch cancer early for better outcomes.

**• Other lung treatments:** Also useful for clearing airways or sampling suspicious spots.

We are proud of our critical care and lung specialists that use the Monarch – Drs. Malani, Diaz, Angan, Chan, Singh, and Krishnamurthy – for their work and dedication to saving lives. As the first hospital west of the Mississippi to use robotic surgery, San Ramon Regional continues to lead the way in innovation and care.
