Yosemite National Park offers opportunites for restoration and recreation.

Delaware North

Resting in the shadows of the Sierra Nevada, **Yosemite National Park** offers peak tranquility and opportunities to revel in the restorative power of nature. Around a three-hour drive from the East Bay, Yosemite and its surrounding areas promise a weekend getaway that inspires, relaxes, and rejuvenates.

Spring is an ideal time to visit Yosemite, and a good option to potentially avoid the summer crowds. The nearly 1,200-square-mile park is home to over 800 miles of hiking trails, winding through deep valleys and vast meadows. During spring, the hills come alive with vibrant florals, and waterfalls are at their most active after being fueled by the rainy seasons, creating a scenic setting for hiking, swimming, and rock climbing.

Disconnect and breathe deep at the tops of **Half Dome, El Capitan,** and **Clouds Rest,** where you can get 360-degree views of all of Yosemite. A good hike is up **Vernal Fall,** the first part of most Half Dome adventures. The trek features a steep rock staircase and several stop-offs where you can often glimpse rainbows under the mist.

If you have the climbing skills, book a slot to visit Half Dome. The 14- to 16-mile round trip is not for the faint of heart, taking 10 to 12 hours to complete and necessitating metal cables to traverse the final section of the mountain. For extra safety, bring your own tools to clip in. Permits are required.

Another seasonal favorite is the trek to Yosemite Falls, one of the highest waterfalls in the world. The two-mile hike is a steep climb with 1,000 feet of elevation gain; your reward is a close-up view of **Upper Yosemite Fall,** as well as Half Dome in the distance.

Sweeping views of the park are a scenic reward for making the hike to Glacier Point.

Shutterstock

With its limited light pollution, Yosemite offers dark skies and plenty of shimmering stars above. Bring a headlamp, and explore with the celestial bodies (and a compass) as your guide. The National Park Service has recommendations for easily reachable night sky viewing sites at [nps.gov/yose](http://nps.gov/yose). Or book a stargazing tour, which will likely include **Cook’s Meadow Loop;** the short one-mile path touches on some of the park’s best landscapes such as **Glacier Point, Yosemite Falls,** and Half Dome.

Several companies combine wellness activities with a jaunt inside (or near) the park. **Yogasemite,** for example, offers yoga, meditation, and breath work ecotours at various outdoor locations. **Mindful Café** guide Susan Madden leads meditation groups, forest bathing outings, and labyrinth walks. If you want to fully submerge yourself in the park, nonprofit **Balanced Rock** provides transformational programming and wellness-minded retreats. For more adventurous options, try the **Yosemite Mountaineering School and Guide Service** that hosts everything from rock climbing lessons to multiday backpacking excursions.

## Rejuvenating Treatments

Recover from your exertions—or just make time for pampering—at one of the top-quality spas in the region.

**Rush Creek Lodge** ’s spa is open to guests of the resort as well as visitors with a day pass or appointment. Every element here is inspired by the landscape, including the Cool Mist Room that mimics the splash of water hitting your face on a misty trail and Warm Waterfall Coves that re-create the feeling of sitting on sun-baked stone under a waterfall. An aromatherapy steam room and Himalayan salt block sauna offer healing and serenity in a stylish environment.

The sustainability-minded **Ascent Spa** at **Tenaya at Yosemite** is open to all resort guests and provides a curated lineup of facial treatments and bodywork. **Yosemite Bug Rustic Mountain Resort** is indeed rustic; its **Yosemite Health Spa** features an oxygenated stainless-steel tub, sauna, yoga workshops, and massage therapies.

## Relaxing Resort Stays

There are abundant lodging options inside the park, ranging from the recently upgraded upscale **Ahwahnee** to more basic campgrounds, but visitors can also find welcoming stays in the neighboring towns.

**Firefall Ranch Yosemite** in Groveland is the newest in a trio of accommodations owned by First Light Resorts, which also operates **Evergreen Lodge** and Rush Creek Lodge. The sprawling, summer-camp-style resort is made up of individual cottages and villas that boast luxurious amenities; these include private outdoor spaces complete with a comfy couch and two-sided fireplace that serves both the primary bedroom and private balcony.

![DM2503_037_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Explore Tenaya at Yosemite and its environs via mountain bike.

The resort also features a 24/7 pool, hot tubs, complimentary s’mores every night, two restaurants, a beach volleyball court, an 18-hole mini golf course, an 18-hole disc golf course, ping-pong, and more. Free cruiser bikes are also available so guests can ride around the property at their leisure.

On the other side of the park, Tenaya at Yosemite leans into scheduled events to build guest itineraries, including guided hikes, archery lessons, and yoga. There are also indoor and outdoor pools (with poolside movie screenings), several restaurants, fire­pits, and mountain bikes that you can take to a hidden waterfall. Well-appointed rooms and suites are available in the main lodge. Book a cottage room or suite, or an Explorer Cabin, situated on 27 forested acres.

## Mosey Through Mariposa

For a sense of the region’s history, head into neighboring **Mariposa.** The charming town provides a robust look at its Gold Rush past and is home to several historic sites, including the **Old Stone Jail** (dating back to 1858) and the **Mariposa County Courthouse,** the oldest superior courthouse west of the Mississippi.

A top Mariposa stop is **Sierra Cider,** which makes hard ciders on-site from an orchard brimming with apple trees. Depending on the time of year, you can try different seasonal flavors alongside the classics. Spend the day sipping at the two-story tasting room, picking apples in the grove, or playing lawn games.

Other Mariposa drinking and dining establishments well worth a visit are farm-to-table eatery the **Grove House** and **1850 Restaurant and Brewery.**

Whether you are looking for ways to challenge the body or simply want to escape the hustle and bustle of life, a relaxing weekend in Yosemite may be just what you need.
