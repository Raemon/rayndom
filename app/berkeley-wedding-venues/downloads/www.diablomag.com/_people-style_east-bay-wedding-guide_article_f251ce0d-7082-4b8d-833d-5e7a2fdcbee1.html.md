(And don’t forget the much-lauded Mediterranean climate.) Prepare for wedding season with _Diablo_ ’s list detailing many of the services, venues, flowers, and fare that make our region such an ideal location for blissful events.**

### 1\. THERE ARE BEAUTIFUL OUTDOOR VENUES

Who wouldn’t want to exchange marriage vows in the most beautiful flower garden in the world? **The Ruth Bancroft Garden and Nursery** was recently given that honorific based on Tripadvisor user ratings, a sentiment echoed by _Diablo_ ’s readers, who declared it the top wedding venue in Best of the East Bay 2024. Ceremonies and cocktail receptions can take place in the dry garden (sit-down meals are now limited to the Plaza or Great Room to protect the plant life), and the succulents, cacti, and blooming natives make a world-class backdrop for photos. For more flowers, **Morcom Rose Garden** and the **Gardens at Heather Farm** feature a bevy of blooms and inviting landscapes; the Gardens at Heather Farm also recently redid its indoor reception hall with new flooring and lighting. The **University of California Botanical Garden at Berkeley** has two nature-centric facilities: the historic Julia Morgan Hall in the garden and the Mather Redwood Grove and Amphitheater, where couples can wed under stands of towering trees. Other woodsy settings include **Redwood Canyon, Wildwood Acres Resort,** and **Little Hills;** the latter is situated in the Las Trampas Wilderness and has a tented reception area with glowing Scandinavian chandeliers and market lights.

Golf course venues such as the **Clubhouse at Boundary Oak** —where you can hold your ceremony at the renovated Lakeview site, next to a water feature and eucalyptus trees—the **Club at Ruby Hill,** or **Callippe Preserve** promise pastoral settings, while **Fairview Metropolitan** has panoramic views of the San Francisco skyline. **Canyon View Event Center** offers hill and valley scenes, along with indoor and outdoor spaces and its new tailored wedding packages. Golden meadows and rows of grapevines add to **Purple Orchid Wine Country Resort and Spa** ’s bucolic charm, as do its fountains, rose gardens, and manicured grounds. Exchange rings near a sparkling lake at **San Ramon Waters** before partying in the refreshed ballroom and cocktail lounge.

### 2\. THESE HISTORIC PROPERTIES WILL ADD DEPTH TO YOUR DAY

Honor the past while looking toward your bright future at these storied spots. **Claremont Resort and Club** marks its 110-year anniversary in 2025, and the landmark property with sweeping views continues to be a sumptuous spot for gatherings. The **Brazilian Room** in Tilden Regional Park—dedicated in 1941—was constructed from hardwood paneling that was part of the Brazilian Pavilion featured in the 1939 Golden Gate International Exposition. The popular venue just replaced its flooring with dark Brazilian cherry wood that complements the historic panels. Architecture buffs will love the Gothic-Moorish ambience of **Berkeley City Club,** designed by Julia Morgan, and the **Buehler Estate,** the only authentic Frank Lloyd Wright property in the East Bay. The site features 400 trees and two waterfalls and was originally landscaped by Henry Matsutani, who helped design the restored Japanese Tea Garden in Golden Gate Park. Morgan also designed the Hacienda del Pozo de Verona estate in Pleasanton, which was the clubhouse for the Castlewood Country Club in the 1920s. Now the **Club at Castlewood,** the venue specializes in cultural and multiday celebrations, including traditional Indian weddings. **Hacienda de las Flores,** a historic Spanish-style estate, is equally striking, while **Jefferson Street Mansion,** built in 1861, features neoclassical architecture, designed grounds, and period decor.

The roots of **Murrieta’s Well** hark back to 1884, when it was first purchased by Louis Mel, who later sold the property to Ernest Wente. The vineyard estate has natural charm and sophistication, and includes an atmospheric barrel room. **Shadelands Ranch Museum** is a time capsule reflecting Walnut Creek at the turn of the century but provides a gracious landscape for a modern wedding under large oak trees.

### 3\. TRUSTED JEWELERS WILL HELP YOU PUT A RING ON IT

Marking betrothals and weddings with rings is a tradition that stems back to the Roman Empire, but designs and materials have evolved over the centuries. Engagement rings in particular may highlight a variety of jewels, cuts, and settings, though the pear-shaped diamond solitaire is still the most popular option, says Sarah Heller, director of operations and branding for **Heller Jewelers** in San Ramon.

“Everyone wears a ring on the same finger to symbolize their love, but people can take the ring in so many different directions and make it their own,” says Heller. “We love being a part of that process. We pride ourselves on being a trustworthy member of the East Bay community and making people happy.”

Heller has seen a move away from delicate, thin bands that were popular during the last two years, with customers now preferring more sculptural-looking or heavier, wider bases. Round brilliant cuts remain classics for a reason, but ovals and other fancy cuts are increasingly taking center stage. Recent trends include three-stone rings—with one center diamond and two smaller jewels on either side—and pieces featuring multiple stones in intricate designs.

“Diamonds are still the most popular, but every now and then we work with a bride who wants to use different gemstones. We just did a custom piece for a bride who repurposed a family heirloom emerald ring. It’s always exciting to do something a little different and help somebody match their vision,” says Heller.

The second-generation, family-run business frequently designs custom rings, and all their diamonds are ethically sourced.

When it comes to the wedding band, most women tend to select a diamond band that stacks with their engagement ring or a plain gold or platinum band. Grooms often want their ring to match the bride’s ring, though others go for their own unique look. “Some men want diamonds or black diamonds, or etching, or engraving, or millegrain—things that add detail and interest to the ring,” says Heller. “There’s no right or wrong answer.” _[hellerjewelers.com](http://hellerjewelers.com)._

### 5\. YOU CAN BOOK A CAKE PRO

Mitra Asgari’s wedding cakes are more than mouthwatering confections—they are artistic masterpieces. Asgari, who has been in the culinary industry for 25 years, designs custom cakes that are influenced by her longtime love of the visual arts.

Most of the cakes Asgari sculpts at **Mitra Art Cake** feature three to four tiers in a round, square, or unique shape. Each is made from scratch and features high-quality ingredients from brands such as Ghirardelli Chocolate and IfiGourmet. Asgari is well-versed in all the classic flavors, but among her most-requested offerings are chiffon vanilla cake with strawberry cream mousse and fresh strawberries, devil’s food chocolate cake with dark chocolate cream mousse, and red velvet cake with cream cheese frosting. Many clients also add fruit, caramel, nuts, or whipped cream.

“It’s not routine,” says Farhad Panbehchi, Asgari’s husband, who handles marketing for the company. “We can do [anything with] design and flavors. She can deliver.”

Asgari works with clients from different cultures and is passionate about learning new traditions, which influence each cake’s presentation. Her embellishments are what truly shine, however, as she adds flowers, fondant designs, and 2D and 3D features to her creations. Over the years, she’s crafted cakes shaped like bridal gowns and castles and featuring religious iconography. (Cakes for other occasions have been shaped like sports gear, luxury shopping bags, and a knight’s shield—there’s even been one with a Godzilla figure.)

Asgari worked at a Sunnyvale bakery and with Katrina Rozelle for several years in Alamo before founding her home business. Prospective clients are encouraged to set up a cake tasting, held Monday through Wednesday, where they can sample eight to ten flavors, sip tea, and chat about their vision. Mitra Art Cake also provides transportation and setup services on the big day.

“We hope the cake becomes a memorable centerpiece that not only looks beautiful but also brings joy and excitement to our guests,” she says. “We want it to reflect our story and style—something unique, delicious, and unforgettable that ties everything together with sweetness.” _[mitraartcake.com](http://mitraartcake.com)._

### 6\. YOU’D LIKE TO EXCHANGE VOWS IN A UNIQUE SPACE

For a truly memorable wedding, consider a venue that goes beyond the ordinary. You can create a celestial event at **Chabot Space and Science Center** ’s updated ceremony space in the full-dome Planetarium. Advanced digital projection capabilities allow couples to set the stars to the date and time they met and have the moonrise as a background for their vows or colliding galaxies overhead during their first kiss. Those who prefer a marine theme might exchange promises in front of the colorful swimming fish in the Steinhart Aquarium at the **California Academy of Sciences** and hold their reception in the Tusher African Hall, complete with penguins. Go nautical sans wildlife at the **Lighthouse at Glen Cove Marina,** a waterfront oasis on the Carquinez Strait. Outdoor spaces such as the East and West Gardens give tropical vibes, while the Waterfront Banquet Room’s floor-to-ceiling windows overlook the marina. **Oakland Zoo** also hosts events at the Snow Building; the historic venue is away from the animal residents but promises panoramic views.

Write your own storybook romance at **Children’s Fairyland,** with an Ever After Grand Experience, which grants access to the park’s 10 acres for a six-hour party, or its new Ever After Intimate Experience for those who want a magical micro­wedding (three hours). Add a whimsical note with a magician, puppet, or fairy as your officiant. Or transport your guests to a French château while staying close to home at **Lafayette Park Hotel and Spa,** where venues such as the Wishing Well Courtyard and Independence Ballroom are perfect for chic gatherings of varying sizes. ### 25\. THE HONEYMOON STAGE AWAITS

What could be better than celebrating a union with your soulmate in front of family and friends? How about taking your first trip as a married couple to an exotic locale where your only task is to relax, rest, and explore?

Some newlywed couples prefer a minimoon (typically lasting under a week) after their big day, saving the major trip for a later date when they might have more time and financial resources available, says Meg Kan of **MK Travel** in Danville. Kan is a certified wedding planner who specializes in destination weddings and honeymoons. Her first step when helping couples with their honeymoon is determining what exactly they both want in the travel experience.

“Everyone has their own vision,” says Kan, who works with clients in Walnut Creek, Pleasanton, and Danville. “Are they picturing it as a once-in-a-lifetime trip … or a chance to decompress and just be with each other?”

When it comes to minimoons, luxurious, all-inclusive resorts in locales such as Mexico or the Caribbean are popular, while Kan says private African safaris, expedition cruises to Antarctica or the Galápagos Islands, and tropical destinations such as the Maldives or Fiji are favorite bucket-list picks.

Japan was a top spot last year, with many couples mixing cultural and historical itineraries with hot springs or beach time. France and Spain offer a similar combination of relaxation and cultural opportunities, while Thailand is also trending in 2025. Looking ahead, Kan anticipates couples opting for locations such as Scotland and Ireland. “Not everyone likes the beach,” she says. “You can get that laid-back country feeling. When you stay at a manor or a castle, you can do falconry, go horseback riding, go fishing, play golf. You get to pretend that you’re a country lord and lady.” The Philippines and Vietnam may also attract more honeymooners next year. “People are looking for lesser-known places and unique experiences,” adds Kan. “My clients don’t want cookie-cutter [trips].” _[mktravelinc.com](http://mktravelinc.com)_.
