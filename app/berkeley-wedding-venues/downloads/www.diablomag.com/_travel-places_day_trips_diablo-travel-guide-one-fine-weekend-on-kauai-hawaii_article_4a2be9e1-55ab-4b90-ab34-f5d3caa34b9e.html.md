Among Hawaii’s many natural marvels and lush vistas, **Kauai** is dubbed “the Garden Island”—and even from your window seat before the plane touches the earth, it’s obvious as to why. Roughly five hours’ flight from the Oakland San Francisco Bay Airport, Kauai is Hawaii’s oldest island and smaller than the tourist meccas of Maui or the island of Hawaii; it is also home to Mount Waialeale, one of the wettest locations on the globe, with 450 inches of rain per year on average. The island is full of abundant rainforests, towering volcanic rock formations, and glistening white beaches. 

Once you arrive at Lihue Airport, head to the rental car hub before making your way around the coastline to Kauai’s northern shore. About 50 minutes away, **Hanalei Bay** awaits with picturesque views and thrilling adventures.

### **Where Comfort Meets Culture**

For a truly rejuvenating stay on Kauai, try **1 Hotel Hanalei Bay,** which promises the latest in luxury travel while still demonstrating a fierce dedication to the island and its people and a firm commitment to sustainable living. The resort reopened in February 2023 with a refreshed, open design that aims to bring the outside in. Drop your bags off at one of 252 well-appointed rooms and suites before heading straight to the pool. The main and lagoon pools feature breathtaking views of the bay and Mount Makana and are ideally situated next to the Sandbox restaurant and the treats at Wai’Oli. Don’t miss the latter’s shaved ice, available in flavors such as Kealia, a combo of lilikoi, ube, honey, lemon, and coconut, or in build-your-own varieties.

Steps away, Puu Poa Beach is dotted with cabanas that make for a peaceful afternoon of reading and watching the waves. The curious can find crabs and other marine life in the tide pools, or explore Kamani and Leolani coves. Fitness enthusiasts will appreciate the open-air gym, featuring exercise equipment crafted from invasive Abizia wood.

Additionally, guests can focus on well-being at the indoor Anatomy Gym and Vitality Kauai, which offer vitamin IV infusions, B12 injections, non-invasive body composition scans, and Botox and other aesthetic treatments (with a comprehensive blood panel and biomarker review available as of January)—or they can visit the adjoining studio to take part in a variety of classes, including Gym Jumpstart sessions for beginners, yoga, and myofascial release.

The wellness offerings are part of a larger Daylife at the 1 program that also prioritizes science and culture. From coral reef talks and stand-up paddleboarding sessions to environmentally friendly trunk shows at the Goodthings shop, there’s something for every traveler on the schedule. A highlight is lau hala bracelet weaving, an opportunity to immerse yourself in island culture through a tradition practiced by native Hawaiians. Shake off the stresses of 2025 at the Bamford Wellness Spa, which provides both cutting-edge and traditional Hawaiian treatments rooted in local products. Bamford, Anatomy Gym, and Vitality Kauai just teamed up to create Wellness Journey Retreats, with expert-led itineraries featuring themes such as “fuel your inner fire” and “retreat and recharge,” to their slate of services, in addition to sleep, brain health, and longevity add-on packages.

Visitors have endless ways to explore the outdoor attractions on- and off-site—from surfing Hanalei Bay to scuba diving around the lava tunnels at **Makua Beach** (also known as **Tunnels Beach**) to zooming down the **Koloa Zipline** (the longest on the island). Other potential adventures include hiking at **Waimea Canyon State Park,** kayaking the **Wailua River,** horseback riding at **Princeville Ranch,** and snorkeling at **Anini Beach.** If you are looking for a low-key activity, **Kauai Mini Golf and Botanical Gardens** at **Anaina Hou Community Park** boasts mini golf surrounded by eye-catching flora.

### **Tropical Flavors**

In keeping with the rest of its amenities, 1 Hotel Hanalei Bay’s dining options are rooted in seasonal, sustainable, and regional ingredients (with sources as close as the resort’s rooftop garden). Guests can enjoy coffee at Neighbors café in the morning and wrap up the night with a cocktail at Welina Terrace. 1 Kitchen, the hotel’s signature eatery, serves dishes such as seafood skewers, coriander-sesame ahi, and banana-wrapped snapper in a dining room designed with reclaimed and sustainably sourced materials. The restaurant serves a robust, island-centric breakfast buffet as well. 1 Hotel Hanalei Bay also debuted its Sunrise Nourishment Bar this fall, which provides everything from fresh-pressed juices to restorative broths to a four-day juice detox, with each drink option crafted to promote wellness. 1 Hotel Hanalei Bay is also hosting international food heavyweights, including _Chopped_ ’s Amanda Freitag and Michelin-starred SanBrite restaurateur Riccardo Gaspari, at the quarterly Chefs by Nature series.

This holiday season, 1 Kitchen will feature a four-course prix-fixe dinner on Christmas Eve and a day-long buffet on Christmas Day. Guests who make their trips in December can also enjoy special room offers that involve the planting of native trees and donations to the Kauai Humane Society in their honor. (For other winter celebrations, consider the Waimea Light Parade and Kauai’s Festival of Lights marketplace.) Kick off 2026 by penning a letter to yourself about your forthcoming goals, which the hotel will place in the mail in a year, before attending a New Year’s party at 1 Kitchen and Welina Terrace.

Nearby, **Hanalei** features more chances for culinary exploration. At **Tahiti Nui,** featured in George Clooney’s 2011 film _The Descendants,_ diners can choose from a long cocktail list while listening to live music. Stop by **Hanalei Market** to grab a bag of **Outpost Coffee Kauai** beans, which are cultivated on the island, or order a mouthwatering pastry at **Hanalei Bread Co**. For a sit-down meal, **Bar Acuda** and the **Terrace** are both worthy choices. Try the Terrace’s Lava Burst—a nonalcoholic piña colada with strawberry puree—and fresh poke. Just outside of town, the **Waipa Foundation** hosts a Tuesday farmers market that highlights local food purveyors and artisans; you can also tour the foundation on select days to explore Hawaiian peoples’ history and connection to the land.

### **Island Adventures**

In nearby Princeville, golfers will want to visit the **Princeville Makai Golf Club,** which offers Robert Trent Jones Jr.–designed 9- and 18-hole courses and acts as a safe haven for near-threatened and endangered indigenous bird species, including the nene, Hawaiian coot, and the Laysan albatross. The course also hosts a Sunset Golf Car Tour, during which you can drive your own cart and take in glorious views from the last stop as the sun dips below the horizon.

A two-hour rum safari at **Kilohana** is a 21+ introduction to agriculture on the island, featuring rum tastings, cocktails, and visits with farm animals. About 45 minutes from Hanalei, **Lydgate Farms** offers a deep dive into Hawaii’s blossoming role as the “Napa Valley of chocolate.” On the property tour, an experienced guide will discuss details about the growing and harvesting processes; elaborate on the health benefits of chocolate; and provide samples of various Lydgate confections, craft chocolates from as far as Ghana and the Dominican Republic, and tropical fruits such as soursop and pineapple. Pick up a bar of 70 percent dark chocolate with Hawaiian sea salt or chocolate-covered macadamia nuts at Lydgate’s shop to take a little piece of the island home with you. You’ll be planning a return trip as soon as you land back in the East Bay.
