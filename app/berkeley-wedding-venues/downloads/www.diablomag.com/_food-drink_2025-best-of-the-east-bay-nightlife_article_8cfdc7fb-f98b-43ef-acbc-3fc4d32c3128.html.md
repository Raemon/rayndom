## EDITORS' PICKS

#### BEST INTIMATE LIVE MUSIC EXPERIENCE

**ALMOST FAMOUS WINE COMPANY, LIVERMORE**

Well-loved by both performers and audiences, Almost Famous Wine Company is a cozy 200-person-capacity live music venue in the heart of Livermore Valley wine country that features premier lighting and sound. The lounge doubles as the award-winning winery’s tasting room, showcasing more than 15 wines on the beverage menu. The welcoming space is enhanced by brick walls, bistro tables, and dynamic, music-themed murals by Spencer McCarty, who transformed the warehouse into its current clubby environment in 2021. _[almostfamouswine.com](http://almostfamouswine.com)._

#### BEST PLACE TO ROCK OUT OUTDOORS

**TOYOTA PAVILION AT CONCORD, CONCORD**

Toyota Pavilion at Concord has undergone several transformations since it first opened in May 1975, but one thing has remained the same: The venue, which celebrates its 50th anniversary this year, is a favorite open-air destination for music fans in the East Bay. The amphitheater has hosted a wealth of big names over the past five decades, including Dave Brubeck, Bing Crosby, Ella Fitzgerald, B.B. King, Imagine Dragons, Tom Petty, Frank Sinatra, Tina Turner, and Stevie Wonder, among many others. With a capacity of 12,500, guests can enjoy shows from the sprawling lawn, floor seats, or VIP box seats. _[toyotapavilionatconcord.com](http://toyotapavilionatconcord.com)._

#### BEST SPOT TO BE ON ISLAND TIME

**TIKI TOM’S, WALNUT CREEK**

Tiki Tom’s is an island-infused restaurant and bar that has brought tiki fans to Walnut Creek from all over the region. One such enthusiast, Oscar-winning composer Michael Giacchino (_The Incredibles, Up_), contributed an original composition to accompany the establishment’s signature cocktail Release the Kraken; the dramatic score plays over the sound system whenever a customer receives the 50-ounce rum-heavy libation. Tiki Tom’s—which was initially founded in 2000—underwent a major refresh a few years ago and features a family- friendly menu with a Hawaiian-fusion focus. Co-owner Darrin DeRita has plans to spread the adventure vibes even further in the near future with a new themed restaurant and bar in the Tri-Valley “that promises pure escapism,” he says. _[tikitoms.co](http://tikitoms.co)._

### Best Dance Club, Singles Spot

**RETRO JUNKIE, WALNUT CREEK**

Retro Junkie has a little something for everyone: dancing, karaoke, comedy, trivia nights, pinball, arcade games, pool tables, kitschy interior spaces populated by neon signs and memorabilia, and of course, music. Lively tribute bands from the past several decades will take you back to when Prince (purple) reigned or Britney Spears did it again. The venue also hosts nostalgic touring acts and local bands on occasion, plus Country Night with line dancing lessons, Salsa Tuesdays, and regular DJ action. The dance floor can get crowded, but there is still room to find your groove. Reward yourself with on-theme cocktails like the Ghostbuster or Whisky Business. No wonder Retro Junkie is a dance destination for party people from around the Bay Area. _[retrojunkiebar.com](http://retrojunkiebar.com)._
