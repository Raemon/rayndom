## EDITORS' PICKS

#### BEST NEWCOMER

**THE MAKER’S MERCANTILE, WALNUT CREEK**

This art collective boutique, co-owned by Jennifer Spencer and her two daughters, opened its doors in September 2023. It’s moody and eclectic, and features a curated mix of vintage items and handmade pieces from makers in the East Bay. Starting this month, more home decor—such as house plants and candles—will be included in the offerings.

#### **BEST TRIED-AND-TRUE SPOT**

**ELISA WEN, DANVILLE**

Having opened in 2001 and been located at its current Danville spot for over a decade, Elisa Wen is a staple shopping destination of the area. With a classic-meets-modern aesthetic (think fun details on traditional pieces), the shop carries an appealing selection of women’s jewelry, totes, handbags, and clothing from such brands as Hammitt, Heartloom, Velvet, and Veronica M. _[elisawen.com](http://elisawen.com)._

#### BEST HIDDEN GEM

**ROTHSCHILDS, ALAMO**

Tucked away in a downtown Alamo strip mall, this women’s boutique has a plethora of goodies to explore. Owner Linya Rothschild has created a bright, open, stylish, and fun environment and offers her customers a selection of on-trend clothes and accessories as well as personal wardrobe consultations. Notable brands featured on a regular basis include Bella Dahl, Michael Stars, Paige, and Sundry. _[shoprothschilds.com](http://shoprothschilds.com)._
