Sometimes the best spot for a memorable yuletide gathering is a hotel, where the halls are beautifully decked and festive foods are prepared by professional chefs. For a luxury staycation befitting your immediate family or one that includes out-of-towners, consider these top-tier accommodations.

### Celebrate in the Hills

Mention the **Claremont Club and Spa, a Fairmont Hotel,** to most East Bay residents, and you’re likely to hear: “I’ve always wanted to stay there!” The palatial estate on 22 acres in the Berkeley and Oakland hills offers stylish rooms and suites, family-friendly amenities, fine dining, and sweeping views of the San Francisco Bay.

The holidays are a particularly special time to stay at the 109-year-old Claremont, with events such as a tree lighting ceremony on December 4 and high teas held in the Lobby Lounge on weekends through December 29. Chef Joseph Paire III will provide a memorable Christmas Eve and Day dinner at **Limewood Bar and Restaurant,** along with the Claremont’s signature brunch (served December 25 through January 1). Limewood is worth a visit even without an occasion, with a well-executed menu showcasing California cuisine and locally sourced ingredients. Start your meal with a craft cocktail in the airy dining room or on the outdoor patio, where the panoramic vistas add flavor to the experience. Try the excellent steak slider appetizer that includes tender shaved prime rib, black garlic aioli, and Fiscalini cheddar cheese (made for sharing, it doubles as a hearty entrée), or the duck hash when it is available on the brunch menu.

![DM2412_106_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Enjoy the season with s’mores at the Claremont Club and Spa, a Fairmont Hotel.

Courtesy of Claremont Club and Spa, a Fairmont Hotel

Although the Club at the Claremont’s three heated outdoor pools might not be quite as much of a draw during the colder months, the spacious pool deck is great for sunning, and kids will love the splash pool. The nearby Kids’ Club is also a good option for young guests—it hosts movie nights on Fridays—while the fitness center and tennis and pickleball courts are perfect for older exercise enthusiasts. A treatment at the spa can ease muscles or supply pre-party pampering.

Nearby attractions such as the **Oakland Zoo, Chabot Space and Science Center, Children’s Fairyland,** and **Plank** (bowling, bocce, and arcade games) have more opportunities for family fun.

The elegant guest rooms and suites feature contemporary design elements but still reflect the hotel’s rich history. For a true treat, book the Presidential Suite, which has a fabulous bedroom with en suite bathroom (complete with soaking tub), a formal dining room, a wet bar and pantry with a service entrance (yes, you can book a private chef), and a private office. There is even a connecting room for an additional guest. Other luxury suites feature expansive views, refined decor, and separate seating areas, while Bay View rooms can accommodate four guests and provide a stunning look at the cityscape and San Francisco Bay.

### Gather in Old-World Grandeur

Located on the other side of the Caldecott Tunnel from the Claremont, **Lafayette Park Hotel and Spa** feels like it’s been transplanted from the French countryside. The property has been a popular getaway among locals for years—it was a _Diablo_ Readers’ Pick for Best Staycation in “Best of the East Bay 2024”—and is a sophisticated holiday haven close to home.

Guests will love the romantic atmosphere of the award-winning hotel, which has charming garden courtyards, a serene pool, a newly updated on-site restaurant, and welcoming day spa. The 138 rooms in the château-style building include refined options such as the La Grande Suites, with dining tables and generous living rooms that work well for small gatherings. Those looking to host an intimate party in their suite can work with banquet services on the menu and request serving staff as desired. Lafayette Park Hotel also has multiple event spaces that can host celebrations of all sizes. Guests can even get a decorated Christmas tree for their suite—petite versions are available in rooms as well—for a fee, as long as the request is made at least two weeks prior to their stay.

![DM2412_088_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

The Park Bistro serves beautifully crafted classic cocktails.

Katie Newburn

For Christmas Eve and Christmas Day, the hotel hosts a special buffet and menu in the **Park Bistro and Bar.** Look for traditional favorites such as prime rib, roast half chicken, leg of lamb, or honey-glazed ham on the buffet and dishes such as filet mignon or a duck duo at the restaurant. The Park Bistro underwent a refresh this spring, providing an inviting setting for chef Rodrigo Alvarez’s farm-to-fork cuisine. Another seasonal tradition atthe hotel is the Teddy Bear Tea, a beloved community event featuring a buffet and activities for children that is held in early December. An outing to **Broadway Plaza** in Walnut Creek is good for last-minute shoppers, and hotel staff can provide information about other area happenings.

“Lafayette Park Hotel and Spa is a great place for families to come and relax and be pampered,” says managing director Nick Bozych. “It’s somewhere you want to go for the holidays. It’s like a home away from home. Guests feel the warmth here.”

### Relax in Wine Country Refinement

Escape from holiday craziness at **Purple Orchid Wine Country Resort and Spa** in Livermore. The family-owned bed-and-breakfast is located on 24 acres of golden hills, with Manzanilla olive trees, rose gardens, and landscaped grounds that are ideal for hosting parties of various sizes. Most of the country-chic accommodations provide views of the gracious property and surrounding Livermore landscape. The on-site spa offers a range of services such as massage (including choices for couples), body treatments, and hydrafacials that are especially appealing in the winter.

![DM2412_084_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Purple Orchid Wine Country Resort and Spa provides tempting complimentary breakfasts.

Courtesy of Purple Orchid Wine Country Resort and Spa

Guests seeking a comforting respite will certainly appreciate the fireplaces and charming sitting areas found in all of the resort’s 10 rooms; some suites also have tubs made for two. The complimentary nightly happy hours feature wine, cheese, and olive oil tastings; stays also include a full hot breakfast that spotlights seasonal ingredients.

Those still in need of holiday gifts can check out products easily on hand at Purple Orchid. Proprietor Derek Eddy recommends “our award-winning estate extra virgin olive oil and balsamic vinegars” for home chefs, as well as locally made soaps, spices, and other goods. The resort is close to downtown, where guests can hop on the **Livermore Wine Trolley** for a Lights of Livermore outing; the resort can also arrange for a private lights tour.
