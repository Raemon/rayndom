Eye for Pretty at Home’s rattan bells.

Courtesy of Eye for Pretty

Holiday cheer doesn’t have to be cheesy. For a sophisticated look, try these tips from interior designer Nicole Salceda, creative director of Eye for Pretty in Danville. She suggests layering natural material, combining “rattan, linen, and wood to add organic warmth. Think woven baskets, chunky knit throws, and heirloom ornaments for a cozy, collected look.” Among the top sellers at Salceda’s Eye for Pretty at Home store on Hartz Avenue are rattan bells, perfect for adorning the Christmas tree or grouping together as a mantel display. Soft lighting is also key; bring a hospitable glow to your space by layering candles, string lights, and table lamps. Glass ornaments on your tree bring more reflected luminescence.

![DM2512_087_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

A Celestial Angel Stocking from M by Maggie.

Courtesy of Mon Ami

Earthy color tones add a refined look as well. “Swap the traditional red and green for a warm neutral palette with caramels, olives, and rust,” says Salceda. “Mixed with a little holiday sparkle, these hues feel timeless and elevated.” Don’t go overboard with the glittery accents, however; use other metals such as antique bronze and gold finishes. Finally, she recommends incorporating handmade items and personal touches in your decor. “I love the nostalgia of the holiday season,” she adds. _Rattan bell in small, medium, and large, $34–$54,_ [_eyeforpretty​athome.com_](http://athome.comhttps//eyeforprettyathome.com/) _._

#### Angelic Host

Give Santa a sweet repository for small gifts and sugary treats with Mon Ami’s Celestial Angel Stocking (no coal allowed). Sold at M by Maggie at City Center Bishop Ranch in San Ramon, the polyester stocking features delicate embroidery and 3D embellishments. _$33,_ [_mbymaggie.com_](http://mbymaggie.com) _._

#### Brass Act

Bring a European flourish with vintage brass candlesticks collected from French flea markets and sold at Elsie Green in Concord. “Mix a few shapes and heights on the mantel or down the table—each one carries its own quiet history, so your holiday glow feels personal, not prescribed,” says Kelsey Schmidt of Elsie Green. Add some beeswax tapers for natural lighting and a gentle scent. _Pair of tapers, $12; candlestick, $49,_ [_elsiegreen.com_](http://elsiegreen.com) _._
