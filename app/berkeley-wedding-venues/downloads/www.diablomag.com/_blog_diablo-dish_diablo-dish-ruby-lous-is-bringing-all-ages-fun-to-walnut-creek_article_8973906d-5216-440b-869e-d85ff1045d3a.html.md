### Ruby Lou’s Set to Open Soon Downtown

So what is the inspiration behind [**Ruby Lou’s Boujee Bites and Games**](https://www.instagram.com/rubylouswalnutcreek/), the colorful, whimsical new concept coming to downtown Walnut Creek? That would be Ruby Lou, the 4-year-old daughter of owner **Megan Benshalom** , who (shocker) came up with the name herself.

“I wanted to take her rose-colored glasses outlook on life and joy, and bottle that and share it with the world,” explains Benshalom.

But even more than that, Benshalom simply wanted to spend more time with her daughter. The Walnut Creek native and resident has spent most of the last two decades working as a bartender, mixologist, and beverage director at a collection of high-end San Francisco institutions, from Michael Mina and Mourad to the Virgin and Beacon Grand hotels. 

“I’ve worked five nights a week for her whole life,” she says. “So that’s part of it: creating a world where she and I can coexist, because I missed a lot of her first four years.”

![Ruby-Lou’s-\(2\);-Courtesy-Ruby-Lou’s.gif](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Ruby Lou's

Her plans accelerated when she learned of the closure of Skipolini’s, a casual, family-friendly downtown Walnut Creek pizzeria with a kids’ play area where she and her husband Tamir (who owns Bull Valley Roadhouse in Port Costa) would often take their daughter for an easy family meal. Benshalom realized there weren’t really any other venues downtown that catered specifically and creatively to parents with younger kids. So she pulled the trigger on taking over the former Buon Vino wine bar space just off North Main Street with the idea of doing just that, but through a contemporary lens.

And then some. Benshalom—who studied set design in college and embraces a modern DIY design ethos—completely transformed the interior, packing it with an exploding rainbow of playful furniture (cookie couches), Pop Art–esque paintings and posters (“Too much? Never” declares one), and even a 10-foot gumball machine she snagged from a closing sale at Party City. For kids, there are game tables with built-in board games courtesy of local artist Todd Betts, a KerPlunk Corral with oversized KerPlunk games sourced from Facebook Marketplace, and a party room that will feature a sensory activities table and claw vending machines along one wall. She also plans to eventually do kids’ tea parties and install a full-on video arcade game room (which would be closed off and soundproofed from the rest of the interior). For adults, there will be a full bar with signature cocktails, including a spin on a hot toddy inspired by Mourad’s popular Moroccan mint tea service with whiskey, spiced honey, lemon, crème de menthe, and Jenwey tea. There is also a “Queen’s Quarters” section overlooking the street, which Benshalom envisions as a Happy Hour destination “for people who want to see and be seen.” With a vintage elementary bake sale motif, the colorful front bar will serve as pickup point for both drinks and elevated-casual menu items that will include crispy nachos with slow-braised beef short rib and charred citrus–thyme crème fraiche, and a Snake River Farms Wagyu beef “haute dog” in a pretzel bun topped with popping mustard pearls. And, of course, a stacked ice cream sundae with whipped cream and chocolate chunks for dessert. Opening hours will reflect Ruby Lou’s multigenerational approach (and its working tagline of “Family by day—Fabulous by night!”), serving all ages until 8 p.m. before transitioning to 21-plus from 8 p.m. until close.

![Ruby-Lou’s-\(3\);-Courtesy-Ruby-Lou’s.gif](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Ruby Lou’s

But the ultimate goal at Ruby Lou’s is get families off screens to “create space where people will do activities together; that will be really healthy for community building and memory creating.”

As for the opening, Benshalom is aiming for a soft launch later in December with an official debut in the new year. In the meantime, Ruby Lou’s eclectic concept and design has attracted plenty of attention from passersby, as well as on her Instagram page, where she posts frequent updates.

“Creativity is kind of my thing,” she says. “People have come in here, and they tell me there’s nothing like this in Walnut Creek—or anywhere, really.”

### Brava Owners Debut Planta Coffee House in Downtown Pleasanton

The owners of [**Brava Garden Eatery**](https://www.bravagardeneatery.com) are expanding in downtown Pleasanton. The young husband-wife team of **German Carlucci** and **Yanina Calamari** just debuted [**Planta Coffee House and Deli**](https://www.instagram.com/plantacoffeehouse)**,** a daytime café and sandwich shop that, like Brava located a couple of doors down, merges their Italian and Argentinian roots. Occupying a bright, contemporary space at 700 Main Street, Planta offers espresso drinks (made on an Italian La Marzocco espresso machine), salads, homemade empanadas and Argentinian pastries such as _medialunas,_ and sandwiches on fresh-made bread that include mortadella, crema de pistachio, and stracciatella cheese on focaccia and homecooked turkey with lemon aioli, pickles, and mustard on ciabatta. In 2019, the couple (and now parents of two young children) opened Brava, known for its charming outdoor patio dining and menu that mixes elements of Carlucci’s native Italy—homemade pastas—and Calamari’s native Buenos Aires, such as empanadas, Argentinian steak, and _milanesa_ breaded cutlets. For Planta, they saw an opportunity to fill a void in the downtown Pleasanton daytime dining scene.

![Planta-\(2\);-Courtesy-Planta.gif](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Courtesy of Planta

“There’s really no sandwich shop here in downtown, so we thought it would be a great addition to the city,” says Carlucci. “People seem to really like it so far.”

Planta is open daily from 7 a.m. to 3 p.m. 

### More Action in Downtown Pleasanton

That’s not all that’s happening in downtown Pleasanton. Upscale South Bay restaurant Mantra India has long advertised on its website that it was opening **Mantra India Rooftop Restaurant and Bar** at the newly developed property at 725 Main Street (previously home to Union Jack Pub). In a promising sign suggesting actual progress, the restaurant was just issued a full ABC liquor license from the state. Meanwhile, **Primrose Bakery** and **Oyo** restaurant have recently expanded (with the latter rumored to be looking into a separate project downtown), Danville’s **Domenico’s Delicatessen** announced its intention to open a second sandwich shop at 201 Main Street, and an Afghan fusion restaurant with full bar is reportedly taking over a long-vacant corner spot at 401 Main.

### Pleasanton Teen Pizza Queen

And more Pleasanton news: A big congratulations to local teenager **Zoe Zabrowski** , who was crowned national champion at the Young Pizza Makers Competition, a national contest held at the Florida Restaurant Show/Pizza Tomorrow Summit in Orlando. Perhaps it shouldn’t be a surprise considering Zabrowski is the daughter of Mark and Kira Zabrowski, who run celebrated Pleasanton pizzeria [**Much Ado About Pizza**](https://www.muchadoaboutpizzaca.com). And for those curious about that prize-winning pie, Zoe’s “Pig in Paradise” (which appears to mix pork with roasted mango) will soon make an appearance on Much Ado’s menu as a limited-time Spotlight Pizza.
