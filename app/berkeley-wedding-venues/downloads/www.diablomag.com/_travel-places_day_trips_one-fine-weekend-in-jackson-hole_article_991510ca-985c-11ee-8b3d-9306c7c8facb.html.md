With its rugged peaks, expert terrain, and stellar snowfall, **Jackson Hole** is a favorite among snow enthusiasts. Set in northwest Wyoming, between the Teton and Gros Ventre mountain ranges (a stone’s throw from the Idaho border), the 48-mile-long and 15-mile-wide valley boasts a trio of bustling ski resorts.

### Animal Attraction

But Jackson Hole isn’t just about playing in the snow. Home to **Grand Teton National Park** and about 60 miles from **Yellowstone National Park** ’s southern entrance, the area is a wildlife lover’s paradise. An overwhelming majority of the land not within national park boundaries is wildlife refuge, forest, or some other form of public space.

While grizzly and black bears sleep their way through the coldest months of the year, the presence of myriad other animals and a sparkling white backdrop can aid sighting possibilities. In winter, some 7,000 elk migrate into the **National Elk Refuge,** a nearly 25,000-acre sanctuary bordering the town of **Jackson. Jackson Hole Wildlife Safaris** customizes tours based on visitors’ interests, weather, and wildlife sighting reports. The list of creatures that call this area their winter home is staggering—along with bison, moose, and coyotes, it’s not unusual to catch a glimpse of trumpeter swans, eagles, and even wolves.

“Nobody gets bored with wolves,” says Lee Ramella, a naturalist with Jackson Hole Wildlife Safaris. “It is an animal of myth and legend.”

### Welcome to the Wolf Pack

For those set on spotting wolves, the **Four Seasons Resort and Residences Jackson Hole** , along with Jackson Hole Wildlife Safaris and **Yellowstone Safari Company,** offers a meticulously planned, day-long adventure to see the elusive gray wolf population of Yellowstone National Park. Offered October through May, the once-in-a-lifetime outing begins with a private plane flight over the greater Yellowstone basin. On a clear day, early risers are rewarded with a bird’s-eye view of the park’s landmark features, including the **Grand Prismatic Spring.**

As you make your way through the Roosevelt Arch into Yellowstone, possibly passing bison, elk, and moose, other guides are already on the ground in the Lamar Valley, tracking gray wolves in an effort to boost spotting success. In the mid-1990s, 41 wild wolves from Canada and northwest Montana were reintroduced in the park. More than 25 years later, around 100 of the estimated 500-plus wolves in the Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem remain within the national park, making the stretch of land one of the best-known places in the world to consistently see these animals in the wild. With luck, you will spend your day watching wolves in their natural habitat while learning about their importance in the region’s ecosystem.

### Best in Snow

After a scenic return flight from Yellowstone, continue your deep dive into winter in the Old West at the Four Seasons Resort. Slope-side access to **Jackson Hole Mountain Resort** and après-ski cocktails at **Fahrenheit Forty-Seven** champagne bar are benefits of a stay in one of the Four Seasons’s 155 rooms and suites.

Visitors preferring to spend the day in secluded pockets of snow can ditch the skis and strap on snowshoes instead (no special skills required). Jaw-dropping views of the Teton Range and Gros Ventre mountains can be found along the **Taggart Lake Trailhead** in Grand Teton National Park. Naturalist-guided tours with the **Hole Hiking Experience** are a great introduction to snowshoeing, and they highlight countless panoramas and wildlife tracks on trails.

### Exploring Jackson

Jackson, Jackson Hole’s main town, looks like the setting of a Hollywood western. Arches constructed of elk antlers on each of the four corners of **George Washington Memorial Park,** commonly known as Jackson’s Town Square, make a worthy photo stop for visitors. (Elk shed their antlers annually, so no animals were hurt to build the arches.) Just across the street, under the glowing neon lights of the **Million Dollar Cowboy Bar,** a line of folks can be seen patiently waiting to step inside the honky-tonk. The landmark watering hole features western dancing, live music, and an Instagram-worthy bar lined with stools topped by horse saddles.

Pedestrian-friendly Jackson is the type of place where you’ll want time to wander, whether it’s shopping for locally crafted jewelry and keepsakes from **Made,** sipping on a milkshake while seated at the soda fountain at **Jackson Drug,** or sampling a glass of pinot noir from the **Jackson Hole Winery** tasting room, found at the back of **New West KnifeWorks** store and gallery.

![DM2312_009_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Kick up your heels at Jackson’s Million Dollar Cowboy Bar.

Courtesy of Visit Jackson Hole/@erica.b.photo.co

**Bin22** bottle shop stocks hundreds of bottles of vino from around the world. Sit at one of the community tables at the adjoining restaurant, where you can sip and swirl with no corkage fee. Beer and cocktails are also available alongside a Spanish- and Italian-style tapas menu, which includes dishes such as paella and patatas bravas served with chipotle-cilantro aioli.

Downtown Jackson is about a 20-minute drive from **Jackson Hole Airport;** East Bay travelers can take direct flights from San Francisco International Airport (flying time is a bit over two hours). Jackson Hole Airport is the only airport in the United States located inside a national park, so try to get a window seat. You’ll begin your adventure excited and head home with a stunning incentive to plan another stay.
