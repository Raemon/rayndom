## ➳ Tastes of Oahu

**Hawaii’s most populous island is a crossroads for culinary trends from the east and west as well as those bubbling up from its own diverse population. The latest waves include memorable takes on Mediterranean food, artisanal baked goods, and an appreciation of island roots.**

By Jeanne Cooper

![DM2503_005_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Arden Waikiki at Lotus Honolulu Hotel at Diamond Head serves artful Asian-influenced fare.

Arden Waikiki

### Delectable Waikiki

In Waikiki, be sure to sample the creamy uni (sea urchin) pasta and caprese salad with tomato gelato at La Bettola Waikiki, the first United States outpost from Japan’s virtuoso of Italian cuisine, chef Tsutomu Ochiai. The ambience is also distinctive, since La Bettola sits next to the 280,000-gallon aquarium inside the Alohilani Resort Waikiki Beach.

Across Waikiki at the Romer Waikiki at the Ambassador, the cheerily informal Favorite Son serves island-Italian food, including cheese-laden square pizzas, hearty hoagies, and bowls with pasta, farro, or orzo bases and a variety of toppings. Fun fact: Executive chef Chris Ramos began his career as a pastry assistant at Va de Vi Bistro and Wine Bar in Walnut Creek, with stints at Kitchen Door in Napa, Ciccio in Yountville, and the Culinary Institute of America at Greystone before moving to Oahu.

Hidden on the second floor of Lotus Honolulu Hotel at Diamond Head, Arden Waikiki has been wowing residents and visitors alike with its elegant and modern interpretations of Hawaii’s Asian-influenced local cuisine, sourced from island ranches and farms. Chefs Makoto Ono and Amanda Cheng also design enticing menus for holiday brunches, with dishes such as chicken _katsu_ with corn bread and chili crunch hot honey and furikake home fries.

Speaking of breakfast fare, Peter Merriman’s oceanfront Monkeypod Kitchen at the Outrigger Reef Waikiki Beach Resort won’t force you to choose between flapjacks made with island blueberries, ricotta, and passion fruit butter or a mai tai version with Kula rum butter syrup, honey lilikoi (passion fruit) foam, and Maui pineapple—just ask for one of each. You’ll also want to choose more than one croissant or _kouign-amann_ from the nearby Halekulani Bakery, under the direction of executive pastry chef Nassim Bounoua.

Some of the most coveted seats in Waikiki are the dozen available at 1 p.m. each Saturday for afternoon tea at Mugen, inside Espacio the Jewel of Waikiki. Service begins with a glass of rosé and a caviar amuse-bouche, then progresses with a platter of gourmet tea sandwiches, mini-pastries presented in hanging baubles, and buttery scones, with a choice of international and local tea blends.

![DM2503_011_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Enjoy baked treats at Mille Fête.

Sean Marrs

### Farther Afield

Outside Waikiki, mother-and-daughter chefs Nili Yildirim and Ahu Hettema beckon diners to Istanbul Hawaii in urban Kakaako with their uniquely island-influenced Turkish menu. Indulge in a lamb shank tagine, _imam bayildi_ (tomato-stuffed eggplant), and a meze platter, or just order a multi-dish “feast.”

In Chinatown, James Beard Award–winning chef Robynne Maii and her restaurateur husband Chuck Bussler of Fête restaurant have partnered with New York pastry chef Katherine Yang of Gigi Blue on a new bakery and wine bar, Mille Fête. It debuted in February with an array of sweet and savory baked goods, benefiting from local ingredients such as Laie vanilla, lilikoi, ahi confit, and kalua pork.

![DM2503_008_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Chef Claudette Zepeda serves dishes at the Hawaii Food and Wine Festival.

Tom Takata

### Celebrating Local Foods

Oahu is home to the most events in the multi-island Hawaii Food and Wine Festival. It’s worth planning a trip for the 15th edition, October 30 through November 2, especially for the Roots program, which honors indigenous food from around the world, including Hawaii.

A visit to Oahu is not complete without sampling locally made goods, and one not-to-be-missed stop is Sweet Land Farm, on the rural North Shore. Sweet Land Farm sells goat milk products, including luscious goat milk caramel, at its farm store in Waialua. You’ll also find Sweet Land Farm items and other Oahu-made treats at the House of Mana Up stores in Honolulu’s Ward Village and Waikiki’s Royal Hawaiian Center.

## ➳ Mouthwatering Maui

**Known as the Valley Isle for its rich agricultural land flanked by two major mountains, Maui is popular with world-class chefs who operate everywhere from five-star resorts to tucked-away strip malls. And with almost 900,000 total agricultural acres, Maui has become synonymous with farm-to-table cuisine.**

By Ginny Prior

![DM2503_006_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Ulu Kitchen, in the Westin Maui Resort and Spa Ka‘anapali, features locally sourced ingredients in a gorgeous setting.

Westin Maui Resort and Spa Ka‘anapali

### Resort Dining and Star Chefs

One of Maui’s newest celebrity-chef-driven restaurants is Ulu Kitchen, located in the Westin Maui Resort and Spa Ka‘anapali on Maui’s northwest coast. Chef Peter Merriman is a passionate advocate for cooking with local, sustainably sourced ingredients, and he now brings his celebrated creative flair to Ulu Kitchen. One of Merriman’s signature restaurants (he has others on Oahu, Kauai, and the Big Island), Ulu Kitchen features creative twists on classic island fare like Hawaiian Chilaquiles with kalua pork and house-made ancho chili salsa. Grilled Tako Escargot is a fresh take on a traditional offering with tako (octopus), served alongside ahi tuna sashimi and coconut corn chowder.

Another popular beachfront restaurant, this one owned by Iron Chef and sushi master Masaharu Morimoto, draws visitors to Andaz Maui at Wailea Resort. Morimoto Maui is a modern Japanese beach house with 180-degree ocean views and a sleek modern aesthetic. The menu features a tantalizing selection of sushi and sashimi, all artistically presented. Diners who opt for a dish cooked table side—such as Ishi Yaki Buri Bop, which is yellowtail or _kakuni_ on rice in a hot stone bowl—get an up-close look at the skill and precision used in the preparation of their meal.

Other star-powered restaurants include Spago in Four Seasons Resort Maui at Wailea with master chef Wolfgang Puck, which features dishes like Macadamia-Nut-Crusted Mahi Mahi and Seared Big Eye Tuna “Tataki.” Another beloved culinary celebrity with local roots is _Top Chef_ alum Lee Anne Wong. While Wong unfortunately lost her popular Papa’aina restaurant in the 2023 Lahaina wildfire, her legions of fans are hoping she will rebuild.

![DM2503_013_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Star chef Sheldon Simeon whips up irresistible meals at Tin Roof in Kahului.

Kevin J. Miyazaki

### Indie Gems

An unassuming island hot spot is Tin Roof in Kahului, operating out of a tiny take-out space that looks like a neighborhood pop-up shop. Raised in Hilo, Hawaii, chef Sheldon Simeon is a two-time _Top Chef_ finalist who uses fresh island offerings to make his poke bowls, Mochiko Chicken, and the Beet Box with roasted, then flash-fried, beets. An outside-the-box take on a simple salad uses locally grown kale tossed in house-made Maui onion dressing, topped with dried craisins, Maui onion potato chips, and crushed kimchi peanuts.

Simeon’s cookbook, _Cook Real Hawaii,_ coauthored by Garrett Snyder, features 100 recipes that celebrate the many cultures represented in island cuisine, including influences from China, Japan, Korea, Portugal, and the Philippines.

Foodies also love Maui microgreen grower Hua Momona Farms, run by executive chef Zach Laidlaw. Laidlaw was a finalist on Gordon Ramsay’s _Next Level Chef_ and spends much of his time promoting sustainable agriculture on Maui.

### Culinary Events

Maui holds several food-driven events annually, including the East Maui Taro Festival in April and the prestigious Kapalua Wine and Food Festival held each summer.

The Hawaiian Airlines Made in Maui County Festival in November is another popular event, where dozens of artisans sell specialty foods and other products. Recent participants included Hi Spice hot sauces, Haleakala Creamery, Hawaiian Sweets, the Maui Cookie Lady (an island boutique bakery), Hula Cookies, Surfing Goat Dairy, Maui Bone Broth Company, Maui Chili Chili Oil, Maui Coffee Roasters, Maui Ku’ia Estate chocolate, and Maui Gold Pineapple Company.

## ➳ Big Island Bounty

**The diverse ecosystems and population of the island of Hawaii, nicknamed the Big Island, provide a homegrown smorgasbord. Yet with farm-raised abalone, lobster, and oysters supplementing the abundant local seafood, it is easy to see why new sushi experiences often create the most excitement.** —J.C.

### Sake and Sushi

One of the rare women to earn the title of master sake brewer, Chiaki Takahashi opened the Islander Sake Brewery at the Hapuna Golf Course at the Mauna Kea Resort in 2022. Initially offering just tastings and later a monthly full-moon dinner, Takahashi and partner Tama Hirose now serve an elegantly plated prix fixe Japanese dinner six nights a week, with two seatings in the brewery’s Engawa restaurant. Most of the menu changes monthly, but it always includes a glass of her Junmai Daiginjo sake and seven small plates; dessert is a scoop of sake kasu ice cream, made from the aromatic leftovers of sake fermentation.

![DM2503_007_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Martin Yan presented unique dishes at the 2024 Hawaii Food and Wine Festival.

Hawaii Food and Wine Festival

The sushi bar at Ulu restaurant at Four Seasons Resort Hualalai has always had a sterling reputation. Now it has been spun off into its own sushi and omakase restaurant, Noio, above the fully remodeled Ulu. Sushi chef Nuri Piccio’s new menu includes delicacies such as _dobin mushi,_ a tea of snapper fins with yuzu; _otsumami,_ a variety of Japanese snacks; and a selection of rare game from Waimea Butcher Shop.

### Going First Class in Hilo

For hearty “local food”—a mix of Hawaiian, Japanese, Chinese, Korean, Filipino, and Portuguese favorites—and moderately priced seafood and sushi, you can’t go wrong in Hilo. But you’d be remiss in thinking it has no fine dining. Chef Mark Pomaski and wife Soni Pomaski, a craft cocktail expert, have steadily lured residents to their intimate downtown restaurant, Moon and Turtle, since its opening in 2013 with a heady blend of island favorites with a twist (silky ahi sashimi with kiawe-smoked soy sauce) and continental dishes with local ingredients (risotto with Hamakua Alii mushrooms, macadamia nut pot de crème).

![DM2503_048_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Mark Pomaski of the Big Island’s Moon and Turtle restaurant is one of Hawaiian Airlines’ featured chefs.

Hawaiian Airlines

Now Moon and Turtle is drawing wider notice, thanks to Hawaiian Airlines’ new showcase of chef Mark’s cuisine in its first-class cabins, and his appearance with Martin Yan, Brandon Jew, and other top Asian American chefs at the Big Island’s part of the recent Hawaii Food and Wine Festival, held at Mauna Kea Beach Hotel. While the chef list has yet to be set, mark your calendar for the next edition on October 18, and book well ahead for a table at Moon and Turtle.

![DM2503_010_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Kona Sea Salt farm is a must-visit on the Big Island.

Kona Sea Salt Farm

### Flavorful Farm Tours

For some of the best edible souvenirs, take a farm tour that combines tastings with insights into the island of Hawaii’s unique environment and the culinary techniques that enhance the island’s bounty.

Believed to be the country’s first commercial grower of vanilla, the Hawaiian Vanilla Co. offers tours of its hillside farm on the lush Hamakua Coast. Choose a tour paired with a luncheon or culinary tasting, or enjoy an afternoon tea where vanilla shines in every sweet and savory confection as well as several tea blends. Besides beans and extract, the store carries vanilla mango chutney, vanilla honey pepper pecans, coffee, tea, and other vanilla-scented products.

On an island with a long history of cacao growing and chocolate making, Honokaa Chocolate has the best selection of single-origin chocolates and distinctive island flavors, including dark and milk chocolate infused with barrel-aged rum from local Kuleana Rum Works. Led by founder Mike Pollard, the two-hour VIP Cacao Farm Tour and Chocolate Tasting includes a walk through the cacao orchard and tasting of nine chocolates, followed by a visit to the retail store and production facility in quaint downtown Honokaa, a mile from the farm.

At the oceanfront Kona Sea Salt farm, guides will explain the ancient and modern methods of making salt by evaporating seawater. You’ll sample the delicately crystallized salt flakes and a variety of salt blends sprinkled on pineapple, cucumbers, and cherry tomatoes, available to purchase in single bottles or gift sets.
