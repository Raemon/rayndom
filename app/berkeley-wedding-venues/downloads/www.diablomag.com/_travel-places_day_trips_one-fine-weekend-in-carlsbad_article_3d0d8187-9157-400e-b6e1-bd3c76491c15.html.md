On a stretch of coastline often skipped over on the way to or from San Diego, **Carlsbad** is a mellow and welcoming destination. Famous for its **Flower Fields at Carlsbad Ranch,** a 55-acre farm of giant tecolote ranunculus that bloom March through May, the “Village by the Sea” is perfect for a spring visit.

With direct one-and-a-half-hour flights to San Diego from Oakland Airport, the trip is an easy one for visitors from the East Bay. Grab a rental car at the airport for the 40-minute-or-so drive to Carlsbad, or jump on the Coaster commuter train, which has two stops in Carlsbad and lasts about an hour.

### It Takes a Village

For a transportation-light visit, consider staying at the recently renovated **Beach Terrace.** This one-time apartment motel has been transformed into a chic hotel, with spacious rooms, a prime location on the beach, and amenities such as a daily breakfast spread, in-room yoga kits, woolly surf ponchos, and a pillow menu featuring seven varieties on which to lay your head.

Some of the most beautiful beaches in San Diego County are nearby. Perhaps best known is the secluded **South Carlsbad State Beach,** but ocean lovers will also appreciate **Tamarack State Beach, South Ponto Beach,** and **Terramar Beach,** with its photogenic cliffs.

The neighborhood surrounding Beach Terrace is very walkable, and the hotel offers free cruiser bike rentals to guests. Start at nearby **Carlsbad Village,** a quaint district that is the pulse point of the town, with plenty of shopping, art galleries, charming buildings, and an expanding collection of excellent restaurants.

It’s easy to explore this historic area through your stomach. An assemblage of whitewashed cottages, aptly called the **Cottages,** is home to several establishments featuring enticing bites and sips. Start at **Shoots Fish and Beer,** an outpost by the popular Oceanside brewery collective done in collaboration with sustainable seafood champion and chef Davin Waite. You’ll find tasty seafood tacos and ceviche, along with great beer on tap.

**Pop Pie Co.** serves personal pot pies in a variety of flavors—all baked fresh, with a buttery aroma that draws passersby into the shop. There’s also a selection of sweet pies available by the slice, as a whole pie, or in hand pie form, with a few à la mode menu options served with craft ice cream from next-door neighbor **Stella Jean’s.** If you simply want a scoop, order Stella’s seasonal flavors like calamansi creamsicle or strawberry oat crumble.

For more adult refreshments, head to **Same Same.** Opened in 2022, this vibrant bar is serious about its cocktail program and serves a selection of Thai-inspired bites. Pair the Hail Sagan cocktail (tiki gin and syrup made with stargazer lily) with Thai chicken wings or spicy fingerling potatoes.

![DM2505_082_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

Wildland’s rustic menu draws from the coastal culinary fare of the Mediterranean.

Courtesy of Visit Carlsbad

Given the diminutive size of Carlsbad, the high-caliber dining scene here might seem surprising. Much of that quality comes from the team behind **Campfire,** a local favorite for its live fire cooking, energetic atmosphere, and memorable dishes; make sure to order the whole broccoli with chermoula, lime, and peanuts. Campfire executive chef Eric Bost is also the culinary mastermind behind **Jeune et Jolie,** a French bistro–inspired sister spot on State Street that opened in 2018. Buzzy yet sophisticated, Jeune et Jolie is noteworthy in all aspects, from its prix fixe menu to its cocktails (try the Swizzle) to its gorgeous design. Recognized with a Michelin star in 2021, the service-oriented eatery feels like a polished city restaurant with a beach town vibe.

The newest member of the culinary family is **Wildland,** which opened this past winter. The all-day restaurant is a bakery, coffee shop, and date-night destination with a rustic, Mediterranean-inspired menu centered on supreme ingredients. The large establishment could feel cavernous if not for its glowing candlelight, cool soundtrack, and clean design. Another new venture is **Lilo,** an intimate 22-seat chef’s counter experience located behind Wildland.

### Flower Power

Yes, Carlsbad is home to **Legoland California Resort** theme park, but another top draw is the Flower Fields at Carlsbad Ranch. Spend a couple of hours in the fields, which are full of every color of ranunculus you can imagine, and the adjacent gardens (advance tickets are required). The attraction is also hosting the _Glass in Flight_ exhibit by artist Alex Heveri, who has installed 38 stunning Dalle de Verre glass and steel pieces throughout the property.

The ranch features new programming every year, and the 2025 season welcomes the Butterfly Encounter, where you can walk among hundreds of butterflies. Nearby is the fragrant sweet pea maze and a greenhouse full of cymbidium orchids. Group sales and foundation director Joni Miringoff says that Tom Ford’s Black Orchid perfume was the result of a special cymbidium hybridization that took place at the Flower Fields.

![DM2505_084_DIG.jpg](data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAQAAAADCAQAAAAe/WZNAAAAEElEQVR42mM8U88ABowYDABAxQPltt5zqAAAAABJRU5ErkJggg==)

A bevy of shops, galleries, and restaurants draw visitors to Carlsbad Village.

Courtesy of Visit Carlsbad

Learn more about the history of the region and grab a bite to eat on the award-winning **Carlsbad Food Tours.** While introducing participants to yummy discoveries at spots like the **Goods** donuts and **Pure Project** brewing, lead guide David Jenson shares local lore, including the intriguing history of how Carlsbad came to be a town. Fittingly, “a lot of Carlsbad’s history is about flowers,” says Jenson. Also, this place “has always been defined by our relationship with water,” he adds.

The natural mineral water here was initially discovered in the late 1800s by sea captain John Frazier, who sold it to train travelers passing through town. A luxury spa is currently under construction in the spot where the original 1800s Carlsbad Hotel once stood. The future **Carlsbad Mineral Springs Health Resort** will maximize these healing waters, similar to its counterparts in Calistoga or Saratoga Springs.

Before packing up to head home, enjoy a loaded avocado toast or green juice off the Beach Terrace’s “brunchy” menu; order ahead for delivery to your room in the morning. Or walk up the street to the hidden **Interim Coffee,** a true roaster’s vision in a cubby-sized spot. Stroll on the beach path with your cappuccino, and soak up the salty air and Southern California sun.
