Splatter celebrates the variety of cultural traditions present in the Tri-Valley community.

Courtesy of the City of Dublin

Dublin’s Splatter festival is a favorite tradition for many, perfect for celebrating the people and cultures that make up the Tri-Valley. On September 13, the free festivities return to Emerald Glen Park, showcasing more art and open-air entertainment than ever before.

Splatter was first held in 2011 and saw more than 28,000 attendees last year. Guests can shop in the World Craft Bazaar, interact with art experiences and roaming performers, and close out the night with a dance party. This year, Dublin plans to expand the kids’ games area and emphasize even more arts and crafts.

“Luckily for us, Dublin has such a diverse community, so we really encourage feedback and recommendations,” says recreation coordinator Shelby Perry. “We also [feature] a lot of diverse community, performance, and theater groups.”

Local eateries and food trucks are on hand to serve up a variety of cuisines during the event, and the city collaborates with the Rotary Club of Dublin to spotlight Livermore Valley wineries and local breweries. Splatter also includes a trio of stages, with a particular emphasis on multicultural music and dance programming. “The performan­ces throughout the day are phenomenal, and I look forward to all of them because they all are so different,” says Perry. “But I do look forward to Boombox Heroes closing the night and the drone show after.

“A really big thing with any of our Dublin events is that everyone just feels included,” she adds. “Dublin really comes together, and [I hope people] feel that community aspect.”
