#  Discover The Best Tasting Water with Virtual Water Tasting 

April 12, 2021 

You drink water everyday, but have you ever really thought about how it tastes? Noticed the flavor, the mouthfeel, how it smells? 

It‚Äôs easy to think that the way water tastes isn‚Äôt all that special‚Äî in fact, the best tasting water shouldn‚Äôt taste like anything at all. But that doesn‚Äôt mean that you can‚Äôt treat water with as much respect as a fine wine. How? ## **Where does drinking water come from?**

[![fjord-where-best-tasting-water-comes-from](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/5/2021/04/best-tasting-water-fjord.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/5/2021/04/best-tasting-water-fjord.jpg)

Before you embark on your Virtual Water Tasting, let‚Äôs talk about the source of your drinking water. Just like a wine‚Äôs terroir, a water‚Äôs location impacts the flavor profile. If images of glassy pools surrounded by a verdant forest are coming to mind, well, you‚Äôre not wrong. However, from Spanish geysers to glaciers in Norway, water sources can vary. 

Like from a 5,000 year old glacier! 

Yup, [Isbre](https://www.isbrewater.no/en/the-source.aspx) prides itself on sourcing some of the world‚Äôs purest spring water from the Hardanger fjord in Ulvik, Norway. Since the water comes from a glacier surrounded by mountains, trees, fjords, and waterfalls, and isn‚Äôt impacted by any negative environmental factors, it means the levels of minerals, salt, and metal compounds are low. So the flavor is pure, clean, and crisp. Pretty cool when you think about drinking water from a 5,000 year old glacier! 

And we‚Äôd be remiss if we didn‚Äôt mention France, home of more than [200 brands of bottled waters.](http://www.finewaters.com/bottled-waters-of-the-world/france) Surely you‚Äôve heard of Perrier, but did you know that it comes from a naturally-carbonated spring in southern France? Although the water is purified and then carbonated once more before it makes it to your bottle, this spring isn‚Äôt some recent discovery. The Romans used it as a spa, recognizing the power of mineral water back in the early days. Just like drinking from a glacier, drinking from a spring holds more history in your bottle than you realize. In your Avital virtual water tasting, we send you waters from around the world to get the full experience. 

## **How do you taste water?**

[![doing-a-water-tasting](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20700%20467'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![doing-a-water-tasting](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/5/2021/04/how-to-taste-best-tasting-water.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/5/2021/04/how-to-taste-best-tasting-water.jpg)

Whether you‚Äôre drinking your water from the Mediterranean or Patagonia, you‚Äôll need to understand what the best tasting water should taste like‚Äî nothing! That doesn‚Äôt mean it won‚Äôt have any flavor, as water tasting experts will tell you. Surely you‚Äôve noticed a difference just from traveling from one city to another in a single glass of tap water! We won‚Äôt get into it, but there‚Äôs a reason why some New York bagelmakers insist on _only_ using New York City tap water. 

So what are you tasting for? First, you should give it a smell. Odors can impact water taste because a lot of your sensory experience from eating and drinking comes from smell. Give your water a sniff, and notice if it‚Äôs fragrant, flowery, or fruity. On the opposite end of the spectrum, notice if it‚Äôs fishy, musty, or even rancid. Picking up notes of chlorine? Now you‚Äôre on your way to discovering better tasting water.
