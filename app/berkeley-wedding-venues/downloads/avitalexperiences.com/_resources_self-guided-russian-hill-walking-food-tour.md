#  Self-Guided Russian Hill Walking Food Tour 

October 7, 2015 

Every neighborhood in San Francisco is filled with taste treats, well-respected restaurants, and bustling bars, but I have a special place in my heart for Russian Hill, the hood I have called home for the past five years. The Russian Hill neighborhood extends from Columbus Avenue up the hill and back down it to the main faraway, Van Ness. Broadway and North Point Streets are the Southern and Northern boarder streets. However, there are a few delicious spots on this walking food tour that aren‚Äôt technically within this parameter, but no matter. You‚Äôll most definitely enjoy the good eats at all of the following places.

Assuming that you will partake in food or drink at each of the stops, our self-guided walking tour will take several hours. Start in the mid-afternoon and walk your way through appetizers, dinner, and dessert. It will visit five distinct establishments ‚Äî four restaurants and one iconic ice cream parlor. No walk is more than twenty minutes long.

  1. Swan Oyster Depot
  2. La Folie Restaurant & Lounge
  3. Union Larder
  4. Stones Throw
  5. Swensen‚Äôs Ice Cream



[![Swan Oyster Depot in Russian Hill San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/01.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/01.jpg)

**1) Swan Oyster Depot**

Swan Oyster Depot is a San Francisco institution that has been serving locals and tourists fresh-shucked oysters for over 100 years. If you‚Äôve ever watched any of Anthony Bourdain‚Äôs culinary travel shows, you‚Äôll definably remember Swan; every time he comes to the city by the bay, he enjoys a platter of seafood at the oyster house. The depot specializes in the freshest seafood around, from oysters on the half shell to cracked Dungeness crab. The menu is basic ‚Äî it‚Äôs all fresh raw seafood with a few smoked fishes dishes and salads ‚Äî and the San Francisco treat, clam chowder thrown in for good measure. Note that there‚Äôs almost always a line at Swan, so come prepared to wait. When you get close to the front you can order a pint of Anchor Stream or glass of bubbles while you wait for a spot to clear at the counter. The atmosphere is unpretentious and casual. There‚Äôs no frills, the walls are covered in old photos and newspaper clippings, and the staff works with military precision, but it‚Äôs some of the best seafood you‚Äôll ever taste. Address: Phone: 1517 Polk Street (in between California and Sacramento Streets) Phone: (415) 673-2757 http://www.sfswanoysterdepot.com/

[![La Folie Restaurant & Lounge in Russian Hill ](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![La Folie Restaurant & Lounge in Russian Hill ](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/29622_10151299942008256_1717255_n.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/29622_10151299942008256_1717255_n.jpg)

**2) La Folie Restaurant & Lounge**

Twenty seven years ago French native Roland Passot opened La Folie Restaurant & Lounge. It immediately became an award-winning spot for excellent French contemporary cuisine made with local ingredients. Today, the chef serves updated classics, like foie gras with caramelized peaches and spiced peach consomm√© and lobster risotto with mushrooms and corn, on a fixed-price tasting menu. Since this is a walking food tour, you probably won‚Äôt want to eat an entire tasting menu as you‚Äôll be too full to enjoy food at the remaining three stops. However, you can enjoy all that Passot has to offer at the La Folie Lounge. The bartenders at the sexy lounge shake up a mean basil lime gimlet made with New Amsterdam gin and green chartreuse. The extensive wine list is primarily features French varietals and there is even a sparkling pear cider to sip. Snack on Passot‚Äôs addictive truffled popcorn, gougeres with emmental cheese, or burrata with aged balsamic and toasted hazelnuts. Address: 2316 Polk Street (at Green Street) Phone: (415) 776-5577 http://www.lafolie.com

[![Union Larder wine bar in Russian Hill](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Union Larder wine bar in Russian Hill](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/12039209_1060946217251015_7814830501257604768_n.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/12039209_1060946217251015_7814830501257604768_n.jpg)

**3) Union Larder**

At the next stop, Union Larder, you‚Äôll enjoy another glass of vino in an entirely different setting. Walk into Hyde Street‚Äôs recently opened wine bar and shop and you‚Äôll feel like you walked into a modern day hot spot in a European capital. Dried cured meats hang from the ceiling above the open bar. Organized clutter is everywhere you look and the vibe is industrial and unfussy. A long metal bar runs along a wall of windows which over look Hyde Street and the passing cable car line. The seats are backless circular leather stools and the wall opposite the window is covered in a dizzying array of wine and artisinal eats. There‚Äôs black truffle potato chips from Spain, champagne mustard from San Diego, and charcuterie from Oregon. Grab a stool and a glass of wine off the list, which is updated weekly. If you‚Äôre planning a picnic in the next couple of days, stock up on provisions. In need of a snack? Order something decadent and scrumptious like uni pate or gruyere, cantal, and taleggio fondue. Address: 1945 Hyde Street (in between Union and Green Streets) Phone: (415) 272-7567 http://unionlarder.com/

[![Stones Throw Restaurant in Russian Hill San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Stones Throw Restaurant in Russian Hill San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Stones-600x400.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Stones-600x400.jpg)

**4) Stones Throw**

After all this wine, I bet you‚Äôre ready for dinner, right? Head up the street (away from the Bay) on Hyde and you‚Äôll come to a charming restaurant front that is painted a sunny yellow. The small restaurant is a neighborhood hot spot, so if you‚Äôre with a large group, you may have to wait. Grab a beer from the extensive list that includes Spanish pale lager, French cider, and South Carolinian saison among many others. Once you‚Äôre nestled into a cozy table, you‚Äôre in for a real treat. The food at Stones Throw is phenomenal. Start with chef Jason Halverson‚Äôs signature dish, puffed potato and eggs with cauliflower mousse, chives, and crispy chicken skin. It‚Äôs basically a potato croquette filled with an oozing egg yolk, but it‚Äôs mind-blowingly good. The housemade squid ink pasta with clams, calamari, shrimp, and tender greens is a revelation of rich pasta and briny seafood. These items are never taken off of the seasonal menu that changes frequently, so you know they are absolute culinary perfection and definite must orders. . Address: 1896 Hyde Street (at Green Street) Phone: (415) 796-2901 http://stonesthrowsf.com

[![ice cream cone at Swensen‚Äôs in Russian Hill](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![ice cream cone at Swensen‚Äôs in Russian Hill](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/11954703_10152970452061755_3519198652825889590_n.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/11954703_10152970452061755_3519198652825889590_n.jpg)

**5) Swensen‚Äôs Ice Cream**

Resist the urge to order dessert at Stones Throw and instead walk to the final stop on this whirlwind Russian Hill Walking Food Tour, Swenson‚Äôs Ice Cream. Although Swensen‚Äôs dreamy ice cream is served at over twenty locations world wide, the Swensen‚Äôs of Russian Hill was the original and first shop ‚Äî they‚Äôve been slinging scoops of fresh-churned ice cream to the denizens of Russian Hill since 1948. As the first American ice cream parlor to serve all-natural ice cream, Swensen‚Äôs, with it‚Äôs old school menu boards that have to be manually changed by hand, is as classic as it gets. The only problem you‚Äôll encounter while at Swensen‚Äôs is deciding what flavor of ice cream to order. Sticky chewy chocolate or strawberries bananas ‚Äôn cream? Turkish coffee or almond praline delight? There‚Äôs the ideal flavor for everyone, so ask the friendly staff to give you a taste until you‚Äôre really ready to commit to that scoop of macadamia nut ice cream. Address: 1999 Hyde Street (at Union Street) Phone: (415) 775-6818 http://www.swensens.com
