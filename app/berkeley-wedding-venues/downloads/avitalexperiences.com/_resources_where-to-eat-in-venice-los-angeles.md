#  Where to Eat in Venice 

May 22, 2017 

Venice is known for its colorful, character-filled boardwalk, its skateboarding culture, Muscle Beach, and its canals. But Venice Beach, California, is also home to a rapidly growing food scene that was kicked off by Gjelina in 2008, which is arguably one of the hardest tables to get in Los Angeles. It‚Äôs not hard to find a great place to eat in the neighborhood, but choosing where to eat in Venice isn‚Äôt always easy. These five places are where locals love and where visitors shouldn‚Äôt miss either.

## **The Anchor**

[![Where to Eat in Venice: The Anchor](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%202048%201365'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Where to Eat in Venice: The Anchor](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/03/8137902893_0aaa569495_k.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/03/8137902893_0aaa569495_k.jpg)

Lobster rolls are an East Coast food, but owner Kristen Ciccolella of The Anchor has brought them to Venice, put them in a warm Hawaiian roll, and topped them with shaved truffles. And The Anchor serves the rolls up with a side of rose so you can relax and enjoy the taste of the Atlantic Ocean while sitting just a few blocks from the Pacific. If the lobster run roll runs out (which it sometimes does), have the braised octopus, ceviche, or lobster mac and cheese. If you come for brunch, you can even have lobster Benedict. 

235 Main St, Venice, CA 90291  
[**http://theanchorvenice.squarespace.com/**](http://theanchorvenice.squarespace.com/)

## Felix

[![Eat at Felix on Abbot Kinney: Where to Eat In Venice](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201024%20768'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Eat at Felix on Abbot Kinney: Where to Eat In Venice](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/05/3446206082_81ed795d5c_b.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/05/3446206082_81ed795d5c_b.jpg)

Felix is Latin for ‚Äúhappy‚Äù or ‚Äúlucky‚Äù and the people of Venice are lucky that Felix Trattoria recently joined the neighborhood right on Abbot Kinney. The homemade pasta is where Felix really shines. The pasta menu is split into four: Le Paste del Nord, Le Paste del Centro, Le Paste delle Isole, and Le Paste del Mezzogiorno. Hearty pasta like gnocci with oxtail ragu and pappardelle with ragu bolognese are standouts. There‚Äôs also Italian staples like pizzas with mozzarella di bufala and pork meatballs. And don‚Äôt forget the antipasti menu. 

1023 Abbot Kinney Blvd, Venice, CA 90291  
<http://felixla.com/>

## **Salt and Straw Ice Cream**

[![Salt and Straw: Where to Eat in Venice](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20640%20640'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Salt and Straw: Where to Eat in Venice](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/05/14539730852_bea9471a7e_o.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/05/14539730852_bea9471a7e_o.jpg)

LA is home to lots of transplants and luckily Salt and Straw is one of them. The ice cream shop came from Portland, Oregon and brought its funky flavors with it. The Abbot Kinney location has LA-specific flavors as well. Try the Black Olive Brittle & Goat Cheese, Honey Lavender, and seasonal flavors like Rhubarb Crumble with Toast Anise. Can‚Äôt decide? Salt and Straw has ice cream flights so you can try four scoops. (And we won‚Äôt judge you if you eat ice cream before dinner..or for dinner.)

1357 Abbot Kinney Blvd, Venice, CA 90291  
<http://saltandstraw.com/la-locations/>

## **Gjusta**

![16505455286_0a2ede0c59_z](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20640%20480'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![16505455286_0a2ede0c59_z](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/05/16505455286_0a2ede0c59_z.jpg)

When sister restaurant Gjelina is too crowded, locals head to neighborhood spot Gjusta for food that‚Äôs just as good. Their photo-worthy fish and egg plate is one of the most Instagrammed dishes in Venice and Gjusta‚Äôs other dishes are just as beautiful. Get flatbreads, burgers, and salads to go, or squeeze into a spot on the back patio. Or use Gjusta as more of a local market and take smoked fish, cheeses, pastries, and pickled provisions home to stock your fridge. 

320 Sunset Ave, Venice, CA 90291  
<http://www.gjusta.com/>

## Abbot Kinney First Fridays

[![Where to eat in Venice: first friday](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201024%20768'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Where to eat in Venice: first friday](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/05/4879969045_4d7f2656c8_b.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/05/4879969045_4d7f2656c8_b.jpg)

If you happen to be in Venice on the first Friday of the month, head over to Abbot Kinney Boulevard for the First Friday celebration. The food truck lineup changes, but it‚Äôs strong each month featuring trucks like Roy Choi‚Äôs Kogi truck, Baby‚Äôs Badass BBQ, and Border Grill. Some of the best meals we‚Äôve had have come from a kitchen on four wheels, and eating on a truck and visiting the art galleries and shops on Abbot Kinney is a great way to kick off the weekend.

Abbot Kinney Blvd.  
<http://abbotkinneyfirstfridays.com/>

## **Rose Cafe and Restaurant**

[![Where to Eat in Venice: Rose Cafe and Restaurant](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%202048%201536'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Where to Eat in Venice: Rose Cafe and Restaurant](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/05/24404697032_87e5f9f57d_k.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/05/24404697032_87e5f9f57d_k.jpg)

Rose Cafe is one of those places where you can get a great avocado toast at brunch (topped with a fried duck egg, pickled Fresno chiles, and pistachios), yellowtail sashimi for dinner, and great croissants and pastries all day long. It‚Äôs casual, but the food is delicious. It‚Äôs not hard to find a winner among dishes like beet spaghetti and scallop crudo. Don‚Äôt skip dessert. Treats like passionfruit mousse with caramelized coconut cream and cherimoya sorbet look at you from the counter and its hard to look away.

220 Rose Ave, Venice, CA 90291,  
[http://rosecafevenice.com/](http://rosecafevenice.com/)
