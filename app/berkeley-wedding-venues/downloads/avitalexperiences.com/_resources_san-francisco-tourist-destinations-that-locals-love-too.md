#  10 San Francisco Tourist Destinations That Locals Love Too 

April 17, 2015 

[![Golden Gate Park in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Golden-Gate-Park.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Golden-Gate-Park.jpg)

It doesn‚Äôt matter who you are, where you‚Äôre from, what you‚Äôve done, or how much you used to love N‚ÄôSync: There‚Äôs a lot to love in San Francisco. Here‚Äôs a 100% local opinion of the Top 10 San Francisco Tourist Destinations that locals love too!

![cocktails at The Alembic](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-The-Alembic.jpg)[![Paxton Gate shop in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20700%20515'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Paxton Gate shop in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Paxton-Gate.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Paxton-Gate.jpg)[![Brunch at Brendas French Soul Food in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20700%20465'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Brunch at Brendas French Soul Food in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Brendas-French-Soul-Food.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Brendas-French-Soul-Food.jpg)

## **1\. Golden Gate Park**

[Golden Gate Park ](http://sfrecpark.org/parks-open-spaces/golden-gate-park-guide/)is a kachina doll, a wonderland with miles and miles of paths like picnicking meadows ([Hellman Hollow](http://sfrecpark.org/destination/golden-gate-park/ggp-helman-hollow-picnic-area/)), a [bison paddock](http://sfrecpark.org/destination/golden-gate-park/the-bison-paddock/), and [a lake with paddle boats for rent](http://sfrecpark.org/destination/golden-gate-park/stow-lake/).

It also has other fantastic attractions, like the [Japanese Tea Garden](http://japaneseteagardensf.com/), the [Botanical Garden](http://www.sfbotanicalgarden.org/), the [California Academy of Sciences](http://www.calacademy.org/), [the DeYoung Museum](https://deyoung.famsf.org/), the [Conservatory of Flowers](http://www.conservatoryofflowers.org/), and a [carousel](http://sfrecpark.org/destination/golden-gate-park/herschell-spillman-carousel/)! Tourist or local, there‚Äôs always more to discover in Golden Gate Park.

_Insider Tip:_ From April 19th through October 11th, there‚Äôs free music at 1:00 pm every Sunday at the [Music Concourse](http://sfrecpark.org/destination/golden-gate-park/music-concourse/), courtesy of the Golden Gate Band.

[![Exploratorium in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20700%20467'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Exploratorium in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Exploratorium.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Exploratorium.jpg)

_Where:_ The park starts roughly at Stanyan and Haight and ends at the ocean. Many attractions are near 9th and Lincoln.

## **2\. The Exploratorium**

[The Exploratorium](http://www.exploratorium.edu/) is the perfect place to nerd out. Combining art, science, and design, this is a destination guaranteed to fascinate both kids and adults.

_Insider Tip:_ On the first Thursday of every month, The Exploratorium has a program called [After Dark](http://www.exploratorium.edu/visit/calendar/after-dark). From 6pm till 10pm, adults can sip on cocktails while playing with gorgeous science experiments. Achieve the dream of looking cool while having fun!

_Where:_ Pier 15, The Embarcadero, San Francisco, CA 94111 (Embarcadero)

## **3\. Cocktails and Bone Marrow at The Alembic**

You made it to San Francisco, one of the top culinary destinations in the world, and you need to eat. [The Alembic](http://www.alembicbar.com/) is a craft cocktail and food destination in the Haight Ashbury Neighborhood where you can sit back and sip on a cocktail creation before having your mind blown by their famous bone marrow.

_Insider Tip:_ Get the summer-flavored Southern Exposure cocktail. It‚Äôs been on their menu since day one.

[![ice cream cone at Bi Rite Creamery](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20700%20499'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![ice cream cone at Bi Rite Creamery](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Bi-Rite-Creamery.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Bi-Rite-Creamery.jpg)

_Where:_ 1725 Haight St, San Francisco, CA 94117 (Haight Ashbury)

## **4\. Ice Cream at Bi-Rite Creamery**

Yes, it‚Äôs completely famous and there‚Äôs always a line around the block, but ice cream at [Bi-Rite Creamery](http://biritecreamery.com/) really is that good. You should definitely try the salted caramel ice cream and while you‚Äôre at it, also go for the malted vanilla with peanut brittle pieces or the ricanelas.

_Insider Tip:_ If you‚Äôre checking out Alamo square, go to the Bi-Rite location on Divisadero and Hayes street where there‚Äôs usually less of a wait.

[![view from Dolores Park in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20800%20533'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![view from Dolores Park in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Dolores-Park.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Dolores-Park.jpg)

_Where:_ 3692 18th St, San Francisco, CA 94110 (Mission) or 550 Divisadero St, San Francisco, CA 94117 (NoPa)

## **5\. The View from Dolores Park**

[Dolores Park](http://sfrecpark.org/destination/mission-dolores-park/) is consistently packed with San Franciscans old and new, and from the southwest corner, you can get a heartbreakingly-beautiful view of downtown San Francisco with the Bay behind it and palm tree-lined Dolores street stretching out in front of you like a California dream.

_Insider Tip:_ Pick up a sandwich from [Ike‚Äôs Place](http://www.ilikeikesplace.com/) and bring it to the park for a delicious picnic. Make sure to bring extra napkins because it‚Äôs going to get messy.

[![Castro Theatre in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20700%20525'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Castro Theatre in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Castro-Theatre.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Castro-Theatre.jpg)

_Where:_ 20th St and Church St (Dolores Heights)

## **6\. Sing-Alongs at the Castro Theater**

[The Castro Theater](http://www.castrotheatre.com/) is pure whimsy and magic. While you can always catch an interesting film or documentary, on special occasions throughout the year you can bust out your vocal chords and sing along to the movies that provided the soundtrack to your childhood. The sing-along is a guided experience with a host, karaoke-style lettering on the screen, and most importantly, a costume contest.

_Insider Tip:_ Get something sinful from [Hot Cookie](https://www.yelp.com/biz/hot-cookie-san-francisco) while you‚Äôre waiting in line to get in to the theater.

_Where:_ 429 Castro St, San Francisco, CA 94114 (Castro)

## **7\. Window Shopping at Paxton Gate**

Were you looking for a tastefully-dressed mouse skeleton or a unicorn head? Of course not, but they sell them here anyways. [Paxton Gate](https://paxtongate.com/) is a delightful purveyor of unique gifts flavored with botany, alchemy, and taxidermy. You‚Äôre guaranteed to find something unique for that friend who‚Äôs . . . a little strange.

_Insider Tip:_ Be sure to check out the [pirate store](http://826valencia.org/) next door, and then head to [Dandelion Chocolate](http://www.dandelionchocolate.com/) for some hand-crafted delicious chocolate.

[![Green Apple Books bookstore in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20700%20466'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Green Apple Books bookstore in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Green-Apple-Books2.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10-Tourist-Destinations-Locals-Love-too-Green-Apple-Books2.jpg)

_Where:_ 824 Valencia St, San Francisco, CA 94110 (Mission)

## **8\. Green Apple Books in the Richmond**

Though big-box book retailers rule the roost in other American cities, San Francisco boasts many independent book merchants such as [Green Apple Books](http://www.greenapplebooks.com/), which has two locations in the city. The one in the Inner Richmond smells like knowledge and has nooks and crannies to comb through while trying to find the perfect read for the trip home.

_Insider Tip:_ Get some delicious dim sum at [Hong Kong Lounge II](http://www.hongkonglounge2.com/) before stopping by.

_Where:_ 506 Clement St, San Francisco, CA 94118 (Inner Richmond)

## **9\. Brunch at Brenda‚Äôs French Soul Food**

Brunch is one of the most popular kids on the block, and [Brenda‚Äôs](http://frenchsoulfood.com/) is the prettiest of them all. This is cajun comfort food like you wouldn‚Äôt believe, and it is worth the wait just for the beignets, the crawfish beignets to be exact.

_Insider Tip:_ Get there early. You also won‚Äôt regret smothering the house-made cream biscuits in their fresh strawberry jam.

_Where:_ 652 Polk St, San Francisco, CA 94102 (Tenderloin)

## **10\. Be a Kid Again**

San Francisco is an eternal home to Peter Pans, kids of all ages, and all those who refuse to believe that growing older means growing old. Where else can you eat at a [carnival-themed restaurant](http://www.strawsf.com/) before going to a [bar featuring an arcade](http://www.brewcadesf.com/), then climbing [secret staircases](https://www.yelp.com/biz/the-16th-avenue-tiled-steps-project-san-francisco) and staying out too late trying to find the perfect view?

San Francisco is a city for wandering and discovering. If you leave a little space in your itinerary to explore any neighborhood by foot, you‚Äôre guaranteed to find something wonderful. So get here already! What are you waiting for?
