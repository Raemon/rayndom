## Menu Options for Large Group Dining

When booking a private dining room for a large group birthday party or otherwise, the menu options typically fall into these categories. When booking your restaurant, ensure that they offer the style of menu you‚Äôd like for your party. 

  * **Prix Fixe Menu** : The most common option for private dining. The restaurant offers a set multi-course menu at a fixed price per person. Often, guests get a choice between a few appetizers, entr√©es, and desserts.
  * **√Ä La Carte** : Less common for private dining but sometimes available for smaller groups. Guests order individually from the restaurant‚Äôs regular menu.
  * **Family-Style Dining** : A popular option where large platters of food are served to the table for sharing, creating a more communal experience.
  * **Buffet or Stations** : Some restaurants offer buffets or food stations, especially for larger groups. This allows guests to serve themselves from a variety of dishes.
  * **Custom or Off-Menu Options** : Some venues allow you to work with the chef to create a bespoke menu, either based on seasonal ingredients or specific guest preferences.

## Birthday Dinner Dress Code or Theme

Check to verify if the restaurant has a dress code. Dress code varies greatly by restaurant, but typically falls between casual and business casual. Once you know the dress code of the restaurant, make sure to communicate this to guests so no one feels caught off guard or under dressed. 

  * **Casual (Come as You Are)** : Jeans, t-shirts, sneakers, casual dresses, shorts.
  * **Smart Casual (Polished but Relaxed)** : Dark jeans or chinos, polo shirts, button-downs, sundresses, stylish sneakers or loafers.
  * **Business Casual (Work-Appropriate)** : Slacks or khakis, blouses, dress shoes, blazers, midi dresses.
  * **Cocktail Attire (Chic & Stylish)**: Dress pants, stylish blouses, knee-length dresses, heels, dressy boots.
  * **Formal / Semi-Formal (Elegant but Not Black-Tie)** : Cocktail dresses, dress shirts with ties, suits, polished shoes.
  * **Black Tie (Ultra Formal)** : Floor-length gowns, tuxedos, formal dress shoes.



If the restaurant doesn‚Äôt have a strict dress code, consider adding a [themed dress code to your birthday party](https://www.thebash.com/articles/party-themes-for-adults). You can choose a theme based on the groups favorite series or movie (Friends), an era (‚Äô90s nostalgia), or keep it simple with a color (everyone wears all white). This can really make the event feel fun and special. However, keep in mind that the theme should be relatively easy to achieve. You don‚Äôt want your guests running around town, breaking the bank trying to find an outfit for your birthday celebration.

## How to Handle the Bill

One of the trickiest parts of a large group dinner is figuring out who pays and how. Here are a few ways to avoid awkward money moments.   * **Host Pays for Everything** : A generous option if you want to treat your guests.
  * **Host Pays for Food, Guests Pay for Their Own Drinks** : A great compromise that keeps costs manageable.
  * **Everyone Pays Individually** : Works best for √† la carte menus, but confirm with the restaurant if they can split checks. Can also be a little confusing and can bring down the vibe if there is any confusion about who ordered what. Use Venmo, Zelle, or CashApp to make transactions smoother.
