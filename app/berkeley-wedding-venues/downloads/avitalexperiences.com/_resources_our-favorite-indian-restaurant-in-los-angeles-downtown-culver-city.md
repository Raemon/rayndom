Perhaps the most popular Indian restaurant in downtown Culver City is [Mayura](http://mayura-indian-restaurant.com/). Like so many great LA restaurants it‚Äôs hidden in a strip mall. The food is regionally specific to Kerala, South India. _<http://mayura-indian-restaurant.com/>[  
](http://mayura-indian-restaurant.com/)__10406 Venice Blvd,__Culver City, CA 90232_

## **Samosa** **House**

[![Samosa is one of Our Favorite Indian Restaurant in Los Angeles Downtown Culver City](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2018/05/Get-Samosas-at-Samosa-House-some-of-The-Best-Indian-Food-in-Los-Angeles.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2018/05/Get-Samosas-at-Samosa-House-some-of-The-Best-Indian-Food-in-Los-Angeles.jpg)

Samosa lovers unite! This is your place. Head to [Samosa House East](http://www.samosahouse.com/) in Culver City. Though the westside branch has one of Southern California‚Äôs largest Indian supermarket, this location still has all the samosas, curries, chaats, and pakoras you want. _<http://www.samosahouse.com/>_  
_10700 Washington Blvd,__Culver City, CA 90232_

[![Don't forget the Momos when getting Our Favorite Indian Restaurant in Los Angeles Downtown Culver City](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20640%20424'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Don't forget the Momos when getting Our Favorite Indian Restaurant in Los Angeles Downtown Culver City](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2018/05/Momos-like-the-ones-served-at-Tara‚Äôs-Himalayan-Cuisine-on-the-list-of-The-Best-Indian-Food-in-Los-Angeles.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2018/05/Momos-like-the-ones-served-at-Tara‚Äôs-Himalayan-Cuisine-on-the-list-of-The-Best-Indian-Food-in-Los-Angeles.jpg)

Folks from Northern India love [Tara‚Äôs Himalayan Cuisine](http://tarashimalayancuisine.com/), which serves food a little different than our other favorite spots. Tara‚Äôs serves Himalayan cuisine from Nepal and Tibet and their mountain grown herbs and spices come directly from Nepal. Tara, the owner, is a classically trained dancer who wanted to share her love of Nepali culture. The momos (dumplings) are a must as are the curries.
