#  5 Best Places to Drink a Mai Tai in the Bay Area 

May 7, 2015 

Everybody loves a good story; it‚Äôs even better when it‚Äôs paired up with a delicious cocktail. Here‚Äôs a little bit of history of the Mai Tai Cocktail followed by the 5 best places to drink a Mai Tai in the Bay Area.

## History of the Mai Tai Cocktail

The Mai Tai ‚Äî which is the quintessential tiki drink ‚Äî delivers both. And it was created right in Oakland, California, just like the Hell‚Äôs Angels and the Black Panthers.

Vic Bergeron‚Äôs original 1944 Mai Tai recipe is well known now, but during the early days, old Trader Vic kept the exact recipe to himself. ‚ÄúIt was the drink everybody wanted to do and because it was a secret, everyone failed,‚Äù says cocktail historian Wayne Curtis. ‚ÄúThat‚Äôs why we have so many bad Mai Tais.‚Äù

The best ones stick to the original formula of aged Jamaican rum, fresh lime, a splash of orange cura√ßao, simple syrup and orgeat, a syrup made from almonds and a little orange flower or rose water. But what makes a Mai Tai really stand out is the atmosphere that goes with it, because the essence of a tiki culture is making you feel like you‚Äôve been transported to another place.

Maybe the search for the perfect Mai Tai is akin to the quest for the mythic tropical paradise that birthed the tiki culture. We like to imagine it as a place where rivers flow with Daiquiris, Scorpions are relatively tame and a Zombie apocalypse is a good thing.

Fortunately, a satisfying Mai Tai is much easier to find. If you want to experience the king of all tiki drinks, then here are five San Francisco Bay Area spots to check out.

## 5 Best Places to Drink a Mai Tai in the Bay Area

[![bar at Smuggler's Cove in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![bar at Smuggler's Cove in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Smugglers-Cove-Mai-Tai-Cocktail1.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Smugglers-Cove-Mai-Tai-Cocktail1.jpg)

[Smuggler‚Äôs Cove](http://smugglerscovesf.com/trapdoor/) ‚Äì As the name suggests, Smuggler‚Äôs Cove is rather rather obscure from the outside. Inside, it‚Äôs moody with pirates and rigging and water features. But don‚Äôt be fooled: you‚Äôre in a temple to rum and one of the most respected bars in the country, and they serve a classic Mai Tai to match. 650 Gough St., San Francisco, 415-869-1900.

![mai tai cocktail at Longitude in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![mai tai cocktail at Longitude in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Longitude-Mai-Tai-Cocktail.jpg)

[Longitude](http://longitudeoakland.com/) ‚Äì Right in the heart of Oakland, Suzanne Long has created an exotic pan-tiki escape. The verdant walls dotted with African masks, the staff‚Äôs game warden attire and the giraffe swizzle sticks add to the kitschy charm. What sets her Mai Tai apart is the housemade almond-macadamia syrup and the elegant Pierre Ferrand orange cura√ßao. It‚Äôs a Mai Tai that‚Äôs truly out of this world. 347 14th St., Oakland, 510-465-2008.

[![Forbidden Island bar in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Forbidden Island bar in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Forbidden-Island-Mai-Tai-Cocktail.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Forbidden-Island-Mai-Tai-Cocktail.jpg)

](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Forbidden-Island-Mai-Tai-Cocktail.jpg)

[Forbidden Island](http://www.forbiddenislandalameda.com/) ‚Äì This is retro tiki at its finest, with puffer fish, hand-carved totems, a cocktail umbrella art installation and even madcap movies from the era to put you in the right frame of mind. They make the Mai Tai two ways: classic Trader Vic‚Äôs style, plus a Hawaiian version that includes pineapple juice and a splash of dark rum. Which one is better? You be the judge. 1304 Lincoln Ave., Alameda, 510-749-0332.

[![Chinese Mai Tai at Po Cocktail Lounge in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Chinese Mai Tai at Po Cocktail Lounge in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Li-Po-Mai-Tai-Cocktail.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Li-Po-Mai-Tai-Cocktail.jpg)

[Li Po Cocktail Lounge](http://www.lipolounge.com/) ‚Äì If you Google ‚Äúdive bar San Francisco,‚Äù Li Po is on the first page of the results. But what dive bars lack in hip d√©cor, they make up for in authentic atmosphere and strong drinks. So, don‚Äôt be put off by the neon over the door, the tattered red lantern swinging overhead or the blas√© bartender ‚Äî Anthony Bourdain wasn‚Äôt. Order the Chinese Mai Tai, sweet-but-deadly mix of three rums, a Chinese liqueur distilled from sorghum and rose petals, plus a splash of pineapple juice. 916 Grant Ave, San Francisco, 415-982-0072.

[![Mai Tai at the Tonga Room in San Francisco](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Mai Tai at the Tonga Room in San Francisco](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Tonga-Room-Mai-Tai-Cocktail-2.jpg)](http://avitaltours.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Tonga-Room-Mai-Tai-Cocktail-2.jpg)

[The Tonga Room](http://www.tongaroom.com/) ‚Äì It‚Äôs a bit jarring going from the posh atmosphere of the Fairmont Hotel into the legendary Tonga Room. Imagine walking into the Love Boat or a commercial for Carnival Cruise Lines. Tune it all out and pay attention to your nuanced and balanced drink served in a ceramic coconut that seems to get frostier as you sip. You‚Äôll be smiling and maybe even singing along to R&B favorites from the ‚Äò60s to the ‚Äò80s. No need to be embarrassed; everyone else is doing it too. 950 Mason St., San Francisco, 415-772-5278.
