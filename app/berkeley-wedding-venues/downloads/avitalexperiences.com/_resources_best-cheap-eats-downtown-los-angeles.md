#  Best Cheap Eats in Downtown Los Angeles 

April 2, 2017 

[![Best Cheap Eats in Downtown: this one comes out of an airstream trailer](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/26248409220_bdef4fb382_k.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/26248409220_bdef4fb382_k.jpg)Sometimes you want to treat yourself to a fancy meal, but other times call for cheap eats. Luckily, Los Angeles is filled with lots of trucks, stands, and restaurants that don‚Äôt break the bank. Whether you‚Äôre looking for a quick bite (tacos, anyone?), or to sit down and enjoy something new, these are the best cheap eats in downtown.

## **Guisados**

[![One of the Best Cheap Eats in Downtown: Guisados](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20640%20480'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![One of the Best Cheap Eats in Downtown: Guisados](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/6949523259_14f658366f_z.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/6949523259_14f658366f_z.jpg)

Mexican food is a classic when it comes to inexpensive eating. Grabbing a taco or burrito off of a food truck is a basic part of living in Los Angeles. Lots of the best cheap eats in downtown are tacos, but an LA favorite is Guisados. The meat here is all braised or stewed, which makes Guisados tacos different than most in the city. The masa is handmade daily next to their Boyle Heights location, so your tortillas will always be fresh. Best of all? All tacos are $2.75, except for seafood tacos which top out at $3.00. Try our favorites-the Mole Poblano and Bistek en Salsa Roja. And for $2.75 each, you can probably get through most of the menu without emptying your wallet. 

_<http://www.guisados.co/>_

_541 S. Spring St. # 101, Los Angeles CA, 90013_

## **Wurstkuche**

[![Wurstkuche is one of the Best Cheap Eats in Downtown for its sausages](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20640%20360'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Wurstkuche is one of the Best Cheap Eats in Downtown for its sausages](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/5720653608_b995eb8d50_z.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/5720653608_b995eb8d50_z.jpg)

If you‚Äôre in the Arts District, Wurstkuche is a must for sausages. Don‚Äôt be fooled by the small front, there‚Äôs lots more seating in the back of the restaurant. Classics such as Bratwurst and Hot Italian are some of the most popular, and even vegetarians will find something they can eat, like the Vegetarian Smoked Apple Sage or the Vegetarian Mexican Chipotle. Wurstkuche also has gourmet sausages like Mango Jalape√±o and Filipino Maharlika pork sausage. The ‚Äúexotic‚Äù menu includes meat from animals such as buffalo, duck, rabbit, and pheasant. Best of all, all the sausages are filling and they‚Äôre all less than $9.00.

<http://www.wurstkuche.com/>

800 E. 3rd St., Los Angeles, CA 90013

## Rice Bar [![Ricebar is a favorite spot and one of Best Cheap Eats in Downtown](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20640%20359'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Ricebar is a favorite spot and one of Best Cheap Eats in Downtown](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/6330547994_d93843b889_z.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/6330547994_d93843b889_z.jpg)

If you can find a seat at this tiny DTLA counter, you‚Äôre in for a treat. Ricebar serves Filipino rice bowls made with heirloom rice from the Philippines and topped with local ingredients. All the rice bowls are filling. We love the Bisteg Tagalong, the Pork Longganisa, and the Mushroom Tamale steamed in a lotus leaf. The most expensive item on the menu is $11.00, and most are around $8.00 or $9.00, so you‚Äôll have some extra cash leftover for a coconut ice pop or homemade rice crispies. But the dishes are filling, so you might not have the room. 

_<http://www.ricebarla.com/>_

_419 W. 7th St. Los Angeles, CA 90014_

## Bar Ama

[![Bar Ama is one of Best Cheap Eats in Downtown](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20926%20940'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Bar Ama is one of Best Cheap Eats in Downtown](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/Screen-Shot-2017-01-30-at-6.28.58-PM.png)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/Screen-Shot-2017-01-30-at-6.28.58-PM.png)

Opened by downtown Los Angeles restaurant ‚Äúmayor‚Äù Chef Joseph Centeno, Bar Ama is a Tex-Mex homage to the women that raised him. The best and cheapest time to visit is during ‚ÄúSuper Nacho Hour‚Äù when the special menu is all $10.00 and under, including drinks. Frito Pie, Chalupa, Taquitos, and are served at the bar. The specials can be found Monday-Friday 5:30-7 pm, Monday-Saturday 10 pm-close, and weekends from 3-5:30 pm.

_<http://bar-ama.com/>_

_18 W. 4th St., Los Angeles, CA 90013_

## **Grand Central Market**

[![Best Cheap Eats in Downtown: Grand Central Market](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201280%20960'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Best Cheap Eats in Downtown: Grand Central Market](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/2273489260_4ce5aff6ff_o.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/2273489260_4ce5aff6ff_o.jpg)

****It‚Äôs impossible to pick just one favorite at Grand Central Market, but it‚Äôs definitely possible to find a plethora of cheap eats. From Eggslut‚Äôs breakfast sandwiches to bagels at Wexler‚Äôs Deli to soup at Ramen Hood, it‚Äôs almost impossible to leave here unsatisfied. We like to start our mornings here at G &B Coffee and stock up on something of substance before having a McConnell‚Äôs Ice Cream. Take some snacks to go, like an array of cheeses from DTLA Cheese or load up on carbs for the road at Clark Street Bread. 

_<http://www.grandcentralmarket.com/>_

_317 S. Broadway, Los Angeles CA, 90013_

## Pirozhki

[![Best Cheap Eats in Downtown: this one comes out of an airstream trailer](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/26248409220_bdef4fb382_k.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2017/01/26248409220_bdef4fb382_k.jpg)

If you grew up in a Russian or Eastern European family, then these pirozhki will taste like grandma used to make. And if you didn‚Äôt, you‚Äôll get to try something new and delicious. Served from an air stream trailer. These Russian hand pies are filled with meat or vegetables. At Pirozhki the fillings are beef, chicken, cheese, spinach, or potato. For just $5.00 a piece you can try a few and split them with friends in the seating area behind the trailer.
