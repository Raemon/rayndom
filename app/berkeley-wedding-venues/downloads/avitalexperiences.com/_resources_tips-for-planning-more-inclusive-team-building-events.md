#  Not-So-Obvious Tips for Planning More Inclusive Team Building Events 

April 17, 2024 

Inclusivity rocks. But that doesn‚Äôt mean it‚Äôs easy to pull off an event that hits your budget requirements, navigates tricky logistics, AND makes everyone feel included. ## **1\. Choose inclusive activities**

Ok ok, so this first one might be just a teensy bit obvious. But it‚Äôs so crucial that I felt the need to include it. Choose team building activities that are accessible and inclusive for all guests. Period. Consider things like walking/mobility aspects, timing that doesn‚Äôt conflict with family or religious obligations, and topics like food or nature that have a universal appeal and bring people together.

‚û°Ô∏è Check out our **[inclusive team building events](https://avitalexperiences.com/experiences/?_sft_occasion=teambuilding)**! **We accommodate all (yes, all!) dietary, allergy, and non-alcoholic requests.**

Slap ‚Äútask force‚Äù onto anything and I‚Äôm automatically in. Doing the Dishes Task Force. Yes! Waiting in Line Task Force. Where do I sign up? Watching Paint Dry Task Force. Umm, not convinced, but I‚Äôd like to learn more. In all seriousness, inviting employees to a team where they can collaborate on how to promote better inclusivity in events and the workplace overall can be hugely effective and valuable.

## **3\. Incorporate storytelling** üìñ

We‚Äôre big on stories, and for good reason. Sharing personal stories that highlight cultural heritage, identity, or life experiences can help build empathy and strengthen connections on your team.

## **4\. Build in team feedback**

You may think that your team building event is 110% inclusive, but that may not be how everyone attending feels. By creating an easy way for your team to provide feedback, you can learn from shortcomings and improve future events. Use Google Forms or a good old fashioned suggestion box. ü§ì
