#  5 Best Vegan Restaurants in Los Angeles 

January 15, 2016 

All stereotypes aside, New England has a much longer and more profound history of veganism than California. After all, there were actual vegan colonies in Massachusetts way back in the 1800s. But it could be argued that Los Angeles is where the practice of avoiding all animal products was perfected.

The rise of vegan culture in Los Angeles started in the early 1970s in L.A., when hippies started vegetarian restaurants such as The Source, a Sunset Strip spot that was immediately a celebrity draw. They made vegetarian food mainstream in a very Age of Aquarius kind of way. There wasn‚Äôt much movement on the no-meat front for a few decades ‚Äî until the 1990s, when, ironically, The Source closed. Then, raw food, which is almost always necessarily vegan, became a momentary trend, with underground raw milk ‚Äúdispensaries‚Äô operating in Venice, and later, restaurants like Cru and Juliano‚Äôs RAW opening in Silver Lake and Venice. (Venice always has something wacky going on, and its restaurants are no exception.)

Though ‚Äúraw food‚Äù as a concept may have been a flash in the cold pan, vegan food has stuck around in L.A., and vegan cooks here even push the boundaries of the dishes that can be made with plant-based products ‚Äî vegan cheese, anyone?

Here are our recommendations of the 5 Best Vegan Restaurants in Los Angeles to eat at:

[![Vegan Cuisine at Plant Food + Wine Restaurant in Los Angeles ](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/01/Rise-of-Vegan-Cuisine-in-Los-Angeles-and-5-Best-Vegan-Restaurants-to-Eat-At.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/01/Rise-of-Vegan-Cuisine-in-Los-Angeles-and-5-Best-Vegan-Restaurants-to-Eat-At.jpg)

**[Plant Food + Wine](http://matthewkenneycuisine.com/hospitality/plant-food-and-wine/)**

Owned by a company that has also created lines of vegan gelato, chocolate, and cheese, Plant has a big menu, including an entire ‚Äúcheese‚Äù section. Most options there are made with a macadamia-cashew mix, and one almond-based ricotta. There‚Äôs also a cashew raclette, if you‚Äôre into dipping. The pristine restaurant has marble-topped tables and lots of Instagram-friendly natural light, and the well-composed plates are photographable as well: roasted cauliflower with green harissa and pomegranates; socca (a Mediterranean chickpea flatbread) with fennel, radicchio, green goddess dressing and gremolata; and in what may be the hippest dish in L.A. right now, seared millet in polenta form served with spigarello greens and Rancho Gordo beans. It hits all the right meat-free, rare vegetable, local farm notes. http://matthewkenneycuisine.com/hospitality/plant-food-and-wine/, 1009 Abbot Kinney Blvd., 310-450-1009

[![Flore vegan restaurant in Los Angeles](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20508%20381'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Flore vegan restaurant in Los Angeles](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/01/Rise-of-Vegan-Cuisine-in-Los-Angeles-and-5-Best-Vegan-Restaurants-Flore.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/01/Rise-of-Vegan-Cuisine-in-Los-Angeles-and-5-Best-Vegan-Restaurants-Flore.jpg)

**[Flore](http://florevegan.com/)**

The thing to know about Sunset Junction, the intersection at the heart of Silver Lake and the home of Flore, is that its brunch culture is the strongest in the city. Saturdays, when the farmers‚Äô market goes on, are especially popular, and the sidewalks literally spill over with tables full of beautiful people. Flore itself isn‚Äôt much to look at, but it‚Äôs usually fully of artfully disheveled-looking people, many of whom you recognize from various screens. There‚Äôs a big reliance on seitan and tempeh here, which is probably tongue-in-cheek: the restaurant also offers a ‚Äúhippie sandwich‚Äù of avocado, cashew cheese, and sprouts. www.florevegan.com, 3818 Sunset Blvd., 323-953-0611

[![Moby at Little Pine vegan restaurant ](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20640%20427'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![Moby at Little Pine vegan restaurant ](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/01/Rise-of-Vegan-Cuisine-in-Los-Angeles-and-5-Best-Vegan-Restaurants-Little-Pine.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/01/Rise-of-Vegan-Cuisine-in-Los-Angeles-and-5-Best-Vegan-Restaurants-Little-Pine.jpg)

**[Little Pine](http://www.littlepinerestaurant.com/)**

![vegan dish at Shojin in Los Angeles](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201024%20682'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![vegan dish at Shojin in Los Angeles](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/01/Rise-of-Vegan-Cuisine-in-Los-Angeles-and-5-Best-Vegan-Restaurants-Shojin.jpg)

Recording artist Moby probably doesn‚Äôt take the easy way out on anything, so it makes sense that his vegan restaurant only uses certified organic products. No one is surprised to learn this brand-new (as of December 2015) restaurant is hard to get into already: it‚Äôs in the ritzy section of Silver Lake, where people can afford both regular nights out and a social conscience. Sweets are particularly good here: chocolate and strawberry milks made with cashew cream, lime ‚Äúcheese‚Äùcake, and s‚Äômores ganache are all memorable. www.littlepinerestaurant.com, 2870 Rowena Ave., 323-741-8148

**[Shojin](http://www.theshojin.com/)**

This mall is known for its raucous izakaya and Korean barbecue joints and a huge arcade, pool hall, and bowling alley, so quiet little Shojin is an unexpected veggie surprise. It‚Äôs a good option for people with food sensitivities, as the menu mentions which items are free of gluten, soy, sesame, onions, and garlic, and they‚Äôre happy to make substitutions. It‚Äôs a very non-flashy, homey place, with mismatched decorations and friendly servers bringing you sushi rolls and soup. This humble attitude has served Shojin well: they‚Äôve just opened a second location, in Culver City. www.theshojin.com, Little Tokyo Mall, 333 Alameda St., 213-617-0305

[![vegan meal at Gadarene Swine restaurant](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20617%20347'%3E%3C/svg%3E)![vegan meal at Gadarene Swine restaurant](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/01/Rise-of-Vegan-Cuisine-in-Los-Angeles-and-5-Best-Vegan-Restaurants-Gadarene-Swine.jpg)](https://avitalexperiences.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/01/Rise-of-Vegan-Cuisine-in-Los-Angeles-and-5-Best-Vegan-Restaurants-Gadarene-Swine.jpg)

**[Gadarene Swine](http://www.thegadareneswine.com)**

Named after a type of hog mentioned in the Bible (the ones Jesus cast demons into), Gadarene Swine is very playful. The chef is a self-aware omnivore who still serves meat at his other restaurant, but wanted to focus on vegetables at this Valley location. He eschews imitation meat and cheese, focusing instead on interesting ways of cooking and presenting fresh produce; relatively easy to do in Southern California. Try the tasting menu, or just relax with wine on the year-round patio ‚Äî another SoCal mainstay. www.thegadareneswine.com, 11266 Ventura Blvd., Studio City, 818-508-5500
