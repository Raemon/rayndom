## Presented by Fort Mason Center for Arts & Culture, West Coast Craft, Off The Grid & FMCAC Resident Groups

Make it a monthly affair starting with the Night Market 2025 at Fort Mason Center for Arts & Culture (FMCAC). Following the success of an inaugural night market that drew more than 10,000 attendees in December 2024, FMCAC and its partners, [West Coast Craft](https://www.westcoastcraft.com/attendee-calendar/wcc-market-sf-may) and [Off the Grid](https://offthegrid.com/event/fort-mason-center/2025-12-19-5PM), are teaming up again for the launch of a monthly  _Fort Mason Friday Night Market_ series.

The 2025 Market season concludes on Friday, December 19, 2025. This outdoor market transforms the historic FMCAC campus into a dynamic showcase of local artisans, cuisine, and entertainment. This free public event combines the San Francisco Bay Area’s premier craft vendors with outstanding food offerings against a backdrop of stunning waterfront views.

## Monthly Market Features

**_The Monthly Markets Feature_ :**  
· More than 50 artist and designer craftspeople, curated by [West Coast Craft](https://www.westcoastcraft.com/attendee-calendar/wcc-market-sf-may) (Outdoors)  
· 20 [beverage and food purveyors](https://offthegrid.com/event/fort-mason-center) curated by [Off The Grid](https://offthegrid.com/event/fort-mason-center/2025-12-19-5PM) (Outdoors)  
· Creative workshops from FMCAC residents  
· DJs at the Waterfront “Secret Spot” Lounge Stage between Piers 2 and 3 (Gateway and Festival pavilions)

· [San Francisco Children’s Art Center](https://www.childrensartcenter.org/) [Family Artmaking Workshop](https://www.childrensartcenter.org/community-events) in Gallery 308, Landmark Building A, **5:00 p.m.** to **8:00 p.m.**  
· Seasonal offerings from FMCAC residents [Radhaus Alpine Beerhall & Restaurant](https://radhaussf.com/), [Greens Restaurant](https://greensrestaurant.com/), and [The Interval at Long Now](https://theinterval.org/).  
· Public art installations throughout campus  
· Extended hours at [FLAX Art & Design](https://flaxart.com/) store (Building D, First Floor)  
· Stunning waterfront views and ambiance with seating at the “Secret Spot” Lounge between Piers 2 and 3 (Gateway and Festival pavilions)

# 

“We are thrilled to partner with Fort Mason Center, our longtime home base, and Off the Grid, to offer this Friday Night Market series to the Bay Area,” said Paulina Nassar, Co-founder of West Coast Craft.

“This collaboration creates an incredible opportunity for our community of artists and designers to connect with their customers in a new, vibrant format, shopping from exceptional vendors while enjoying the arts programming and amazing dining options that make Fort Mason a unique destination.”

“Fort Mason Center is known for being a gathering place, a space for people to exchange ideas, experience beauty, and connect with different modes of art and cultural expressions,” said Mike Buhler, FMCAC’s President and CEO. “We’re excited to collaborate with our partners to offer another way for people to come together, support local arts organizations and small businesses, and enjoy the natural beauty of the campus.”

_**NOTE:** Craft vendors interested in applying to be part of the Fort Mason Night Market should contact [West Coast Craft](https://www.westcoastcraft.com/apply) directly._

**About FMCAC** : Fort Mason Center for Arts & Culture (FMCAC): A decommissioned military installation converted into a nonprofit cultural center, FMCAC hosts a lively mix of arts, educational, and cultural programming. Each year FMCAC provides more than $2 million in support to local arts organizations, enabling groups to produce diverse and innovative artworks at the historic waterfront campus. With a four-decade history as an arts and culture destination, FMCAC is now focused on reinvigorating its programming and amenities to better engage the evolving Bay Area creative community.

**About West Coast Craft** : the premiere craft event in the U.S., West Coast Craft produces indoor and outdoor markets featuring hundreds of the Bay Area’s best artists and designer craftspeople. Since its launch in 2013, West Coast Craft has featured more than 3,000 artist and designer vendors whose works are inspired by the cultural and natural histories of the West Coast.

**About Off the Grid** : Founded in San Francisco in 2010, Off the Grid is a leading mobile food innovator changing where and how food is consumed and created by transforming underutilized and temporary spaces into community gathering places. Through public markets, corporate dining services and catering options, Off the Grid brings communities together around food while increasing economic opportunity for mobile food entrepreneurs. Acclaimed for reinvigorating the Bay Area street food scene through 60 public events that feed more than 100,000 people weekly, Off the Grid also offers a suite of B2B products and services, including Off the Grid Catering, Off the Grid at Work, and Cubert, a modular mobile kitchen.
