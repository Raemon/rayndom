## About

The Oakland Scottish Rite Center is a wedding venue situated in Oakland, California. Combining tradition and modernity, this historic facility provides an unforgettable setting for weddings and other special occasions. Here, couples and their loved ones will be able to enjoy luxurious furnishings, a welcoming team, scenic views, and opportunities to make beautiful wedding memories.

**Facilities and Capacity**

This venue boasts multiple options that can assist in bringing clients’ wedding visions to life, whether they consist of intimate gatherings or grand galas. Their rooms of varying sizes have the ability to accommodate groups of 10 to 300 seated guests. The Grand Auditorium serves as a memorable site for ceremonies, with room for up to 1,250 guests and full audiovisual capabilities. Celebrations with extensive guest lists may be held in the Grand Ballroom, with space to seat up to 720 people. This 10,400-square-foot room offers a decadent ambience, a full stage, and two projector screens, allowing clients to showcase their one-of-a-kind love.

**Services Offered**

The Oakland Scottish Rite Center is an ideal location for wedding-related celebrations of all kinds, including wedding showers, rehearsal dinners, ceremonies, and receptions. Aiming to help craft an enjoyable experience for couples and their guests alike, this venue’s staff is delighted to assist in any way they can. They offer items such as tables, chairs, dishware, and flatware on site for use during events. In addition, the team is happy to work with other vendors and suppliers to make sure that each client’s dream wedding becomes a reality.

**History**

Designed by architect Carl Werner, the Oakland Scottish Rite Center was built in 1927, with over 1,000 craftsmen working to complete its construction. Consisting of an ornate design, hand-carved ceilings, and majestic staircases, this art-deco building is one of the few of its kind that’s still in use. Since its opening, the venue has hosted a wide range of events, including birthday parties, corporate events, graduation ceremonies, presidential functions, state dinners, and more.
