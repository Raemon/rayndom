Ready to pop the question? With a wedding on the horizon for you and that special someone, you might want to save some coin on your proposal. If you're on the hunt for affordable proposal ideas that will ensure an amazing moment without spending a ton of money, you've come to the right place. 

Here, we've rounded up 20 of our favorite cheap wedding proposal ideas for you to browse as you decide how you'd like to ask for your partner's hand in marriage. And remember: when it comes to [planning a proposal](https://www.theknot.com/content/wedding-proposals-how-to-propose), it's all about creating a special and meaningful experience rather than spending a lot of money.

## How to Make a Proposal Special on a Budget 

According to a recent survey by [The Knot](https://www.theknot.com/content/top-proposal-trends-engagement#:~:text=According%20to%20The%20Knot's%20new,cost%20of%20the%20engagement%20ring!), the average amount spent on a proposal is an astounding $2,900, and that doesn't even include the engagement ring! You've likely seen plenty of lavish, personalized proposals on social media, but it's totally possible to plan an amazing proposal without spending a lot of money.

In fact, you can pop the question in a meaningful way without spending any money at all. No matter the type of proposal you decide on, the tips we've listed below can help you save money and put together a low-key proposal that's still amazing. 

  * Choose a free or inexpensive location
  * Hire a friend to take pictures or videos
  * Pop the question at home
  * Make it meaningful rather than lavish
  * Keep decorations simple
  * Pop the question during an already-scheduled vacation
  * Scale it back



Here, we've rounded up several free or inexpensive ideas you can borrow if you're looking for a simple way to propose marriage that's sure to steal their heart.

## Affordable Proposal Ideas 

You don't have to spend a lot of money to plan a proposal that your special someone will never forget. Whether you put together an [at-home proposal](https://www.theknot.com/content/propose-at-home) or head to a location that's meaningful, we're bringing you lots of cheap marriage proposal ideas you can use.

### 1\. Picnic proposal

One of the best [affordable date](https://www.theknot.com/content/cheap-date-ideas) ideas can also work for an affordable proposal! Pack a special picnic with each of your favorite hors d'oeuvres and a bottle of wine or champagne, spread out a blanket and plan a proposal in a relaxed and private setting in the place of your choice. In fact, your own [backyard](https://www.theknot.com/content/backyard-marriage-proposal-ideas) even works! 

### 2\. Recreate your first date

Bring things full circle when you pop the question at the same place you shared your first date, assuming it's feasible. Even if it's a simple setting like a park or a restaurant, this meaningful gesture is a great way to steal your loved one's heart without spending a lot of money.

### 3\. Take a hike

One of our favorite affordable proposal ideas is to use beautiful scenery as your backdrop when you pop the question. If you and your loved one [enjoy the great outdoors](https://www.theknot.com/content/outdoor-proposal-ideas), there's no better way to propose than to head out on a hike together and find the perfect spot for that all-important question. 

### 4\. Plan a scavenger hunt

Embrace your inner child when you plan and execute a fun [scavenger hunt proposal](https://www.theknot.com/content/scavenger-hunt-proposal) for your love. You can make it as simple or as difficult as you'd like, and choose whether to have it at home or somewhere meaningful. At the end, a sparkly bauble will await, along with a very important question. This fun proposal is a great option if you're looking for an inexpensive idea they'll never forget. 

![a couple collecting leaves ](https://media-api.xogrp.com/images/e9535a9a-75a6-49ef-ade6-f895d51bc6a5~rs_768.h-cr_0.200.1414.2085)

_Photo: Getty Images_

### 5\. Set up a romantic dinner at home

If you're on a tight budget or simply don't want to splash out on a proposal, there's nothing wrong with planning an intimate dinner right in the comfort of your home. Pop the question at the dinner table with soft candlelight for ambiance. Bonus points if you do the cooking! 

### 6\. Have a beach proposal

Or, head to the seashore! Especially if you live close to, or on the beach, a sandy proposal can be a great way to plan a memorable event without spending much money at all. Draw a heart in the sand or lay out a boho beach blanket and some cozy pillows as you take in the sunset together after the proposal. 

### 7\. Breakfast in bed

When it comes to free or cheap wedding proposal ideas, why not surprise your partner with breakfast in bed? This proposal allows them to stay cozy in their bed while enjoying their favorite breakfast foods. And won't they be delighted to find a ring on the breakfast tray too? 

### 8\. Propose at a museum

Many museums charge only a nominal fee for entry, while others are completely free. This makes them a great option if you're looking for inexpensive proposal ideas outside the home. Art lovers might appreciate a proposal in an art museum, but it's also a great proposal idea for history buffs of any kind. 

### 9\. Wrap up in a blanket 

This is more than just your standard cozy blanket for an evening together watching your favorite series: this fleecy blanket from [Personalization Mall ](https://personalizationmall.sjv.io/o4X39Y)is customizable with up to three lines of text with a price tag under $100. That's more than enough room to write one very important question, which makes it perfect as an inexpensive proposal idea. We're guessing this will be their favorite blanket for a long time to come! 

### 10\. Create a photo album

Here's a fun idea for a simple way to propose marriage. Create a photo album full of your favorite photos together, and have the last photo be of the engagement ring, or of you holding a ["Will you marry me" sign](https://www.theknot.com/content/will-you-marry-me-signs). What a unique way to propose! What's more, the album can become a very special keepsake for the two of you.

_Photo: Getty Images_

### 11\. Backyard proposal

Keep your proposal simple and intimate when you plan a backyard proposal. There are tons of ways you can do this, but if you're looking to save money, we suggest decorating with a few [proposal balloons](https://www.theknot.com/content/proposal-balloons) or use other inexpensive decorations like tealights to set the mood. 

### 12\. At an amusement park

Sure, you'll have to pay the entrance fee, but outside of that, an [amusement park](https://www.theknot.com/content/disney-proposal-ideas-romantic) is an adorable way to propose to your ride-or-die adventure-loving partner. Pop the question as a roller coaster crests that first steep incline, or get down on one knee at a popular photo spot and allow the camera to capture your partner's reaction. Top off the day with a memorable dinner or watching fireworks if the park offers them each night. 

### 13\. Go camping

If you're both lovers of the great outdoors, what better way to propose than over a campfire before you retire to your cozy tent for an evening under the stars? This cheap wedding proposal idea is so simple, yet so romantic! 

### 14\. Use a cake 

Satisfy your sweetie's sweet tooth by popping the question on top of her favorite confection. If you're kitchen savvy, you could bake a cake. But if you're not, simply purchase an undecorated cake from your local grocery store or bakery and either write "Will you marry me" on the top or have a bakery employee do it for you. In any case, this inexpensive proposal idea should do the trick for well under $50. 

### 15\. Do a puzzle together

Here's a great affordable proposal idea they're sure to get a kick out of. Have a friend take a picture of you holding a sign that says "Will you marry me", then send it to [Artifact Uprising](https://artifactuprising.sjv.io/3JjkBn). They'll make the picture into a [jigsaw puzzle](https://www.theknot.com/content/will-you-marry-me-puzzles) you and your special someone can work on together. Imagine the surprise when they see the finished result!

### 16\. Sunset proposal at a scenic lookout

You don't need a private helicopter to make a sunset proposal feel special. Head to a local lookout point, cliffside, rooftop or a quiet waterfront path and time the question for golden hour. Bring a thermos of something warm or sneak a bottle of bubbly in your backpack so you can say cheers together after the big moment.

![a couple amidst a proposal on a rooftop](https://media-api.xogrp.com/images/9f07b036-8571-4349-9d5a-640c9b155dc4~rs_768.h-cr_118.0.2003.1414)

_Photo: Getty Images_

### 17\. Write them a letter

Sometimes the simplest ideas hit the hardest. Write a heartfelt letter about your relationship, what you admire about them and why you want to spend your life together. Read it aloud, then get down on one knee at the end. No props, no frills, no pressure. Just real feelings.

### 18\. Holiday or seasonal proposal

Leverage a moment that already feels special. Just imagine holiday lights, fall foliage, spring blossoms or a snowy evening walk. Since the decorations and ambiance are already built in, you really won't need to add much extra.

### 19\. Proposal during a game night

If you're a competitive couple, sneak the question into game night. Customize a Scrabble board, Monopoly card, trivia question or charades clue so it ends with "Will you marry me?" If game nights are your thing, they'll love this unique (and affordable) proposal idea.

### 20\. Coffee date proposal

Turn your usual coffee run into something unforgettable. Slip a note on the cup, write the question on a sleeve or ask the barista to help deliver the surprise if they're game. It's low-key, adorable and perfect for couples who appreciate the everyday moments just as much as the milestones.
