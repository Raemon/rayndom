## Wedding Reception Venues in New Orleans

New Orleans is synonymous with music, Creole cuisine, and of course, Mardi Gras! Often described as the most unique city in the United States, it is a melting pot of different cultures and communities. This is where the free-spirited couple will find themselves most at home.

### Finding Your New Orleans Wedding Venue

If you dream of a quintessential New Orleans wedding, look no further than the historic heart of the French Quarter. There are a number of restaurants and cathedrals to choose from here that are bursting with life. You're sure to find the art deco, Gatsby-style venue of your dreams. The Garden District and Crescent City are also great areas full of stylish venues.

### Amenities in New Orleans

Each nook and cranny of NOLA is more beautiful than the next. You and your loved ones can stroll through the markets, cafes, and bars while also treating yourselves to the best of jazz and jambalaya. The city is also known for its haunted history, and there are plenty of ghost and cemetery tours for guests who want a more supernatural adventure.

### FAQs

#### What time of year is best for a wedding in New Orleans?

Spring and fall are the best times of year to have a wedding in New Orleans. If you're planning an outdoor wedding, it's best to have a backup plan, as the city can be rainy and has a hurricane season that runs from June through November.

#### How do you get a marriage license in New Orleans?

You will need a photo ID and two witnesses to obtain your marriage license. You will also need to wait at least 72 hours before you're issued one, so factor this in while choosing a date.

#### What is the second-line wedding tradition?

A second line is a common wedding tradition in the city where the wedding party travels on foot from the ceremony to the reception, usually following a live band. If you'd like to celebrate in true New Orleans fashion, parade through the streets and spread the love!
