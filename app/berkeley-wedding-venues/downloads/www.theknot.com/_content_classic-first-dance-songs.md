Looking for first dance songs? The music you choose for your first dance is one of the most important [wedding song selections](https://www.theknot.com/content/best-wedding-songs) for your big day. This special moment kicks off the [order of dances at a wedding](https://www.theknot.com/content/order-of-dances-wedding), usually taking place just after you enter the reception as newlyweds, so you'll need an exceptional tune for your first spin on the dance floor. After all, your first dance song will set the tone for the rest of your wedding music. (And if you want your guests on the dance floor all night, you'll need a good first dance song to start strong.)

Your first dance song should have significance for you as a couple, whether it's a tune that played during your first date, from a movie you both love or something that captures the essence of your relationship in song form. While there's no strict [first dance length](https://www.theknot.com/content/when-should-we-have-our-first-dance), we recommend finding a song that's short and sweet. 60–90 seconds is usually ideal, though that often means choosing just a _portion_ of your first dance song. One of the many [questions for DJs](https://www.theknot.com/content/questions-to-ask-dj) before your big day, you can ask them to help you get the timing just right, along with any [first dance tips](https://www.theknot.com/content/first-dance-tips) they may have. 

Whether you're looking for a country ballad, a soulful hit or [popular first dance song](https://www.theknot.com/content/wedding-songs-the-knot-spotify-playlist) that all of your guests can sing along to, we've compiled the very best marriage first dance songs to kick off your reception. Browse the all-time best first dance wedding songs below, along with standout lyrics and reasons we love them (and suspect you may too). After you pick your favorite, don't forget to ask your [wedding DJ](https://www.theknot.com/marketplace/wedding-djs) or [band](https://www.theknot.com/marketplace/live-wedding-bands) about your request. ## 1\. "Can't Help Falling in Love," Elvis Presley

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Like a river flows/Surely to the sea/Darling, so it goes/Some things are meant to be/Take my hand/Take my whole life, too/For I can't help falling in love with you"

**Why press play:** Arguably one of the best first dance songs at weddings, the tender message paired with Elvis's velvet voice makes it an unforgettable choice for a slow, romantic dance. Its gentle sway and heartfelt delivery make it nearly impossible not to fall in love all over again.

## 2\. "Then," Brad Paisley

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Now you're my whole life/Now you're my whole world/I just can't believe/The way I feel about you girl/Like a river meets the sea/Stronger than it's ever been"

**Why press play:** "Then" is Brad Paisley's love story about his wife, actress Kimberly Williams-Paisley. It captures the idea that love only deepens over time and is the perfect song for couples who know that their wedding is just the first page of a much longer story.

## 3\. "Amazed," Lonestar

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I don't know how you do what you do/I'm so in love with you/It just keeps getting better"

**Why press play:** This wedding favorite was written by songwriters Marv Green, Aimee Mayo and Chris Lindsey when the latter two first started dating. Their feelings were perfectly captured in the song—and the two are now happily married. If that's not a good sign, we don't know what is!

## 4\. "How Sweet It Is (to Be Loved by You)," James Taylor

**Lyrics you'll love:** "With sweet love and devotion, deeply touching my emotion/I want to stop and thank you baby"

**Why press play:** Originally recorded by Marvin Gaye, James Taylor's version turned this Motown classic into a soft rock favorite in the '70s. It's a feel-good song that will get people singing along and swaying to the beat.

## 5\. "Stand by Me," Ben E. King

**Lyrics you'll love:** "No I won't be afraid/Oh, I won't be afraid/Just as long as you stand, stand by me"

**Why press play:** Written in 1962, this classic has been a friendship anthem, inspired a movie title, and yes, has become one of the most popular wedding songs of all time. Everyone in the crowd will know it and be undoubtedly moved by its simple but moving message. 

## 6\. "Make You Feel My Love," Adele

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I could make you happy, make your dreams come true/Nothing that I wouldn't do/Go to the ends of the Earth for you/To make you feel my love"

**Why press play:** Though originally written by Bob Dylan, Adele's cover introduced this good first dance wedding song to a new generation. It was the only track on her debut album she didn't write herself, but she said it "spoke to her" so deeply that she had to sing it. It's tender and understated.

## 7\. "Halo," Beyonce

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I swore I'd never fall again/But this don't even feel like fallin'/Gravity can't begin/To pull me back to the ground again"

**Why press play:** "Halo" was inspired by Beyoncé's sense of finding a love that felt redemptive and safe—like light breaking through. Emotional and cinematic, this R&B first dance song feels like the start of something heavenly.

## 8\. "Perfect," Ed Sheeran

**Lyrics you'll love:** "'Cause we were just kids when we fell in love, not knowin' what it was/I will not give you up this time/Oh, darling, just kiss me slow, your heart is all I own/And in your eyes, you're holding mine"

**Why press play:** Sheeran wrote "Perfect" as a love letter to his then-girlfriend (now wife), drawing from real memories for the lyrics, making it feel wholesome and authentic—the _perfect_ way to kick off a wedding and marriage.

## 9\. "All Of Me," John Legend

**Lyrics you'll love:** "'Cause all of me/Loves all of you/Love your curves and all your edges/All your perfect imperfections/Give your all to me/I'll give my all to you/You're my end and my beginning"

**Why press play:** John Legend wrote this song for Chrissy Teigen just before they got married (and she even stars in the black-and-white music video). The lyrics capture the feeling of two people becoming one through an all-encompassing love—much like what a wedding dance symbolizes.

## 10\. "Maybe I'm Amazed," Paul McCartney

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Maybe I'm amazed at the way you help me sing my song,/Right me when I'm wrong/Maybe I'm amazed at the way I really need you."

**Why press play:** Paul McCartney wrote this raw, emotional ballad for his wife Linda shortly after The Beatles broke up—a time when he was feeling lost and leaning heavily on her love. He never released it as a single, yet it became one of his most beloved solo tracks. It's honest, powerful and vulnerable.

## 11\. "If I Ain't Got You," Alicia Keys (feat. Usher)

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Some people want diamond rings/Some just want everything/But everything means nothing/If I ain't got you"

**Why press play:** This beautiful piano ballad is all about what's most important in life—namely, that special someone. The remix with Usher transforms it into a beautiful duet, making it all the more powerful. 

## 12\. "The One," Kodaline

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Tell me, tell me that you want me/And I'll be yours completely, for better or for worse/I know, we'll have our disagreements/Be fighting for no reason, I wouldn't change it for the world/'Cause I knew, the first day that I met you/I was never gonna let you, let you slip away"

**Why press play:** Equal parts modern and heartfelt, this track feels like wedding vows wrapped in melody—perfect for couples looking for a first dance featuring a modern song. The soaring chorus is a goosebump-inducing moment that will have everyone smiling through happy tears.

## 13\. "Electric Love," Børns

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Baby, you're like lightning in a bottle/I can't let you go now that I got it/And all I need is to be struck/By your electric love/Baby, your electric love"

**Why press play:** While not necessarily a traditional wedding first dance song, this upbeat track captures the exhilarating rush of love—ideal for adding a playful, joyful touch to your day. 

## 14\. "(Everything I Do) I Do It For You," Bryan Adams

**Lyrics you'll love:** "There's no love, like your love/And no other, could give me more love/There's nowhere, unless you're there/All the time, all the way"

**Why press play:** Originally written for the _Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves_ soundtrack, this song became an instant classic that guests will instantly recognize and feel nostalgic about. Its swoony lyrics and cinematic feel make it a powerful, standout moment at any wedding.

## 15\. "Wonderful Tonight," Eric Clapton

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I feel wonderful/Because I see the love light in your eyes/And the wonder of it all/Is that you just don't realize how much I love you"

**Why press play:** Eric Clapton famously wrote "Wonderful Tonight" about his now-wife Pattie Boyd while she was getting ready to attend Paul and Linda McCartney's annual party when they were still dating. If that's not romance, we don't know what is! 

## 16\. "You're Still The One," Shania Twain

**Lyrics you'll love:** "You're still the one I run to/The one that I belong to/You're still the one I want for life/(You're still the one)/You're still the one that I love/The only one I dream of/You're still the one I kiss goodnight"

**Why press play:** Co-written with her then-husband Robert John "Mutt" Lange, this top first dance song is a celebration of Twain's relationship beating the odds. A crowd favorite, the country ballad is warm, romantic and has the perfect rhythm for a relaxed slow dance.

## 17\. "You Make It Easy," Jason Aldean

**Lyrics you'll love:** "You're my sunshine in the darkest days/My better half, my saving grace/You make me who I wanna be/You make it easy"

**Why press play:** Co-written by Florida Georgia Line members Brian Kelly and Tyler Hubbard, this groovy country tune was inspired by Hubbard's wife, who makes their relationship special, despite him constantly being on the road. The lyrics are all about someone being easy to love.

## 18\. "Unchained Melody," The Righteous Brothers

**Lyrics you'll love:** "And time goes by so slowly/And time can do so much/Are you still mine?/I need your love/I need your love/God speed your love to me"

**Why press play:** Originally written for a 1955 prison film (yes, really), this version by The Righteous Brothers became legendary after _Ghost_ turned it into one of the most romantic songs ever. The combo of its lyrics and melody makes it nearly impossible not to get swept up in your emotions.

## 19\. "At Last," Etta James

**Lyrics you'll love:** "At last, my love has come along/My lonely days are over/And life is like a song/Ooh yeah, yeah/At last, the skies above are blue/My heart was wrapped up in clover/The night I looked at you"

**Why press play:** For first dance wedding song oldies**,** you can't get more iconic than "At Last." Etta's powerhouse vocals and the song's simple but sweet lyrics make it one of the most celebratory love songs in history. 

## 20\. "Home," Edward Sharpe & the Magnetic Zeros

**Lyrics you'll love:** "That's true, laugh until we think we'll die/Barefoot on a summer night/Never could be sweeter than with you (hey)/And in the streets you run a-free/Like it's only you and me/Geez, you're something to see/Oh, home, let me come home/Home is wherever I'm with you/Oh, home, let me come home/Home is wherever I'm with you"

**Why press play:** If you're considering a modern song for your first dance, this quirky folk anthem celebrates love as the greatest adventure—great for couples with a free-spirited streak. The playful call-and-response vocals make it a crowd favorite for sing-alongs.

## 21\. "Best Part," Daniel Caesar & H.E.R.

**Lyrics you'll love:** "You're the coffee that I need in the morning/You're my sunshine in the rain when it's pouring/Won't you give yourself to me?/Give it all, oh"

**Why press play:** A good first dance wedding song for R&B lovers? Sultry and intimate, this duet feels like a private conversation set to music, making it perfect for a slow dance or quiet moment together. 

## 22\. "Baby, I Love Your Way," Peter Frampton

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I can see the sunset in your eyes/Brown and gray, blue besides/Clouds are stalking islands in the sun/I wish I could buy one/Out of season/But don't hesitate/'Cause your love won't wait, ay/Ooh, baby, I love your way, every day/Wanna tell you I love your way/Wanna be with you night and day"

**Why press play:** Breezy and feel-good, this '70s soft rock staple brings sunny charm and pure romance to any wedding playlist. It's the kind of song that instantly lifts spirits and gets toes tapping.

## 23\. "All My Life," K-Ci & JoJo

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Said I promise to never fall in love, with a stranger/You're all I'm thinking of/I praise the Lord above/For sending me your love, I cherish every hug/And I really love you"

**Why press play:** A soaring R&B ballad brimming with devotion, this song is tailor-made for declaring lifelong love in the most heartfelt way. Its emotional build makes it a powerhouse choice for a first dance.

## 24\. "I See the Light," Mandy Moore & Zachary Levi

**Lyrics you'll love:** "And at last I see the light/And it's like the fog has lifted/And at last I see the light/And it's like the sky is new/And it's warm and real and bright/And the world has somehow shifted/All at once, everything looks different/Now that I see you"

**Why press play:** Featured in Disney's _Tangled_ , this duet perfectly captures the magic of finding "the one" after a long search—sweet, whimsical and romantic, making it a top first dance wedding song in recent years.

## 25\. "Fly Me to the Moon," Frank Sinatra

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Fill my heart with song/Let me sing forever more/You are all I long for/All I worship and adore"

**Why press play:** This timeless hit, immortalized by Sinatra's velvety voice, was famously played on the Apollo 10 mission orbiting the moon—talk about out of this world! Its swinging rhythm and breezy charm will have guests swaying in time, cocktails in hand, feeling like they've stepped into a glamorous 1960s dance hall.

## 26\. "Your Song," Elton John or Ellie Goulding

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I hope you don't mind that I put down in words/How wonderful life is while you're in the world"

**Why press play:** Whether you go with Elton's warm piano ballad or Ellie Goulding's ethereal cover, it's a tender, intimate moment to share in front of your nearest and dearest.

## 27\. "Everything," Michael Bublé

**Lyrics you'll love:** "And in this crazy life and through these crazy times/It's you, it's you, you make me sing/You're every line, you're every word, you're everything"

**Why press play:** Written by Bublé for his then-girlfriend Emily Blunt, this great first dance song exudes joy and sunshine. Its jazzy swing and big-band sound make it the perfect choice to kick off your wedding first dance music.

## 28\. "Sweetest Devotion," Adele

**Lyrics you'll love:** "You will only be eternally/The one that I belong to/The sweetest devotion"

**Why press play:** Inspired by Adele's love for her son, this track radiates warmth, gratitude and deep commitment. With its soaring vocals and gospel sound, it's a modern first dance wedding song for couples who want to declare that their fierce love is here to stay.

## 29\. "You Are the Best Thing," Ray LaMontagne

**Lyrics you'll love:** "The way you move me, it's crazy/It's like you see right through me/And make it easier/Believe me, you don't even have to try"

**Why press play:** This horn-filled track has a laid-back vibe that feels like an old-school soul record you'd play on vinyl. Ray's raspy, heartfelt delivery makes it feel personal and authentic.

## 30\. "A Thousand Years," Christina Perri

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Darling, don't be afraid/I have loved you/For a thousand years/I'll love you for a thousand more"

**Why press play:** This ballad was originally written for _The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 1_ and though it was used in the credits instead of the wedding scene, it resonated so deeply with Twihards that it became a blockbuster wedding anthem in its own right. With the lush orchestra and timeless promise of "a thousand years," it instantly transports guests into a cinematic moment.
