## Wedding Reception Venues in Philadelphia

As the nation's former capital, the historic city of Philadelphia has a lot going for it. Although considered the birthplace of America and steeped in history, the city stays moving forward; it's also known as a hub for technology, enterprise, and culture, offering something for every visitor.

### Finding Your Philadelphia Wedding Venue

Despite its size, certain areas of Philadelphia retain a charming, small-town feel. You'll find this in its many parks and waterfront spaces, which offer some peace from the hustle and bustle of the streets.

If vibrancy is what you're looking for, though, consider a rooftop venue where the city skyline will form the backdrop to your wedding photos. There are plenty of options to set a chic scene for your big day.

### Amenities in Philadelphia

Immerse yourself in the nation's roots. Take a trip to see the Liberty Bell, check out the many museums, or admire the public art scattered across the city. If you want to soak up the sun, make the most of Philadelphia's impressive park system and visit one of its many green spaces.

When it's time for a break, help yourself to a Philly cheesesteak or root beer float - both of which were created here.

### FAQs

#### What time of year is best for a wedding in Philadelphia?

The best time of year for a wedding in Philadelphia is late spring or early autumn. If you have your heart set on a winter wedding, don't be surprised to see the thermometer hovering around freezing.

#### Who can officiate a wedding in Philadelphia?

Weddings in Philadelphia can be officiated by a justice or judge; a mayor of any city or borough in the state; or a minister, priest, or rabbi of any established church or congregation.

You can also perform a self-uniting marriage ceremony, which is a Quaker tradition, and marry yourselves in the company of two witnesses.

#### How do I get a marriage license in Philadelphia?

To get a marriage license in Philadelphia, you must book an appointment at the Marriage License Bureau in City Hall and bring two forms of ID each, and pay a fee. If you've been married before, bring a certified copy of your divorce decree.
