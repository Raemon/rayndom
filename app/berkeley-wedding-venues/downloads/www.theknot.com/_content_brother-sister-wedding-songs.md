Whether you've[ lost a parent](https://www.theknot.com/content/honoring-a-deceased-parent-at-wedding), grown up as each other's biggest cheerleader or simply share a deep, unshakable bond, dancing with your sibling to great brother-sister wedding dance songs is one of those rare and special moments you'll carry with you for a lifetime. It's a meaningful way to honor the person who's been by your side through every chapter of life. And while dynamics naturally shift as you step into marriage, the relationship you have with your sibling remains a constant since they will continue to be by your side for decades to come. The best songs for siblings can help capture that connection, turning a simple dance into a magnificent memory you'll both treasure.

When it comes to selecting songs about siblings for your wedding day, what matters most is choosing something that feels meaningful to both of you. For example, it could be a track you both sang together in the car on the way to school, a hit from a band you both loved or a song from your favorite family movie. But before you narrow down your choices, it's worth ensuring the[ lyrics are appropriate](https://www.theknot.com/content/inappropriate-wedding-songs) for a brother and sister dance. With that in mind, it's a good idea to steer clear of anything overly romantic; instead, opt for lyrics that celebrate friendship, loyalty or family.

Once you've landed on the perfect track, be sure to share it with your wedding band or DJ ahead of time so they're prepared and the moment goes off without a hitch. If you're still unsure which song (or songs) to choose, don't hesitate to[ ask your wedding DJ](https://www.theknot.com/content/questions-to-ask-dj) for guidance: Music pros are an excellent resource for selecting meaningful, appropriate songs for every part of your celebration. Still searching for the perfect sibling songs for your wedding day? We've rounded up some of our favorite brother-sister wedding dance songs to help you get started. From timeless ballads and country favorites to feel-good pop hits, there's something for every vibe and style.

## 1\. "You've Got a Friend in Me," Randy Newman 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "You got troubles, I've got 'em too/There isn't anything I wouldn't do for you/We stick together and see it through"

If you and your sibling grew up watching _Toy Story_ together, then you'll love (and recognize!) Randy Newman's "You've Got a Friend in Me" in a heartbeat. The sweet song tells the tale of true friendship and unconditional support.

## 2\. "Stand By Me," Ben E. King 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "When the night has come/And the land is dark/And the moon is the only light we'll see/No, I won't be afraid/Oh, I won't be afraid/Just as long as you stand, stand by me"

If your sibling is unquestionably loyal and devoted, select "Stand By Me" as your brother and sister dance song. Besides, nobody will have your back quite like your sibling does!

## 3\. "Lean on Me," Bill Withers 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Lean on me/When you're not strong/And I'll be your friend/I'll help you carry on"

Show your sibling how much they mean to you by dancing with them to Bill Withers' "Lean on Me." Thanks to its heartfelt lyrics about loyalty and unwavering support, the fan-favorite track has truly withstood the test of time.

## 4\. "I Hope You Dance," Lee Ann Womack 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I hope you never fear those mountains in the distance/Never settle for the path of least resistance/Livin' might mean takin' chances, but they're worth takin'"

This Lee Ann Womack track is truly timeless—and for good reason. Its uplifting, inspiring message about living life to the fullest has made it a popular song choice for wedding receptions.

## 5\. "Count on Me," Bruno Mars 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "You can count on me like one, two, three/I'll be there/And I know when I need it, I can count on you like four, three, two/And you'll be there"

This Bruno Mars song features a catchy chorus and an upbeat rhythm. All in all, the cheery tune is sure to put a smile on everyone's faces.

## 6\. "You're My Best Friend," Queen

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Ooh, you make me live/Whenever this world is cruel to me/I got you to help me forgive"

Your sibling is your built-in best friend, which is why "You're My Best Friend" is the perfect song for your brother and sister song. The sweet lyrics and catchy melody will have all of your wedding guests singing and dancing along in their seats.

## 7\. "He Ain't Heavy, He's My Brother," The Hollies 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "For I know/He would not encumber me/He ain't heavy, he's my brother"

"He Ain't Heavy, He's My Brother" by The Hollies tells the story of a supportive, devoted "brother," which can be interpreted literally or figuratively, such as a friend. Ultimately, the slow, sentimental ballad is ideal for dancing with your sibling at your wedding reception.

## 8\. "You've Got a Friend," James Taylor 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "When you're down and troubled/And you need a helping hand/And nothing, oh, nothing is going right/Close your eyes and think of me/And soon I will be there"

Your brother or sister was your first friend, which is why "You've Got a Friend" is especially fitting for a sibling song. James Taylor's cover of the classic song, which features beautiful acoustic guitar instrumentals, is oh-so-sweet. Its mellow rhythm is especially ideal for slow dancing.

## 9\. "Wind Beneath My Wings," Bette Midler 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Did you ever know that you're my hero/And everything I would like to be"

## 10\. "What a Wonderful World," Louis Armstrong 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I see trees of green/Red roses too/I see them bloom/For me and you"

Louis Armstrong's "What a Wonderful World" is a beloved jazz classic. The stunning lyrics remind us to take a step back and admire the beauty that surrounds us in everyday life.

## 11\. "My Wish," Rascal Flatts 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "If it's cold outside, show the world the warmth of your smile/But more than anything, more than anything/My wish, for you, is that this life becomes all that you want it to/Your dreams stay big, your worries stay small"

Calling all country music fans: This one's for you. Rascal Flatts' "My Wish" is an uplifting, heartwarming song, to say the least. It discusses all things true love, hopes, dreams and happiness.

## 12\. "I Learned From You," Miley Cyrus (ft. Billy Ray Cyrus) 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I learned from you that I do not crumble/I learned that strength is something you choose"

"I Learned From You" is a pop ballad performed by Miley Cyrus and her father, Billy Ray Cyrus. The two sing about the lessons they've learned over the years while also expressing their appreciation. The stunning harmonizing is an added bonus.

## 13\. "It Don't Have to Change," John Legend

**Lyrics you'll love:** "I pray to God that we can remain the same/All I'm trying to say is our love don't have to change/No, it don't have to change"

In "It Don't Have to Change," John Legend croons about his childhood memories and special family moments. Despite the changing times, Legend reminds his family—and his audience—that traditions don't have to change just because time has passed. All in all, the sweet messaging makes for a beautiful song for a sister and brother.

## 14\. "There You'll Be," Faith Hill 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "When I look back on these days/I look and see your face/You were right there for me"

"There You'll Be" shows off Faith Hill's powerful vocals, while the meaningful lyrics about love and gratitude will tug at your heartstrings. The string instrumentals add an extra-special touch.

## 15\. "Hero," Mariah Carey 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "There's a hero/If you look inside your heart/You don't have to be afraid"

Honor your sibling by dancing with them to "Hero." The uplifting ballad shows off Mariah Carey's famous pipes. Your sibling will be touched by the inspirational song lyrics.

## 16\. "Whenever You Remember," Carrie Underwood 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Whenever you remember, I'll be there"

Carrie Underwood croons about unconditional love, loyalty and support in this slow and steady piano ballad. The poetic lyrics will have you and your sibling shedding happy tears as you dance together.

## 17\. "Wildflowers," Tom Petty 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "You belong among the wildflowers/You belong in a boat out at sea/You belong with your love on your arm/You belong somewhere you feel free"

Any classic rock fan will love dancing with their sibling to Tom Petty's "Wildflowers." It features cheery instrumentals and a catchy chorus that'll capture the hearts of all of your wedding guests.

## 18\. "Mudfootball," Jack Johnson 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Nothing's gonna change there's no need to complain"

"Mudfootball" is a feel-good tune by singer-songwriter Jack Johnson. Its breezy, bouncy melody makes it a fun sister-brother song at a wedding reception.

## 19\. "Sister, Brother," F.J. McMahon

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Sister brother come and hold my hand/Don't let me walk away but help me stand"

This tender, stripped-down track is simple but stunning, withstanding the test of time. All in all, it's an understated, heartfelt choice that works beautifully for a low-key moment between a brother and sister, especially for those who appreciate '60s folk.

## 20\. "Forever Young," Rod Stewart 

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Be courageous and be brave/And in my heart, you'll always stay/Forever young, forever young"

Rod Stewart's "Forever Young" is a timeless classic that makes for a beautiful brother and sister song. With its heartfelt wishes for a life full of happiness and love, this track is perfect for your wedding celebration.

## 21\. "My Sister," Reba McEntire

**Lyrics you'll love:** "Wish we were kids again/My sister, my friend"

For a heartwarming and sentimental song about sisters, Reba McEntire's "My Sister" is a perfect choice. The song's lyrics reflect the special bond between sisters, capturing the essence of growing up together.
