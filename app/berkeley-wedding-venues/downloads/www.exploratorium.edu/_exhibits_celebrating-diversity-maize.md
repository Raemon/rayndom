##### Maize Under the Microscope

There are hundreds of varieties of corn. Also known as maize, ixi’im (Maya), centli (Náhuatl), or sara (Quechua), corn was domesticated from a grass called _teosinte_ by Indigenous people in Mexico thousands of years ago.

## Cooking with Maize

![Pib](/sites/default/files/styles/webp/public/2023-02/PIB.jpg.webp?itok=0gDKND4Q)

###### Pib is a Maya dish from the Yucatan in which a large bowl of masa (dough) is filled with a thick paste called k’ol, as well as chicken, onions, and epazote leaves.

![Chicha de jora](/sites/default/files/styles/webp/public/2023-02/chicha-de-jora-peru.jpg.webp?itok=A8_lENa3)

###### Chicha de jora is an alcoholic fermented corn beer from the Andes, made from various types of sprouted corn.

![Pupusas](/sites/default/files/styles/webp/public/2023-02/CoCocinado_las_pupusas.jpg.webp?itok=tXaY4JUO)

###### Pupusas from El Salvador are made from masa, and typically stuffed with cheese, pork, refried beans, or loroco flower buds.

![toasted-peruvian-corn](/sites/default/files/styles/webp/public/2023-01/Cancha.toasted-peruvian-corn-toasted-canchita-chulpe-corn-often-served-alongside-ceviche-close-up_127101-279_0.jpg.webp?itok=csDwcrrL)

###### 

###### Peruvian cancha is toasted corn, such as the orange/yellow ch’ullpi, which grows in the Andes above 2,400 meters (8,000 feet) in elevation.

![diverse varieties of corn](/sites/default/files/styles/webp/public/2023-02/alternative%20to%20corn%20wheel.jpg.webp?itok=h5dgBEoo)

###### Compare these diverse varieties of corn, each bred for a specific use or appearance.

![Native maize](/sites/default/files/styles/webp/public/2023-02/IMG_4074.jpg.webp?itok=_t3QYDln)

###### In Oaxaca, Mexico, Indigenous people domesticated maize thousands of years ago. Today they still grow more than 30 varieties. To protect this Indigenous intellectual property, exports of native maize from Mexico are tightly regulated.
