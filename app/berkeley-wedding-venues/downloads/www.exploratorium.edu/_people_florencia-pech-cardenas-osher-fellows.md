# Florencia Pech-Cárdenas: Osher Fellows

![Florencia Pech-Cárdenas headshot](/sites/default/files/styles/scale_image_500x600/public/2024-07/FlorenciaPech4.jpg.webp?itok=HcOWCrMh)

Florencia Pech-Cárdenas

Osher Fellow • July 2024

![Florencia Pech-Cárdenas headshot](/sites/default/files/styles/scale_image_500x600/public/2024-07/FlorenciaPech4.jpg.webp?itok=HcOWCrMh)

Florencia Pech-Cárdenas is a Yucatec Maya woman from the Yucatan Peninsula, Mexico. She is an Indigenous scholar and scientist that works at the intersection of environmental studies, natural resources sciences, Indigenous studies, international development, sustainability, tourism, and gender studies. Florencia is interested in bringing, highlighting, and centering Indigenous perspectives and voices into the forest management field and practice. She holds a BA in biology from the Autonomous University of Yucatan, an MA in biological sciences: natural resources from the Scientific Research Center of Yucatan, and is currently a PhD candidate in natural resources management and science: tribal natural resources at the University of Minnesota.
