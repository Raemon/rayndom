# Heather Knight: Osher Fellows

![Dr. Heather Knight surrounded by robots](/sites/default/files/styles/scale_image_500x600/public/2023-07/Heather-Knight_headshot.jpg.webp?itok=0ygj3nkf)

Heather Knight

Osher Fellow • February 2020

![Dr. Heather Knight surrounded by robots](/sites/default/files/styles/scale_image_500x600/public/2023-07/Heather-Knight_headshot.jpg.webp?itok=0ygj3nkf)

**Dr. Heather Knight** is a computer science professor at Oregon State University. Her research interests include human-robot interaction, non-verbal machine communications and non-anthropomorphic social robots. Her BA and MA degrees in electrical engineering and computer science are from MIT where she worked with Cynthia Breazeal on a touch sensitive skin for Huggable (an interactive teddy bear) and the Cyberflora garden project. She went on to earn a second MA degree and a PhD in robotics from Carnegie Mellon University. Following her Phd, Knight was a research associate at NASA Ames and a post doc at Stanford University. Outside of her core academic endeavors she has also founded and leads robot theater company Marilyn Monrobot, presents an annual [Robot Film Festival](http://robotfilmfestival.com/) and produces robot comedy performances. Past honors include being on 2011 Forbes List for 30 under 30 in Science, a [TED talk](https://www.ted.com/talks/heather_knight_silicon_based_comedy) about robot comedy, and the award winning "[This too shall pass](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qybUFnY7Y8w)" OK-GO music video.
