I usually perform a 2 step sedation process, as I find that giving just one sedative can sting and they fall asleep so quickly that it can be alarming to some. With a 2 step, there is no discomfort with the first injection and they gently drift off to a peaceful state where they are still aware of you, however they are pain free and comfortable. The exception to this protocol, only occurs in an emergent state where the process needs to be speeded up in order to avoid any suffering in the part of your pet. The final injection is given in a vein and is administered slowly in order to avoid any reactions. Unfortunately sometimes, due to illness and/or age, the veins can be compromised and then an area in the belly or chest needs to be accessed. Just know that if this were to happen, your pet is completely in a deep anesthetic state and will not feel or experience anything uncomfortable. This injection is very peaceful in that it goes directly to the brain centers that control the heart and breathing. Your pet will gently stop breathing.
